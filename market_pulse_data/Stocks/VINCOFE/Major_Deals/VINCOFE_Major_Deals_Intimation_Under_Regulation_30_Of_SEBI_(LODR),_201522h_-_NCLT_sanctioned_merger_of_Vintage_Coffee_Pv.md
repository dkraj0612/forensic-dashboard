22. 
In light of the rival submissions, the principal issue that arises for 
consideration is whether the Applicant has established any legal right 
or interest in the proposed Scheme of Amalgamation so as to entitle him 
to be impleaded as a Respondent in the main Company Petition. 
23. 
Before examining the Applicant's claim, it is necessary to advert to the 
scope of the jurisdiction exercised by this Tribunal while considering a 
Scheme of Amalgamation under Sections 230 to 232 of the Companies 
Act, 2013. The jurisdiction of the Tribunal in such proceedings is 
supervisory in nature. The Tribunal is required to examine whether the 
statutory requirements prescribed under the Act have been duly 
complied with and whether the proposed Scheme is fair, reasonable and 
not contrary to law or public policy. 
24. 
It is a settled legal position that while exercising its jurisdiction to 
sanction a Scheme of Amalgamation, the Court/Tribunal is required to 
examine whether the provisions of the statute have been complied with.

commercial wisdom of the shareholders who have approved the 
Scheme. The aforesaid legal position has been reiterated by the Hon'ble 
NCLAT, New Delhi in Accelyst Solutions Pvt. Ltd. v. Freecharge 
Payment Technologies Pvt. Ltd.3, after considering the judgments of 
the Hon'ble Supreme Court in Miheer H. Mafatlal Vs. Mafatlal 
Industries Ltd. (1997) 1 SCC 579 and Hindustan Lever & Anr. Vs. 
State of Maharashtra & Anr. (2004) 9 SCC 438.  
25. 
Keeping the above principles in view, we now proceed to examine the 
Applicant's claim. The Applicant seeks impleadment in the present 
Company Petition on the ground that, despite furnishing his immovable 
property as collateral security for the credit facilities extended to 
Transferor Company No.1 on the alleged assurance of allotment of 10% 
equity shares in the Transferor Companies, the said assurance has not 
been fulfilled and his property continues to remain under mortgage. It 
is on this basis that he claims a right to oppose the proposed Scheme 
of Amalgamation. 
26. 
We are unable to accept the aforesaid contention. Admittedly, the 
Applicant is not a shareholder, creditor, member or any other 
recognised stakeholder of any of the Companies involved in the 
proposed Scheme. His claim is founded upon an alleged promise of 
allotment of equity shares which, apart from a bald assertion, is not 
supported by any agreement, board resolution, allotment records or any 
other material to prima facie establish such right. Likewise, the mere 
fact that the Applicant had furnished his immovable property as 
collateral security for the credit facilities extended to Transferor 
Company No.1 does not, by itself, confer upon him the status of a 
stakeholder in the proposed Scheme. The rights, if any, arising out of

has no independent right to be added as a party merely because he 
claims an interest in the subject matter of the proceedings. The power 
under Order I Rule 10(2) CPC is discretionary and can be exercised only
