Dear Stakeholders, 
The Board of Directors of the Company, at its meeting held today, i.e., May 29, 2026, 
considered, accepted, and took on record the resignation letter dated May 12, 2026, 
submitted by Ms. Khusbu Prajapati from the office of Company Secretary and Compliance 
Officer (Key Managerial Personnel) of the Company. 
The Board, after due consideration of t he contents of the resignation letter and the reasons 
cited therein, noted the same and deliberated upon the circumstances surrounding the 
resignation. In the interest of maintaining transparency and providing appropriate 
clarification to all stakeholders, the Board has approved the issuance of the following 
clarification by the Company: 
With respect to the observations contained in the resignation letter regarding salary 
deductions despite approval of leave, the Company clarifies that although the leave 
applications submitted by Ms. Khusbu Prajapati were duly approved by the competent 
authority, the leave balance available to her credit was insufficient for the relevant period. 
Accordingly, the excess leave availed was treated as "Leave Without Pay" ("LWP"), and 
the corresponding salary deductions were effected in accordance with the Company's 
Leave Policy, applicable terms and cond itions of employment, and other prevailing internal 
policies, rules, and procedures. 
The Company categorically states that the aforesaid salary deductions were administrative 
in nature and were carried out in the ordinary course of business in accordance with the 
applicable policies of the Company. The same were neither arbitrary nor discriminatory 
and were applied consistently in line with the Company's established practices. 
This clarification is being issued by the Company in the interest of transparency, good 
corporate governance, and to ensure that the shareholders, investors, and other 
stakeholders are appropriately informed regarding the matters referred to in the 
resignation letter.

Subject: Resignation from the post of Company Secretary and Compliance Officer with immediate effect 
Respected Sir/Ma'am, 
I hereby tender my resignation from the post of Company Secretary and Compliance Officer of the Company 
with immediate effect. 
The decision has been taken owing to the repeated and unjustified deductions from my salary despite prior 
approvals and communications regarding my leaves. My salary for the month of April 2026 has been deducted 
for the leaves availed by me for travelling post my wedding, even though the same had been informed and 
approved well in advance by Shri Rajendrasinh Rana, the Chief Financial Officer of the company (reporting head) 
as well as Shri Ashok Khurana, the Promoter and Director of the Company. 
Further, my salary for February 2026 was also deducted for the leaves taken for my wedding preparations, 
despite the leave having been duly communicated through email and WhatsApp messages to the Chief Financial 
Officer and the Promoter and Director of the Company. 
Despite these circumstances, I remained patient and formally sought clarification along with issuance of my 
salary slips through my email dated 4 th March 2026. However, no satisfactory justification or resolution has been 
provided to me till date. 
In view of the above and the lack of fair treatment, I am constrained to resign from my position with immediate 
effect. 
I further request the Company to reimburse the payment made by me to Jhaveri Securities Limited towards
