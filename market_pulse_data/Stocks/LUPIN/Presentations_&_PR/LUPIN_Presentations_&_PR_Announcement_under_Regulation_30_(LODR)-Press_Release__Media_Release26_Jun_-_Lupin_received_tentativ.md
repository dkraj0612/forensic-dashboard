Lupin Receives Tentative Approval from U.S. FDA for Enzalutamide Tablets 
 
Mumbai, India, Naples, Fla, June 26, 2026: Global pharma major Lupin Limited (Lupin) today announced 
that it has received tentative approval from the United States Food and Drug Administration (U.S. FDA) for 
its Abbreviated New Drug Application for Enzalutamide Tablets, 40 mg, 80 mg, 120 mg, and 160 mg. 
 
The U.S. FDA has tentatively approved Lupin’s Enzalutamide Tablets, 40 mg and 80 mg as bioequivalent to 
reference listed drug (RLD) Xtandi® Tablets of Astellas for the indication in the approved labeling.  While 
Xtandi Tablets are traditionally available in 40 mg and 80 mg strengths, Lupin’s tentatively approved 120 
mg and 160 mg strengths will provide healthcare providers and patients with alternative dosing options.
