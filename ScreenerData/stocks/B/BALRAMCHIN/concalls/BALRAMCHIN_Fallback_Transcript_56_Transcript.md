### 👤 
Minimum Selling Price (MSP) of sugar was first fixed at Rs. 29 per kg in June 2018 and later increased to Rs. 31 per kg in February 2019. MSP is the ex-factory price (excluding GST and transportation charges) below which no mill can sell sugar in India. Due to surplus sugar scenario, the MSP environment is expected to continue. The Group of Ministers’ recommendation to revise the MSP from Rs.31 to Rs.33 per kg is awaiting Cabinet approval.

### 👤 
Along with MSP, stock holding limits on mills regulates the supply of sugar in domestic market which in return supports the local prices.

### 👤 
The export quota of 6 MMT of sugar from India was announced for the sugar season 2020-21, with a competitive WTO-compliant financial assistance. Recent interventions by both the Central Government and the State Government reflects a clear shift in the mind- set of policy makers which augurs well for the industry
