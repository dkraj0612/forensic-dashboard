Why does the company not revalue its older assets after its listing via the reverse merger route in 2020? The company has not revalued its Fixed Assets (i.e. Plant and Machinery) as the Indian Accounting Standard (IND-AS) allows to book fixed assets at amortized cost or at original acquisition cost less accumulated depreciation i.e. WDV value. There is no mandatory requirement in companies act 2013 to record the Fixed Assets at revalued amount. Even if we do the revaluation of fixed assets now the income tax law will not allow depreciation on the revalued amount. Therefore, our Fixed Assets i.e. plant and machinery stand at WDV value.

13. The tax outgo from the cash flow statement is not line with the tax liability, please kindly clarify the same as to why is it not reflecting in the cash flow statement? Any movement related to tax liabilities is captured under changes in working capital as part of operating cash flows. The difference between the reported tax liability and the tax outflow in the cash flow statement arises primarily from the timing of actual tax payments. In our case, a significant portion of the tax is paid post the financial year-end, based on self-assessment. As a result, these payments do not appear in the current year’s cash flow from operations but will be reflected in the subsequent year’s cash flows. 14.
