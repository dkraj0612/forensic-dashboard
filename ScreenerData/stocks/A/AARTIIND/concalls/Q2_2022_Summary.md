## Executive Overview

Aarti Industries Limited (AIL) reported a resilient Q2 FY23 performance amidst inflationary pressures on raw materials and utilities, global market uncertainties, and forex volatility. The company highlighted its expertise in managing complex chemistry processes and strong execution, leading to deepened customer relationships. A significant development was the NCLT approval for demerging its Pharma business into Aarti Pharmalabs Limited, with an expected listing in December 2022. The Specialty Chemicals business demonstrated revenue growth driven by stable demand in essential end-uses and volume gains, although demand for dyes and pigments remained subdued, with expectations of recovery from Q4 FY23. AIL continued its expansion initiatives, including progress on long-term contracts and brownfield expansions, with full benefits of ongoing projects anticipated from FY24 onwards. The company's R&D pipeline remains robust with over 50 products in development.

## Key Financial Metrics

*   **Q2 FY23 Revenue:** Rs. 1,847 crore (29% YoY growth)
*   **Q2 FY23 EBITDA:** Rs. 267 crore (5% YoY growth; >30% growth excluding FY22 shortfall fees)
*   **Q2 FY23 PAT:** Rs. 124 crore
*   **H1 FY23 Capex:** Rs. 577 crore
*   **FY23 Capex Target:** Rs. 1,100-1,200 crore
*   **FY23 EBITDA Guidance:** ~Rs. 1,100 crore
*   **FY24-25 Capex Target:** Rs. 3,000 crore
*   **Exports Contribution:** >50% of total revenues in Q2 FY23

## Business Strategy & Drivers

*   **Specialty Chemicals Focus:** Leveraging expertise in Benzene and Toluene derivatives and expanding into adjacent chemistries. Focus on value-added products (78% of sales).
*   **Growth Initiatives:** Scaling up Phase 1 and Phase 2 long-term contracts, setting up Phase 3 contract at Jhagadia. Brownfield expansion of NCB facility at Vapi. Tripling Ethylation capacity at Dahej SEZ (Rs. 200 crore, onstream H1 FY25). Debottlenecking Nitro Toluene capacities (targeting 45,000 tons).
*   **R&D and Innovation:** Over 50 new chemical products in the R&D pipeline. Focus on developing new value chains and high-potential products.
*   **Pharma Demerger:** Demerger of Pharma business into Aarti Pharmalabs Limited to unlock stakeholder value and enhance operational efficiencies.
*   **Industry Tailwinds:** Capitalizing on global supply chain shifts and domestic demand. Targeting emerging mega-trends in EV, battery chemicals, renewables, and synthetic foods.
*   **Capex Driven Growth:** Significant capex planned for FY24-25 to create new chemical value chains and expand market opportunities. Strong Gross Profit to EBITDA conversion anticipated from FY24 onwards due to volume ramp-up with relatively stable fixed costs.

## Management Accountability & Consistency Audit

**Audit of Previous Promises & Guidance (from historical context and current transcript):**

*   **Pharma Demerger:** The Hon'ble NCLT has approved the demerger of the Pharma business into Aarti Pharmalabs Limited, with expected listing in December 2022. This aligns with previous indications and is a significant positive step.
*   **Long-Term Contracts:** Phase 1 and Phase 2 units for long-term contracts at Dahej SEZ are scaling up as expected, with Phase 3 contract at Jhagadia expected to commission by end of Q3 FY23. This shows continued execution on securing and operationalizing long-term contracts.
*   **FY23 EBITDA Guidance:** Management has guided for FY23 EBITDA to be around Rs. 1,100 crore, with approximately half achieved in H1. This implies flat to modest EBITDA growth in H2 FY23, attributed to continued weakness in dyes & pigments and increasing fixed costs from new capacities coming online. This is a more conservative outlook than potentially implied by aggressive capex.
*   **ROCE/Margins:** While specific ROCE figures for FY23 are not yet available, management acknowledges margin pressure due to elevated input costs and demand slowdown in certain segments. They expect margin improvement as input costs soften and new capacities ramp up. The target EBITDA margin for new chemical products (25-30%) remains a key focus for future profitability.
*   **Capex Program:** FY22 Capex was Rs. 1,100-1,200 crore, with Rs. 577 crore spent in H1 FY23. The target for FY23 remains Rs. 1,100-1,200 crore. The planned capex for FY24-25 is Rs. 3,000 crore, indicating continued aggressive investment.
*   **New R&D Centre:** Not explicitly mentioned in this transcript, but the R&D focus remains strong with 50+ products in the pipeline.
*   **New Greenfield Site:** Construction for new initiatives at the Jhagadia site is progressing, with new product lines at the new location expected to commission from H2 FY25 onwards.
