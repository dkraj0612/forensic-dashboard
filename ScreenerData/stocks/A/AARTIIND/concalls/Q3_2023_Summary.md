## Executive Overview

Aarti Industries Limited (AIL) reported a resilient Q3 FY23 performance, driven by a higher contribution from value-added products, which constituted 81% of revenue. Despite a demand slowdown in certain end-user categories like textiles, AIL navigated the dynamic environment by optimizing its product mix and market opportunities. Key developments include a significant 20-year term-sheet with Deepak Fertilisers for Nitric Acid supply, valued at over Rs. 8,000 crore, ensuring raw material security. Furthermore, the demerger of its pharmaceutical business into Aarti PharmaLabs Ltd. was completed, with the new entity listing on January 30, 2023. The company reported a 12% YoY revenue increase to Rs. 1,854 crore and a 26% YoY EBITDA improvement to Rs. 289 crore for Q3 FY23.

*   **Q3 FY23 EBITDA:** Rs. 289 crore (+26% YoY)
*   **Q3 FY23 PAT:** Rs. 137 crore
*   **9M FY23 EBITDA:** Rs. 837 crore (Against a guided Rs. 1,100 crore for FY23)
*   **Q3 FY23 Production:**
    *   Nitro chlorobenzene: 18,199 MT
    *   Hydrogenation: 2,995 TPM
    *   Nitro-Toluene: 7,528 MT

*   **Interim Dividend:** Rs. 1/- per share (20%)

## Business Strategy & Drivers

*   **Value-Added Products:** Continued focus on increasing the share of value-added products (currently 81%) to drive profitability.
*   **Long-Term Contracts & Capacity Expansion:**
    *   Plant for the 3rd long-term contract commercialized by end of Q3 FY23 at Jhagadia. Ramp-up expected from coming quarters.
    *   Brownfield expansion of NCB facility at Vapi and other specialty chemical blocks progressing for H2 FY24 contribution.
    *   Ethylation capacity at Dahej SEZ expanding 3x (Rs. 200 crore investment), targeting commercialization in H1 FY25.
    *   Debottlenecking of Nitro Toluene capacities to increase by 50%, targeting H1 FY25 commercialization.
*   **Backward Integration:** Partnership with Deepak Fertilisers for Nitric Acid supply (effective April 1, 2023) eliminates the need for direct CAPEX in this area.
*   **Pharma Segment:** Demerger completed, with Aarti PharmaLabs Ltd. listed.
*   **Aggressive Capex:**
    *   FY23 Target Capex: Rs. 1,100-1,200 crore.
    *   FY24-FY25 Collective Capex Target: Rs. 3,000 crore, focused on new chemical value-chains and high-potential products.
*   **R&D-Led Projects:** Pipeline of 50+ products for new value chains, with over 3/4ths targeting new value chains, expected to commence commissioning from FY25 onwards.
*   **Import Substitution & Global Sourcing:** Positioning to benefit from structural China Plus One strategy and potential Europe Plus One opportunities over the next 2-3 years.

## Management Accountability & Consistency Audit

**Audit of Previous Promises & Guidance:**

*   **FY23 EBITDA Guidance:** Management is maintaining the Rs. 1,100 crore EBITDA guidance for FY23, despite some global slowdown, stating they will "try to reshuffle the products and everything" to meet it.
*   **Demand Outlook:** The expected recovery in demand from the first half of next fiscal year (FY24) is maintained, acknowledging the current slowdown in textiles and noting some global recessionary trends in other segments like auto.
*   **Long-Term Contract 1 Utilization:** Management revised the guidance for Contract 1, indicating that the previously guided 70% utilization by FY24 may not materialize due to slowdown in its downstream product (Dicamba Intermediate). Current utilization is around 30-40%.
*   **Long-Term Contract 2:** Performing as per original guidance, with EBITDA generally protected.
*   **Long-Term Contract 3:** Commissioned by end of Q3 FY23; ramp-up expected from coming quarters.
*   **Capex Program:** FY23 capex of Rs. 840 crore incurred in 9 months. FY24-FY25 capex target of Rs. 3,000 crore remains unchanged.
*   **Nitric Acid Backward Integration:** The planned CAPEX for a Concentrated Nitric Acid unit has been obviated by the Deepak Fertilisers deal. The capital will be reallocated to new chemical value-chains and multipurpose plants.
*   **Pharma Demerger:** Completed with the listing of Aarti PharmaLabs Ltd.
*   **Input Cost Pass-Through:** Management continues to have robust pricing mechanisms to pass on inflationary cost pressures and protect absolute profitability, with raw material and freight costs generally passed on to customers.

**New Forward-Looking Promises & Guidance to Track:**

*   **FY24 EBITDA:** Guidance remains at a 25% CAGR for FY24-FY25, but management clarifies this growth will be "more towards FY25 driven" as FY24 might be a "turbulent year" due to potential lingering slowdowns.
*   **FY25 EBITDA:** Expected significant contribution from new products.
*   **Long-Term Contract 1:** Utilization for Contract 1 is unlikely to meet the previous 70% FY24 target due to demand for Dicamba Intermediate. Evaluating downstream integration or conversion to other products.
*   **Long-Term Contract 3:** Commissioned in Q3 FY23, ramp-up expected from FY24 onwards.
*   **New Chemistry Projects:** Over 50 products in the pipeline, primarily for new value chains, with commissioning expected from FY25 onwards. Real contribution to EBITDA from new products is anticipated from FY26 onwards.
*   **Capex:**
    *   FY24 Capex Target: Rs. 1,100-1,200 crore.
    *   FY24-FY25 Collective Capex Target: Rs. 3,000 crore.
*   **Project Commercialization Timeline:**
    *   NCB facility expansion & Specialty Chemical blocks: Contribution from H2 FY24.
    *   Ethylation & Nitro Toluene capacities: Commercialization in H1 FY25.
    *   Chlorotoluene & Multipurpose Plants: Commissioning in FY25, with multi-block commissioning for Chlorotoluene over FY25-FY26.
*   **Demand Outlook:** Expectation that demand recovery will materialize in FY24. Global recessionary trends and slowdowns in textiles and auto are acknowledged. Agrochemicals demand is seen as molecule-specific with potential inventory correction lasting about a quarter.
*   **Margin Profile:** Newer projects targeting 25-30% EBITDA margins will begin impacting blended margins post-FY25 as their share increases.
*   **R&D Spend:** Approximately 1% of sales.
*   **Debt:** Gross debt expected to be around Rs. 2,600-2,700 crore by year-end FY23, lower than the peak.
*   **Tax Rate:** Expected to remain softer than the benchmark due to depreciation benefits from new facilities, SEZ units, and other tax swaps.
*   **China Plus One / Europe Plus One:** China Plus One is a long-term structural trend. Europe Plus One opportunities are expected to emerge over 2-3 years, particularly for energy-intensive products.
*   **Pharma Segment (Aarti PharmaLabs):** 9-month PAT Rs. 151 crore, EBITDA Rs. 264 crore, annualized EPS Rs. 22. Regular interaction for Pharma planned post-Q4.
