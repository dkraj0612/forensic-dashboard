## Executive Overview

Aarti Industries Limited (AIL) reported a solid Q4 FY17 and full-year FY17 performance, demonstrating resilience against a generally sluggish economic environment. Gross revenues grew 11% YoY to Rs. 835 crore in Q4 FY17 and 5% YoY to Rs. 3,053 crore for FY17. Net revenues saw an 11% YoY increase in Q4 FY17 to Rs. 773 crore and a 5% YoY increase for FY17 to Rs. 2,827 crore. The company maintained strong profitability with EBITDA rising 12% YoY to Rs. 153 crore in Q4 FY17 and 15% YoY to Rs. 608 crore for FY17, resulting in EBITDA margins of 20% and 22% respectively. Profit After Tax (PAT) grew 22% YoY to Rs. 307 crore for FY17. The Specialty Chemicals segment, a core growth driver, posted an 16% YoY revenue increase to Rs. 664 crore in Q4 FY17 and an 8% YoY volume expansion for the full year. The Pharmaceuticals segment showed improved EBIT growth of 23% YoY to Rs. 48 crore in Q4 FY17, with USFDA inspection cleared.

## Business Strategy & Drivers

*   **Global Partner of Choice:** Continued focus on enhancing customer relationships and meeting evolving needs through product innovation, Capex, and SH&E initiatives.
*   **Differentiated Business Model:** Leveraging a de-risked product portfolio, scale, integrated operations, innovation, and "green chemistry."
*   **Formula-Based Pricing:** Realizations are linked to raw material prices with fixed per-kg margins, ensuring profitability is driven by volumes and product mix.
*   **Capacity Expansion:** Strategic multi-pronged expansion plans are underway to drive the next level of growth.
*   **Operational Efficiencies:** Operationalization of captive co-generation power plants to significantly reduce power costs.
*   **Value-Added Products:** Focus on optimizing product mix and strengthening profitable growth momentum through increased contribution from value-added products.
*   **Pharmaceutical Segment Growth:** Aiming for revenue growth of 20-25% and EBIT growth to 14-15% in FY18, supported by increased engagement in regulated markets and API intermediate business growth.
*   **Home & Personal Care (HPC) Demerger:** In-principle approval for demerger, with discussions initiated with merchant bankers.
*   **Shareholder Value Creation:** Demonstrated commitment through a share buyback and a recommended final dividend.

## Management Accountability & Consistency Audit

**Audit of Previous Promises & Guidance:**

*   **FY2017 CAPEX (Rs. 4.4 billion):** Q4 FY17 transcript states Rs. 445 crore was invested, with approximately Rs. 60 crore on land and Rs. 385 crore on machinery. This is broadly in line with previous guidance, with minor adjustments for land acquisition.
*   **Q4 FY17 / Q1 FY18 Expected Commercialization:** The Nitro Toluene plant at Jhagadia, previously guided for Q4 FY17/Q1 FY18, is now expected for commissioning towards the end of Q4 FY17 or early Q1 FY18. This indicates a slight delay. The co-generation power plants and solar plants were operationalized in Q4 FY17 as guided.
*   **FY2017 Volume Growth Expectation:** While Q3 FY17 saw 10% volume growth, the full year FY17 volume growth was reported at 8% YoY. This is slightly below the earlier expectation of >10% for Q4 and a possible revision downwards for the full year.
*   **Pharmaceutical Segment (Full Year FY17):** Q4 FY17 was reported as better than Q3, and the USFDA inspection was successfully closed with EIR received, addressing previous concerns.
*   **Toluene Facility Utilization (FY17):** The Nitro Toluene plant commissioning has been pushed to Q4 FY17/Q1 FY18. The previous expectation of reaching 75-90% capacity utilization in 2-3 years remains a future target.
*   **Management Tone Regarding Headwinds:** Management acknowledged the impact of currency appreciation (INR vs. USD and Yuan) on profitability, stating it affects 10-15% of total revenue. This is a nuanced shift from the previous emphasis on passing on raw material costs, as currency appreciation can negatively impact exporters. They also noted some pricing pressure on calcium chloride due to the oil and gas sector and indirect impact from Chinese imports due to INR appreciation.
