## Aarti Industries Limited (AIL) - Q1 FY26 Earnings Call - Executive Summary

This report synthesizes the Q1 FY26 earnings transcript for Aarti Industries Limited (AIL), providing a concise overview of the company's performance, strategic direction, and forward-looking outlook.

---

## Executive Overview

Aarti Industries (AIL) navigated a complex Q1 FY26 marked by significant external headwinds, including steep raw material price volatility, geopolitical disruptions impacting global logistics, and evolving US tariff policies. Despite these challenges, the company reported revenue of ₹1,867 crore, a 16% sequential decline, with EBITDA at ₹215 crore (down 19% QoQ) and PAT at ₹43 crore. These figures were impacted by inventory valuation losses, temporary production issues, export deferments, and margin pressures. However, management emphasized that underlying demand remains stable, supported by product diversification and ongoing capacity expansions. Key upcoming catalysts include the phased commissioning of Zone IV projects from H2 FY26 and continued focus on scaling up strategically important products like MMA.

## Business Strategy & Drivers

*   **Volume-Led Growth:** Despite sequential revenue decline, underlying demand is stable. Management is focused on leveraging existing capacities and new expansions to drive volume growth.
*   **Product Diversification & Capacity Expansion:**
    *   **MMA:** Capacity expanded to 260 KTPA with further potential. Focus on increasing customer base and geographic reach, though new US tariffs pose a challenge. Management remains confident in volume growth and market leadership.
    *   **Nitrotoluene & Ethylation:** New capacities are in the ramp-up phase, poised to meet rising demand.
    *   **Zone IV Projects & MPP:** Phased commissioning from H2 FY26, adding new, higher-margin products. The Multipurpose Plant (MPP) and Calcium Chloride unit are expected to commercialize by December 2025. These projects are targeted towards advanced polymers, agrochemicals, and pharmaceuticals, with potential for 20%+ EBITDA margins.
*   **Cost Optimization:** Ongoing initiatives, including co-generation and yield improvements, are on track and expected to contribute to cost savings. Implementation is advanced, with full accrual expected over time.
*   **US Tariff Impact Management:** Acknowledged as a significant near-term challenge, particularly for MMA and DCB. Management is actively assessing impacts and exploring mitigation strategies. Products on exemption lists will be unaffected.
*   **Sustainability Initiatives:** JVs for Augene Chemical Pvt Ltd (UPL) and Re Aarti Pvt Ltd (circularity) are progressing as planned, with commissioning expected in H1 CY26 and early FY27 respectively.

## Management Accountability & Consistency Audit

### Audit of Previous Promises & Guidance

*   **Q1 FY26 Performance vs. Expectations:** The reported Q1 FY26 financials were softer than anticipated, with revenue and EBITDA declining QoQ. Management attributed this to unforeseen external headwinds (raw material correction, geopolitical issues, US tariffs) rather than a structural demand issue. This narrative of navigating external headwinds is consistent with the previous quarter's cautious outlook.
*   **Volume Recovery:** While overall revenue declined, management stated that underlying demand conditions remained stable, and volumes for certain products were impacted by temporary disruptions. The July MMA export figures (20,000-22,000 tons) suggest a recovery from geopolitical deferments.
*   **Capacity Utilization & New Projects:** The ramp-up of Nitrotoluene and Ethylation capacities is underway. Zone IV projects are progressing towards H2 FY26 commercialization, with the MPP and Calcium Chloride unit targeted for December 2025. This timeline is broadly in line with previous guidance.
*   **Cost Optimization:** Management confirmed that cost-saving initiatives are well underway and slightly ahead of implementation timelines, with full accruals expected to materialize over the coming quarters.
*   **US Tariffs:** The "mixed impact" described previously is now more detailed. While some products benefit from differential tariffs, others like MMA and DCB face headwinds. The explicit mention of a 25% tariff on MMA and its impact on affordability is a new, specific concern.
*   **MMA Market Development:** The strategy to diversify geographic reach remains, but the US tariffs have introduced a "speed bump," requiring strategic revisions and continued focus on developing other markets like Europe.
*   **Agrochemicals & Dyes/Pigments:** Pricing pressure persists in agrochemical intermediates, while dyes, pigments, and printing inks remain stable. Management acknowledges that improvements in downstream demand take time to reflect in intermediate pricing.
*   **DCB & NCB Volumes:** The decline in DCB volumes is attributed to US customer inventory correction and automotive demand uncertainty, with recovery expected in H2 FY26. NCB, primarily domestic, was impacted by operational challenges and customer-specific issues, with expected recovery.

### New Forward-Looking Promises & Guidance to Track

*   **FY26 Revenue/EBITDA Guidance:** Management reiterated commitment to the 3-year EBITDA guidance of ₹1,800-2,200 crore but declined to provide specific yearly targets for FY26, citing market uncertainties.
*   **Zone IV & MPP Commissioning:**
    *   **MPP & Calcium Chloride Unit:** Commissioning expected by December 2025.
    *   **Remaining Zone IV Blocks:** Phased commissioning from January to May 2026.
    *   **Full Ramp-up:** Expected from FY27 onwards.
    *   **EBITDA Contribution:** Full potential from Zone IV investment to be realized in approximately 1.5 years, with initial contributions from MPP and Calcium Chloride unit starting next calendar year. Targeting 20%+ EBITDA margins for these new products.
*   **MMA Business:** Target to utilize current capacity over the next two years. Focus on geographic diversification (Europe, India) due to US tariff impact. Affirmation of cost competitiveness.
*   **Plastic Recycling JV (Re Aarti Pvt Ltd):** Commercial operations expected to start in early FY27. Technology selection complete; pre-processing design and capex finalization in progress.
*   **Renewable Energy PPAs:** Solar component expected to commission Nov-Dec 2025; hybrid component expected April-May 2026. Actual accruals to P&L to start from these timeframes.
*   **Debt-to-EBITDA:** Sticking to guidance of reducing Net Debt by ₹200-300 crore in FY26. Net debt expected to remain roughly at the peak level of ~₹3,500 crore, with working capital optimization.
*   **Working Capital Cycle:** Expected between 70 to 80 days on a gross basis for FY26. Temporary uptick in Q1 FY26 due to deferred shipments and customer receivables.
*   **EBITDA Margin:** Management is not factoring in significant margin recovery for FY26 due to ongoing demand-supply imbalances. Margin improvement is contingent on market opportunities. Confident that profitability growth will track volume growth going forward. Profitability has "bottomed out" and should improve from here on a steady-state basis.
*   **Tax Rate:** Expected to be a bit lower than mid-single digits for FY26.
*   **Trade Payables:** Expected to increase by approximately ₹100-200 crore in FY26.
*   **CAPEX Allocation FY26:** Bulk of ₹950-1,000 crore CAPEX is for Zone IV. Maintenance CAPEX is estimated at ₹150-200 crore.
*   **PDA Growth:** Significant uptick in utilization expected due to increasing demand in the US driven by tariffs.
*   **NCB Growth:** Volumes were down due to operational issues and customer challenges; expected to recover.
*   **DCB Growth:** Recovery expected in H2 FY26 as US customers complete inventory correction.
*   **Nitrotoluene & Ethylation Growth:** Ramp-up process continues, higher utilization expected.
*   **Long-term Contracts:** Performance varies by end-market conditions.
*   **FY26 Volume Growth:** Base assumption is volume-led growth, potentially better than FY25, but significant margin recovery is not factored in.
*   **PAT Projection for FY28 EBITDA Target:** At ₹1,800 crore EBITDA, PAT could be ₹900-1,000 crore; at ₹2,200 crore EBITDA, PAT could be ₹1,200-1,300 crore (factoring in increased depreciation and interest).
*   **MMA Tariffs:** 25% tariff expected to be applicable, impacting affordability for end customers in the US. Mitigation strategies are being explored.
*   **US Tariffs Impact:** Detailed impact analysis provided for Phenylenediamine, Agrochemical intermediates (exempt), DCB (headwinds vs. European competition), and MMA (affordability challenge). Indirect exposure via domestic sales also being assessed.
*   **Cash Flow from Operations:** Number not immediately available, to be shared separately.
*   **Cost Structure:** Ongoing cost optimization initiatives are on track and expected to contribute positively. Approximately 60-70% of cost-saving initiatives to be implemented within FY26.
