## Executive Overview

Aarti Industries Limited (AIL) reported a resilient Q2 FY26, demonstrating sequential revenue growth of 21% to ₹2,250 crore. EBITDA surged by 36% QoQ to ₹292 crore, driven by improved capacity utilization and cost optimization. Profit After Tax was ₹106 crore, up 150% QoQ. Despite facing headwinds from U.S. tariffs on select chemical exports, AIL has proactively diversified its market reach towards Europe, the Middle East, and Africa, while recalibrating its U.S. strategy. The company reiterated its commitment to FY28 EBITDA aspirations, focusing on cost optimization, volume ramp-up, and monetizing ongoing capex projects. Key developments include the commissioning of a Calcium Chloride facility and a multipurpose plant (MPP) in Zone 4, alongside progress on the PEDA project and a strategic partnership with DCM Shriram for chlorine supply.

## Key Financial Metrics

*   **Q2 FY26 Revenue:** ₹2,250 crore (+21% QoQ)
*   **Q2 FY26 EBITDA:** ₹292 crore (+36% QoQ)
*   **Q2 FY26 PAT:** ₹106 crore (+150% QoQ)
*   **Q2 FY26 CAPEX:** ₹267 crore
*   **Full Year FY26 Planned CAPEX:** ₹1,000 crore
*   **Export Mix:** >60% (increased due to global diversification efforts)

## Business Strategy & Drivers

*   **Market Diversification:** Proactive shift towards Europe, Middle East, and Africa to mitigate U.S. tariff impacts.
*   **Capacity Expansion & Commissioning:**
    *   Calcium Chloride facility commissioned in Q2 FY26.
    *   Multipurpose Plant (MPP) in Zone 4 targeted for commissioning in Q4 FY26.
    *   PEDA (2-Phenyl Ethyl Diethyl Aniline) project (4,000 TPA) in Zone 4 to be commissioned.
    *   Zone 4 expansion project progressing with 5 blocks to be commissioned sequentially over FY27.
    *   MMA capacity expansion through debottlenecking in Q4 FY26.
*   **Innovation & R&D:** Intensified efforts in advanced materials, polymer chemistry, and sunrise applications; development of CDMO capabilities.
*   **Strategic Partnerships:** Long-term partnership with DCM Shriram for exclusive chlorine supply to Zone 4 facility.
*   **Cost Optimization:** Ongoing initiatives, with significant benefits expected from renewable power purchase agreements and raw material contracts in FY27.

## Management Accountability & Consistency Audit

### Audit of Previous Promises & Guidance

*   **FY25 EBITDA Guidance:** Management had previously withdrawn the FY25 EBITDA guidance. The current Q2 FY26 performance of ₹292 crore EBITDA indicates continued operational leverage, with management suggesting current performance levels could be considered "steady-state" without further project commissioning.
*   **Volume Growth:** Management reiterated confidence in achieving volume growth, with specific projects like MMA debottlenecking and Zone 4 blocks contributing. The external environment remains volatile, but internal execution is a focus.
*   **Project Commissioning:**
    *   Calcium Chloride facility commissioned in Q2 FY26.
    *   MPP in Zone 4 commissioned by Q4 FY26.
    *   PEDA project commissioned.
    *   Zone 4 blocks (chlorotoluene value chain) phased commissioning from FY27.
*   **U.S. Tariffs:** Management acknowledged that U.S. tariffs weighed on volumes and margins in Q2 FY26, particularly impacting the Polymer business and DCB chain. Proactive renegotiations and market diversification efforts are underway. The company stated it did witness "pain" from the U.S. in Q2, with a lower share of U.S. business compared to Q1, which was offset by sales in other geographies.
*   **Energy Application Segment (MMA):** MMA delivered strong QoQ volumes, driven by spill-over demand and geographical diversification. However, U.S. tariffs impacted volumes and margins. Renegotiations are in progress. Management confirmed that the energy segment's contribution is expected to settle around 30-40% in the long term, down from the current 43%.
*   **Agrochemicals Segment:** Select agrochemical products are showing promising volume recovery, but margins remain under pressure due to intense competitive pricing from China.
*   **Cost Cutting Initiatives:** The previously guided INR150-200 crore cost-cutting program is completed in terms of action, but approximately 40-50% of the benefits are yet to flow through the bottom line, with significant contributions expected from renewable power purchase agreements in FY27.

### New Forward-Looking Promises & Guidance to Track

*   **FY27 Capex:** Substantially lower than FY26's ₹1,000 crore.
*   **Tax Rate:** Anticipated to be below 15% for FY27 and between 15-20% for FY28.
*   **Zone 4 Commissioning:** 5 blocks commissioned sequentially over FY27.
*   **PEDA Project:** 4,000 TPA capacity, utilization ramp-up expected to take time due to customer qualification cycles.
*   **MMA Capacity:** Further debottlenecking initiatives underway to scale volumes from Q4 FY26; peak utilization achieved for expanded capacities.
*   **Energy Segment Contribution:** Expected to settle around 30-40% in the long term.
*   **U.S. Market Strategy:** Recalibrating U.S. strategy to sustain long-term growth, with continued focus and potential resumption of volumes as trade normalization occurs. Ex-tariff, U.S. volumes generally have a better margin profile than other geographies.
*   **CDMO Opportunities:** Initial success with 3-4 R&D level projects; conversion to large-scale manufacturing is an estimated 18-24 month journey.
*   **Debt-to-EBITDA:** Management believes the peak in debt-to-EBITDA may have been reached and expects improvement going forward, with a focus on medium-scale capex projects in the next 2-3 years.
*   **Working Capital Cycle:** Expected to remain in the range of 45-50 days, with a shift from inventory reduction to receivable increase due to higher export mix.
