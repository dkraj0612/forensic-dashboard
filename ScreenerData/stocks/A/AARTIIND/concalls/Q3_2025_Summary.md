## Executive Overview

Aarti Industries Limited (AIL) reported a resilient Q3 FY25, demonstrating sequential revenue growth of 8% to ₹2,035 crore. EBITDA grew by 17% QoQ to ₹236 crore, driven by strong volume growth in both non-energy (14% YoY, 8% QoQ) and energy businesses (10% QoQ). Profit After Tax was ₹46 crore, impacted by a ₹23 crore forex mark-to-market loss on an ECB loan due to rupee depreciation. Despite ongoing pricing pressures, particularly in agrochemical intermediates, AIL is focusing on cost efficiencies, product diversification, and geographic expansion. Key developments include the commissioning of its nitrotoluene plant expansion (30 to 45 KTPA) and ethylation facility expansion (8 to 10 KTPA to 25 to 30 KTPA). The company also announced two major sustainability initiatives: renewable energy power purchase agreements (PPAs) expected to bring renewable share to over 75% by Q1 FY27, and a joint venture for plastic material recycling using advanced chemical recycling technology, targeting 500 tons per day capacity by 2030. AIL reiterated its confidence in its medium-term guidance, targeting 20-25% EBITDA CAGR over 3-5 years.

## Business Strategy & Drivers

*   **Volume-Led Growth:** Focus on driving volume in both energy (despite spill-over) and non-energy segments, with non-energy volumes up 14% YoY and 8% QoQ.
*   **Market Diversification & Geographic Expansion:** Continued focus on exports to the U.S., Europe, and the Middle East, particularly for MMA, to mitigate regional slowdowns and pricing pressures.
*   **Product Diversification & Backward Integration:** Development of new products, especially within the ethylation value chain, and pursuing backward integration into select downstream products to enhance margins and supply control.
*   **Capacity Expansion & Commissioning:**
    *   Nitrotoluene (NT) plant expansion (30 to 45 KTPA) commissioned in Q3 FY25.
    *   Ethylation facility expansion (8-10 KTPA to 25-30 KTPA) commissioned in Q3 FY25, providing flexibility.
    *   MMA capacity expansion to 200 KTPA completed.
    *   Zone IV greenfield site: Pilot plant operations commenced; further blocks expected to commission sequentially through FY26 and FY27, focusing on chlorotoluene, dichlorotoluene, and toluene photochlorination value chains, catering to agro, pharma, and dyes.
*   **Sustainability Initiatives:**
    *   Signed renewable energy PPAs with Cleanmax and Prozeal, aiming for over 75% renewable power share by Q1 FY27, with significant cost savings expected.
    *   Joint venture for plastic material recycling using advanced chemical recycling technology, targeting 500 TPD capacity by 2030.
*   **Cost Optimization:** Progressing on cost reduction initiatives, with significant benefits expected from renewable energy and raw material contracts in FY27.
*   **Innovation & R&D:** Developing new products, particularly in the ethylation value chain, and focusing on CDMO opportunities.

## Management Accountability & Consistency Audit

### Audit of Previous Promises & Guidance

*   **FY25 EBITDA Guidance:** The company has not provided specific EBITDA guidance but is on track to meet previous short- to medium-term guidance. The management reiterated confidence in achieving its EBITDA target of ₹1,800 - ₹2,200 crore for the medium term (3-5 years), implying a 20-25% CAGR.
*   **Volume Growth:** Confirmed strong volume growth in Q3 FY25, with non-energy up 14% YoY and 8% QoQ, and energy up 10% QoQ. This aligns with previous confidence in volume ramp-up.
*   **Project Commissioning:**
    *   Nitrotoluene plant expansion commissioned in Q3 FY25.
    *   Ethylation facility expansion commissioned in Q3 FY25.
    *   MMA capacity expansion to 200 KTPA is complete.
    *   Zone IV commissioning is progressing with the pilot plant operational; phased commissioning of other blocks expected through FY26-FY27.
*   **U.S. Tariffs:** While not directly addressed in this quarter's transcript, the previous mention of U.S. tariffs impacting volumes and margins seems to be implicitly managed through geographic diversification and a focus on increasing market share in global markets like the U.S. and Japan. The company is recalibrating its U.S. strategy.
*   **Energy Segment (MMA):** Management acknowledged that weak gasoline and naphtha cracks in Q3 impacted octane boosting economics but anticipates improvement in Q4. They are diversifying offerings and customer base and have established bulk shipment capabilities. The ramp-up is expected to be gradual but consistent. The earlier mention of the energy segment settling around 30-40% long-term contribution is not directly revisited, but the focus on MMA's strategic importance remains.
*   **Agrochemicals Segment:** Pricing pressure continues, especially due to competition from China, with margins remaining under pressure in this segment. Inventory levels for agrochemicals are believed to have normalized.
*   **Cost Cutting Initiatives:** Management confirmed they are 30-40% through their cost reduction plans, with the full program expected to be completed within 12-18 months. Previously guided INR150-200 crore savings are being actively pursued.

### New Forward-Looking Promises & Guidance to Track

*   **FY26 CAPEX:** Expected to be lower than FY25's ₹1,300-1,500 crore, with an indication of being lower than ₹1,000 crore.
*   **Tax Rate:** Expected to be marginally negative or closer to 0 for FY25 due to depreciation benefits, and in the low single-digit range for FY26.
*   **Zone IV Commissioning:** Pilot plant operational; remaining blocks commissioned sequentially through FY26 and FY27. Focus on flexible, multi-purpose production of chlorotoluene, dichlorotoluene, and related downstream products.
*   **MMA Business:**
    *   Anticipate improvement in Q4 due to better gasoline-naphtha spreads and established bulk shipping capabilities.
    *   Aiming to thrive even at historical average gasoline-naphtha spreads, not just the unusually high levels seen post-Russia-Ukraine war.
    *   Ramp-up in volumes is expected to be gradual.
*   **Plastic Recycling JV:** Aspiration to complete 100 TPD in the first 18 months and 500 TPD by 2030. Initial capital commitment of INR100 crore (INR50 crore each) for the first year.
*   **Renewable Energy PPAs:** Renewable share to exceed 75% by Q1 FY27. One contract starts within CY2024, the second in Q1 FY27.
*   **Debt-to-EBITDA:** While not explicitly stated, the net debt remains around ₹3,600 crore. Management's historical confidence in improving debt-to-EBITDA is implied by growth targets.
*   **Working Capital Cycle:** No specific update, but previously indicated to remain in the range of 45-50 days.
*   **EBITDA Margin:** Mid-term target of 14-15% for ROCE/ROIC. Current EBITDA margins are around 11.6% (₹236 Cr / ₹2035 Cr).
*   **Gross Margin:** Expected to improve in subsequent quarters due to improving product mix and normalization of inventory impacts (like aniline build-up in Q3). The more representative gross margin is estimated to be in the 13% range.
*   **ECB Loan:** Approximately $140 million exposure is largely unhedged, with repayment over 9 years.
*   **Nitrotoluene (NT) Chain:** Expect improvement in capacity utilization and numbers in Q4 FY25 following commissioning.
*   **Agrochemicals Competition:** Pricing pressure from China persists, particularly in export markets.
*   **PNCB Business:** Strong double-digit growth in the domestic market is expected, with capacity utilization around 75-80% for NCB.
*   **Fluorochemicals:** New product developments involve complex chemistry (4-6 steps) with potentially improved margin profiles over bulk chemicals.
*   **MMA Pollution Profile:** No adverse impact on customers; blended at <1-2% in fuel. USP is high octane number (400+). Not anticipated to be banned, with scaling usage as handling education increases.
*   **JV Capital Commitment:** INR100 crore for the first year for the plastic recycling JV.
