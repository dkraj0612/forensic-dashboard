## Executive Overview

Aarti Industries Limited (AIL) reported a strong Q4 FY22, driven by robust revenue growth of 58% to Rs. 7,919 crore and a 96% increase in EBITDA to Rs. 1,930 crore. This performance was bolstered by a Rs. 631 crore termination fee from a long-term contract. Excluding this one-off, revenue stood at Rs. 7,288 crore and EBITDA at Rs. 1,320 crore, representing a 35% EBITDA growth over FY21. The company demonstrated resilience by navigating significant input cost inflation, logistics pressures, and global supply chain disruptions, primarily due to the Russia-Ukraine conflict, by successfully passing on costs and optimizing product mix. Both Specialty Chemicals and Pharmaceuticals segments exhibited strong revenue growth. Management reiterated its commitment to aggressive capex and R&D initiatives to drive future growth, focusing on value-added products and import substitution.

## Business Strategy & Drivers

*   **Value-Added Products & R&D:** Focus on developing new chemistries and downstream products with a pipeline of 40+ products for Specialty Chemicals and 50+ for Pharma. Targeting ~25-30% EBITDA margins for new products.
*   **Capacity Expansion:** Commercialization of the 2nd Long Term Contract in Q4 FY22 (expected to add Rs. 500 crore incremental revenue by FY23). Projects for the 3rd Long Term Contract, NCB capacity expansion, and Acid Unit expansion are expected to come online in FY23 with ramp-up over FY23-FY24.
*   **Backward Integration:** Proposal to set up a Concentrating Nitric Acid (CNA) unit (225-250 tpd) by end of FY24 to mitigate sourcing challenges for Nitric Acid.
*   **Import Substitution & Global Supply Chain:** Leveraging opportunities arising from global supply chain diversification and aiming to become a preferred partner for global chemical majors.
*   **Pharma Segment Growth:** USFDA approved API facility expansion nearing commissioning in Q1 FY23, with a revenue potential of $25-30 million and 17-20% EBITDA margin.
*   **Aggressive Capex:** Commitment to investing Rs. 3,000 crore+ by FY24 (Specialty Chemicals: Rs. 2,500-3,000 crore; Pharma: Rs. 350-500 crore) for R&D-led projects and capacity expansion.

## Management Accountability & Consistency Audit

**Audit of Previous Promises & Guidance:**

*   **FY22 Revenue Growth:** The company exceeded its FY22 guidance of 25-35% growth, reporting a 58% increase to Rs. 7,919 crore (or 20% excluding termination fees).
*   **FY22 EBITDA Growth:** Excluding termination fees, the 35% EBITDA growth met or exceeded expectations, demonstrating strong operational performance despite inflationary pressures.
*   **Long-Term Contracts:** The 2nd Long Term Contract was commissioned in Q4 FY22. The 3rd Long Term Contract at Jhagadia and NCB capacity expansion at Vapi are on track for commissioning in FY23. The ramp-up of these capacities is progressing as expected.
*   **Capex Program:** FY22 capex of over Rs. 1,300 crore was in line with the planned outlay. The aggressive capex plan for FY22-24 of Rs. 3,000 crore+ is being pursued.
*   **R&D and Innovation:** The company is actively developing new products with 40+ in Specialty Chemicals and 50+ in Pharma. The new R&D centre is mentioned as being underway.
*   **Pharma Demerger:** The NCLT hearing for the demerger of the Pharma business into Aarti Pharmalabs Ltd is scheduled for June 2022, indicating progress towards completion.
*   **Input Cost Pass-Through:** Management reiterated their ability to pass on RM cost spikes, contributing to maintaining margins despite significant inflation.

**New Forward-Looking Promises & Guidance to Track:**

*   **FY23 EBITDA Growth:** High single-digit EBITDA growth is projected for FY23, largely due to the absence of the shortfall fee from FY22 and ongoing capacity ramp-up.
*   **FY24/FY25 EBITDA Jump:** A significant jump in EBITDA is expected in FY24 and FY25 as capacity utilization reaches 80-90% for newer projects.
*   **Long-Term Contract Ramp-up:** First contract (Dicamba chain) to reach 70-80% utilization by FY24. Second contract to see high utilization from the first year.
*   **New Chemistry Projects:** Investment in new chemistries (ring chlorination, photo chlorination, AHF fluorination, ammoxidation, Grignard reaction) with projected EBITDA margins 30-40% higher than current levels. Major outlets in Pharma and Agro.
*   **Concentrating Nitric Acid (CNA) Plant:** To be commercialized by end of FY24, with an estimated capex of Rs. 150-200 crore, to mitigate Nitric Acid sourcing risks. Comprehensive nitric acid strategy to be clarified by Q1 FY23.
*   **Pharma API Facility:** Commercialization targeted in Q1 FY23, with a revenue potential of $25-30 million and EBITDA margins of 17-20%.
*   **New Greenfield Site Development:** Site development work is set to commence on over 100 acres at Jhagadia and over 120 acres acquired at Atali, Gujarat.
*   **Talent Management:** Proactive hiring and internal training to manage a projected talent shortage in the chemical industry.
*   **R&D Spend:** FY22 R&D spend was around Rs. 60-80 crore, with potential for further increase in the next 1-2 years.
*   **Employee Costs:** Addition of ~1,500 personnel in FY22, with ongoing recruitment for upcoming projects. Total workforce around 8,000-8,500.
*   **Demand Outlook:** While some pushback in demand is seen in dyes & pigments due to inflation, the agrochemical sector remains robust. Management is continuously reshuffling product and geographical mix.
*   **China Impact:** Management stated they are fully backward integrated and do not foresee a significant impact from China's pricing or supply chain dynamics on their product lines.
