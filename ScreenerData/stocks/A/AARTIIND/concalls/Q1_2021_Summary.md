## Executive Overview

Aarti Industries Limited (AIL) presented its Annual General Meeting materials from September 28, 2021, providing an overview of its performance and future strategy. The company highlighted its established position as a leading Benzene-based derivative specialty chemicals manufacturer with integrated operations and a strong R&D focus. AIL emphasized its diversification across multiple dimensions, including products, customers, geographies, and end-user industries, which de-risks its business model. The company also reiterated its commitment to sustainability, with significant investments in EHS and ESG initiatives, evidenced by awards from Responsible Care, EcoVadis (Gold), and CRISIL (3rd in chemical sector). AIL sees significant tailwinds from global supply chain diversification away from China and robust domestic demand, positioning it to benefit from industry growth. The company outlined aggressive capex plans for FY22-24, anticipating robust growth fueled by R&D, innovation, and expansion into new chemistries and value-added products. A notable announcement was the demerger proposal for its Pharma undertaking, aiming for a more focused approach and strategic agility.

## Business Strategy & Drivers

*   **Global Player in Benzene Derivatives:** Leverage integrated operations and cost optimization.
*   **Diversified Business Model:** Multi-product, multi-customer, multi-geography, and multi-end-user industry approach for de-risking.
*   **Pharma Segment Focus:** Continued growth in APIs, intermediates, and Xanthine derivatives; proposed demerger to enhance strategic focus.
*   **R&D and Process Innovation:** Strong emphasis on developing downstream products through advanced processes like chlorination and hydrogenation; aiming for 40+ new chemical products and 50+ new pharma products.
*   **Sustainability & ESG:** Continued investment in EHS, Responsible Care, and EcoVadis recognition; ESG factors integrated into decision-making.
*   **Industry Tailwinds:** Capitalizing on opportunities arising from China's environmental shutdowns and strong domestic demand growth.
*   **Aggressive Capex Program:** Significant investment in new capacities, including a 100+ acre site at Jhagadia and over 120 acres at Atali, Gujarat.
*   **Strategic Alliances:** Pursuing new value-added product ranges and chemical chain expansions.
*   **Market Position:** Aiming to improve market position across isomers and increase presence in regulated pharma markets.
*   **Growth Capital:** Utilizing funds from QIP to support growth capital needs.
