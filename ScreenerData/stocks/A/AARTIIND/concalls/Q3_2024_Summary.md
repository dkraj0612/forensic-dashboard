## Executive Overview

Aarti Industries Limited (AIL) demonstrated a robust sequential improvement in Q3 FY24, with EBITDA rising by 15% to ₹268 crore. This performance was achieved despite ongoing global headwinds including inventory destocking, high interest rates, recessionary pressures in developed markets, and Chinese dumping. The company's agile product mix management and customer partnerships were key drivers. Discretionary application segments like dyes, pigments, and polymers are showing early signs of demand rebound, while non-discretionary segments (agrochemicals and pharmaceuticals) remain soft due to inventory management by customers. AIL secured two significant long-term contracts: a ₹3,000 crore agrochemical intermediate contract with a global major and a ₹6,000 crore specialty chemical contract with a multinational conglomerate, underscoring its manufacturing excellence and customer engagement. The company is on track for its FY24 EBITDA guidance and projects FY25 EBITDA between ₹1,450-1,700 crore, with a growing contribution from value-added products and strategic capacity expansions.

*   **Projected CAPEX (FY24):** ₹1,200-1,300 crore
*   **Projected CAPEX (FY24-FY25):** ₹2,500-3,000 crore

## Business Strategy & Drivers

*   **Contractual Business Growth:** Secured significant long-term contracts for niche agrochemical intermediates and specialty chemicals, enhancing revenue visibility and predictability.
*   **Demand Normalization:** Anticipates broader demand normalization in the agrochemical and pharmaceutical sectors within the next 1-2 quarters, following the current soft phase.
*   **Product Mix Agility:** Continues to dynamically manage product mix based on evolving demand, prioritizing products with favorable demand and margin scenarios.
*   **Capacity Expansion:** Projects for Ethylation, NT capacity increase, and Chlorotoluenes value chain are progressing well, with phased commissioning expected from FY25.
*   **Value-Added Products:** Increased focus on value-added products is expected to contribute to improved EBITDA margins.
*   **Import Substitution:** The Chlorotoluene project targets significant import substitution, with an estimated India import market of ₹1,500 crore.
*   **Geographic Diversification:** US and Europe are showing better volume recovery in discretionary segments, while the domestic market remains steady.

## Management Accountability & Consistency Audit

### Audit of Previous Promises & Guidance

*   **FY24 EBITDA Guidance:** The FY24 EBITDA guidance of ₹950-1,000 crore appears to be on track, with management now expecting to close the year closer to ₹1,000 crore. This is consistent with previous guidance.
*   **FY25 EBITDA Guidance:** The FY25 EBITDA guidance has been refined to ₹1,450-1,700 crore, an upward revision from the previous implicitly guided ₹1,700 crore. Management highlights that the new contracts contribute significantly to this increased visibility.
*   **Project Commissioning:** Projects like Ethylation & NT are still targeted for phased commissioning from FY25 onwards, aligning with previous timelines.
*   **Contract III Ramp-up:** Contract III's contribution to EBITDA is still projected from FY25, with major contributions expected thereafter.
*   **Inventory Correction:** Management reiterates that inventory correction in FY24 was a significant driver of volume degrowth, and normalization is expected.
*   **Headwinds Narrative:** Management acknowledges persistent external challenges but emphasizes their ability to navigate these through portfolio strength and customer partnerships. They note that the worst of headwinds is expected to normalize in the next 1-2 quarters.

### New Forward-Looking Promises & Guidance to Track

*   **EBITDA Outlook FY25:** ₹1,450-1,700 crore.
*   **Project Commercialization & Ramp-up Timelines:**
    *   Ethylation and NT Expansion, Chlorotoluenes value chain: Phased commissioning from FY25 onwards.
    *   First Agrochemical Contract (₹3,000 crore over 9 years): Approximately ₹300 crore revenue in FY25, and ₹350 crore+ from FY26 onwards. Expected EBITDA margin of ~20%.
    *   Second Specialty Chemical Contract (₹6,000 crore over 4 years): Revenue of ~₹900 crore in FY24, increasing to ~₹1,500 crore in FY25. Expected EBITDA margin of 15-17% at constant prices.
    *   Chlorotoluene Project: Commissioning in FY26, with sizable EBITDA contribution from FY27 onwards. Target revenue >₹2,000 crore with EBITDA margin of 25-30%.
*   **Volume Growth (FY25):** Composite volume growth target of 20-25% over FY24. Headroom for another 10-15% volume growth in existing assets (Zone 1, 2, 3).
*   **Margin Trajectory:** Expected EBITDA margins to progressively increase towards 18-20% at higher revenue levels (~₹9,000 crore turnover). Discretionary segments to improve further, with agrochemicals and pharmaceuticals expected to improve quarter-on-quarter in FY25.
*   **Debt Levels:** Projected gross debt to be in the range of ₹3,500-3,800 crore by the end of FY25, assuming current raw material prices.
*   **CAPEX Program:** FY24 CAPEX projected at ₹1,200-1,300 crore. The total FY24-FY25 CAPEX of ₹2,500-3,000 crore remains on track, with most existing site expansions expected to complete in CY24.
*   **Export Mix:** Discretionary to Non-discretionary application mix is currently ~60:40, up from ~50:50 in the previous year.
*   **Nitro Toluene (NT) Production:** NT volumes have been slower due to agrochemical demand; recovery expected with broader agro sector normalization.
*   **Gross Profit per Kg:** Management emphasizes focusing on absolute gross profit and gross profit per kg for specific products rather than composite averages, as raw material prices and product mix influence this. Expects improvement in gross profit per kg as volume pressures ease.
*   **Contractual Business Contribution:** Contractual business with the two new contracts expected to be 30-40% of overall business.
*   **Chlorotoluene Project:** Asset turnover estimated at 1.2x. Targets import substitution worth ~₹1,500 crore in India.
*   **Freight Costs (Red Sea):** Acknowledged impact with a quarter lag on passing costs to customers; minimal impact on working capital expected.
