## Executive Overview

Aarti Industries Limited (AIL) demonstrated resilience in Q2 FY24, achieving a sequential improvement in EBITDA to ₹233 crore from ₹201 crore in Q1 FY24, despite prevailing external pressures such as global inventory destocking, high interest rates, and geopolitical tensions. The company's strategy of agile navigation to products with more favorable demand, coupled with operational excellence, contributed to this recovery. While agrochemicals remain challenging, segments like dyes, pigments, and polymer additives are showing signs of recovery and sequential margin improvement. Management anticipates the worst of the headwinds to be over in H1 FY24, with a gradual demand revival expected in H2 FY24 and FY25 projected as a normalizing year. A significant CAPEX of ₹2,500-3,000 crore is planned over FY24-FY25 for growth initiatives.

## Key Financial Metrics

*   **Q2 FY24 Revenue:** ₹1,597 crore (up 2% QoQ)
*   **Q2 FY24 EBITDA:** ₹233 crore (up 16% QoQ)
*   **H1 FY24 EBITDA:** ₹433 crore
*   **Q2 FY24 Profit After Tax (PAT):** ₹91 crore (up 30% QoQ)
*   **CAPEX (FY24 YTD):** ₹575 crore
*   **Projected CAPEX (FY24):** ₹1,200-1,300 crore
*   **Projected CAPEX (FY24-FY25):** ₹2,500-3,000 crore
*   **EBITDA Guidance (FY24):** ₹950-1,000 crore (H1 actual ₹433 crore, H2 expected upwards of ₹500 crore)
*   **EBITDA Guidance (FY25):** Approximately ₹1,700 crore (with a potential decline of 5-15% from this figure)

## Business Strategy & Drivers

*   **Market Agility:** Swift navigation to products with favorable demand scenarios and increased focus on traditional markets with better margins.
*   **Project Execution:** Revamp of the Acid unit completed. Capacity increases in Ethylation & NT, and the Chlorotoluenes value chain are progressing, with phased commissioning expected from FY25.
*   **Growth Initiatives:** Deployment of ₹2,500-3,000 crore CAPEX over two years to enhance capabilities in existing and new chemistries and expand the addressable market.
*   **Demand Revival:** Expectation of normalized demand across segments in H2 FY24, leading to better performance, with FY25 anticipated as a normalizing year.
*   **Customer Engagement:** Continuous engagement with customers to increase market share and widen the market base.
*   **Value-Added Products:** Increasing percentage of value-added products in the overall mix is expected to improve EBITDA margins.
*   **Export Focus:** Direct exports are showing stronger momentum than the domestic market, with a sustainable export mix anticipated.

## Management Accountability & Consistency Audit

### Audit of Previous Promises & Guidance

*   **FY24 EBITDA Guidance:** The company provided an EBITDA guidance for FY24 of ₹950-1,000 crore, with H1 FY24 actuals at ₹433 crore and expectation of H2 FY24 exceeding ₹500 crore. This guidance appears to be on track, indicating a potential for the higher end of the range.
*   **FY25 EBITDA Guidance:** The previous guidance for FY25 was around ₹1,700 crore. Management has now refined this to be "approximately ₹1,700 crore with at least 5% to 15% decline over that number" due to current conditions. This suggests a moderation and a more cautious outlook compared to prior implicit expectations.
*   **Project Commissioning:** Projects like Ethylation and NT expansion are now targeted for commissioning towards the end of Q1 FY25, a slight shift from "next year" in general terms, indicating progress.
*   **Contract III Ramp-up:** Contract III is still in its stabilization phase and is not expected to yield significant EBITDA in FY24, with major contributions anticipated from FY25. This aligns with previous commentary.
*   **First Contract Strategy:** Management has not yet taken a firm decision on repurposing the first contract's plant, continuing to evaluate possibilities for another couple of quarters. This indicates a lack of definitive action on a previously raised point.
*   **Inventory Correction:** Management confirms that inventory correction was a substantial driver of volume degrowth in FY24, and this is expected to normalize, leading to a return to consumption-sales matching.

### New Forward-Looking Promises & Guidance to Track

*   **EBITDA Outlook FY25:** Approximately ₹1,700 crore, with a potential variance of 5-15% decline.
*   **Project Commercialization & Ramp-up Timelines:**
    *   Ethylation and NT Expansion: Targeting commissioning towards the end of Q1 FY25, with progressive volume and EBITDA increase throughout FY25, and a stronger second half.
    *   Contract III: Major EBITDA contribution expected in FY25.
    *   Acid Unit Revamp: Completed.
    *   Other projects (Ethylation & NT capacity increase, Chlorotoluenes value chain): Progressing well, phased commissioning from FY25.
*   **Volume Growth (FY25):** Expected overall volume growth across board, driven by normalization of inventory correction and consumption-sales matching.
*   **Margin Trajectory:** Margins are considered to have bottomed out in Q1 FY24, with sequential improvement observed in Q2 FY24, particularly in discretionary segments. The mix of value-added products is expected to improve EBITDA margins.
*   **Debt Levels:** Net debt expected to be between ₹2,700-2,900 crore by the end of FY24. Debt levels are expected to increase in FY25 and likely peak in FY26 before tapering off. Specific future debt numbers are difficult to provide currently due to evolving raw material prices and business performance.
*   **CAPEX Program:** ₹1,200-1,300 crore for FY24 is part of the ₹2,500-3,000 crore guidance for FY24-FY25.
*   **Export Mix:** The current export mix is expected to sustain, with exports potentially continuing to be a dominant piece.
*   **Nitro Toluene (NT) Production:** Sequential decline in NT volumes is attributed to agrochemical side demand and temporary shutdowns.
*   **Nitric Acid Prices:** Impact of falling Nitric Acid prices will be reflected gradually due to contractual structures linked to ammonia prices.
*   **Octane Booster Product:** Demand is growing and sustainable, with other export products also showing growth.
*   **MPDA Sunset Review:** Not expected to have a significant impact due to slow local demand and the company's ability to continue the anti-dumping duty.
*   **Value-Added Product Contribution:** Increasing percentage of value-added products is positively impacting EBITDA margins.
