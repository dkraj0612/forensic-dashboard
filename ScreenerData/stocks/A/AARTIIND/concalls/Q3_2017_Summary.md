## Executive Overview

Aarti Industries Limited (AIL) delivered a resilient Q3 FY17 performance amidst a subdued global chemical market and domestic challenges due to demonetization. While gross revenues saw a modest 3% YoY increase to Rs. 770 crore and net revenues grew 6% YoY to Rs. 718 crore, the company demonstrated strong profitability. EBITDA rose 16% YoY to Rs. 150 crore, resulting in an improved EBITDA margin of 20.9%. Profit After Tax (PAT) saw a significant 22% YoY increase to Rs. 74 crore for the quarter and a 27% YoY increase to Rs. 232 crore for the nine-month period (9MFY17). The Specialty Chemicals segment, a key growth driver, reported a 4% YoY revenue increase to Rs. 629 crore, with a notable 10% volume growth and a 12% YoY EBIT expansion to Rs. 133 crore. The Pharmaceuticals segment experienced a slight revenue increase of 3% YoY to Rs. 103 crore in Q3 FY17, though EBIT saw a marginal decline due to a USFDA inspection and December holiday season slowdown, with a 32% YoY EBIT growth for 9MFY17. Management reiterated its commitment to a "Partner of Choice" strategy, leveraging global customer relationships and focused capital allocation for sustained value creation.

## Business Strategy & Drivers

*   **Global Partner of Choice:** AIL continues to leverage its relationships with over 500 domestic and 150 global customers, emphasizing its role as a strategic supplier aligned with customer expansion.
*   **Execution Focus:** The company highlights its operational strength in multi-product, multi-geography, and multi-industry capabilities for driving value.
*   **Value-Added Product Mix:** Management emphasizes optimizing the product mix to enhance value addition and utilize capacities effectively, irrespective of raw material price fluctuations.
*   **Capacity Expansion:** Strategic investments in large capacities, including the upcoming Nitro Toluene and its downstream projects, are underway to meet future demand.
*   **Pharmaceutical Growth Focus:** Continued engagement with regulated markets, with incremental volumes expected to significantly boost segment profits due to built-in fixed costs.
*   **Home & Personal Care Optimization:** Efforts to scale up this segment and achieve sustained margin improvements, despite its relatively small capital employment.
*   **Global Supply Chain Dynamics:** Management notes a favorable shift in global sourcing towards India due to stricter environmental regulations in China and a conscious effort by customers to diversify supply chains.
*   **R&D and New Product Development:** Focus on expanding into new value chains, such as Chloro Toluene, targeting agrochemicals and engineering polymers.

## Management Accountability & Consistency Audit

**Audit of Previous Promises & Guidance (from Q1 FY17 transcript):**

*   **FY2017 CAPEX (Rs. 4.4 billion):** Approximately Rs. 300 crore spent on plant and machinery as of Q3 FY17. The company anticipates around Rs. 400 crore CAPEX for FY18.
*   **Q2 FY17 Expected Commercialization (Ethylation unit, PDA 2nd Phase):** Ethylation unit commissioning expected in Q2 FY17. The PDA 2nd phase is now expected to be commissioned in Q4 FY17/Q1 FY18.
*   **Next 9 Months (Rest of FY17):**
    *   **NCB Production:** Production of around 6,630 MT in Q3 FY17. Total production for 9MFY17 was 16,400 MT. Nitric Acid supply issues are resolved, enabling expected production of around 50,000 tonnes of NCB for the full year.
    *   **Nitro Toluene Plant at Jhagadia (30,000 tpa):** Expected to be commissioned in Q4 FY17 / Q1 FY18 (aligned with prior guidance).
    *   **Ethylation Plant (8,000-10,000 tpa):** Expected to start commissioning in Q2 FY17.
*   **FY2017 Volume Growth Expectation:** Q3 saw 10% volume growth. Q4 is expected to see >10% YoY volume growth. Management anticipates reaching around 15% volume growth in FY18, revising down the earlier ~10-15% guidance for FY17 and suggesting the lower end of the range might be more accurate for the current year, while projecting higher growth for FY18.
*   **Pharmaceutical Segment (Full Year FY17):** Q4 is expected to be better than Q3. The company is working towards improving performance post-USFDA inspection.
*   **Toluene Facility Utilization (FY17):** Nitro Toluene capacity of 30,000 tons per year is being set up. It is expected to reach 75-90% capacity utilization in 2-3 years, generating Rs. 250-300 crore revenue. Full benefit expected in FY19-FY20.
*   **Debt Management:** Gross debt is Rs. 1,430 crore, with an increased proportion of rupee debt. Financing costs have marginally increased due to this shift.
*   **Management Tone Regarding Headwinds:** The company reiterates that raw material price fluctuations (like benzene) are passed on via formula pricing with margins fixed on an absolute basis per kg. While inventory gains/losses can occur, quarterly average valuations are used. Demonetization impacted textile and FMCG segments, affecting turnover by Rs. 8-10 crore in Q3 and potentially a similar impact in Q4.
