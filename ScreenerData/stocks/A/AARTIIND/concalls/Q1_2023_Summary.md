## Executive Overview

Aarti Industries Limited (AIL) presented its investor presentation for the 39th Annual General Meeting on September 26, 2022. The presentation highlights robust FY22 performance and outlines strategic growth initiatives. Revenue for FY22 reached INR 7,919 crore, a 59% increase year-on-year, driven by better realisations and volume gains. EBITDA stood at INR 1,930 crore, up 45% YoY, bolstered by operating leverage from high plant utilization and effective cost pass-through mechanisms. Notably, FY22 financials include INR 1,200 crore in termination income. The company is focused on expanding its specialty chemicals and pharmaceuticals segments through capacity additions and R&D-driven projects, while emphasizing sustainability and ESG initiatives.

## Business Strategy & Drivers

*   **Value-Added Products:** AIL continues to focus on high value-added products, particularly in the chlorotoluene chain, with a target of ~90% value-added products.
*   **Capacity Expansion & Long-Term Contracts:** The second long-term contract unit has been commercialized. Projects related to the third long-term contract, NCB capacity expansion, and Pharma USFDA expansion are progressing well for commercialization in FY23-24. The first long-term contract is targeted for 70%+ utilization by FY24.
*   **Backward Integration:** Plans for a Concentrated Nitric Acid (CNA) unit are underway, with a full nitric acid strategy being evaluated.
*   **Pharma Segment Growth:** Expansion of the USFDA-approved API facility at Tarapur is in the final stages and expected to commercialize in Q1 FY23. A new API block at Tarapur has commenced production in early Q2 FY23.
*   **Aggressive Capex:** Capex of approximately INR 2,500-3,000 crore for Chemicals and INR 350-500 crore for Pharma is planned for FY23-24, with site development commencing at Jhagadia and Atali.
*   **Import Substitution & Global Sourcing:** Positioning to benefit from global supply chain diversification and become an alternative sourcing destination to China.
*   **R&D-Led Projects:** A pipeline of over 40 products for Specialty Chemicals and 50+ for Pharma, aiming for EBITDA margins of 25-30% in the long term.
*   **Sustainability & ESG:** Significant capex in Environment, Health, and Safety (EHS), with strong ESG ratings from EcoVadis and CRISIL.

## Management Accountability & Consistency Audit

**Audit of Previous Promises & Guidance:**

*   **FY22 Revenue and EBITDA Growth:** The reported FY22 results (INR 7,919 crore revenue, INR 1,930 crore EBITDA) show substantial growth. However, these figures are significantly boosted by INR 1,200 crore of termination income. Without this, revenue growth was robust at 37% YoY. The EBITDA growth, when normalized for termination income, reflects the strong operational performance and cost pass-through capabilities.
*   **Long-Term Contracts:** The presentation confirms commercialization of the unit for the 2nd long-term contract in Q4 FY22. Projects for the 3rd long-term contract and other expansions are progressing as planned for FY23-24.
*   **Capex Program:** FY22 capex initiatives were progressing, and the aggressive capex plan for FY23-24 is outlined with substantial investments.
*   **Pharma Demerger:** The presentation reiterates the proposal for a Pharma demerger to facilitate focused growth and strategic decision-making. NCLT order was pending at the time of the historical summary, and the current transcript does not provide an update.
*   **Input Cost Pass-Through:** Management successfully passed on higher input costs, utility costs, and logistics costs, evidenced by strong revenue growth and maintenance of absolute delta margins.

**New Forward-Looking Promises & Guidance to Track:**

*   **FY23 EBITDA Guidance:** Management is maintaining a high single-digit growth guidance for FY23, but will re-evaluate after Q2 performance, considering higher fixed costs from new assets and volume ramp-up.
*   **FY24/FY25 EBITDA Jump:** A significant jump in EBITDA is anticipated in FY24 and FY25 as newer capacities reach higher utilization levels and normalize fixed costs.
*   **Long-Term Contract Ramp-up:**
    *   Contract 1: Targeted for ~70%+ utilization by end of FY24.
    *   Contract 2: Progressive ramp-up, utilization expected to increase.
*   **New Chemistry Projects:** Pipeline of 40+ specialty chemical and 50+ pharma products aiming for 25-30% EBITDA margins beyond FY24.
*   **Capex:** Approximately INR 2,500-3,000 crore for Chemicals and INR 350-500 crore for Pharma planned for FY23-24. Site development for new land parcels (Jhagadia and Atali) to commence.
*   **Pharma API Facility:** New API block at Tarapur commissioned in early Q2 FY23. Dombivli unit awaiting USFDA EIR (status not updated from previous).
*   **Demand Outlook:** Slowdown observed in domestic textiles and pigments due to cost pressures and recession fears. Export markets (polymers, agro, pharma) are less impacted.
*   **Nitric Acid Supply:** Expectation of domestic capacity coming up in H2 FY23, with full mitigation by FY24.
*   **FY24 Growth (over FY21):** Target Turnover of Rs. 5,000 Cr, EBITDA of Rs. 980 Cr, PAT of Rs. 523 Cr.
*   **FY27 Growth (over FY21):** Target Turnover of 2.5x-3.5x, EBITDA of 3x-4x, PAT of 3x-4x.
*   **Gross Debt:** Target debt-to-equity ratio between 0.7-0.9.
*   **GST Payments:** Lower GST paid in Q1 FY23 due to shipments under bond for exports.
