## Executive Overview

Aarti Industries Limited (AIL) concluded FY25 with a resilient Q4, reporting total revenue of ₹8,046 crore, a 15% increase YoY, and EBITDA growth of 3% to ₹1,016 crore. The increase in depreciation and finance costs due to project commercialization and higher interest rates impacted PAT to ₹331 crore. Q4 FY25 revenue was ₹2,214 crore, a 9% sequential increase driven by volume recovery across end applications, with EBITDA at ₹266 crore, up 13% QoQ, and PAT at ₹96 crore due to improved cost efficiencies. The company recommended a final dividend of ₹1 per share for FY25. Volume growth was robust, with the energy segment up 21% QoQ and the base business up 14% QoQ, supported by new capacity additions. AIL continues to focus on sustainability, with renewable energy PPAs on track and maintaining high ESG scores. CAPEX for FY25 was ₹1,372 crore, and FY26 CAPEX is projected at ₹950-1,000 crore, with the bulk allocated to Zone IV projects. AIL remains focused on volume-led growth and agile cost control to navigate market volatility.

## Business Strategy & Drivers

*   **Volume-Led Growth:** Emphasis on driving volume growth across existing assets, with significant upside potential from current capacity utilization.
*   **Product Diversification:** Continued focus on market development for new products like MMA, expanding its customer base and geographic reach.
*   **Capacity Utilization:** Aiming to increase capacity utilization across various value chains, including PDA, NCB, and Nitrotoluene/Ethylation, driven by market demand and new capacities.
*   **Sustainability Initiatives:** Renewable energy PPAs on track for implementation. AIL recognized for strong ESG performance by CDP, S&P Global DJSI, and EcoVadis.
*   **Cost Optimization:** Successful implementation of initiatives like Back-Pressure Turbine Project and Hybrid Power Phase-1, with ongoing efforts for fixed cost optimization. Full accrual of savings from hybrid power expected from FY27.
*   **US Tariff Impact Management:** Mixed impact across the product portfolio; some products see a positive tailwind, while others require pricing adjustments. Second and third-order impacts are expected to unfold over the next few months.
*   **Zone IV Projects:** Pilot plant operational and generating commercial revenue. Phased commissioning of other blocks scheduled through FY26 and FY27, with a focus on multi-purpose production.
*   **MMA Market Development:** Focus on expanding the customer base and geographic spread (US, Europe, India) beyond its traditional Middle East concentration.

## Management Accountability & Consistency Audit

### Audit of Previous Promises & Guidance

*   **FY25 Performance:** The company achieved YoY revenue growth of 15% and EBITDA growth of 3%, aligning with the expectation of ending the challenging year on a positive note. The PAT was impacted by increased depreciation and finance costs, a factor acknowledged in the management's commentary.
*   **Volume Growth:** The transcript confirms strong volume recovery, with the non-energy business growing 14% QoQ and energy business 21% QoQ. For FY25 vs FY24, overall volume growth was around 17%, meeting expectations for volume-led growth.
*   **Project Commissioning:** The Zone IV pilot plant has commenced commercial operations, and phased commissioning of other blocks is progressing through FY26. The company reiterated that full capacity utilization from new assets will reflect from FY27 onwards.
*   **Sustainability Initiatives:** Renewable energy PPAs are on track, with one contract expected within CY2024 and the second by end FY26/early FY27, aligning with previous timelines.
*   **Cost Optimization:** Management confirmed that bulk of the implementation will be done in FY26, with full EBITDA accruals from hybrid projects expected from FY27. This slightly delays the full realization of savings compared to the previous notion of immediate accrual.
*   **Agrochemicals:** Pricing pressure persists due to overcapacity in China, with the US tariffs' second and third-order effects still unfolding and expected to take a few months to play out. This aligns with the previous assessment of ongoing margin pressure.
*   **MMA Business:** Efforts to diversify geographic and customer base are ongoing. Management acknowledges the link between MMA pricing to aniline and gasoline/naphtha economics.
*   **US Tariffs:** Management explicitly states a "mixed impact" due to a diverse product portfolio, with some products benefiting and others requiring price adjustments. This is a more detailed breakdown than previously implied.

### New Forward-Looking Promises & Guidance to Track

*   **FY26 Revenue/EBITDA Guidance:** Management reiterated their confidence in achieving the previously stated 3-year EBITDA guidance of ₹1,800-2,200 crore but declined to provide specific yearly targets for FY26.
*   **Zone IV Commissioning:** Pilot plant operational; phased commissioning of remaining blocks through FY26 and FY27. Multipurpose plant and calcium chloride plant expected to start up in Q3 FY26. Full volume ramp-up from these assets is expected from FY27.
*   **MMA Business:** Target to utilize current capacity over the next two years. Competitive advantages including supply chain capabilities and freight cost positioning are being leveraged. New competition from China is acknowledged but deemed manageable.
*   **Plastic Recycling JV:** No new explicit guidance in this transcript.
*   **Renewable Energy PPAs:** One contract to start within CY2024, the second by end FY26/early FY27. Operational costs expected to lower.
*   **Debt-to-EBITDA:** Net debt is expected to be lower by ₹200-300 crore in FY26, potentially peaking out, with expected unlocking of cash flow from working capital and lower CAPEX intensity.
*   **Working Capital Cycle:** Expected to be between 70 to 80 days on a gross basis for FY26.
*   **EBITDA Margin:** Management is not factoring in significant margin recovery for FY26, assuming demand-supply imbalances from China will not correct soon. Margin improvement is contingent on capturing opportunities.
*   **Tax Rate:** Expected to be in the mid-single digits for FY26, compared to a negative rate in FY25 due to high depreciation benefits.
*   **Trade Payables:** Expected to increase by approximately ₹100-200 crore in FY26, assuming continued volume growth.
*   **CAPEX Allocation FY26:** Bulk of the ₹950-1,000 crore CAPEX is for Zone IV. Maintenance CAPEX is estimated at ₹150-200 crore.
*   **PDA Growth:** Significant uptick in utilization expected due to increasing demand in the US driven by tariffs.
*   **NCB Growth:** Capacity utilization expected to increase due to large customer capacity expansions in the pharma sector.
*   **DCB Utilization:** Expected to maintain current levels, with demand dependent on the polymer end-application (PPS) and automotive sector.
*   **Nitrotoluene & Ethylation:** Higher utilization expected compared to FY25, with a ramp-up process as new capacities were fully proven in Q4 FY25.
*   **Long-term Contracts:** Nitric acid and polymer application contracts performing as per contract. Specialty polymer intermediate contract expected to perform better in FY26. Other supply contracts vary based on end-market conditions.
*   **FY26 Volume Growth:** Base assumption is volume-led growth, potentially better than FY25, but significant margin recovery is not factored in.
*   **PAT Projection for FY28 EBITDA Target:** At ₹1,800 crore EBITDA, PAT could be ₹900-1,000 crore; at ₹2,200 crore EBITDA, PAT could be ₹1,200-1,300 crore, factoring in increased depreciation and interest costs.
*   **MMA Tariffs:** MMA is not part of the US exempt list, and tariffs are expected to be applicable.
*   **NPDA Benefit:** Upside from tariffs expected from Q4 FY25 onwards, with stabilization dependent on the long-term tariff situation.
