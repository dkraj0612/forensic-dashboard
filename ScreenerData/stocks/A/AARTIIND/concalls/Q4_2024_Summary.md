## Executive Overview

Aarti Industries Limited (AIL) reported a resilient Q4 FY24 performance despite global macroeconomic headwinds, including sluggish demand, inventory adjustments, and supply chain disruptions. The company achieved FY24 revenue of ₹7,012 crore and EBITDA of ₹984 crore, aligning with guidance. Sequential Q4 FY24 revenue grew 3.5% to ₹1,955 crore, with EBITDA increasing 5.6% to ₹283 crore, driven by optimized costs, improved operating efficiency, and a favorable product mix. Management reiterated the FY25 EBITDA guidance of ₹1,450-1,700 crore, supported by capacity ramp-ups, demand recovery in discretionary segments, and anticipated revival in non-discretionary sectors. Key growth initiatives, including greenfield projects at Jhagadia (Chloro Toluene, Multipurpose Plant) and expansions at existing sites, are progressing as scheduled.

## Business Strategy & Drivers

*   **Capacity Expansion & Commissioning:** Several projects are slated for commissioning in FY25, including Acid Phase-2, Specialty Chemicals Blocks, Ethylation, NT Capacities, and Asset Upgradation. The Chloro Toluene project is projected for initial phase commencement by FY26.
*   **Long-Term Contracts:** Revenue ramp-up from existing long-term contracts is a key driver, with specific contributions expected from agrochemical and specialty chemical segments.
*   **Import Substitution:** The Chloro Toluene project is positioned to capture a significant share of the Indian import market.
*   **New Growth Areas:** Exploration of sunrise sectors like battery storage, circularity of chemicals, and biochemistry is underway, with R&D in progress.
*   **R&D and New Chemistry:** In-house R&D is supporting the development of new chemistries for Chloro Toluene range and multipurpose plants, including photochemistry, nitrilation, and hydrolysis.
*   **Cost Leadership & Integrated Operations:** Continued focus on optimized cost structures and leveraging expertise in benzene and toluene derivatives.
*   **Geographical Diversification:** Maintaining a balanced domestic and export revenue mix.

## Management Accountability & Consistency Audit

### Audit of Previous Promises & Guidance

*   **FY24 EBITDA Guidance:** Management confirmed FY24 EBITDA of ₹984 crore was consistent with guidance provided in previous quarters.
*   **FY25 EBITDA Guidance:** The guidance of ₹1,450-1,700 crore for FY25 has been maintained, with management expressing confidence driven by capacity ramp-ups, demand recovery, and operating leverage. However, they stated it is difficult to narrow down the guidance at this stage and will depend on volume growth, demand uptick, and project stabilization.
*   **Project Commissioning:** Greenfield initiatives at Zone IV (Jhagadia), including the Chloro Toluene project, are on track for phased commercialization by FY26. The NCB expansion project's commercialization was completed. Specific commissioning timelines for NT and Ethylation downstream blocks are targeted for Q2 FY25.
*   **Contract Ramp-up:** The agrochemical contract is expected to see increased sales from FY25, with a full-year impact in FY26. The ₹6,000 crore specialty chemical contract (MMA) started ramping up from Q1 FY25, with y-o-y revenue increases of 50-60% expected from this contract.
*   **Volume Growth:** While Q4 FY24 saw weaker volumes sequentially due to agrochemical and nitric acid pressures, management anticipates sequential volume growth starting Q1 FY25, with significantly stronger growth expected in H2 FY25 as new projects come online. The overall volume growth target for FY25 has been revised to 20-30%.
*   **Headwinds Narrative:** Management acknowledged pressures in the agrochemical segment and some impact from nitric acid supply disruptions. They indicated that agrochemicals would remain challenging in H1 FY25, with recovery expected in H2 FY25.

### New Forward-Looking Promises & Guidance to Track

*   **EBITDA Growth Drivers for FY25:** Volume recovery, ramp-up of commissioned capacities (NT, Ethylation, Zone 4), higher operating leverage, stabilization of new capacities, and discretionary segment growth.
*   **Project Commercialization & Ramp-up Timelines:**
    *   Acid Phase-2, Specialty Chemicals Blocks, Ethylation, NT Capacities, Asset Upgradation: Expected commissioning within FY25.
    *   Greenfield initiatives at Zone IV (MPP, Chloro Toluene project): Phased commercialization by FY26.
    *   Chloro Toluene Project: Significant EBITDA contribution from FY27 onwards.
    *   UPL JV: Commercial supplies expected by Q1FY27 (not directly mentioned in this transcript but from historical context).
*   **Contractual Business Contribution:**
    *   Agrochemical Intermediate Contract (previously ₹3,000 Cr.): Expected revenue of ~₹250 crore in FY25.
    *   Specialty Chemical Contract (previously ₹6,000 Cr. / MMA): Expected 50-60% y-o-y revenue increase in FY25 from this contract; significant sales to other customers also anticipated.
*   **Volume Growth (FY25):** Expected sequential volume growth from Q1 FY25, with significantly stronger growth in H2 FY25 from new projects. Overall volume growth target revised to 20-30%.
*   **Margin Trajectory:**
    *   Chloro Toluene and downstream products: Expected EBITDA margin of 25-30%.
    *   Overall EBITDA margin: Weighted average margin expected to increase from FY26-27 onwards with the contribution of higher-margin value-added products. Peak EBITDA from existing assets (excluding new Chloro Toluene and MPP) is estimated at ~₹2,000 crore.
*   **CAPEX Program:** ₹1,280 crore spent in FY24. Planned CAPEX of ₹1,500-1,800 crore for FY25, with over 50% allocated to the new Jhagadia site. FY26 CAPEX to be upward of ₹1,000 crore. All CAPEX is considered productive.
*   **Export Mix:** 52% export revenue in FY24.
*   **Nitro Toluene (NT) & Ethylation:** Commissioning of expanded capacities expected in Q2 FY25, with volumes largely tied up, mainly for the agrochemical segment.
*   **Tax Rate:** Expected tax rate for FY25-FY26 to be around 12-15%.
*   **Cost of Debt:** Approximately 7.5-8%, expected to remain similar in FY25.
*   **Debt Profile:** Absolute debt levels are not expected to reduce due to ongoing capex, but efforts will focus on optimizing working capital.
*   **Domestic Market:** While direct domestic consumption is ~25%, a significant portion of domestic sales indirectly feeds into exports.
*   **Sunrise Sectors:** R&D is ongoing for battery storage, circularity, and biochemistry, with potential for future investment.
