## Aarti Industries Limited (AIL) - Q3 FY26 Earnings Transcript - Executive Summary

This report synthesizes the Q3 FY26 earnings transcript for Aarti Industries Limited (AIL), providing a concise overview of the company's performance, strategic direction, and forward-looking outlook.

---

## Executive Overview

Aarti Industries (AIL) reported a strong Q3 FY26, demonstrating resilience and adaptability in a challenging global operating environment marked by geopolitical tensions and trade realignments. The company achieved revenue of ₹2,492 crore, an 11% Q-o-Q increase, driven by volume growth in key products like MMA, NT, and DCB. EBITDA surged to ₹323 crore (11% Q-o-Q), with PAT reaching ₹133 crore (25% Q-o-Q), despite a one-time exceptional expense of ₹15 crore for the implementation of the new labor code. Exports constituted a record 65% of total revenues, contributing to higher capacity utilization and operating leverage. Management highlighted positive shifts in global trade dynamics, including the India-EU FTA, China's "anti-involution" strategy, and the US-India Trade Deal, which are expected to structurally benefit the chemical sector and AIL. The company is strategically expanding its MMA capacity and focusing on the advanced materials space for future growth.

## Business Strategy & Drivers

*   **Volume-Led Growth & Capacity Utilization:** Increased volumes across MMA, NT, and DCB led to higher capacity utilization and better operating leverage. Management anticipates further volume growth in PDA following the US-India trade deal.
*   **MMA Expansion & Diversification:** Capacity is being scaled up from 290+ KT to 360 KT by end of FY26. The strategy focuses on geographic diversification (Europe targeted), customer base expansion, and value chain integration to ensure stable margins. MMA is expected to settle between 30-40% of the portfolio in the medium term.
*   **Advanced Materials Focus:** AIL is strategically pivoting towards high-value, application-led solutions in the advanced materials space, leveraging R&D and specialized capacities.
*   **China "Anti-Involution" Impact:** Management expects this policy shift to lead to a more rational global pricing environment and structural margin recovery for integrated, quality-focused players like AIL. Initial impact observed in NCB chain pricing.
*   **US-India Trade Deal & EU FTA:** These agreements are expected to boost US business, provide export opportunities to Europe, and facilitate strategic partnerships.
*   **Polymers Segment:** PDCB demand is increasing due to PPS growth in the EV segment. Debottlenecking efforts are underway to increase DCB capacity from 120 to 140 KTPA.
*   **Agrochemicals & Pharmaceuticals:** Stable volumes continue, though pricing remains subdued due to Chinese dumping. Margin improvement is anticipated with China's evolving stance.
*   **Operational Excellence & AI:** Cost-efficiency programs are nearing completion, with AI and digital transformation tools being deployed for enhanced productivity, process control, and energy reduction.
*   **Indigenous Technology:** Zone 4 projects are being commissioned using in-house, indigenous technology, enabling lower production costs and flexibility in commercializing new products.
*   **Joint Ventures:** JV with Superform (agrochemical, paints, coatings) expected Q1 FY27; JV with RESL (circularity) expected H1 FY27.

## Management Accountability & Consistency Audit

### Audit of Previous Promises & Guidance

*   **Q1 FY26 Performance vs. Expectations:** Q3 FY26 performance significantly improved Q-o-Q, directly addressing the softer Q1 FY26 results. Revenue and EBITDA saw robust double-digit growth, contrary to the sequential decline reported in Q1 FY26.
*   **External Headwinds Management:** The narrative regarding navigating external headwinds has shifted. While acknowledging ongoing challenges, the company now highlights positive structural shifts (India-EU FTA, China's policy, US-India Trade Deal) and the positive impact of tariff reductions, which were a significant concern in Q1 FY26. The company explicitly states they are "absorbing part of US tariffs" and witnessing a "resumption of US volumes leading to higher capacity utilisation."
*   **MMA US Volumes & Tariffs:** The transcript confirms that MMA faced "full tariff applicability" in Q3 FY26, contradicting the earlier narrative that some products were on exemption lists. However, the company also notes that "the recently announced US-India Trade deal provides a sigh of relief and is expected to boost the business in the US in coming times," and that with tariff reductions, "margin accrues to all players in the value chain."
*   **Capacity Expansion & New Projects:** MMA capacity expansion to 360 KT is confirmed. Zone 4 projects (MPP, Chloro toluene, downstream blocks) are on track for commissioning within the current calendar year (CY26), with ramp-up expected over the next two years, broadly aligning with previous timelines.
*   **Cost Optimization:** Cost-saving initiatives are reported as "nearing completion," with AI deployment aimed at further driving efficiency, a continuation of previous efforts.
*   **US Tariffs Impact:** The impact is now viewed more positively due to tariff reductions. The company states that "with the settlement of the U.S.-India trade deal now, we expect utilizations to improve" for products like PDA. For MMA, it's stated that the tariff reduction "will definitely help" in better realisations.

### New Forward-Looking Promises & Guidance to Track

*   **MMA Market Share & Margin:** Management believes MMA will settle between 30-40% of the portfolio long-term. Current strategy focuses on geographic diversification (Europe), customer base expansion, and value chain integration to stabilize margins. Debottlenecking to 360 KT is a priority.
*   **Zone 4 Commissioning & Ramp-up:** MPP and Calcium Chloride units expected to commission within Q4 FY26. Remaining Zone 4 process blocks to commission through Q1-Q3 FY27. Meaningful ramp-up in utilization for specialty products expected over a two-year timeframe. Total Zone 4 CAPEX to be ₹1,600-1,800 crore, with the majority deployed by end of FY26.
*   **China Anti-Involution Impact:** Expected to drive margin improvement, with initial pricing impacts seen in the NCB chain. Further actions and product-specific impacts to be monitored.
*   **DCB Capacity Expansion:** Debottlenecking to 140 KTPA underway to capitalize on PPS growth in the EV segment.
*   **NCB Debottlenecking:** Under active consideration.
*   **JV with Superform:** Commissioning expected Q1 FY27, targeting ₹300-400 crore revenue potential in agrochemical, paints, and coatings.
*   **JV with RESL:** Construction commenced, commissioning expected H1 FY27.
*   **FY27 CAPEX:** Expected to be "significantly lower" than FY26 (₹1,100 crore), barring any further board-approved expansions.
*   **Agrochemicals Outlook:** Expected to grow, with potential to reach 20%+ of overall top line as Zone 4 commissions. Margin recovery is anticipated.
*   **MMA Tariff Advantage:** The tariff differential between India (18%) and China (34%) is seen as a support for better volumes and margins, particularly for PDA.
*   **MMA Value Chain Integration:** Initiatives under consideration, involving both backward and forward integration, to make the business more stable.
*   **Midterm Targets:** Management remains focused on achieving midterm EBITDA targets, reiterating commitment despite potential short-term fluctuations.
*   **Contract Renewals:** MMA contract renewals vary from quarterly to 6-month terms, making specific timing difficult to ascertain.
*   **US Tariff Impact on Specific Assets:** Increased utilization expected for specific contract assets sold in the US due to lower tariffs.
*   **New Partnerships:** Expectation of closing more growth partnerships due to increased clarity on global trade realignments. Announcement of exclusive distribution arrangement with Actylis for PCBTF cited as an example.
