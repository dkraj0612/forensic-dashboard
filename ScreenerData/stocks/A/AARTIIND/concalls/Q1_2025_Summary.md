## Executive Overview

Aarti Industries Limited (AIL) reported a resilient Q1 FY25, demonstrating sequential revenue growth of 3% to ₹2,012 crore, and a significant YoY revenue increase of 28%. Volume growth was robust at 6% QoQ and over 30% YoY. EBITDA grew by 10% QoQ to ₹311 crore, a substantial 55% YoY increase, driven by higher volumes leading to operating leverage and a favorable product mix. Despite ongoing pricing headwinds, global overcapacity from China, and supply chain pressures, AIL remains focused on execution and anticipates a volume-led recovery for the year. Key developments include a strategic 50-50 Joint Venture with UPL (Augene Chemical Private Limited) to manufacture downstream amines derivatives, expected to generate ₹400-500 crore peak annual revenue. The company also welcomed Mr. Suyog Kotecha as Executive Director and CEO, tasked with driving new growth opportunities. Management reiterated a mid-to-long-term EBITDA CAGR target of 20-25%.

## Key Financial Metrics

*   **Q1 FY25 Revenue:** ₹2,012 crore (+3% QoQ, +28% YoY)
*   **Q1 FY25 EBITDA:** ₹311 crore (+10% QoQ, +55% YoY)
*   **Q1 FY25 PAT:** ₹137 crore (+4% QoQ)
*   **Q1 FY25 Volume Growth:** +6% QoQ, +30% YoY
*   **FY25 Planned CAPEX:** ₹1,500-1,800 crore (₹270 crore spent in Q1 FY25)
*   **Current Debt Level:** ~₹3,300 crore
*   **Projected FY25 Peak Debt:** ₹3,500-3,800 crore
*   **Q1 FY25 Export Mix:** ~60% (increased from ~52% in FY24)

## Business Strategy & Drivers

*   **Joint Venture with UPL:** Augene Chemical Private Limited formed for downstream amines derivatives (agrochemicals, paints), with commercial supplies expected by Q1 FY27 and peak revenue potential of ₹400-500 crore within 2-3 years. Each partner to invest ~₹150 crore over two years.
*   **Capacity Expansion & Commissioning:**
    *   Acid unit expansion commissioned in Q4 FY24.
    *   Expanded Nitrotoluene and Ethylation capacity targeted for commissioning by end of Q2 FY25.
    *   New pilot plant at Zone-IV commissioned in Q2 FY25.
    *   Greenfield site at Zone-IV progressing as planned, with phase-wise commissioning from FY26.
*   **New Growth Areas & R&D:** Focus on sunrise sectors (circularity, battery chemistries, technology) and leveraging R&D for new product development.
*   **Focus on Cost Leadership & Integration:** Continued emphasis on optimized cost structures and integrated operations.
*   **Customer Base Diversification:** While prioritizing Tier-1 customers, strategically engaging Tier-2/3 customers to optimize inventory and production.
*   **Long-Term Contracts:** Existing contracts with SABIC and another unnamed party performing well, with SABIC contract ramping up significantly.

## Management Accountability & Consistency Audit

### Audit of Previous Promises & Guidance

*   **FY25 EBITDA Guidance:** Management explicitly **withdrew** the previously stated FY25 EBITDA guidance of ₹1,450-1,700 crore due to "volatility" observed in the business, particularly in new energy applications. They cited pricing pressures from China and the energy applications segment as key reasons, indicating difficulty in committing to a precise guidance at this stage and preferring to "watch out."
*   **Volume Growth:** Management reiterated confidence in achieving volume growth in the range of 20-30% for FY25, citing new project commissioning and better capacity utilization as drivers. This aligns with the previously stated revision to 20-30%.
*   **Project Commissioning:**
    *   The commissioning of Nitrotoluene and Ethylation downstream blocks is confirmed for Q2 FY25, with targets of 50% capacity utilization in FY25 and 70-80% in FY26.
    *   The Zone-IV Greenfield site (including Chlorotoluene value chain) remains on track for phased commissioning from FY26.
*   **Contractual Business:** Both the SABIC contract and the other unnamed contract are performing well and on track with revenue and volume expectations.
*   **Agrochemicals Segment:** Management noted "small green shoots of demand recovery" in agrochemicals, acknowledging it was previously a challenging segment. While expecting volumes to move, they indicated margins would remain under pressure due to Chinese competition.
*   **China Competition:** Management consistently highlighted ongoing "intense" pricing pressure from China across a broad product portfolio, particularly aggravated in the Agchem chain.

### New Forward-Looking Promises & Guidance to Track

*   **EBITDA Guidance (FY25):** **Withdrawn**. Management will provide further guidance after monitoring margin trends over the next 2-3 months.
*   **Volume Growth (FY25):** Target range of **20-30% YoY**.
*   **Project Commercialization & Ramp-up Timelines:**
    *   Nitrotoluene & Ethylation: Commissioning by end of Q2 FY25, targeting 50% utilization in FY25 and 70-80% in FY26.
        *   Nitrotoluene capacity: Ramping up from ~30,000 to ~45,000 tons.
        *   Ethylation capacity: Ramping up from ~8,000-10,000 tons to ~25,000 tons (approx. 3x).
        *   CAPEX for NT & Ethylation: ₹400-500 crore.
    *   Zone IV Greenfield projects (Chlorotoluene value chain): Phased commissioning from FY26.
*   **New Product Margins:** While volumes for NT & Ethylation are expected to ramp up, profitability might be "slightly skewed towards a bit of a downside" compared to original business plans due to current pricing trends, with hopes for margin recovery with Tier-1 customer engagement.
*   **Energy Application Segment:** Expected to remain a significant part of the application mix in the near-term (next few quarters) with continued volatility. Long-term outlook remains optimistic due to its sustainability and cleaner profile compared to traditional fuel additives, fitting the trend of replacing metal-based additives.
*   **UPL JV (Augene Chemical):** Commercial supplies expected by **Q1 FY27**. Peak annual revenue potential of **₹400-500 crore** within 2-3 years post-commercialization.
*   **CAPEX Program:** FY25 CAPEX of **₹1,500-1,800 crore**, with the bulk of remaining CAPEX (over ₹1,200 crore) allocated to Zone-IV for the Chlorotoluene value chain. FY26 CAPEX expected to be significantly lower.
*   **Raw Material Trend:** Softening observed in benzene prices in Q2 FY25. Aniline prices remain volatile but also show some softening.
*   **ROC / ROCE Profile:** Management aims to improve ROCE performance and expects an uptick from FY26 onwards as CAPEX normalizes post the current peak year.
*   **Gross Margins:** Expected to see continued volatility due to a combination of raw material prices (benzene, aniline), changing geographical footprint, and product mix complexity, particularly with the increasing share of energy applications.
*   **Long-Term Demand for Fuel Additives:** Remain optimistic, seeing it as a sustainable alternative to traditional fuel additives and fitting the trend towards cleaner solutions, despite the EV transition.
