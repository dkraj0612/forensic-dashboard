## Aartech Solonics Limited: Q4 & FY26 Executive Summary

**Date:** May 28, 2026

## Executive Overview

Aartech Solonics Limited conducted its first virtual Investor/Analyst Meet for the quarter and financial year ended March 31, 2026. Management expressed a very positive sentiment, highlighting consistent organic growth, operational discipline, and a strong focus on innovation-led entrepreneurship. The company is actively pursuing its revised vision to become a globally recognized Indian multinational enterprise, solving real-world energy application problems through specialized products, services, and solutions. Key growth areas include the defense sector, energy storage, and renewable solutions, alongside expanding its international footprint. Aartech emphasizes a "Go Global - Act Local" approach, leveraging its DSIR-approved R&D center and taking entrepreneurial risks in new technologies.

## Key Financial Metrics

**FY 2025-26 (Audited Standalone/Consolidated)**

*   **Revenue from Operations:** Achieved a record turnover of ₹40 Crores, demonstrating a doubling of revenue in the last five years, growing at an organic rate of 12-15% annually.
*   **EBITDA:** Grew 3x higher than FY25, significantly boosted by the absence of a ~₹4 Cr exceptional expense incurred in FY25.
*   **Net Profit After Tax (PAT):** Significantly improved compared to FY25.
*   **Debt-to-Equity Ratio:** Significantly better than industry standards, with minimal long-term debt and primary reliance on cash credit for working capital. The company considers itself largely debt-free.
*   **Return on Equity (ROE):** Steadily increasing, with a focus on delivering 5-10% returns to investors annually through bonuses or dividends.
*   **Earnings Per Share (EPS):** Drastically improved in FY26.
*   **Cash and Cash Equivalents:** A healthy ₹328 Lakhs at year-end, up from ₹168 Lakhs last year.
*   **Order Book (as of beginning of FY26):** ~₹10 Crores.
*   **Bids in Progress:** ~₹15 Crores.

## Business Strategy & Drivers

Aartech Solonics continues its multi-pronged strategy to drive growth and expand its market presence:

*   **Global Expansion:** Secured a significant Bus Transfer System (BTS) project in Indonesia, winning against ABB. Established strategic entries into Middle East markets (Qatar, Oman) with utilities like Jindal and engineering firms like TRAGS, for both BTS and Fault Current Limiters. Successfully exported Control & Relay Panels to China and Africa, marking initial inroads into these promising geographies.
*   **Defense Sector Focus:** Leveraging "Make in India" and "Atmanirbhar Bharat" initiatives, the company continues to innovate in rugged mil-grade solutions. Key projects include Electromagnetic Launch Systems (EMLS), approved by R&D Pune, and the Alternative Adaptive Power Module (AAPM), which has undergone successful validations and trials across all Indian Army commands. Two patents are currently in application/review, including one for AAPM.
*   **Energy Storage and Renewables:** A new manufacturing unit near Narmadapuram is under development in a renewables and power equipment cluster. This facility will focus on next-generation energy storage systems for renewable energy, defense, and transportation.
*   **Product Diversification & Innovation:** Continuously developing advanced solutions like SAS and SCADA systems and targeting the 220 Kv market for Control and Relay Panels. The product portfolio includes BTS 2000, Fault Current Limiters, AAPM, Kranking Ultracapacitors, Oxto Flywheel (under development for data centers), and BestCase industrial plastic enclosures.
*   **Operational Excellence & Margin Enhancement:** Focus on high-margin flagship products like BTS 2000 (35-40% margins), contributing to overall EBITDA margins of 10-15%.
*   **Innovation-led Entrepreneurship:** A core philosophy driving new product development and market entry, supported by a DSIR-approved R&D center and an emphasis on taking calculated risks in new technologies.
