## Executive Overview

Aarti Pharmalabs Limited (AARTIPHARM) reported Q1 FY24 results with consolidated revenues declining by 6% year-on-year to INR 458 crores. However, consolidated EBITDA saw a 6% increase to INR 85 crores, and Profit After Tax (PAT) rose by 10% to INR 48 crores compared to the previous quarter. The company continues to focus on its core segments of API & Intermediates, CDMO/CMO, and Xanthine derivatives, emphasizing backward integration and a strategy to become China-independent. A key development is the anticipated completion of a project to manufacture a main raw material for Xanthine products in Q2 FY24, aiming for complete independence from China in this chemistry. Management confirmed its commitment to quarterly investor calls.

## Business Strategy & Drivers

Aarti Pharmalabs' strategy remains anchored on:

*   **Backward Integration:** Demonstrated by the upcoming in-house production of a key Xanthine raw material, reducing import reliance.
*   **Regulated Market Focus:** Continued emphasis on exports to regulated markets.
*   **Specialized Verticals:** Focus on Anti-Cancer, Cortico Steroids, and High Potent APIs (HPAPIs).
*   **CDMO & CMO Services:** Aiming for growth beyond the current 6% contribution, with 16 products commercialized and 12 under development. Management is shifting focus to scale-up and commercial manufacturing expertise, moving away from pure contract development.
*   **Xanthine Derivatives:** Debottlenecking capacity from 4,000 to 5,000 tons per annum. Management is seeking opportunities for further debottlenecking to capture market share, as current capacity is utilized at ~90%. The business is independent of API and intermediates.
*   **R&D and Innovation:** Developing approximately 40 new products annually, with a focus on process innovation.
*   **Capacity Expansion:** The Greenfield project at Atali, Gujarat, is progressing, with completion expected in H2 FY25. This facility will primarily handle intermediates and CDMO/CMO projects, with a reactor volume of 400+ KL in the first phase.
