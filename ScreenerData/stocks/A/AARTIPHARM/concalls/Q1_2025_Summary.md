## Aarti Pharmalabs (AARTIPHARM) - Q1 FY25 Earnings Call - One-Page Executive Summary

**Date:** August 9, 2024

---

### ## Executive Overview

Aarti Pharmalabs reported a solid Q1 FY25, with consolidated revenue increasing by a robust 27% year-on-year to ₹555 crores. Consolidated EBITDA grew 14% year-on-year to ₹96.4 crores, and Consolidated PAT saw an 18% year-on-year increase to ₹65.3 crores. The company operates across three segments: Xanthine derivatives (54% of Q1 turnover), API & Intermediates (44% of Q1 turnover), and CDMO/CMO (2.4% of Q1 turnover). While CDMO/CMO's current revenue contribution is low due to order timing, the segment saw a significant increase in projects to 53 (from 40 in Q4 FY24), with 27 commercial and 26 development stage projects. The company reiterated its FY25 EBITDA growth guidance of 10-12%. Significant capital expenditure is planned for FY25, including the Atali project, a solar power plant, and Xanthine capacity expansion.

### ## Business Strategy & Drivers

*   **Segment Focus:**
    *   **Xanthine Derivatives:** Maintaining leadership position amidst aggressive Chinese competition by leveraging customer relationships and focusing on export growth (57% of Q1 Xanthine revenue was exported). Expanding end-use and seeking entry into regulated pharma space.
    *   **API & Intermediates:** Strategic focus on regulated markets for higher profitability and stable demand, supported by R&D and process innovation.
    *   **CDMO/CMO:** Strong pipeline growth with 53 projects. Targeting 25-30% YoY growth in FY25. Focus on late-stage projects and commercial scale-up. Benefiting from "China Plus One" and US Biosecure Act.
*   **Capacity Expansion:**
    *   **Atali Project (CDMO/CMO & Intermediates):** Commissioning expected by Q4 FY25. Adding ~450-500 KL capacity.
    *   **Vapi USFDA Intermediate Manufacturing Site (Semi-Commercial Block):** Expected to be operational in Q2 FY25. Bridges gap for small to medium batch sizes.
    *   **Xanthine Capacity Expansion:** Acquiring adjacent land for brownfield expansion to reach 9,000 MT total capacity by early FY26.
    *   **Solar Power Plant (Akola):** Commissioning expected in Q1 FY25, to fulfill ~1/3rd of power requirements and reduce costs.
*   **R&D:** New R&D center for CDMO/CMO showing good traction, expanding capabilities.
*   **Sustainability:** Solar project to reduce manufacturing costs and power dependency.

### ## Management Accountability & Consistency Audit

#### Historical Targets Audit:

*   **FY24 EBITDA Growth:** Previously guided at 10-15%, revised to 8-10%. Actual FY24 consolidated EBITDA grew 13% YoY, meeting or slightly exceeding the revised guidance.
*   **Xanthine Raw Material Production Commencement:** Asset is operational but not continuously run due to market pricing. This is a deviation from the previous operational timeline, though the asset was built.
*   **Atali Project Completion:** Previously targeted for H2 FY25, reiterated for Q4 FY25. Consistent.
*   **Vapi Semi-Commercial Block Completion:** Previously targeting Q2 FY25. Management stated it's nearing completion and expected to be operational in Q2 FY25 (consistent).
*   **CDMO/CMO Segment Growth:** Previously guided for 40-50% annual growth in FY25. The current Q1 FY25 guidance is 25-30% YoY for FY25, a slight adjustment downwards from prior high expectations. Management highlighted a strong increase in project numbers (16 to 53 over ~6 months).
*   **Capex for Atali:** Previously INR 350-500 Crs. Stated at INR 375 crores for the Atali project. Consistent.
*   **Xanthine Capacity Expansion:** Previously targeting 9,000 MT/month by end of FY25. Now stated to achieve this by early FY26.

*   **FY25 EBITDA Growth Guidance:** 10% to 12%.
*   **Long-term EBITDA Growth Guidance:** Around 15% annual growth for the next three years.
*   **CDMO/CMO Segment Growth:** Projecting 25% to 30% YoY growth in FY25.
*   **Atali Project Commissioning:** By Q4 FY25.
*   **Vapi Semi-Commercial Block Operationalization:** In Q2 FY25.
*   **Xanthine Capacity Expansion:** To 9,000 MT/month by early FY26.
