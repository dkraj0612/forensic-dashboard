## Aarti Pharmalabs (AARTIPHARM) - Q3 Fiscal Year 2025 Earnings Call - One-Page Executive Summary

**Date:** February 7, 2025

---

### Executive Overview

Aarti Pharmalabs (APL) reported a record-breaking Q3 FY25, achieving its highest-ever quarterly profit on both standalone and consolidated bases. The company demonstrated strong execution across its three core segments: Xanthine derivatives, API Intermediates, and CDMO/CMO. Consolidated revenue increased by 20% year-on-year to INR 538 crores, with EBITDA growing 34% YoY to INR 129 crores and Profit After Tax surging 40% YoY to INR 74 crores. The Xanthine segment achieved its highest-ever quarterly volume sales, operating at near full capacity despite price declines in the spot market. The API and Intermediate business delivered its best quarterly revenue performance, with a strategic shift towards regulated markets. The CDMO/CMO segment showed promising growth in active and commercial projects, contributing 13% to turnover with an expanded customer base. Management expressed optimism for exceeding FY25 EBITDA growth guidance and highlighted ongoing capacity expansions and sustainability initiatives, including solar power projects.

### Business Strategy & Drivers

*   **Segment Diversification & Growth:** APL continues to leverage its three core segments, with all performing well in Q3 FY25. The company is actively managing price pressures in Xanthine by focusing on long-term customer relationships and pursuing regulatory filings (EU GMP CEP) to shift towards higher-value customers. The API and Intermediate segment is strategically focusing on regulated markets, aiming for sustained growth. The CDMO/CMO segment is a key growth driver, with an increasing number of commercial projects and a focus on attracting new customers.
*   **Capacity Expansion & Infrastructure:**
    *   **Xanthine Derivatives:** Brownfield expansion progressing to increase capacity from 5,000 MTPA to 9,000 MTPA, with Phase 1 expected in H1 FY26 and Phase 2 in H2 FY26.
    *   **Atali Greenfield Project:** Phase 1 (450 KL reactor capacity) expected to be commissioned in Q1 FY26, with ramp-up towards the end of FY26. This facility is primarily for CDMO/CMO and Intermediate manufacturing.
    *   **Solar Power Projects:** 21 MW solar plant at Akola commissioned in Q2 FY25. A new 9 MW solar project for Gujarat units is planned for Q2 FY26. These projects aim to cover over half of the company's electricity requirements.
    *   **Multi-Fuel Boilers:** Installation of solid multi-fuel boilers at two Maharashtra plants in Q4 FY25 to reduce fuel costs and carbon footprint.
*   **Regulatory & Quality Focus:** Continued emphasis on regulatory compliance, with EU GMP CEP filings for the pharmaceutical market from Tarapur Unit 3. This strategy aims to reduce reliance on spot volumes and attract higher-paying customers.
*   **R&D and Innovation:** The company invests in R&D across three centers to support innovators, API business, and scale-up/cost-saving initiatives. Over 58 patents have been filed, and the company focuses on developing new APIs and intermediates, with 12 new APIs under development.

### Management Accountability & Consistency Audit

#### Historical Targets Audit:

*   **FY25 EBITDA Growth Guidance (10-12%):** **Exceeded.** Management expressed optimism that they "may exceed our previously provided guidance of 10% to 12% of EBITDA growth for FY25," indicating strong performance in Q3 FY25.
*   **Xanthine Capacity Expansion (5,000 to 9,000 MTPA):** **On Track.** The expansion is progressing as planned, with phased commissioning timelines provided for FY26.
*   **Atali Greenfield Project Commissioning (Q1 FY26):** **On Track.** Phase 1 commissioning is expected in Q1 FY26, with ramp-up towards the end of FY26.
*   **Vapi Plant Shutdown:** Management stated the impact was "lower than 5% of the turnover and 2% of the profit" and that it would delay some CDMO/CMO sales originally scheduled for Q4 FY25 by one quarter, with no long-term impact anticipated.
*   **Solar Power Project Commissioning:** The 21 MW project at Akola was commissioned in Q2 FY25 as planned.

#### New Forward-Looking Promises, Guidance, or Timelines:

*   **CDMO/CMO FY25 Revenue:** APL expects to "exceed last year's numbers" (which were 170 crores), with expectations of significantly more sales in Q4 FY25 than the first three quarters combined (which had ~76-77 crores).
*   **Xanthine Capacity Utilization:** Management anticipates achieving 90% capacity utilization for the expanded 9,000 MTPA capacity in FY27.
*   **API Business Potential:** The API segment can potentially reach INR 800-1,000 crore depending on product mix and site capacity, with potential for further expansion at the same site.
*   **Atali Revenue Contribution:** Atali is expected to take "two years plus to ramp up" and specific revenue contribution will be clarified in the next call. Asset turn is expected to be between 1x to 1.25x.
*   **CDMO/CMO FY26 Growth:** Management expects "significant growth" and will provide detailed guidance in the next quarter, but anticipates numbers will be "significantly higher than 2024 numbers."
*   **Xanthine Margin Bottomed Out:** Management indicated that spot market prices and margins for Xanthine have bottomed out, and with expanded capacity and focus on contractual/pharma customers, absolute numbers are expected to grow.
*   **Future Guidance:** Full FY26 budgeting and guidance will be provided in the Q4 FY25 earnings call.
