## Executive Overview

Aarti Pharmalabs (AARTIPHARM) reported Q3 FY24 results, marking a quarter of record EBITDA and net profit. Consolidated revenue saw a modest 2% increase QoQ to INR 448 crores. EBITDA surged by 10% QoQ to INR 97 crores, while PAT increased marginally to INR 53 crores. The company continues to focus on its core segments: API & Intermediates, CDMO/CMO, and Xanthine derivatives. Strategic initiatives include backward integration for Xanthine raw materials, capacity expansion at Atali, Gujarat, and a strong emphasis on regulated markets. Management has revised its FY24 EBITDA growth guidance downwards to 8-10% and expects continued ramp-up benefits from new capacities and an increasing contribution from the CDMO/CMO segment.

## Key Financial Metrics

*   **Consolidated Revenue:** INR 448 crores (up 2% QoQ)
*   **Consolidated EBITDA:** INR 97 crores (up 10% QoQ)
*   **Consolidated PAT:** INR 53 crores (marginally up QoQ)
*   **Consolidated EPS:** INR 5.82
*   **Interim Dividend:** INR 2 per share

## Business Strategy & Drivers

Aarti Pharmalabs' strategy remains centered on:

*   **Regulated Market Focus:** Maintained a significant focus, with 53% of API & Intermediates sales to regulated markets in Q3 FY24.
*   **CDMO & CMO Services:** This segment demonstrated strong growth, contributing 14.2% of revenue in Q3 FY24, with management aiming for it to reach 20-25% in the next 2-3 years. The company added one new customer, three new commercial products, and six new R&D products in this segment.
*   **Xanthine Derivatives:** The company expects the pricing pressure in this segment to continue until mid-next year, though margin pressure is mitigated by falling raw material costs and expanded capacity. Volume growth for Xanthine YTD was approximately 3.5% compared to the previous year.
*   **API & Intermediates:** Stable to good demand is observed in steroids, anti-cancer, and lifestyle drugs segments. Management aims to grow absolute margins by optimizing asset utilization and focusing on value-added products.
*   **Backward Integration:** The project to manufacture a key Xanthine raw material is now expected to commence in Q4 FY24, aiming to reduce import dependency.
*   **Capacity Expansion:** The Greenfield project in Atali, Gujarat, remains on track for H2 FY25 completion. A semi-commercial production block at the Vapi USFDA intermediate manufacturing site is targeting Q2 FY25 completion. The company is also planning further generic capacity expansion over the next 15-18 months.
*   **Sustainability:** Efforts continue to extend sustainability initiatives across the corporate entity.

## Management Accountability & Consistency Audit

### Historical Targets Audit:

*   **FY24 EBITDA Growth (Previously 10-15%, revised to 8-10%):** Management revised its EBITDA growth guidance for FY24 to 8-10%. This downward revision is attributed to some postponement of orders for certain projects, shifting sales to the next financial year, and continued pricing pressure in the Xanthine market.
*   **Ramp-up of new expanded blocks at Unit IV, Tarapur & Xanthine Derivatives:** Management confirmed that customers have been onboarded onto the new blocks. The ramp-up of these capacities is expected to occur over the next 2-3 years as larger products are moved to newer blocks, freeing up multipurpose blocks.
*   **Xanthine Raw Material Production Commencement:** The project was initially targeted for Q2 FY24, then shifted to Q3 FY24, and is now expected to begin in Q4 FY24. Management stated that procurement from alternate suppliers at similar price points is mitigating any cost impact.
*   **Capex of approximately 350-500 Crs for a new unit in Gujarat, with commercialization expected in FY25:** The Atali project completion is now guided for H2 FY25. The Capex range for Atali remains INR 350-500 Crs.
*   **Adding 40+ value-added products each year:** R&D continues to focus on this. The company currently has 19 commercial products and 20 R&D stage products in the CDMO/CMO segment.
