## Executive Overview

Aarti Pharmalabs (APL) reported Q1 FY26 results with a slight YoY decline in consolidated revenue to INR 386 crores from INR 555 crores in Q1 FY25. This is primarily attributed to the change in accounting for Ganesh Polychem (GPL), which is now treated as a joint venture and reported as a single line item for share of profit, rather than consolidated turnover. Standalone revenue also saw a dip to INR 375 crores from INR 394 crores YoY, impacted by a planned plant shutdown for upgrades in the Xanthine segment, which affected sales by INR 15-20 crores. Despite revenue pressures, APL demonstrated strong operational performance with standalone EBITDA growing 14% YoY to INR 95 crores and PAT up 9% YoY to INR 51 crores. Consolidated EBITDA was INR 95 crores (down 2% YoY, but comparable after accounting for GPL's shift) and PAT was INR 50 crores (down 11% YoY). Management remains confident in the underlying business fundamentals and long-term growth drivers, including capacity expansions and a strategic focus on higher-margin segments like CDMO and regulated API markets.

### Business Strategy & Drivers

*   **Segmental Focus & Performance:**
    *   **Xanthine Derivatives:** Strategic shift towards export and beverage customers (57% export, 65% beverage volume) driving better pricing and performance despite a plant shutdown impacting standalone topline. Expansion from 5,000 to 9,000 MTPA is progressing, with phased commissioning in H2 FY26.
    *   **API & Intermediates:** Aligned with long-term focus on regulated markets (49% of segment revenue). Growth driven by new molecule development targeting patent expiries and increased filings (DMFs/CEPs). Intermediates business is being strategically deprioritized in favor of CDMO projects to optimize asset utilization.
    *   **CDMO/CMO:** On track to achieve FY26 targets with a strong order book. 60 active projects (33 commercial, 27 development) with 21 customers. New capacities from Atali are expected to support significant growth.
*   **Capacity Expansion & Infrastructure:**
    *   **Xanthine Derivatives:** Brownfield expansion to 9,000 MTPA progressing, phased commissioning in H2 FY26.
    *   **Atali Greenfield Project:** Phase 1 (450 KL capacity) mechanical completion achieved. Commercial production to commence by end of Q2 FY26, with full ramp-up by end of FY26. This facility will cater to CDMO and Intermediates.
*   **Sustainability:** Management noted that current tariff rules do not impact pharma products and Xanthine Derivatives are on the exempt list, indicating minimal impact.
*   **R&D and Innovation:** Continued focus on new molecule development for API segment and enhancing capabilities in CDMO for higher molecular weight products and peptides.

### Management Accountability & Consistency Audit

#### Historical Targets Audit:

*   **FY25 EBITDA Growth Guidance:** The company exceeded previous expectations with strong operational performance, as indicated by the 14% YoY growth in standalone EBITDA in Q1 FY26, even with revenue headwinds.
*   **Xanthine Capacity Expansion (5,000 to 9,000 MTPA):** Progress reported as "progressing as planned, with phased commissioning estimated in H2 FY'26," which aligns with previous updates.
*   **Atali Greenfield Project Commissioning:** Mechanical completion of Phase 1 is confirmed by end of Q1 FY26, with commercial production expected by end of Q2 FY26 and full ramp-up by end of FY26. This is consistent with the updated timeline from the previous quarter.
*   **Vapi Plant Shutdown Impact:** The INR 15-20 crore sales impact from the Xanthine plant shutdown is within the expected range and stated to be temporary.
*   **Ganesh Polychem:** The shift to a joint venture structure and reporting as a single line item for profit, rather than consolidated turnover, was executed as communicated.
