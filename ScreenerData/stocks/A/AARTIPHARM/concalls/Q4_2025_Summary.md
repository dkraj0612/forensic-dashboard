## Aarti Pharmalabs (AARTIPHARM) - Q4 Fiscal Year 2025 Earnings Call - One-Page Executive Summary

**Date:** May 16, 2025

---

### Executive Overview

Aarti Pharmalabs (APL) reported a landmark FY25, culminating in a record-breaking Q4. Consolidated revenue for Q4 FY25 grew 11% YoY to INR 564 crores, with full-year revenue reaching INR 2,113 crores, a 14% YoY increase. EBITDA saw a significant 24% YoY jump in Q4 to INR 146 crores, expanding margins to 26% (from 23% in Q4 FY24). Full-year EBITDA reached INR 464 crores, a 20% YoY increase, reflecting strong operational leverage and efficiency. Profit After Tax (PAT) surged 35% YoY in Q4 to INR 88 crores, marking a quarterly record, and full-year PAT grew 26% to INR 272 crores. The company demonstrated robust performance across its three segments: Xanthine derivatives (34% of Q4 turnover), API & Intermediates (39% of Q4 turnover, with 52% in regulated markets), and CDMO/CMO (27% of Q4 turnover, with 21 customers and 61 active projects). Management expressed confidence in sustained growth driven by capacity expansions, R&D, and sustainability initiatives. The board declared a dividend of INR 2.5 per share for Q4, bringing the full-year total to INR 5 per share.

### Business Strategy & Drivers

*   **Segmental Strength & Focus:** The company continues to leverage its three core segments. Xanthine facilities operated at near full capacity, with stable pricing. The API & Intermediate segment achieved record quarterly sales, with a strategic focus on regulated markets (52% of segment revenue). The CDMO/CMO segment is a significant growth contributor, expanding its customer base and project pipeline, particularly focusing on Phase 3 to final approval projects.
*   **Capacity Expansion & Infrastructure:**
    *   **Xanthine Derivatives:** Capacity expansion to 9,000 MTPA is progressing, with phased commissioning expected in H2 FY26. Full capacity utilization target of 80-90% is projected by FY27.
    *   **Atali Greenfield Project:** Mechanical completion of Phase 1 (450 KL capacity) is expected by end of Q1 FY26, with operationalization and full ramp-up targeted by the end of FY26. This facility will cater to CDMO/CMO and Intermediate manufacturing.
    *   **Sustainability:** Achieved EcoVadis Gold Rating and SBTi approval for GHG emission reduction targets. Solar power projects continue to reduce the carbon footprint.
*   **Regulatory & Market Shift:** Completed filings for USDMFs and CEPs for the pharmaceutical market in the Xanthine segment, aiming for higher realizations. The API segment's growth is driven by regulated markets and export-backed demand.
*   **R&D and Innovation:** Focus on product innovation in the API segment, targeting molecules with upcoming patent expiries. The CDMO segment is also developing capabilities for higher molecular weight products and peptides.

### Management Accountability & Consistency Audit

#### Historical Targets Audit:

*   **FY25 EBITDA Growth Guidance:** The transcript indicates strong performance, with management stating, "FY’25 has been a landmark year for the Company. Q4 served as a strong finish to a year defined by business expansion and operational excellence." The full-year EBITDA growth of 20% (vs. a prior guidance of 10-12% in Q3) significantly exceeded previous expectations.
*   **Xanthine Capacity Expansion (5,000 to 9,000 MTPA):** Progress is reported as "progressing as planned, with phased commissioning estimated in H2 FY'26." This aligns with previous updates.
*   **Atali Greenfield Project Commissioning (Q1 FY26 operationalization):** Mechanical completion is now expected by end of Q1 FY26, with full ramp-up by the end of FY26. This reflects a slight shift in the timeline for *full* ramp-up, with operationalization commencing in phases. Management clarified that qualification processes will take 3-4 quarters post-mechanical completion.
*   **Vapi Plant Shutdown Impact:** Management reiterated the limited impact: "lower than 5% of the turnover and 2% of the profit" and that it would delay some CDMO/CMO sales by one quarter, with no long-term impact. This is consistent with prior calls.
*   **Solar Power Project Commissioning:** The Akola project commissioning in Q2 FY25 was as planned.

#### New Forward-Looking Promises, Guidance, or Timelines:

*   **FY26 Standalone EBITDA Growth:** Guidance of 12% to 15% YoY growth over the FY25 base.
*   **Xanthine Capacity Operationalization:** Full operationalization of 9,000 MT capacity expected by Q1 FY27.
*   **Xanthine Capacity Utilization:** Aspires for 80%-90% capacity utilization in the next 3 years, with 50% sales targeted to beverages and regulated customers.
*   **CDMO/CMO Revenue Growth (FY26):** Estimated growth of 30% to 40%.
*   **Atali Project Revenue Contribution:** Full ramp-up expected by end of FY26, with initial commercial products potentially out earlier. Mechanical completion by end of Q1 FY26.
*   **Atali Asset Turn:** Expected to be between 1x to 1.25x.
*   **API Business Growth:** Driven by regulated markets and new molecules (anti-diabetic, anti-cancer) with patent expiries in the next 3-5 years.
*   **FY26 CAPEX:** Guided at INR 400 to 450 crores.
*   **FY26 Borrowings:** Expected to increase by INR 100-125 crores, resulting in a debt-equity ratio of approximately 0.23-0.25.
*   **Xanthine Pricing:** Prices are considered to have "bottomed out" in the spot market. Higher-end prices may slightly decrease, while lower-end prices are expected to rise, leading to an overall balance. Pharmaceutical grade caffeine is expected to contribute to higher realizations.
*   **CDMO Business Maturity:** Bulk of sales (75-80%) come from Phase 3 onwards and commercialized products. Some new products expected to launch in the next 2 years.
*   **Atali Phase 1 Ramp-up:** Expected to be fully ramped up by FY28, with commissioning of additional blocks planned every two years.
*   **FY27 Outlook:** Expected to be a significant growth year due to full ramp-up of Atali Phase 1, Xanthine capacity, and potential debottlenecking projects.
*   **Xanthine Revenue Potential:** Range of INR 1,000 crore to 1,250 crore with optimal utilization and market traction.
*   **CDMO Contribution to Revenue:** While not explicitly quantified as a percentage for FY26, the strong revenue growth of 30-40% implies an increasing contribution.
*   **Peptides & Biotech Capabilities:** Actively working on developing capabilities for higher molecular weight products and mid-size peptides, with attempts at projects in this space.
*   **Ganesh Polychem Business:** Experiencing a temporary slowdown and facility upgrade (3-4 month shutdown), impacting Q4 FY25 and Q1 FY26, but expected to recover with a lower cost production route and expansion in aerospace applications.
*   **Medium-Term Growth:** Reiterated confidence in exceeding the previous 3-year average growth guidance of 15%.
*   **API Segment Growth Drivers:** New product launches, transition with innovators, and targeting second source opportunities where backward integration is strong.
*   **CAPEX Breakdown (FY25-FY26 Cumulative):** Atali: INR 400-425 crores; Solar: INR 85 crores; Xanthine expansion: INR 150 crores; R&D: ~INR 40 crores annually; other smaller projects.
*   **Ganesh Polychem Margin:** Expectation of becoming a competitive source with potential margin expansion post-upgrade.
*   **CDMO Margin:** Confirmed as a higher margin business compared to API and Xanthine.
*   **API Segment Backward Integration:** Approximately 60%-70% of the portfolio has backward integration, with exceptions for some steroid intermediaries.
*   **API Intermediate Client Overlap with CDMO:** Unlikely for existing API intermediate clients to directly transition to CDMO for the same products, as CDMO focus is on newer, protected products. However, some innovators may utilize APL as a second source for older products.
