## Executive Overview

Aarti Pharmalabs (APL) reported Q3 FY26 results, showing a mixed financial performance. Standalone revenue declined YoY to INR 425 crores from INR 471 crores, with EBITDA down to INR 103 crores from INR 115 crores and PAT falling to INR 44 crores from INR 74 crores. Consolidated figures were not explicitly detailed for Q3 FY26 in this transcript, but the standalone decline indicates a challenging quarter. The company acknowledged revised full-year EBITDA guidance, expecting it to be largely in line with last year with only marginal growth, citing delays in Atali plant stabilization and softness in API and intermediates. Despite these near-term pressures, management remains confident in the mid- to long-term growth trajectory, driven by operational efficiencies and prudent scaling of business segments. The company declared an interim dividend of INR 1.5 per share.

## Business Strategy & Drivers

*   **Segmental Focus & Performance:**
    *   **Xanthine Derivatives:** Current operating capacity at 500 tonnes/month. Expansion to 800 tonnes/month (9,600 tonnes/annum installed capacity) with mechanical completion of the new site by March '26 and trial production starting next quarter. China withdrawing rebate benefits is expected to push prices up by 8-10%. India's duty-free status for caffeine to the US provides a competitive advantage.
    *   **API & Intermediates:** Continues to face margin pressure. Management is focused on patent expiries and new product launches, particularly in oncology and improving manufacturing for diabetic APIs. New launches for anti-cancer APIs are expected in FY26. Debottlenecking of steroid and general blocks is underway. Atali plant, once stabilized, will increase intermediate capacity.
    *   **CDMO/CMO:** On track to meet FY26 guidance, but exceeding it now looks difficult due to project delivery pushes. Management is working with 21 customers and 59 active projects (40 commercial). Efforts to increase wallet share with innovator partners are ongoing, with some projects contributing meaningfully. Expanding geographical presence in Europe and North America.
*   **Capacity Expansion & Infrastructure:**
    *   **Atali Plant:** Started production of qualifying batches in Q3 FY26. Initial hiccups impacted the production plan but are expected to be resolved by end of Q4 FY26. Full commercialization is targeted over the next 2-3 quarters, contributing meaningfully from FY27. Capitalized INR 300 crores of the INR 450 crores outlay, with the balance expected in the next couple of months. Incremental quarterly Opex (including depreciation) of ~INR 1.5 crores is being incurred.
    *   **Xanthine Expansion:** Mechanical completion targeted by end of March '26, with incremental capacity available in Q1 FY27.

## Management Accountability & Consistency Audit

#### Historical Targets Audit:

*   **FY26 CDMO/CMO Revenue Guidance:** Management stated they are "on track to meet" the guidance but "exceeding that target... now looks difficult" due to project delivery delays. This represents a slight shift from the previous optimistic tone of exceeding the guidance.
*   **Atali Plant Stabilization:** The acknowledgement of "starting hiccups" impacting the production plan and the expectation of resolution by the end of the current quarter (Q4 FY26) indicates a deviation from the previously stated timeline for full ramp-up and meaningful revenue contribution to begin sooner.
*   **Xanthine Capacity Expansion:** Mechanical completion by end of March '26 is consistent with previous timelines, with incremental capacity available in Q1 FY27.
*   **Full Year FY26 EBITDA Growth:** Management has revised guidance downwards to "largely in line with last year with only marginal growth" from a previously guided 8%-12% YoY. This is a factual revision due to project delays and API softness.

*   **FY26 EBITDA Growth (Full Year):** Expected to be "largely in line with last year with only marginal growth."
