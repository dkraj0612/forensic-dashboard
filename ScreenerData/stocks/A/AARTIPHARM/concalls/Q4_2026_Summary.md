## Executive Overview

Aarti Pharmalabs (APL) reported Q4 FY26 results, showing a mixed financial performance. Standalone revenue saw a 9% year-on-year increase to INR 580 crores in Q4 FY26, compared to INR 530 crores in Q4 FY25. However, for the full year FY26, revenue stood at INR 1798 crores, a marginal increase from INR 1771 crores in FY25. EBITDA for Q4 FY26 was INR 134 crores, down from INR 141 crores in the prior year quarter. Full-year FY26 EBITDA was INR 406 crores, a decrease from INR 428 crores in FY25. Profit After Tax (PAT) for Q4 FY26 was INR 62 crores, down from INR 89 crores in Q4 FY25, and full-year FY26 PAT was INR 176 crores, a significant drop from INR 257 crores in FY25. A net foreign exchange loss of INR 33 crores impacted the P&L for FY26. The board declared a final dividend of INR 2 per share, bringing the total for FY26 to INR 3.50 per share. Management anticipates 15% to 18% growth in both revenue and EBITDA for the next three to four years, with CDMO/CMO expected to lead growth in FY27 at 40% to 50%.

## Business Strategy & Drivers

*   **Segmental Focus & Performance:**
    *   **Xanthine Derivatives:** Achieved highest-ever quarterly revenue in Q4 FY26. Current capacity at 6,000 metric tons/annum, with expansion to 9,000 metric tons/annum expected to ramp up in the coming quarters, starting end of current quarter. Management sees potential to cross INR 1000 Crores in revenue from Xanthine with new capacities. Pricing has seen some increases due to geopolitical issues and withdrawal of Chinese rebates, with management aiming to pass on costs.
    *   **API & Intermediates:** Experienced a soft FY26 with degrowth, attributed to inventory issues and customer-end problems. FY27 is expected to be a better year, surpassing FY25 API/Intermediates revenue numbers (around INR 700-770 Crores) driven by new product launches (oncology, diabetic APIs) and debottlenecking of steroid and general blocks.
    *   **CDMO/CMO:** Showed robust 32% YoY revenue growth in FY26, with all revenue from phase 3 and commercial molecules. Achieved highest ever quarterly revenue of INR 155 Crores in Q4 FY26. Management is exploring a dedicated manufacturing block at Atali for specific long-term projects, potentially yielding INR 250-300 Crores per block, with a 12-month construction timeline.
*   **Capacity Expansion & Infrastructure:**
    *   **Atali Plant:** Phase 1 is nearing full operationalization by the end of the current quarter (Q1 FY27) after overcoming initial startup issues. The plant is multi-purpose with 440 kL reactor capacity. Further expansion in Atali is planned with new blocks in FY27.
    *   **Xanthine Expansion:** Incremental capacity will be available by the end of the current quarter, with ramp-up to 9,000 metric tons/annum over the next few quarters.
    *   **Tarapur Unit-4:** Steroid block debottlenecking increased capacity by one-third. Further brownfield expansions are planned for FY27 for anti-cancer and other blocks.
*   **R&D Investment:** Decision to invest in R&D for peptides and oligonucleotides, exploring newer technologies with long-term potential.
*   **Geopolitical Impact:** Acknowledged the impact of geopolitical tensions in West Asia causing logistics hurdles and rising energy costs, straining profitability, especially in the intermediates segment where price pass-through is difficult. CDMO and Xanthine segments have had more success passing on cost hikes.

#### Historical Targets Audit:

*   **FY26 CDMO/CMO Revenue Guidance:** Management stated they were "on track to meet" guidance and "exceeding that target... now looks difficult." This is consistent with the current transcript indicating the CDMO segment is on track to meet guidance.
*   **Atali Plant Stabilization:** Management acknowledged "hiccups" in the previous quarter and in this transcript confirms Atali Phase 1 is "largely past startup issues" and "likely to become completely operational by the end of this current quarter" (Q1 FY27). This indicates a slight shift in timeline for full ramp-up, aligning with the current quarter's operationalization.
*   **Full Year FY26 EBITDA Growth:** The previous transcript noted a revised guidance of "largely in line with last year with only marginal growth." The current results show a decline in full-year EBITDA by 5%, which deviates from "largely in line" to a slight contraction, but the earlier indication of only marginal growth, rather than significant growth, was accurate.
*   **Xanthine Capacity Expansion:** Mechanical completion by end of March '26 was stated previously, and the current transcript confirms incremental capacity will be available by the end of the current quarter (Q1 FY27), with ramp-up to 9,000 metric tons/annum in the next few quarters, which is consistent.
