## Executive Overview

Aarti Pharmalabs (AARTIPHARM) reported a strong Q4 FY24, achieving its highest-ever EBITDA and net profit for the quarter and the fiscal year. Consolidated revenue for Q4 FY24 was not explicitly stated, but the company highlighted a significant year-on-year EBITDA growth of 47% to ₹117.5 crores and a PAT increase of 52% to ₹65.3 crores. For the full year FY24, consolidated EBITDA grew by 13% to ₹386 crores, and PAT increased by 12% to ₹217 crores, resulting in an EPS of ₹22.9. The company operates across three segments: Xanthine derivatives (44% of Q4 turnover), API & Intermediates (37.6% of Q4 turnover), and CDMO/CMO (18.4% of Q4 turnover). The CDMO/CMO segment showed significant growth, working with 16 customers on 40 projects, including 21 commercial ones. The company has revised its FY25 EBITDA growth guidance to 10-12%, while maintaining a long-term goal of around 15% annual growth. Significant capex is planned for FY25, including expansions at Atali, Vapi, and for Xanthine derivatives.

## Business Strategy & Drivers

*   **Segment Focus:**
    *   **Xanthine Derivatives:** Largest manufacturing facility in India. Brownfield expansion and debottlenecking underway to achieve 750 MT/month capacity by end of FY25. Expanding end-use to food & beverages and entering regulated pharma space for Caffeine and Theophylline (CEP/USDMF).
    *   **API & Intermediates:** Focus on lifestyle drugs (anti-hypertensives, anti-diabetic, oncology) in regulated markets (54% of API & Intermediates turnover), offering lower cost and reduced China dependence for KSM. Stable to good demand observed.
    *   **CDMO/CMO:** Currently working with 16 customers on 40 projects (21 commercial, 19 development). Expanding capabilities in peptides, oligo nucleotides, ADC Linkers. USP is commercial scale-up and manufacturing expertise for long-term partnerships. Targeting 40-50% annual growth for the next fiscal year.
*   **Capacity Expansion:**
    *   Atali Project (CDMO/CMO & Intermediates): Commissioning expected by Q4 FY25.
    *   Vapi USFDA Intermediate Manufacturing Site: Semi-commercial block expected to become operational in Q1 FY25 (current quarter). This block will cater to early development requirements up to 100 kg.
    *   Xanthine Capacity Expansion: Brownfield expansion targeting 9,000 MT/month capacity by end of FY25.
*   **Backward Integration:** Project for a key Xanthine raw material is operational but not continuously run due to current market pricing. Management is ready to operate it if prices increase.
*   **Sustainability:** Setting up a solar power plant in Akola, estimated to fulfill 1/3 of power requirements and reduce manufacturing costs.
*   **R&D:** New R&D center focused on innovators. Expanding capabilities in peptides and flow chemistry.
