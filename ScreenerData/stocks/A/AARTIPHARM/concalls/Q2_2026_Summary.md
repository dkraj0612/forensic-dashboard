## Executive Overview

Aarti Pharmalabs (APL) reported Q2 FY26 results showcasing a mixed performance. Standalone revenue grew 11% YoY to INR 417 crores, driven by the Xanthine Derivatives segment and some improvement in API sales. However, consolidated revenue saw a decline to INR 418 crores from INR 458 crores in Q2 FY25, primarily due to the shift in Ganesh Polychem (GPL) accounting. Profitability faced headwinds, with standalone EBITDA declining 12% YoY to INR 75 crores and PAT down 35% YoY to INR 31 crores, impacted by Forex losses and margin pressures in the API segment. Consolidated EBITDA and PAT also declined YoY to INR 75 crores and INR 28 crores, respectively. Management has revised its full-year EBITDA growth guidance to 8%-12% YoY. Despite these challenges, the company remains focused on its strategic pillars of capacity expansion, particularly the Atali plant and Xanthine derivatives, and growth in the CDMO segment, where it expects to exceed its earlier growth guidance.

### Business Strategy & Drivers

*   **Segmental Focus & Performance:**
    *   **Xanthine Derivatives:** Showed decent performance in Q2 FY26 due to plant turnaround completion, increased capacity from debottlenecking to 6,000 MTPA (operating at 500 MT/month currently), and improved margins from site qualification for large customers. Pricing is considered to have bottomed out and stabilized.
    *   **API & Intermediates:** Experienced high margin pressure and a skewed sales mix towards lower margin APIs. Management acknowledges that last year's strong performance was driven by new product launches, and the second year faces increased competition and inventory correction. However, they anticipate normalization in H2 FY26 with expected new launches, particularly in the anti-cancer segment. The company is exploring backward integration through outside suppliers for certain intermediates to optimize capacity utilization.
    *   **CDMO/CMO:** On track to exceed the 30%-40% YoY growth guidance for FY26. The transition of 6-8 molecules from development to commercial stage is encouraging, providing long-term visibility. Management is actively expanding its geographical presence with an office in Europe and plans for North America. The Atali plant, once fully operational, will bolster manufacturing capabilities for both CDMO and Intermediates.
*   **Capacity Expansion & Infrastructure:**
    *   **Xanthine Derivatives:** Expansion to 9,000 MTPA is ongoing, with phased commissioning expected by the end of FY26.
    *   **Atali Greenfield Project:** Inaugurated in September 2025, with partial commercialization for trial batches and customer qualifications underway. Expected to be fully operational in 2-3 quarters, contributing meaningfully to revenue from FY27. Initial expenses will start hitting the P&L from Q3 FY26.
*   **Financial Discipline:** Management reiterated its commitment to targeting growth while maintaining financial discipline.

### Management Accountability & Consistency Audit

#### Historical Targets Audit:

*   **Q1 FY26 Standalone Revenue vs. Guidance:** Achieved INR 375 Cr, aligning with the expectation of impact from plant shutdown (INR 15-20 Cr).
*   **Q1 FY26 Standalone EBITDA Growth:** Achieved 14% YoY growth, meeting the positive outlook.
*   **Xanthine Capacity Expansion (5,000 to 9,000 MTPA):** Progress reported as "progressing as planned," with current capacity at 6,000 MTPA and full commissioning expected by end of FY26. This is consistent with previous updates.
*   **Atali Greenfield Project Commissioning:** Mechanical completion achieved by end of Q1 FY26, with commercial production expected by end of Q2 FY26 and full ramp-up by end of FY26. This aligns with prior timelines.
*   **GPL Accounting Change:** Executed as communicated, with GPL now treated as a joint venture.
*   **FY26 CDMO/CMO Revenue Growth (30%-40%):** Management stated they are "on track to achieve and exceed" this target.
*   **FY26 Standalone EBITDA Growth Guidance (12%-15%):** Revised downward to 8%-12% YoY for the full year.
