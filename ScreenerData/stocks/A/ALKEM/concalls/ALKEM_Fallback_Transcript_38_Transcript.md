### 👤 Inspiring Healthier Lives
6 key trends in the Indian healthcare landscape and… While pharma will still be <20% of the overall healthcare spend, it will grow to be as large as the US Gx market (in net value) over the next 5-7 years and continue to drive higher value creation given strong valuation multiples. Healthcare in India is at a tipping point: Healthcare expenditure is expected to triple to $450+ Bn (~6% of GDP) by 2030, from ~$150 - 160 Bn today (~4% of GDP today) on the back of rising income levels, consciousness towards health, and enhanced government outlay. Growth will be broad-based, with several sub-segments beyond pharma emerging as attractive opportunities (e.g., medical device, diagnostic); multiple pharma companies are looking to invest to emerge as integrated healthcare leaders in India. Channel power will continue to rise (aggregated distributors will have a 15-20% share by 2030 and Organized pharma retail will become 20-25% of the total market in 5-6 years) and, combined with potential regulatory changes, will create risk for dilution in pharma value pool Integrated ecosystems will emerge across key disease areas as patient involvement and empowerment continues to rise, and they look for holistic improvement in outcomes Within pharma, while some of the traditional growth drivers will continue to be bed-rock of the market (e.g. Chronic therapies, large brands), players will need to evolve operating model to sustain out-performance in the market More and more therapies will get “massified” - Winning the battle of initiation at GPs critical in chronic battlegrounds “India for India R&D”, “Licensing from the East” and “Innovation beyond molecule” will become the norm as players look to differentiate
