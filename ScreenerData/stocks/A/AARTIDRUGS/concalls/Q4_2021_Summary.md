## Executive Overview

Aarti Drugs Limited (ADL) reported a robust performance for FY2021, driven by a significant increase in consolidated revenue and improved profitability. Consolidated revenue grew by 19.5% YoY to ₹2,159.3 Crores, with APIs contributing 79% and formulations 13%. The API segment saw strong demand across multiple therapies, though some segments like anti-protozoal and antibiotics experienced subdued demand due to lockdowns. The company highlighted improved operating leverage and process efficiencies leading to a substantial increase in EBITDA margins to 20.5% for FY2021, from 16.7% in FY2020 (inferred from prior quarter's EBITDA margin). Profit After Tax (PAT) more than doubled to ₹280.4 Crores. ADL successfully navigated supply chain issues due to its backward integration strategy. The company also announced a ₹550 Crores capex plan over three years for backward integration and capacity expansion, predominantly funded through internal accruals. Regulatory compliance, including the conversion of manufacturing locations to zero-liquid discharge, was also a key focus.

## Business Strategy & Drivers

*   **API Dominance & Diversification:** Continued focus on API segment with strong performance in anti-inflammatory products (Nimesulide, Diclofenac Sodium, Celecoxib). Aim to increase share of lifestyle and chronic therapeutic areas.
*   **Backward Integration:** Strategic priority to reduce reliance on external suppliers, mitigate supply chain risks, and improve margins. This has been a key factor in navigating disruptions.
*   **Capacity Expansion:** ₹550 Crores capex planned over three years for backward integration and capacity expansion, primarily funded by internal accruals. Incremental capacities expected to drive future revenue growth.
*   **Formulation Growth:** Targeting growth through existing capacities and contract manufacturing. Capex of ₹40-45 Crores planned for the formulation segment.
*   **Regulated Markets Focus:** Commitment to exploring opportunities in regulated markets with enhanced data integrity and automation.
*   **Zero-Liquid Discharge:** Conversion of manufacturing facilities to zero-liquid discharge to enhance environmental compliance and mitigate risks, despite initial cost implications.
*   **R&D and New Product Development:** Pipeline of products under development for API and finished dosages, with a focus on antifungal, antidiabetic, and anticoagulant therapies. Launch of new antidiabetic product.
*   **Cost Leadership:** Aiming to be a cost leader in high-volume API products through economies of scale, technology, R&D, process improvement, and backward integration.

## Management Accountability & Consistency Audit

*   **Audit of Stated Targets vs. Actual Performance:**
    *   **Revenue Growth:** The company achieved strong consolidated revenue growth of 19.5% in FY2021, exceeding the robust growth seen in previous periods and demonstrating resilience. The API and formulation segments both contributed positively to this growth.
    *   **EBITDA Margins:** Management guided to maintain EBITDA margins in the range of 18% to 20% and achieved 20.5% for FY2021, indicating successful execution in improving operating leverage and process efficiencies. While Q4 FY21 margin was lower at 16.3%, this was attributed to factors like plant shutdowns due to pollution control issues and some raw material price increases, which management expects to normalize.
    *   **Capacity Utilization & Expansion:** The reported utilization of 64% in Q4 FY21 due to pollution issues was a significant deviation from their usual high utilization. However, the company has proactively addressed this by converting affected units to zero-liquid discharge, mitigating future risks. The ₹550 Crores capex plan over three years is a concrete commitment to future expansion.
    *   **Debt-to-Equity Ratio:** The improvement in the Net Debt/Equity ratio to 0.38x demonstrates prudent financial management. Management acknowledged that leverage might increase slightly with the upcoming capex but expects a lower cost of debt to improve return ratios.
    *   **Backward Integration:** The transcript consistently emphasizes the success and strategic importance of backward integration in mitigating supply chain disruptions and improving margins, aligning with previous discussions.
