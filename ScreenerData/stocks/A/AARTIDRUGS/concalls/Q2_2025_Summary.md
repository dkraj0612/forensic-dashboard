## Executive Overview

Aarti Drugs Limited (ADL) reported Q2 FY25 results marked by a year-on-year decline in revenues and profitability. This downturn is primarily attributed to lower realisations driven by negative rate variance and softer market demand in the API business. While volumes remained flat year-on-year, a 8% quarter-on-quarter increase in API volumes offers a glimmer of improvement. Management anticipates margin recovery in H2 FY25, fueled by an upturn in selling prices and anticipated growth in export sales. The new Sayakha, Gujarat facility for Specialty Chemicals is slated for commencement this quarter, with operating leverage expected to benefit the company from H2 FY25. The Salicylic Acid plant, which commenced production, is currently at a low ramp-up (100 tons/month) with challenges in achieving full potential, targeting over 300 tons/month by Q4 FY25. Capex for FY25 is projected at Rs. 200 crores, funded by internal accruals and term loans. The company also completed a share buyback of 665,000 equity shares at Rs. 900 per share in September 2024.

## Business Strategy & Drivers

*   **API Segment:**
    *   **Pricing & Demand:** Facing headwinds due to negative rate variance and softer domestic/export demand (exacerbated by elections in export geographies). Expectation of pricing stabilization in H2 FY25.
    *   **Capacity Utilization:** Current standalone API segment utilization is around 78%.
    *   **Product Mix:** Antibiotics (40%), Antidiabetics (18%), Antiprotozoals (17%), Anti-inflammatory (10%), Antifungal (10%) formed the Q2 FY25 API sales. Adding capacity for Metronidazole and Fluconazole.
*   **Specialty Chemicals (Sayakha Greenfield Project):**
    *   **Status:** Plant is complete, trials are ongoing for derivative plant, main plant trials to begin mid-to-late November 2025.
    *   **Benefits:** Expected to provide operating leverage from H2 FY25. Primarily for captive consumption, with some external sales. Potential to add ~Rs. 40 crores to EBITDA long-term.
*   **Salicylic Acid:**
    *   **Status:** Production commenced, but facing teething issues. Currently producing ~100 tons/month, targeting 300+ tons/month by Q4 FY25. Full potential capacity is 1,800 MT/month, to be ramped up over FY25-FY26.
    *   **Challenges:** Impacted by dumping from China, leading to price drops (from INR 150-160/kg to INR 125-130/kg). Company considering capacity for derivatives (salicylates) if Salicylic Acid itself is not profitable.
    *   **Financial Impact:** ~Rs. 5.5 crores PBT impact in Q2 FY25 due to lower production and unabsorbed overheads.
*   **Formulation Segment:**
    *   **Growth:** Strong performance in registrations across geographies. Chilean subsidiary performing well.
    *   **Regulatory Approvals:** US FDA approval received for oncology block (Baddi plant) in Q1 FY25. UK MHRA approval for OSD facility (Pinnacle) in Q2 FY25.
    *   **Order Book:** Strong order book of over $12 million for formulation business, expecting pick-up in coming quarters.
*   **Sustainability:** Published first sustainability report. Focus on reducing carbon footprint through renewable energy.
*   **Capital Allocation:** Completed share buyback. Committed to shareholder payouts while investing in growth.

### Audit of Stated Targets vs. Actual Performance:

*   **Sayakha Facility Commencement:** Trials are ongoing, with main plant trials expected mid-to-late November 2025. This aligns with the current quarter's (Q3 FY25) expected commencement for trials, though commercial production timing is now focused on operationalization from this quarter onwards. Management expects operating leverage from H2 FY25.
*   **Salicylic Acid Ramp-up:** Production has commenced but is facing "teething issues" and is at a low utilization of ~100 tons/month, significantly below the targeted 300+ tons/month by Q4 FY25. The stated impact of Rs. 5.5 crores on PBT in Q2 FY25 highlights the current underperformance of this project.
*   **API Pricing & Demand:** Management acknowledges year-on-year revenue and profitability decline due to lower realisations driven by negative rate variance and softer market demand. This confirms the headwinds mentioned in the previous summary, with specific mention of election impacts in export geographies.
*   **Margin Performance:** Reported consolidated EBITDA margin of 11.5% in Q2 FY25, a decline from the previous period's implied margins. Management's long-term target of 14.5%-15.5% EBITDA remains, but near-term performance is impacted by the current API pricing environment.
*   **Revenue Growth Guidance:** The previous guidance of "early teens" volume growth for FY26 is now revised. For FY25, management expects a "flattish" year compared to FY24 due to the negative rate variance and lacklustre demand in the first two quarters. FY26 is anticipated as a "turnaround story" contingent on Salicylic Acid ramp-up and Sayakha project success.
*   **US FDA Status:** A surprise audit occurred in September 2025, resulting in 7 observations. Management is confident in their response and expects it to lead to positive results, potentially opening up European markets even before US business kicks in. The impact on financials is expected to be minimal in the first 6 months post-approval, with older projects showing signs of improvement within that timeframe.
