## Executive Overview

Aarti Drugs Limited (ADL), established in 1984, is a prominent Indian pharmaceutical company specializing in the manufacturing of Active Pharmaceutical Ingredients (APIs), Pharma Intermediates, and Specialty Chemicals. As part of the larger Aarti Group of Industries, ADL leverages a robust R&D division and multiple manufacturing facilities across India. The company holds significant global market positions in key products such as Metformin, Fluoroquinolones, Tinidazole, Metronidazole, Ketoconazole, and Nimesulide. With approximately 38% of its revenue derived from exports, ADL serves a diverse global and domestic clientele, emphasizing consistent quality, timely delivery, and competitive pricing. The presentation highlights the company's strategic focus on expanding capacities, developing new products, and strengthening its market leadership in core segments, including APIs and Specialty Chemicals. The recent commissioning of the Pinnacle Life Science plant in Sikkim and ongoing capacity expansions are key initiatives driving future growth.

## Business Strategy & Drivers

*   **Capacity Expansion:** Significant CAPEX (₹75-100 crore annually) is being undertaken to fuel growth over the next 5 years, particularly in the API business, to address regulatory tightening and create entry barriers.
*   **Product Leadership:** Aiming for leadership in core product categories like Metformin (becoming the largest player post-second phase expansion), Fluoroquinolones, and Anti-fungal products.
*   **Backward Integration:** Emphasis on backward integration, as seen in Fluoro-quinolones and Anti-fungal segments, to enhance cost competitiveness and supply chain control.
*   **New Product Development:** Strong R&D pipeline with approximately 30 APIs developed in the last five years. Focus on process development and scaling up new products.
*   **Specialty Chemicals Growth:** Incremental expansion of multipurpose chloro-sulphonation lines and a new dedicated specialty line commissioned in H2 FY19.
*   **Formulations Business:** Developing value-added formulations from existing APIs, toll manufacturing, and expanding registrations in export markets, including Latin America and Africa. Partnerships with European distributors and US companies are also in place.
*   **Targeting Regulated Markets:** Development of a multipurpose facility in Tarapur designed for European markets, with Phase I operational and Phase II planned for H2 FY20.
*   **Geographic Diversification:** Maintaining a diversified export clientele (top client ~5.69%) and a strong domestic market share with a well-diversified local client base (top client ~2.98%).
*   **Industry Tailwinds:** Leveraging growth drivers in the Indian pharmaceutical industry, including increasing API production, CRAMS growth, formulation export potential, and government initiatives like 'Pharma Vision 2020'.
