## Executive Overview

Aarti Drugs Limited (ADL) reported a strong Q4 FY25, with consolidated revenue increasing 9% YoY to INR 679 crores and EBITDA margins improving to 14%. This growth was driven by robust global API demand, a 15.5% increase in API volumes (primarily exports), and improved operating leverage, leading to standalone EBITDA margins of 14.5%. For the full year FY25, revenue declined 5% YoY due to muted demand and volatile raw material prices, yet the company maintained annual EBITDA margins at 12.6% and improved gross margins by over 200 basis points to 35.8% in H2 FY25. Key strategic developments include receiving USFDA clearance for its Tarapur API facility, removing it from import alert 66-40, and commencing trial production at the greenfield Sayakha facility for backward integration of antidiabetic products. The company also made progress on its solar power initiative, acquiring a stake in an SPV for a 24.4 MW peak solar power plant.

## Business Strategy & Drivers

*   **API Segment:**
    *   **USFDA Clearance:** Tarapur API facility removed from import alert 66-40; exports to the US can resume for key APIs (Ciprofloxacin HCl, Zolpidem Tartrate, etc.). Expected to improve regulated sales and margins.
    *   **Volume Growth:** Driven by strong global demand and exports, with a 15.5% volume increase in Q4 FY25.
    *   **Pricing:** Rate variance was negative 4-5% YoY for the quarter, but stabilized QoQ. Anticipates 3-4% negative rate variance in H1 FY26, with stabilization in H2 FY26.
    *   **Capacity Expansion:** Metronidazole capacity increased from 245 to 330 tons/month.
*   **Specialty Chemicals:**
    *   **Sayakha Greenfield Project:** Trial production commenced; expected to stabilize within the current quarter (Q1 FY26). Aims to reduce reliance on external raw materials and expand margins.
    *   **Tarapur Greenfield Project:** Operational challenges resolved; targeting a gradual scale-up to over 700 tons/month by June 2025 and ~1,600 tons/month by end of FY26.
    *   **Chlorosulfonation Line:** Reverted to batch manufacturing process due to quality issues with the new continuous process. Losses curtailed; capacity to be scaled up using the older process over the next 3-4 months.
    *   **Salicylic Acid:** Meaningful contribution expected in FY26 after launch in FY25.
*   **Formulation Segment (Pinnacle Life Science):**
    *   **Capacity Expansion:** Brownfield expansion completed, expecting revenues from Q1 FY26.
    *   **Product Launches:** Targeting approvals for diabetic and cardiac products (DPP-4, anticoagulants) in Q1-Q2 FY26, and 2 oncology drugs by end of FY26 for regulated markets.
    *   **Growth Target:** Aiming for 20-25% revenue growth in FY26.
    *   **Margins:** Expecting EBITDA margins of 14-15% in FY26.
*   **Sustainability:**
    *   **Solar Power Plant:** Acquired 26.25% stake in SPV for a 24.4 MW peak solar plant; total investment INR 8.05 crores. Expected to reduce power costs by INR 10-15 crores in FY26 and INR 25-30 crores annually from FY27.
    *   **Cogeneration Boiler:** Implemented for captive power consumption.
*   **Backward Integration:** Focus on antidiabetic portfolio at Sayakha plant. Methylamine-based integration for antidiabetic portfolio.

## Management Accountability & Consistency Audit

### Audit of Stated Targets vs. Actual Performance:

*   **Q4 FY25 Performance:** Management successfully met and exceeded expectations for Q4 FY25, reporting 9% revenue growth and 14% EBITDA margins, a significant improvement from the previous quarters' 11-12% range. This was driven by strong API demand and volume growth, validating their efforts in operational efficiency.
*   **FY25 Performance:** The 5% YoY revenue decline for FY25 was acknowledged as a result of muted global demand and raw material price volatility. However, the company's ability to maintain EBITDA margins at 12.6% and improve gross margins by over 200 bps demonstrates resilience and effective cost management, aligning with the trend of margin improvement seen in H2 FY25.
*   **USFDA Clearance:** The removal of the Tarapur facility from import alert 66-40 on February 14, 2025, is a significant positive development that directly addresses a key concern from previous periods and opens up crucial regulated markets.
*   **Sayakha Greenfield Project:** Trial production has commenced as planned in Q1 FY26, with stabilization expected within the quarter, fulfilling previous timelines.
*   **Tarapur Greenfield Project:** The ramp-up target has been revised upwards to over 700 tons/month by June 2025, and ~1,600 tons/month by end of FY26, an increase from previous targets of 500+ tons/month.
*   **Chlorosulfonation Line:** Management confirmed the rollback of the new continuous process due to quality issues impacting user tests, and a return to the batch process. While this curtails losses and improves margins in H2 FY25, it signifies a deviation from the intended efficiency gains and capacity expansion plans for this specific line, with a revised scale-up plan now focused on the older process.
*   **Formulation Segment Growth:** Management acknowledges a dip in FY25 due to brownfield expansion and audits but anticipates 20-25% growth in FY26, with planned product launches.
*   **EBITDA Margin Guidance:** Management reiterated its target to maintain standalone margins at 14-15% and achieve 14% consolidated EBITDA margins for FY26, indicating confidence in sustained performance.
*   **Revenue Guidance Revision:** The previously stated INR 4,000 crore revenue target for FY27 has been revised downwards to a more conservative INR 3,000-3,500 crores due to price variations.
