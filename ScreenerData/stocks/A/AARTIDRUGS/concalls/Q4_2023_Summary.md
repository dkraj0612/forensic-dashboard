## Executive Overview

Aarti Drugs Limited (ADL) concluded FY23 with a strong Q4 performance, driven by a 7% year-on-year consolidated revenue growth and sequential improvement in EBITDA margins by approximately 190 basis points. This improvement was attributed to easing input costs and efficient working capital management. The API segment saw a 12% YoY revenue growth for FY23, supported by steady volume off-take in chronic therapies, while the Specialty Chemicals segment continued its high growth trajectory with a 38% YoY increase. Management anticipates further margin expansion in upcoming quarters due to the easing of high-cost inventory, upcoming entry into the dermatology segment, and backward integration initiatives planned for H2 FY24. The company also reported its highest-ever monthly sales in March 2023, leading to a temporary increase in receivables, which are expected to normalize in Q1 FY24. ADL incurred approximately ₹315 crores in capex over the last two years, with ₹250-350 crores projected for FY24, primarily for capacity expansion, backward integration, and new product launches. The balance sheet remains healthy, with leverage at approximately 0.51x.

## Business Strategy & Drivers

*   **Capacity Expansion:** Continued focus on completing significant capex projects by FY24, including Tarapur specialty chemical (by Q1 FY24) and Tarapur dermatology (by Q2 FY24). The majority of the INR 600 crores capex will be completed by FY24.
*   **Revenue Growth:** Driven by volume off-take in APIs, high growth in Specialty Chemicals, and upcoming contributions from new projects like dermatology and backward integration. Formulation segment growth is expected to be supported by new filings in semi-regulated and de-regulated markets, with increasing export share.
*   **Margin Improvement:** Expected from stabilization of input costs, operating leverage from improved capacity utilization, backward integration, and entry into higher-margin dermatology products.
*   **Product Portfolio Expansion:** Focus on niche, high-margin products, including dermatology segment and backward integration for intermediates. Exploration of new launches in antifungal and nutraceutical products.
*   **Market Penetration:** Increasing export share in the formulation segment, targeting semi-regulated and de-regulated markets with new filings. Continued investment in product launches and filings.
*   **Operational Efficiency:** Investment in process improvements and R&D to reduce costs and enhance efficiency.

## Management Accountability & Consistency Audit

### Audit of Stated Targets vs. Actual Performance:

*   **Capex Execution:** ADL has spent approximately 50% of its INR 600 crore capex target over the last two years (₹315 crores). The projected capex for FY24 is INR 250-350 crores. Management reaffirms that the majority of the capex will be completed by the end of FY24, with only brownfield expansion for the anti-diabetic facility at Sarigam remaining. Tarapur specialty chemical capex is on track for Q1 FY24 completion, and Tarapur dermatology capex for Q2 FY24. This aligns with previous guidance on capex completion timelines.
*   **Revenue Performance:** The API segment grew 12% YoY in FY23, and Specialty Chemicals grew 38% YoY. Q4 FY23 standalone revenue grew 9% YoY. This performance is positive, especially given the challenges in the previous year (FY23) mentioned regarding input costs. The management's narrative highlights strong volume off-take in APIs and high growth in Specialty Chemicals as key drivers.
*   **Profitability:** Management expresses confidence in margin improvement in upcoming quarters due to easing input costs and the phasing out of high-cost inventory. For FY24, they project EBITDA margins in the range of 14-16%. They also stated that current EBITDA margins are nearing the 13% mark (12.84% standalone in Q4 FY23), indicating a gradual improvement towards their target. The improved EBITDA margins in Q4 FY23 (190 bps sequentially) due to operating leverage support this outlook.
*   **API Pricing and Inventory:** Management notes that high-cost inventory for antibiotic products impacted Q4 gross margins due to product mix, but this is expected to be phased out. They also mention that API selling prices were very high in FY23 due to input cost surges, which squeezed formulators' margins. Prices have started coming off since December quarter, leading to increased demand. The management expects Q1 FY24 selling prices to be slightly lower (around 5%) than Q4 FY23, but raw material prices have stabilized, suggesting price stabilization. The Ciprofloxacin price is back to pre-COVID levels. Overall, pricing is seen as stable to slightly negative for FY24, with volume being the primary growth driver.
*   **Formulation Segment:** The formulation segment's contribution to consolidated revenue is around 8%. Management is focusing on increasing exports by targeting regulated and de-regulated markets through filings. New capacity at Baddi for oral oncology is completed and expected to contribute revenues in the next 12 months, with a potential for INR 150-200 crores growth from new launches. The total formulation capacity could reach INR 500 crores plus revenue.
*   **US FDA Status:** ADL submitted its final response to the US FDA in September 2022 and received a letter in February 2023 requesting clarifications and additional information, which was also provided in mid-March 2023. Management is planning to send a reminder for follow-up inspection, anticipating a potential unannounced audit. The file remains active.
