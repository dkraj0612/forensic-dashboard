## Executive Overview

Aarti Drugs Limited (ADL) reported a mixed performance for Q3 FY2022, navigating through elevated raw material and freight costs, alongside coal price increases. Despite these headwinds, the company demonstrated robust year-on-year growth in revenue and profitability, with significant sequential improvement in margins. Standalone revenue contributed 91% to the consolidated revenue of ₹641.5 crores for Q3 FY2022. The company saw a notable resurgence in export revenue (up 54.4% YoY) and domestic revenue (up 10% YoY) for its standalone business. The API segment experienced a 12% YoY volume growth, driven by both acute and chronic therapies. The Specialty Chemicals and Intermediates segment was a standout performer, growing 74% YoY to ₹66.6 crores, signaling an inflection point for the business. Consolidated EBITDA stood at ₹96.7 crores with an EBITDA margin of 15.1%. For the nine months of FY2022, revenues reached ₹1,802.7 crores, with an EBITDA margin of 14.0%. Capex for 9MFY22 was ₹103 crores, with an expectation of ₹150-200 crores for the full fiscal year, funded by internal accruals and debt. The company maintained a strong balance sheet with a net debt-to-equity ratio of 0.51x.

## Business Strategy & Drivers

*   **Capacity Expansion:** Commissioning of new chlorosulfonation line in Feb 2022 for specialty chemicals, doubling capacity in the near term. Greenfield Capex for Maharashtra and Gujarat facilities are targeted for commissioning by December 2022 and February-March 2023, respectively. Antibiotic expansion expected within 4 months, derma capacity by December 2022, and intermediate capacities by February-March 2023. Cardiovascular product capacity to double.
*   **Product Portfolio Growth:** Introduction of new products in cardiac and diabetic therapies for formulations, with a focus on Day 1 launches in key markets. Development of 10 oral oncology products targeting patent expiries from 2024-2027. Expansion in antidiabetic APIs (Gliptins), derma APIs, and antifungal APIs. Backward integration for select intermediates.
*   **Market Penetration:** Strengthening distribution in Latin America and Africa for formulations, alongside participation in institutional tenders. Development of products for regulated markets (Europe, North America) for long-term growth. Aiming for significant market share in Gliptins and Metformin.
*   **Cost Optimization & Efficiency:** Proactive price hikes to offset raw material and freight costs. Strict cost control measures. Developing technologies for backward integration to reduce reliance on external sources, particularly China.
*   **USFDA Compliance:** Initiated engagement with US FDA post-receiving responses to queries. Remote review completed, with an onsite audit anticipated soon, delayed by the COVID-19 third wave.

## Management Accountability & Consistency Audit

*   **Audit of Stated Targets vs. Actual Performance:**
    *   **EBITDA Margins:** Management expressed confidence in achieving long-term EBITDA margins of approximately 18%, stating that current negotiated raw material prices are tapering off and coal price variations (which impacted EBITDA by ₹5-6 crores per quarter) are temporary. The previous quarter's margin compression due to raw material price hikes was acknowledged, with proactive selling price adjustments implemented.
    *   **Raw Material Price Stabilization:** The transcript indicates a reversal in raw material price trends, with negotiated prices for over 80% of raw materials tapering off in December. While this will impact Q4 FY22 and Q1 FY23 margins, a normalization is expected thereafter. The company has increased its inventory levels due to market uncertainty and frantic buying.
    *   **API Volume Growth:** API volumes grew 12% YoY, driven by secular growth across therapies. Management anticipates domestic API demand to pick up in Q4 FY22 due to increased formulation production.
    *   **Revenue Growth:** Standalone revenue saw 6% YoY growth (standalone business revenue of ₹579.9 crores), with significant export growth. Specialty chemicals and intermediates showed robust 74% YoY growth. Formulation revenue saw a temporary dip due to lower domestic demand but is expected to recover in Q4 FY22.
    *   **Capex Progress:** Capex pace was impacted by rains, but Maharashtra projects are on track, with Gujarat projects to follow. Commissioning timelines for Maharashtra and Gujarat greenfield expansions remain approximately 12 months out.

*   **New Forward-Looking Promises, Guidance, or Timelines:**
    *   **EBITDA Margins:** Long-term target of approximately 18%. Expectation of gross margin improvement in future quarters due to tapering raw material prices.
    *   **Raw Material Price Trends:** Observed tapering in negotiated prices for over 80% of raw materials in December, which will reflect in Q4 FY22 and Q1 FY23 earnings.
    *   **Formulation Sales:** Expected good recovery in Q4 FY22. Annual growth rate of about 25% anticipated over the next 3 years. Oncology segment to contribute meaningfully post-FY24.
    *   **API & Specialty Chemicals Demand:** Strong demand anticipated for exports due to pending orders. Domestic API demand to pick up in Q4 FY22. Specialty chemicals expected to be easily absorbed due to small current capacities relative to global demand.
    *   **Specialty Chemicals Revenue:** Capacity potential to double in the near future, with revenues from specialty chemicals expected to double in the next 2 years.
    *   **USFDA Inspection:** Onsite audit expected soon, following a remote review, with the timing delayed by the third wave of COVID-19.
    *   **Capex Completion:** Maharashtra greenfield by December 2022, Gujarat greenfield by February-March 2023. Brownfield expansions for antibiotic (within 4 months), derma (by Dec 2022), and intermediates (Feb-Mar 2023) are progressing.
    *   **Metformin Capex:** Current capacity of 1,100 MT/month has rapidly increasing utilization. Plans for US FDA approval and expansion to 3,000 MT/month within a year of project commencement (phased: 2,000 then 3,000 MT/month).
    *   **Gliptin Capacity:** Multi-purpose facility setup, with staged expansion planned. Aim to capture substantial market share, with potential for over 50% market share in the third year of dedicated expansion.
    *   **Debt to Equity Ratio:** Expected to peak around 0.7x, considering planned CAPEX and a 25% shareholder payout, before potentially returning to 0.51x as internal accruals increase. Gross debt around ₹500 crores (less than ₹170 crores long-term debt).
    *   **Budgeted Capex FY23-24:** Beyond announced Greenfield projects (₹550 crores), additional CAPEX of ₹40-50 crores annually for GMP, maintenance, and debottlenecking. Further Greenfield CAPEX to be announced as decided.
    *   **Dependency on China:** Less than 20% of raw material purchases are from China. No immediate business case to significantly reduce this, except for one specific product. Sourcing also from Europe and Middle East.
    *   **Crude Price Impact:** Recent raw material price increases were primarily driven by demand-supply mismatch and plant closures in China/US, not directly by crude prices. However, cost of chemical production in China has permanently increased due to regulatory changes.
    *   **Gross Margin Sustainability:** While short-term margin impacts from price volatility exist, long-term margin reversion is expected through price adjustments and sales mix improvement. Backward integration and increased sales to semi-regulated/regulated markets are key margin expansion levers.
