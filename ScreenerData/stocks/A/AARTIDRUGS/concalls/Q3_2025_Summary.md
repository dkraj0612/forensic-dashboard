## Executive Overview

Aarti Drugs Limited (ADL) reported Q3 FY25 results with consolidated revenue declining 6% year-on-year to INR 568 crores, primarily due to reduced market prices and weaker demand in the formulation and antibiotics API segments. Despite the revenue dip, gross profit saw a marginal 7% increase to INR 207 crores. A significant development was the US FDA approval for its API manufacturing facility in Tarapur, Maharashtra, opening doors for exports to the US market for key APIs like Ciprofloxacin HCL and Zolpidem Tartrate, with expected meaningful contribution beginning in FY27. Additionally, ADL is investing in a solar power plant for captive consumption, aiming for cost optimization and environmental benefits. The company also achieved a Silver medal in its EcoVadis assessment, underscoring its ESG commitment. Management anticipates operating leverage from the Sayakha Specialty Chemicals plant, which will commence trial production in February FY25, and expects EBITDA margins to reach 13-14% for FY26.

## Key Financial Metrics

*   **Consolidated Revenue (Q3 FY25):** INR 568 crores (6% YoY decline)
*   **Consolidated Gross Profit (Q3 FY25):** INR 207 crores (7% YoY increase)
*   **Formulation Revenue (Q3 FY25):** INR 48.6 crores (47% from exports)
*   **Capex (9 Months FY25):** INR 136 crores
*   **Capex (Full Year FY25 - Anticipated):** INR 200 crores

## Business Strategy & Drivers

*   **API Segment:**
    *   **US FDA Approval:** Tarapur API facility cleared US FDA inspection, enabling API exports to the US market. Potential revenue of INR 70-80 crores annually from existing lines, with possibility to double with further expansion. Expected EBITDA margins of 30%+ from this facility.
    *   **Pricing:** Q3 FY25 saw a Y-o-Y price decline of 2-2.5%, with prices stabilizing in the December quarter. Further price declines are not anticipated; stable crude prices could benefit margins.
    *   **Antibiotics:** Ofloxacin noted for higher price decline within this segment.
    *   **Metformin:** Prices are stable.
    *   **Antidiabetic Segment:** Contributes ~12-13% to standalone API revenue, with products including Pioglitazone and Teneligliptin, and Vildagliptin in commercial batches.
*   **Specialty Chemicals:**
    *   **Sayakha Greenfield Project:** Trial production to commence in February FY25. Expectation of operating leverage and improved gross margins from Q4 FY25 onwards. Backward integration for antidiabetic portfolio.
    *   **Tarapur Greenfield Project:** Teething issues resolved; targeting ramp-up to 500+ tons/month by end of March '25.
    *   **Salicylic Acid:** Initial drag on profitability from the greenfield project is acknowledged; higher utilization expected to improve margins.
    *   **Chlorosulfonation Line (Tarapur):** A new continuous process for chlorosulfonation was rolled back due to quality issues impacting derivative prices and market acceptability. Operations have reverted to the older batch format, curtailing losses, with plans to increase capacity using the older process over the next 3-4 months.
*   **Formulation Segment:**
    *   **Growth Strategy:** Focus on export-driven growth. Brownfield expansion added 30% capacity, with revenues expected from Q1 FY26.
    *   **Approvals:** UK MHRA and US FDA approvals obtained for the formulation plant.
    *   **Product Launches:** Planning to launch 5 oncology products (e.g., Dasatinib, Lenalidomide, Abiraterone) and 6-7 antidiabetic products (DPP-4 inhibitors, SGLT2s) in North America, Europe, and emerging markets over the next 1.5 years.
    *   **Short-term Outlook:** Expecting flattish growth in the next quarter, with higher growth anticipated from FY26 onwards due to capacity expansion and new product launches.
*   **Sustainability:**
    *   **Solar Power Plant:** Investment in a solar SPV for captive consumption, expected to commence operation by end of H1 FY26, projecting annual savings of INR 3.6 crores for the Gujarat plant and reducing CO2 emissions by 1,000 tons.
    *   **EcoVadis:** Secured Silver medal with a score of 69 (89th percentile globally), enhancing marketability in regulated markets.
*   **Backward Integration:** Methylamine-based backward integration for the antidiabetic portfolio is a focus at the Sayakha plant.

## Management Accountability & Consistency Audit

### Audit of Stated Targets vs. Actual Performance:

*   **US FDA Approval:** The achievement of US FDA approval for the Tarapur API plant is a significant positive, fulfilling a critical milestone. The previous concerns regarding a warning letter have been addressed.
*   **Sayakha Facility Commencement:** Trial production is slated for February FY25, aligning with the expectation of commencing operations in Q3 FY25. Management has shifted the expectation of operating leverage and improved margins to Q4 FY25 and subsequently FY26.
*   **Tarapur Greenfield Project Ramp-up:** While issues were mentioned as sorted, the targeted ramp-up to 500+ tons/month by end of March '25 is a new specific timeline.
*   **Salicylic Acid Greenfield Project:** Management acknowledges this project caused a "drag on profitability" and confirms that higher utilization is key to margin improvement, echoing previous concerns about under-absorption of costs.
*   **Formulation Segment Performance:** Q3 FY25 performance was weaker than anticipated ("did not do well"), impacting overall EBITDA margins. The expected turnaround is now projected for Q4 FY25 and FY26 with capacity expansion and new product launches.
*   **Chlorosulfonation Line (Tarapur):** The decision to roll back the new continuous process due to quality issues and shift back to the older batch process represents a significant course correction. While this curtails losses, it delays the intended efficiency gains and capacity expansion from the new process. The original plan for this brownfield expansion seems to have been significantly altered.
*   **Revenue Guidance:** While overall revenue for FY25 is impacted by pricing pressures, management reiterates a target of ~INR 4,000 crores in 3 years. FY26 EBITDA margin guidance is revised to 13-14%.
*   **Pricing Pressure:** Management acknowledges Y-o-Y price declines but highlights stabilization in Q3 FY25 and expects this to continue. The previous anticipation of margin recovery due to price stabilization appears to be more nuanced, now emphasizing operational efficiencies and capacity utilization.
