## Executive Overview

Aarti Drugs Limited (ADL) reported its Q3 FY24 results, indicating resilience amidst challenging market conditions. While consolidated revenues saw a slight year-on-year decline, driven by negative rate variance, the company demonstrated improved EBITDA and PAT margins. ADL continues to focus on capacity expansion, with greenfield projects in Gujarat and Tarapur nearing completion. The formulation segment, particularly through Pinnacle Lifescience, showed robust growth, with a strategic shift towards branded generic exports. The company also highlighted stabilization in input costs and cautiously optimistic outlook for its export business.

## Key Financial Metrics

*   **Q3 FY24 Consolidated Revenue:** Rs. 608 crores (down 8.6% YoY)
*   **Q3 FY24 Consolidated EBITDA:** Rs. 72 crores (margin 11.8%, up 100 bps YoY)
*   **Q3 FY24 Consolidated PAT:** Rs. 37 crores (margin 6.1%, up 60 bps YoY)
*   **9M FY24 Consolidated Revenue:** Rs. 1,912 crores (down 3.2% YoY)
*   **9M FY24 Consolidated EBITDA:** Rs. 234 crores (margin 12.2%, up 140 bps YoY)
*   **9M FY24 Consolidated PAT:** Rs. 124 crores (margin 6.5%, up 90 bps YoY)
*   **Q3 FY24 API Volume Growth:** 8.4% YoY
*   **Q3 FY24 API Segment Contribution:** Antibiotic (48%), Antidiabetic (14%), Antiprotozoal (16%), Anti-inflammatory (10%), Antifungal (9%)
*   **Q3 FY24 Formulation Segment Revenue:** Rs. 79.3 crores (up 58.7% YoY), 58% from exports
*   **9M FY24 Formulation Segment Revenue:** Rs. 257 crores (up 18.2% YoY)
*   **Capital Expenditure (9M FY24):** Rs. 162 crores
*   **Targeted FY24 Capex:** Rs. 250-300 crores
*   **Interim Dividend Recommended:** Rs. 1 per share (as of Jan 24, 2024)

## Business Strategy & Drivers

*   **Capacity Expansion & Operational Leverage:** Greenfield projects in Gujarat (Saykha for specialty chemicals) and Tarapur (dermatology product) are nearing completion, expected to drive operating leverage from FY25.
*   **Formulation Business Growth:** Strategic shift towards branded generic exports and new market launches are driving growth in the formulation segment.
*   **API Business Resilience:** Focus on volume growth in APIs despite lower realizations, with anticipation of improved gross margins due to stabilization of input costs and increased export contribution.
*   **Export Market Turnaround:** Cautious optimism for a forthcoming turnaround in the export business, particularly Latin America, driven by anticipated interest rate reductions and the end of destocking cycles.
*   **Specialty Chemicals Expansion:** Introduction of new products like Salicylic Acid and Methylamine to enhance the specialty chemicals portfolio.
*   **Regulatory Approvals:** Progress on US FDA inspections and EU GMP certification for formulation plants.

## Management Accountability & Consistency Audit

### Audit of Stated Targets vs. Actual Performance:

*   **Capex Execution:** The company incurred Rs. 162 crores in 9M FY24, aligning with the target of Rs. 250-300 crores for FY24. The Saykha project experienced a slight delay, now expected to complete by March/April 2024. The Tarapur project is on track for completion by end-January 2024. This indicates generally consistent execution of the capex plan.
*   **Formulation Segment Growth:** The formulation segment under Pinnacle Lifescience continues to show strong growth, with Q3 FY24 revenues up 58.7% YoY and 9M FY24 up 18.2% YoY, validating the strategy of expanding this business.
*   **API Segment Performance:** Despite a YoY revenue decline in Q3 FY24, the API segment achieved an 8.4% volume growth, demonstrating resilience. Gross margins improved due to stabilization of input costs, though negative rate variance impacted absolute revenue. The narrative around export market challenges (destocking, interest rates, dollar shortages) is consistent with previous concerns, but the tone shifts to cautious optimism for a turnaround.
*   **Profitability Margins:** EBITDA and PAT margins showed year-on-year improvement in both Q3 and 9M FY24, indicating effective cost management and operational efficiencies despite revenue pressures. The management's expectation of margin improvement due to stabilization of input costs and increased export contribution aligns with the reported figures.
