## Executive Overview

Aarti Drugs Limited (ADL) reported Q4 FY26 results showcasing a strong sequential recovery and year-on-year growth in revenue. While FY26 was characterized by a challenging operating environment, including macroeconomic and geopolitical uncertainties, supply chain constraints, and elevated costs, the company ended the year on a stronger footing. Key achievements include the meaningful ramp-up of the Sayakha methylamine plant and continued growth in the formulation segment. ADL is strategically shifting towards regulated markets, with exports contributing an increased share of revenue. Management anticipates further profitability improvements driven by capacity utilization, stabilizing pricing, and strategic investments.

## Key Financial Metrics

*   **Consolidated Revenue (Q4 FY26):** INR 721.1 crores (6% YoY growth, 20% QoQ growth)
*   **Consolidated EBITDA (Q4 FY26):** INR 96.6 crores (Flattish YoY growth, 72% QoQ growth)
*   **Consolidated EBITDA Margin (Q4 FY26):** 13.4%
*   **Consolidated PAT (Q4 FY26):** INR 55.3 crores (12% YoY de-growth, 36% QoQ growth)
*   **Consolidated PAT Margin (Q4 FY26):** 7.7%
*   **Standalone Revenue (Q4 FY26):** INR 631.7 crores (vs. INR 623.0 crores in Q4 FY25)
*   **Formulation Revenue (Q4 FY26):** INR 91.3 crores (41% YoY growth)
*   **Formulation Revenue (FY26):** INR 330.5 crores (16% YoY growth)
*   **Exports Contribution (FY26):** 38% of consolidated revenue (vs. 35% in FY25)
*   **Regulated Market Contribution (FY26):** 73% of exports (vs. 66% in FY25)
*   **CWIP (as of Q4 FY26):** INR 214 crores

## Business Strategy & Drivers

*   **API Segment:**
    *   **Volume Growth:** Achieved positive volume growth over the last five years. Internal target for FY27 is 10-15%. Current raw material price elevation due to the West Asia war poses a risk to domestic antibiotic demand if sustained.
    *   **Pricing:** Pricing trends stabilized from September 2025 and strengthened in Q4 FY26 due to rising crude prices, expected to improve realizations.
    *   **Capacity Expansion:**
        *   **Sayakha Greenfield (Methylamines):** Meaningful ramp-up, reaching utilization of over 40% in March 2026 and 60% in the first half of Q1 FY27. Targeted to operate above 70% utilization within a year. Benefits from backward integration for metformin.
        *   **Tarapur Greenfield (Salicylic Acid):** Production was temporarily shut down due to equipment delays and variable losses. Restart contingent on equipment installation, operational efficiency, and cost recovery. Focus shifting to salicylic acid derivatives.
    *   **Regulated Markets:** Increased export contribution to 38% and regulated market share within exports to 73% in FY26. US FDA plant reapproved, with commercial flows expected in 12-18 months. CEPs obtained for antifungal and anti-inflammatory products in Europe.
    *   **Metformin:** Company is the sole backward-integrated player for a key indigenous intermediate. DMF filed with the US, with plans to invite FDA inspection post-capacity ramp-up.
*   **Specialty Chemicals & Intermediates:**
    *   **Sayakha Methylamines:** Expected to reduce dependence on external inputs for metformin and improve self-sufficiency.
    *   **Salicylic Acid Derivatives:** Production nearing completion, with higher selling prices and margin expansion potential. Exploring anti-dumping duties.
*   **Formulation Segment (Pinnacle Life Science):**
    *   **Growth:** Significant YoY growth of 41% in Q4 FY26, driven by direct exports in the non-oncology portfolio and growing approvals in regulated markets. FY26 revenue grew 16% to INR 330.5 crores.
    *   **Product Launches:** Oncology dossier development ongoing, with filing anticipated by December 2026. Commercial sales for oncology products expected from FY27. Targeting expansion of OSD capacity, aiming for EDQM certification.
*   **Sustainability:**
    *   **Solar Power Plant:** 24.4 MW solar project through JV expected to lower power costs from FY27 onwards.

## Management Accountability & Consistency Audit

### Audit of Stated Targets vs. Actual Performance:

*   **Q4 FY26 Performance:** Revenue showed a strong sequential recovery (20% QoQ) and YoY growth (6%), surpassing Q3 FY26 levels. EBITDA margin of 13.4% is within a reasonable range, though EBITDA itself was flattish YoY due to the challenging industry environment. PAT de-grew YoY, impacted by various factors. The formulation segment delivered robust 41% YoY growth.
*   **Sayakha Greenfield Project (Methylamines):** The plant ramp-up is progressing. Utilization reached over 40% in Q4 FY26 and is projected to cross 55-60% in Q1 FY27, with over 70% expected within a year. This is broadly in line with previous expectations for utilization ramp-up, though initial headwinds related to ammonia-based raw materials were noted.
*   **Tarapur Greenfield (Salicylic Acid):** Production was shut down due to the delay in a critical equipment and ongoing losses. This marks a deviation from the prior expectation of calibrated ramp-up and contribution from Q3 FY26. The focus has shifted to derivatives and anti-dumping duties.
*   **Formulation Segment Approvals & Growth:** The segment demonstrated strong growth, driven by non-oncology exports and market extensions. While oncology facilities are still pre-revenue, the growth in other areas is positive.
*   **API Segment Pricing & Volumes:** Pricing trends indeed stabilized and strengthened as anticipated. Volume growth of 8-10% is the target for the existing business, with internal targets for the company at 10-15%. The company sustained volumes over the years despite pricing corrections.
*   **Capex:** Q4 FY26 figures are not explicitly stated but the full-year FY26 capex is consistent with guidance. CWIP of INR 214 crores indicates ongoing project work.

### New Forward-Looking Promises, Guidance, or Timelines:

*   **Sayakha Facility Utilization:** Expected to cross 55-60% in Q1 FY27 and upwards of 70% within a year.
*   **Salicylic Acid Production:** Restart contingent on equipment installation and cost recovery. Derivatives plant expected by mid-June.
*   **Formulation Revenue Target:** Aiming for INR 1,000 crores in 3-5 years.
*   **Oncology Product Commercialization:** Sales expected from FY27 onwards, with R&D and filing anticipated by December 2026.
*   **API Volume Growth Target (FY27):** Targeting 10-15% for the company overall.
*   **API Price Variance:** Dependent on crude oil and West Asia war. Current API prices are elevated. No expectation for further price increases unless crude oil goes significantly higher ($130-$140 per barrel).
*   **Gross Margin:** Aim to maintain Q4 FY26 levels. Potential for improvement from regulated sales and Sayakha backward integration, but could be offset by higher API input costs. No significant movement expected in percentage terms.
*   **EBITDA Margin Target:** Targeting 13.5% to 14% for FY27 (revised down from earlier 14-14.5% due to war). Potential to reach 15-16% in FY27.
*   **Capex Plan:** INR 300-400 crores over the next 2-3 years, focusing on quasi-greenfield/brownfield expansions and formulation R&D.
*   **Formulation Capex Visibility:** Primarily backed by customer discussions and forecast for signed products, with R&D capex initiated ahead of contract signing.
*   **Metformin:** US FDA approval for dedicated metformin facility is a key next step.
*   **EBITDA Loss from New Projects (FY26):** Estimated at INR 18-20 crores for the full year.
*   **US FDA API Flows:** Expected within 12-18 months.
*   **Salicylic Acid Derivatives Plant:** Expected by mid-June.
*   **EBITDA Improvement:** Targeting at least 100 basis points improvement in gross contribution. Return to 16% EBITDA margins is considered achievable with increased regulated sales, US FDA approval for metformin, and formulation growth.
