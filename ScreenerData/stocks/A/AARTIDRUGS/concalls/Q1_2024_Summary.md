## Executive Overview

Aarti Drugs Limited (ADL) reported a decent performance in Q1 FY24, with consolidated revenue growing 6.3% year-on-year to INR 661.7 crores. EBITDA saw a strong 26% YoY increase to INR 84.7 crores, with margins improving by approximately 200 basis points to 12.8%, primarily driven by normalized input costs and improved capacity utilization. Profit After Tax (PAT) grew 38% YoY to INR 48 crores.

The standalone business contributed 86% of consolidated revenue, with domestic revenue growing a robust 13.6% YoY, largely offsetting a 4% decline in exports due to USD shortages in certain geographies. The API segment exhibited significant volume growth of 18%, predominantly in the domestic market, though API export faced challenges. The Formulation business saw remarkable export growth, with exports accounting for 67% of its INR 89.9 crore revenue, fueled by strategic regulatory filings. In contrast, the Specialty Chemicals segment faced headwinds in Q1 FY24, impacted by the absence of campaign-based products and global challenges from China's re-entry into the market with lower-priced exports, coupled with reduced international demand.

ADL incurred INR 48 crores in capital expenditure during Q1 FY24, with total projected capex for FY24 remaining between INR 250-350 crores. Key high-margin accretive projects at Gujarat Saykha and Tarapur (dermatology and specialty chemicals) are slated for completion within this fiscal year, with commercial operations expected to commence in Q3 FY24. The Board approved a share buyback of INR 59.85 crores, signaling commitment to shareholder distribution. Management anticipates sequential margin expansion in upcoming quarters, projecting FY24 to close with quarterly EBITDA margins of 14.5%-15% and aiming for 10% value growth for the full year, driven by strong volume increases despite price pressures.

## Business Strategy & Drivers

*   **Capacity Expansion & New Product Launches:** Progressing with significant capex projects, including Saykha, Tarapur dermatology, and specialty chemicals facilities, with commercial operations targeted for Q3 FY24. Plans for anti-diabetic expansion and new side-chain dermatology products in FY25.
*   **Margin Enhancement:** Focus on higher-margin products through new project commercialization (dermatology, backward integration), operating leverage from increased capacity utilization, and continued stabilization of raw material costs.
*   **Portfolio Diversification & Backward Integration:** Entering niche, high-margin areas like dermatology and methylamine-based backward integration products. The salicylic acid project aims for domestic import substitution, leveraging a robust process despite global competition.
*   **Market Penetration & Export Growth:** Strategic emphasis on expanding the formulation segment's export footprint into semi-regulated and regulated markets through new regulatory filings and product launches. Exploring alternative currency payment options for API exports to mitigate USD shortage impacts.
*   **Operational Efficiency & Cost Management:** Actively working to reduce inventory days for raw materials and work-in-progress to improve working capital utilization. Benefiting from lower freight costs and stabilizing coal prices.
*   **Shareholder Value Creation:** Reinforcing commitment to shareholder returns through the recently approved share buyback program.

## Management Accountability & Consistency Audit

### Audit of Stated Targets vs. Actual Performance:

*   **Capex Execution:** Management reaffirmed the FY24 capex projection of INR 250-350 crores, having spent INR 48 crores in Q1. The majority of the larger projects (Saykha, Tarapur dermatology, specialty chemicals) are still on track for completion this fiscal year, aligning with previous guidance. However, commercial operations for Tarapur dermatology and specialty chemicals projects, previously targeted for Q1/Q2 FY24 completion, are now expected to commence in Q3 FY24, indicating a slight delay in operational readiness. The planned bigger anti-diabetic expansion for later FY24 has been pushed to FY25.
*   **Revenue Performance:** Consolidated revenue grew 6.3% YoY in Q1 FY24, which is decent but below the previously stated aspiration for double-digit value growth in FY24. While API volume growth of 18% (domestic-driven) is strong, API export decline and significant headwinds in the Specialty Chemicals segment due to China's aggressive pricing and reduced global demand have presented challenges not explicitly highlighted as a major risk in the previous summary. Management still targets 10% value growth for FY24, acknowledging negative price variances.
*   **Profitability:** Q1 FY24 EBITDA margin of 12.8% shows a 200 bps YoY improvement, consistent with the narrative of easing input costs. Management maintained its FY24 EBITDA margin guidance, expecting to close the year at 14.5%-15% quarterly EBITDA. This aligns with the previous expectation of sequential improvement towards the 14-16% range.
*   **API Pricing and Inventory:** Previous guidance anticipated Q1 FY24 selling prices to be slightly lower (around 5%) than Q4 FY23 and raw material prices to stabilize. In Q1 FY24, API aggregate pricing declined by 8-9% YoY and 3-3.5% sequentially, while aggregate input prices dropped 15-16% YoY and 2-2.5% sequentially. Management confirmed high-cost inventory is largely utilized and expects sequential margin improvement. Ciprofloxacin prices are back to pre-COVID levels, and raw material prices are generally stable on an aggregate basis, though specific drops exist. The tone has shifted from merely "prices started coming off" to a more explicit acknowledgment of significant year-on-year price declines in both inputs and outputs, directly impacting top-line value growth.
*   **Formulation Segment:** The segment's strong export growth, accounting for 67% of its revenue, directly validates the previous focus on increasing exports by targeting semi-regulated and regulated markets through filings. This segment is performing as guided.
*   **US FDA Status:** The previous summary noted an active FDA file and anticipated an unannounced audit. The current update confirms the receipt of the EU GMP certificate for the same facility, which management intends to leverage to expedite the FDA inspection process. This is a positive development, indicating tangible progress towards regulatory compliance.
*   **Specialty Chemicals Segment:** The previous summary highlighted Specialty Chemicals as a "high growth trajectory" segment (+38% YoY for FY23). The current transcript details significant Q1 FY24 impact due to the absence of specific campaign-based products and, more critically, the global impact of China re-opening and aggressively pricing products. This represents a clear, unanticipated headwind and a shift in the segment's short-term outlook from high growth to managing challenges.
*   **Metformin Capacity:** The planned Q1 FY24 expansion (40% incremental) was successfully completed, resulting in a 30% capacity increase to 1400-1500 tons/month. The facility shutdown for this expansion temporarily impacted Q1 sales, as mentioned by management. A bigger expansion is now planned for next year, instead of later in FY24.
