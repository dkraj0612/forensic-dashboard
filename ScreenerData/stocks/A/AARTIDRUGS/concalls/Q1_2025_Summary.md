## Aarti Drugs Limited (ADL) - Q1 FY25 Earnings Call Executive Summary

### Executive Overview

Aarti Drugs Limited (ADL) reported a decline in Q1 FY25 revenue to INR 557 crores, primarily driven by a negative rate variance and subdued export market demand in the API business. Despite this, the company saw a year-on-year improvement in gross contribution. Management highlighted the ongoing ramp-up of the Tarapur dermatology facility and the planned commencement of the Gujarat Sayakha specialty chemicals project by end of Q2 FY25. The formulation segment, particularly its export component, continues to be a focus for margin enhancement. ADL incurred INR 52 crores in capex during the quarter and anticipates INR 200 crores for the full year, funded by internal accruals and term loans. While acknowledging short-term challenges, management expressed cautious optimism for improved performance in the second half of FY25, driven by new capacities and a potential export market recovery.

### Key Financial Metrics

*   **Q1 FY25 Consolidated Revenue:** INR 557 crores (9% YoY decline, as per earlier transcript context).
*   **Q1 FY25 Consolidated EBITDA:** INR 66 crores (EBITDA margin 11.9%).
*   **Q1 FY25 Consolidated PAT:** INR 33 crores.
*   **Q1 FY25 Formulation Segment Revenue:** INR 70.4 crores (4.2% QoQ growth).
*   **Q1 FY25 API/Spec Chem Standalone Gross Contribution:** Improved by 2%+ YoY and 0.3% QoQ.
*   **Capital Expenditure (Q1 FY25):** INR 52 crores.
*   **Capital Expenditure (FY25 Projection):** INR 200 crores.

### Business Strategy & Drivers

*   **Capacity Expansion & Operational Leverage:** Tarapur dermatology facility commenced operations with a ramp-up planned over the next two quarters. The Gujarat Sayakha specialty chemicals project is on track for end of Q2 FY25 commencement. These are expected to drive operating leverage and improved capacity utilization from H2 FY25.
*   **Formulation Business Growth:** Strategic shift towards value-added export business continues. Focus on international markets for margin enhancement, with ongoing new product development and registrations. Brownfield expansion at Baddi facility to increase capacity.
*   **API Business Resilience & Margin Improvement:** Domestic API volume remained relatively stable, while export volumes declined by approximately 8% YoY. Gross margins have shown sequential improvement due to input cost stabilization. Anticipation of improved overall margins from increased export contribution and new capacities.
*   **Export Market Turnaround:** Management anticipates a demand pick-up from Q3 FY25, driven by expected interest rate reductions and resolution of destocking cycles, although Q2 export demand is expected to remain subdued.
*   **Specialty Chemicals Expansion:** Focus on scaling up production and new product introductions once the Sayakha project is operational in H2 FY25.

### Management Accountability & Consistency Audit

#### Audit of Stated Targets vs. Actual Performance:

*   **Capex Execution:** The company incurred INR 52 crores in Q1 FY25, consistent with the planned capex trajectory. The overall FY25 capex is projected at INR 200 crores. The Gujarat Sayakha project commencement has been slightly pushed to end of Q2 FY25, from the previous Q1 FY25 target.
*   **Formulation Segment Growth:** The formulation segment demonstrated QoQ growth of 4.2%. Management reiterates the strategic focus on exports for margin growth, with plans for capacity expansion to support this.
*   **API Segment Performance:** The Q1 FY25 revenue decline and EBITDA margin pressure were explicitly attributed to negative rate variance and an 8% drop in export volumes, consistent with prior commentary on export market challenges. Domestic API volumes were described as "more or less flat" year-on-year. Gross margins for the standalone business improved sequentially and year-on-year, aligning with expectations of input cost stabilization.
*   **Profitability Margins:** EBITDA margins declined to 11.9% in Q1 FY25, impacted by lower volumes and fixed cost absorption. Management attributes this to a temporary "pain" quarter and expects margins to improve in the second half of the year. The INR 6 crore PBT impact from the Tarapur dermatology facility ramp-up was factored in. The fire incident at the Tarapur unit temporarily disrupted production but was restored within a month with adequate insurance cover.

#### New Forward-Looking Promises, Guidance, or Timelines:

*   **Greenfield Project Commencement:**
    *   Tarapur (Dermatology): Commenced, with ramp-up expected over Q2 and Q3 FY25.
    *   Gujarat Sayakha (Specialty Chemicals): Expected to commence by end of Q2 FY25.
*   **Revenue Potential from New Greenfield Projects:** Expected operating leverage and improved capacity utilization from H2 FY25.
*   **Specialty Chemicals Segment Growth:** Targeting 50% YoY growth in H2 FY25 for the specialty chemicals segment, driven by new capacities.
*   **Formulation Segment Growth:** Expecting continued international formulation business growth. Brownfield expansion at Baddi facility to increase capacity by 15-20% in the next quarter and double capacity in 12-15 months. Domestic formulation business is not expected to decline, with international growth offsetting any domestic rationalization.
*   **FY25 Revenue Outlook:** Cautious optimism for achieving revenue targets with demand picking up in H2 FY25. Management targets INR 4,000 crores revenue in 3-4 years (FY27), assuming current prices and volume growth.
*   **FY25 EBITDA Margin Outlook:** Targeting EBITDA margins of 13-14% for H2 FY25. Long-term target of 14-15% EBITDA margins.
*   **API/Standalone Business Volume Growth (FY25):** Targeting mid-teens volume growth for H2 FY25, aiming for high single-digit volume growth for the full year FY25.
*   **API/Standalone Business Gross Margin Improvement:** Target of ~35% for standalone business gross margins (currently ~34.1%).
*   **Export Market Demand:** Subdued demand expected to continue in Q2 FY25, with demand pickup anticipated from Q3 FY25.
*   **Oncology Formulation Revenue Target:** Budgeted peak revenue of INR 300-400 crores for the oncology segment over the next 2-3 years. Initial sales expected from FY26, with majority sales from FY27.
*   **FY25 Capex Budget:** INR 200 crores, funded through internal accruals and term loans.
*   **Metformin Capacity Expansion:** Process to increase capacity from 1400-1500 tons/month to 1700-1800 tons/month is underway.
*   **API Prices:** Prices are considered stable at or near the bottom.
*   **API Contribution Margin:** Current quarter's contribution was around INR 197 crores. Expecting Q2 to be similar to Q1 in exports, with improvement from Q3.
*   **EBITDA Margin Trend:** Management attributes lower current EBITDA margins (11-12%) compared to pre-COVID (15-16%) to raw material price volatility in recent years and a lower contribution from exports (31-33% vs. 38-40% previously), which generally offer higher gross contribution. They believe better operating leverage and increased volumes would have led to ~14% EBITDA margin in Q1 FY25.
*   **Salicylic Acid Project:** Production started early February 2024. It has multiple uses and will be partially integrated into API and Specialty Chemicals.
*   **Sayakha Project Contribution:** Expected to help increase specialty chemical production capacity and backward integration for diabetic products, improving margins in H2 FY25.
