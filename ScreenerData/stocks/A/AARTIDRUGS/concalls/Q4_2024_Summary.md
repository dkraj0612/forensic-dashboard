## Aarti Drugs Limited (ADL) - Q4 FY24 Earnings Call Executive Summary

### Executive Overview

Aarti Drugs Limited (ADL) reported its Q4 FY24 results, showing a decline in revenue primarily due to negative rate variance and sluggish export market demand. Despite this, the company demonstrated improved EBITDA and PAT margins year-on-year, driven by stabilization in input costs and operational efficiencies. The company has largely completed its significant capital expenditure program, with the Tarapur facility commencing operations and the Gujarat Sayakha project expected to start by Q1 FY25. The formulation segment, particularly through Pinnacle Lifescience, continues its growth trajectory with a strategic shift towards value-added exports. Management expresses cautious optimism for an export market turnaround and forecasts significant revenue and margin potential from new capacities.

### Key Financial Metrics

*   **Q4 FY24 Consolidated Revenue:** INR 621 crores (down 16.4% YoY)
*   **Q4 FY24 Consolidated EBITDA:** INR 87 crores (margin 14.0%, up 130 bps YoY)
*   **Q4 FY24 Consolidated PAT:** INR 47 crores (margin 7.6%)
*   **FY24 Consolidated Revenue:** INR 2,533 crores (down 6.8% YoY)
*   **FY24 Consolidated EBITDA:** INR 321 crores (margin 12.7%, up 140 bps YoY)
*   **FY24 Consolidated PAT:** INR 172 crores (margin 6.8%, up 70 bps YoY)
*   **Q4 FY24 Standalone Revenue:** INR 560.64 crores (down 19.6% YoY)
*   **FY24 Formulation Segment Revenue:** INR 325 crores (up 18.5% YoY)
*   **Capital Expenditure (FY24):** INR 226 crores
*   **Debt-to-Equity Ratio (Consolidated):** 0.44x
*   **Net Cash Flow from Operations (FY24):** INR 359 crores

### Business Strategy & Drivers

*   **Capacity Expansion & Operational Leverage:** Greenfield projects at Tarapur (dermatology) are operational, and the Gujarat Sayakha (specialty chemicals) project is on track for Q1 FY25 commencement. These are expected to drive operating leverage and improved capacity utilization from H2 FY25.
*   **Formulation Business Growth:** Strategic shift towards value-added export business in Latin America, Asia, Africa, and regulated markets is driving margin expansion. Oncology product pipeline development for the US market is progressing.
*   **API Business Resilience & Margin Improvement:** Focus on volume growth domestically and stabilization of input costs are key drivers. Anticipation of improved gross margins from potential selling price upturns and increased export contribution.
*   **Export Market Turnaround:** Cautious optimism for an improved export landscape driven by expected interest rate reductions and resolution of destocking cycles.
*   **Specialty Chemicals Expansion:** Focus on new product introduction and capacity scaling to drive segment growth.

### Management Accountability & Consistency Audit

#### Audit of Stated Targets vs. Actual Performance:

*   **Capex Execution:** The company incurred INR 226 crores in FY24, aligning with the previously targeted range. The Tarapur greenfield project has commenced operations as per schedule. The Sayakha project is slightly delayed, now planned for commencement by end of Q1 FY25 (previously March/April 2024 calendar year). The narrative remains consistent regarding the completion of the majority of the INR 600 crores capex over the last three years.
*   **Formulation Segment Growth:** The formulation segment continues to exhibit strong growth (18.5% YoY for FY24), validating the strategy of expanding this business towards higher-margin exports.
*   **API Segment Performance:** Revenue decline in Q4 FY24 and FY24 was attributed to negative rate variance and export market challenges, which is consistent with prior commentary. However, the company reported good volume growth in the domestic market (16-17% in FY24) while facing a degrowth in export volumes (9-10% in FY24). Gross margins showed sequential improvement due to input cost stabilization. The tone shifted from concern to cautious optimism regarding export market recovery.
*   **Profitability Margins:** Both EBITDA and PAT margins improved year-on-year for Q4 FY24 and the full year FY24. This aligns with management's expectations of margin improvement driven by input cost stabilization and operational efficiencies.

#### New Forward-Looking Promises, Guidance, or Timelines:

*   **Greenfield Project Commencement:**
    *   Tarapur (Dermatology): Commenced, ramp-up planned throughout H1 FY25.
    *   Gujarat Sayakha (Specialty Chemicals): Expected to commence by end of Q1 FY25, scaling up in Q4 FY25.
*   **Revenue Potential from New Greenfield Projects:** New capacities could contribute INR 1000-1200 crores at optimal utilization (previously stated). Optimal utilization is expected beyond FY26.
*   **New Greenfield Project EBITDA Margins:** Expected to be 15-16% at >50% utilization, reaching 18-20% at optimal levels.
*   **Salicylic Acid Project Commercialization:** Production started early February 2024.
*   **Methylamine Project Commissioning:** Expected March/April 2024.
*   **Specialty Chemicals Segment Growth:** Target 30-40% growth in FY25. Potential to double once new capacities are operational.
*   **Formulation Segment Expansion:** Additional capacities of ~30% expected post-April 2024. Oncology plant production commenced, awaiting USFDA/EU audits.
*   **FY25 Revenue Growth Target:** Target 10% top-line growth, despite an anticipated negative rate variance of 5-8%.
*   **FY25 EBITDA Margin Outlook:** Targeting to cross 14% at the company level, with potential to reach 15% if the situation remains stable.
*   **API/Standalone Business Volume Growth (FY25):** Targeting 15% volume growth (increased from previous 10-15% target).
*   **API/Standalone Business Gross Margin Improvement:** Potential to improve gross margins by ~1% sequentially to reach ~35% (currently just below 34%).
*   **Export Market Demand:** Expected to improve in coming quarters due to lower interest rates and completed destocking.
*   **US FDA Inspection:** File moved to ORA department; inspection hoped for by September 2024 (FDA financial year).
*   **Debt-to-Equity Ratio:** Company comfortable with 0.5-0.7 range.
*   **FY25 Capex Budget:** INR 200-250 crores.
*   **FY26 onwards Capex:** Primarily maintenance capex, potentially INR 150-200 crores annually, with specific capex for formulation and API product launches to be announced.
*   **Three-Year EBITDA Trajectory:** Expecting EBITDA to double in three years, reaching INR 4,000 crores turnover with steady-state EBITDA of 15.5%-16%.
*   **Volume Growth (FY25):** Targeting ~15% volume growth.
*   **Price Variance (FY25):** Anticipating negative price variance in H1 FY25, reducing significantly in H2 FY25.
*   **API Molecule Capacities:** Top 15 molecules have seen stable capacities, with increases mainly in metformin and lower-end molecules. Pricing pressure primarily attributed to destocking.
*   **Formulation Oncology Revenue Target:** INR 300-350 crores of additional revenue expected in 24-36 months, bringing total oncology revenue to ~INR 300 crores. Formulation business could double in three years.
