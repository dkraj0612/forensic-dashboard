### 👤 Nationals
GCC Healthcare - Unique traits Healthcare market in GCC states, which is mostly insurance driven, have developed certain unique traits due to the higher expat and working age population

### 👤 Prevalence of Primary and Secondary Healthcare Facilities (Private Sector)
Ø Due to lower % of older population, requirement of tertiary and quaternary care is relatively limited Ø Due to lack of support systems (family, relatives, etc.) expat community travel back to their home countries for major health concerns Ø Hence private healthcare delivery is focused on primary and secondary healthcare Ø Recently there has been a trend towards selective tertiary care focus in UAE, however this will remain proportionately lower Ø Only Saudi Arabia, with its sizeable population of nationals is suitable for tertiary and quaternary care facilities

### 👤 Seasonality of Patient Volumes
Ø Decline in volumes across hospitals, pharmacies and segments during the summer months in the GCC states Ø Expats form a major proportion of the population in GCC states barring Saudi Arabia. During the extreme summer season and school holidays, a large amount of population leave the GCC region Ø Some doctors also travel back to their home country during this period as well Ø Impact visible across industries - reflected particularly more in primary care facilities like clinics and pharmacies Ø H1 and H2 revenues in GCC are usually split around ~ 45%-55% but the EBITDA split can vary as much as ~35% and 65% for H1 and H2 Ø Increase in revenue in H2 results in proportionately larger increase in profitability due to operating leverage Ø Seasonality variation consistently visible over several years, can be expected to continue
