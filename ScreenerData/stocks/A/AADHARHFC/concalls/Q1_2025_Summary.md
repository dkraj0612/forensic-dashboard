## Executive Overview

Aadhar Housing Finance Limited (AADHARHFC) reported a solid Q1 FY25, showcasing continued AUM growth and a focus on its core EWS/LIG segment. Assets Under Management (AUM) reached INR 21,726 crores, representing a 21% year-on-year growth. The company maintained a 100% retail secured book with an average ticket size of INR 10 lakhs and an average LTV of 59%. Disbursements stood at INR 1,497 crores, showing a 4% YoY growth despite the impact of the recent RBI circular on disbursement accounting. Profit After Tax (PAT) grew by 37% to INR 200 crores, with Return on Equity (ROE) at 15.9% post-IPO proceeds. Asset quality remained strong with a decline in Gross Non-Performing Assets (GNPA) to 1.31%, and collection efficiency exceeding 99%. The company expanded its distribution network to 536 branches, entering Himachal Pradesh and focusing on a "deeper impact strategy" in key states. Investments in technology and data analytics continue, including the implementation of a machine learning-based credit risk underwriting scorecard. Management expressed confidence in sustained growth, driven by strong demand in affordable housing and strategic expansion plans.

## Business Strategy & Drivers

Aadhar Housing Finance's strategy remains focused on:

*   **Deepening Penetration:** Expanding into smaller towns and districts through the "deeper impact strategy," with plans for 70-75 new branches annually (50 sales offices, 20-25 larger branches), focusing on states like Rajasthan, Uttar Pradesh, Andhra Pradesh, Telangana, and Maharashtra in Phase 1.
*   **Core Segment Focus:** Maintaining a strong emphasis on the EWS/LIG segment, which forms 71% of AUM and 75% of home loan mix.
*   **Technology & Data Science:** Continued investment in Fin-Tech capacities (bank account aggregator, income tax statement aggregator), enhanced RPA, and data analytics for credit risk underwriting (reject scorecard implemented), predictive analysis, and go-to-market assistance. Cybersecurity enhancements were also noted.
*   **Product Mix:** Maintaining a 75% home loan and 25% non-home loan mix, with a slight increase in non-home loans to 27% compensated by assignments.
*   **Asset Quality Management:** Focus on maintaining healthy collection efficiency (>99%) and proactive management of GNPA.
*   **Diversified Funding:** Continued diversification of borrowing sources, including a new agreement with the Asian Development Bank ($60 million), and maintaining relationships with banks, NHB, and NCDs.
*   **Branch Network Expansion:** Growing the network to 536 branches across 21 states, with plans for continued expansion and entering new states like Himachal Pradesh.
*   **PMAY/CLSS Support:** Anticipating a positive impact on business growth from government initiatives like PMAY Awas Yojana and CLSS.

## Management Accountability & Consistency Audit

**Performance Against Stated Targets (Q1 FY25 vs. FY24 Guidance):**

*   **AUM Growth:** Stated guidance of around 20% AUM growth for FY25. Q1 FY25 achieved 21% YoY growth, aligning with the trajectory.
*   **PAT Growth:** Q1 FY25 PAT grew 37% YoY, exceeding the implicit expectation from previous guidance.
*   **Cost to Income Ratio:** Management aims to drop this by another 50-60 basis points in FY25. The Q1 FY25 figure of 36.7% shows an 80 bps improvement YoY, indicating positive momentum.
*   **Spreads:** The Q1 FY25 spread of 6.0% is higher than the previous year-end and within the expected range. The guidance for exit spreads of 5.8%-5.9% for the year and 5.7%-5.8% in two years suggests anticipated compression.
*   **Asset Quality (GNPA):** GNPA of 1.31% in Q1 FY25 shows a decline from Q1 FY24 (1.46%). Management expects to end the year in the 1.1%-1.2% range, consistent with historical trends.
*   **Branch Expansion:** Target of 70-75 branches annually is being pursued, with 13 new branches added in Q1, including entry into Himachal Pradesh.
*   **Credit Cost:** Guidance for full-year credit cost is 27-28 bps, which is within historical manageable levels.
*   **RPLR Increase:** A 25 bps increase in RPLR effective June 16, 2024, was implemented as anticipated.

**Narrative Shift/Headwinds:**

*   The **RBI circular on accounting for disbursements** was acknowledged as impacting reported disbursement growth, reducing it to 4% YoY from a potential 25%+. Management confirmed full compliance and expressed confidence in achieving the 18-20% disbursement growth target for the year.
*   **Incremental cost of funds** was noted as potentially increasing in Q1/Q2 FY25, which was proactively addressed by the RPLR hike.
*   **Stage 3 Provision Coverage Ratio** saw a sequential decline from 41% to ~35% in Q1 FY25. Management explained this as a result of the provisioning percentage for new Stage 3 assets and expressed comfort in the current range (32-36%), highlighting a significant management overlay and excess provisions.
*   **Top-up loan end-use monitoring** was discussed, with management acknowledging the difficulty in strict monitoring for small ticket sizes but highlighting internal risk mechanisms and undertakings. The component is small (3% of AUM).
*   **NHB funding uncertainty** was mentioned regarding the AHF scheme and potential blended rates, influencing future cost of funds calculations.
