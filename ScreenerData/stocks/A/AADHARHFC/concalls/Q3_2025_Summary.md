## Aadhar Housing Finance - Q3 FY'25 Earnings Call - One-Page Executive Summary

### Executive Overview

Aadhar Housing Finance (AADHARHFC) reported a strong Q3 FY'25, demonstrating continued growth momentum with a 21% YoY increase in Assets Under Management (AUM) to approximately INR 24,000 crores. Disbursements in Q3 FY'25 saw a significant 20% YoY growth, reaching INR 2,094 crores. The company maintains a robust, 100% secured retail loan book with no corporate or developer exposure. Asset quality remains a focus, with Gross Non-Performing Assets (GNPA) improving sequentially by 4 bps to 1.36%, and collection efficiency stable at 98-99%. Aadhar Housing Finance is actively investing in its digital strategy, with 100% paperless onboarding and mobility solutions for its field staff, enhancing efficiency. The company continues its branch expansion, adding 12 new branches in Q3 FY'25, bringing the total to 545 across 21 states, serving over 2.86 lakh customers. Management is optimistic about the housing finance sector, citing favorable macroeconomic trends, government initiatives like PMAY 2.0 and SWAMIH Fund 2.0, and an expected industry growth of 16-17% for FY'25-FY'26, with affordable housing projected at 22-23% CAGR.

### Business Strategy & Drivers

*   **Low-Income Housing Focus:** Continued dedication to serving the low-income segment, leveraging an extensive branch network.
*   **Digital Transformation:** Significant investment in IT and data analytics, transitioning from paper-based to digital lending with 100% paperless onboarding and mobility solutions.
*   **Branch Network Expansion:** Calibrated expansion with 12 new branches added in Q3 FY'25, aiming for similar numbers in FY'26. Focus on "deeper impact" in Tier 4 cities and beyond.
*   **PMAY 2.0 & Government Initiatives:** Actively facilitating customer benefits under PMAY 2.0, SWAMIH Fund 2.0, and the mortgage guarantee scheme to boost demand and address supply-side issues.
*   **Portfolio Prudence:** Maintaining a 100% secured retail book with a strong home loan focus (74%) and prudent LTV ratios.
*   **Risk Management:** Emphasis on underwriting and collection processes, with stable early-stage delinquencies.
*   **Macroeconomic Tailwinds:** Benefiting from improving macroeconomic conditions and government support for the affordable housing segment.

### Management Accountability & Consistency Audit

**Performance Against Stated Targets (Q3 FY'25 vs. Previous Guidance/Expectations):**

*   **AUM Growth:** Achieved 21% YoY growth in Q3 FY'25, aligning with the previously guided 22-24% AUM growth trajectory.
*   **Disbursement Growth:** Q3 FY'25 saw 20% YoY growth, consistent with the guided 18-20% disbursement growth target.
*   **Cost to Income Ratio:** 34.8% in Q3 FY'25, showing a 60 bps YoY improvement, on track for the stated aim of approximately 100 bps reduction for the financial year.
*   **Asset Quality (GNPA):** GNPA at 1.36% shows sequential improvement, and management remains confident in ending the year with GNPA around 1.1% as previously indicated for FY'26.
*   **Branch Expansion:** Added 12 branches in Q3 FY'25, continuing the strategic network growth. Projected to close FY'25 with ~55 branches.

**Narrative Shift/Headwinds Addressed:**

*   **Credit Cost Increase:** Management explained the increase in credit cost for Q3 FY'25 was due to a slight movement in Stage 2 buckets (30-60 and 60-90 days), estimating an INR 18-19 crore impact. This is expected to even out in Q4 FY'25.
*   **Low-Ticket Items Stress:** Management reassured that the company has a 100% secured retail book and minimal exposure to individual MFI loans (less than 9,000 customers out of 286,000), mitigating concerns around broader market stress in unsecured lending.
*   **Fee Income Flatness:** Attributed to one-off contests and promotions on processing and admin fees in Q3 FY'25, resulting in INR 7-8 crores less income. This is not expected to recur.
*   **Balance Transfer (BT) Outflows:** Reduced from 6.7% to 6.3% annualized, with ongoing efforts to bring it down to 5.5%-5.7%. This is an industry-wide challenge.
*   **Interest Rate Environment:** Acknowledged that a rate cut by MPC (expected soon) will have a lag of 3-6 months before impacting their cost of funds, and subsequent pass-through to customers will occur via their internal RPLR mechanism.

**New Forward-Looking Promises, Guidance, Margins, or Timelines:**

*   **AUM Growth Guidance (FY'26):** Continue to grow AUM at 22-24% YoY.
*   **Disbursement Growth Guidance (FY'26):** Maintain disbursement growth at 18-20% YoY.
*   **Branch Addition (FY'26):** Targeting similar numbers to FY'25, aiming for ~55 new branches.
*   **Year-End GNPA (FY'25):** Expected to be in the range of 1.1%.
*   **Credit Cost Guidance (FY'25):** Expected to be in the range of 25-27 bps.
*   **Exit Spread (End of FY'25):** Projected to be in the range of 5.7% to 5.75%.
*   **Sustainable Spread:** Long-term sustainable spread expected in the range of 5.4% to 5.5%.
*   **Fee Income:** Sustainable fee income expected in the range of 2% to 2.1% of AUM.
*   **BT Out Target:** Aim to reduce BT out to sub-6% by year-end FY'25, with a comfort level of 5.5%-5.7%.
*   **Asset Linking to Rate Cuts:** Expect a lag of 3-6 months for rate cut benefits to flow through to their cost of funds, with pass-through to customers within a quarter of receiving the benefit.
*   **Q4 FY'25 Impact:** Expect credit costs to even out, with a potential benign impact from NPA closure.
*   **Employee Cost Growth:** Expect employee cost growth in the range of 17-18%.
*   **Geographical Limit per Branch:** 60 km radius for customer property location.
