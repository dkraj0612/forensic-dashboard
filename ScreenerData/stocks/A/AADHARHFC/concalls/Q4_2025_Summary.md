## Aadhar Housing Finance - Q4 FY'25 Earnings Call - One-Page Executive Summary

### Executive Overview

Aadhar Housing Finance (AADHARHFC) concluded FY'25 with a strong Q4 performance, achieving a significant AUM of INR 25,531 crores, a 21% YoY growth, and marking a milestone as the first low-income housing finance company to cross INR 25,000 crores. Disbursements for FY'25 stood at INR 8,192 crores (16% YoY growth), with Q4 FY'25 disbursements showing 18% YoY growth. The company reported a PAT of INR 245 crores for Q4 FY'25 (21% YoY growth), contributing to a full-year PAT of INR 912 crores (22% YoY growth). Aadhar Housing Finance maintained its focus on a 100% secured retail loan book with no corporate or developer exposure. Asset quality showed improvement with GNPA declining by 3 bps YoY to 1.05%, supported by a stable collection efficiency of over 100%. The company expanded its geographical footprint to 21 states with over 580 branches, adding 57 new branches in FY'25 to serve 2.99 lakh customers. A significant strategic initiative this year was the division of the country into "urban" and "emerging" locations to tailor strategies for ticket size, yield, and BT-out management. The company also successfully explored Euro Commercial Paper (ECB) for the first time.

### Business Strategy & Drivers

*   **Low-Income Housing Focus:** Continued leadership in the affordable housing finance sector, with a commitment to serving low-income groups.
*   **Geographical Segmentation:** Division of country into "urban" and "emerging" (A, B, C) locations to tailor strategies for ticket size, yield, and BT-out management. Emerging markets will drive future growth composition.
*   **Digital Transformation & Technology:** Leveraging TCS-enabled core system for end-to-end digitization, with evolving data science and AI/ML capabilities for smarter decision-making.
*   **Branch Network Expansion:** Continued calibrated expansion, adding 57 branches in FY'25, with a focus on emerging markets to enhance reach and efficiency. Entry into Northeast with a Guwahati branch.
*   **Diversified Liability Management:** Exploration of new funding sources like ECB (US $50 million issuance) to diversify borrowing mix.
*   **Portfolio Prudence:** Maintaining a 100% secured retail book, focus on home loans, and prudent LTV ratios.
*   **Risk Management:** Emphasis on underwriting and collection, with improved GNPA and stable collection efficiency.
*   **Macroeconomic Tailwinds:** Benefiting from government initiatives and rising demand for affordable housing.

### Management Accountability & Consistency Audit

**Performance Against Stated Targets (Q4 FY'25 vs. Previous Guidance/Expectations):**

*   **AUM Growth:** Achieved 21% YoY growth in Q4 FY'25, aligning with the previously guided 20-21% for FY'26.
*   **Disbursement Growth:** FY'25 disbursements grew 16% YoY, aligning with the previously guided 18-19% for FY'26. Q4 FY'25 disbursement growth at 18% YoY was strong.
*   **Cost to Income Ratio:** FY'25 ratio of 36.4% showed a 104 bps improvement, exceeding the previously guided 80-100 bps reduction for the financial year.
*   **Asset Quality (GNPA):** GNPA at 1.05% is one of the best in the last 3-4 years, showing sequential improvement and aligning with the target of ~1.10% - 1.15% for FY'26.
*   **Branch Expansion:** Added 57 branches in FY'25, exceeding the previous year's 12 in Q3 and aligning with a strong expansion trajectory.

**Narrative Shift/Headwinds Addressed:**

*   **Stage-2 Assets:** Management clarified that the steady increase in Stage-2 assets is largely due to conservative provisioning on legacy OTR (One-Time Restructuring) accounts from COVID, which are performing well and may be moved back to Stage-1 in FY'26. This conservative classification is not indicative of underlying asset quality deterioration.
*   **Credit Costs:** Q4 FY'25 credit costs were INR 5-6 crores, primarily due to Stage-2 movements and standard asset provisioning on AUM growth, not elevated write-offs. Write-offs were INR 8-9 crores for Q4 FY'25 and INR 32 crores for FY'25, considered normal.
*   **Balance Transfer (BT) Outflows:** Managed to reduce annualized BT-out to 6.49% in March '25 from 6.93% in the previous year, by implementing a centralized retention team, data analytics, and offering top-ups. The strategy of shifting focus to emerging B & C locations is also intended to mitigate BT-out pressure in urban areas.
*   **Interest Rate Environment:** Management noted that while 18% of borrowings have immediate pass-through benefits from rate cuts, the broader impact on cost of funds through MCLR will likely be seen from Q2 FY'26. Asset yields are expected to be maintained due to the segmentation strategy.

**New Forward-Looking Promises, Guidance, Margins, or Timelines:**

*   **AUM Growth Guidance (FY'26):** Maintain AUM growth guidance of 20-21% YoY.
*   **Disbursement Growth Guidance (FY'26):** Maintain disbursement growth guidance of 18-19% YoY.
*   **PAT Growth Guidance (FY'26):** Target PAT growth of 20-21% YoY.
*   **NPA Target (FY'26):** Expected to be around 1.10% to 1.15%.
*   **Yields:** Expect to maintain current yield levels (around 13.5%-13.6%), with higher yields (14%-16%) in emerging A, B, C locations to offset potential pressure in urban areas. Yields are not expected to grow significantly but will be maintained.
*   **Cost to Income Ratio:** Target a further drop of 30-50 bps in FY'26, aiming for ~150 bps reduction over two years.
*   **Emerging Market Share:** Aim for emerging markets to contribute 55% of growth in a couple of years, reversing the current 55% urban / 45% emerging mix.
*   **Credit Costs:** No expected increase in credit costs due to the shift in strategy towards emerging markets, as the business in these areas is already being conducted.
*   **State AUM Concentration:** Will not breach the 14% mark for AUM per state, with a maximum tolerance of 14-14.5%.
*   **Liquidity:** Aim to maintain balance sheet liquidity at around 8-9% of the borrowing mix, subject to ALCO decisions based on market liquidity.
*   **Attrition Rate:** Working on reducing attrition rates, which is expected to improve sales officer productivity (files per RO).
*   **Credit Rating:** Engaged with rating agencies for an upgrade and expect it not to be prolonged.
*   **Legacy OTR Assets:** May consider moving legacy OTR assets (approx. 0.6%-0.7% of Stage-2) back to Stage-1 in FY'26.
