## Executive Overview

Aadhar Housing Finance (AADHARHFC) reported a steady performance in Q2 FY'25, characterized by robust AUM growth and strategic expansion. AUM crossed the INR 22,000 crore mark, reaching INR 22,817 crores, a significant 21% year-on-year increase. Disbursements grew by a healthy 18% YoY to INR 2,036 crores in Q2 FY'25. Profit After Tax (PAT) saw an 15% YoY growth to INR 228 crores for the quarter and a 24% YoY growth to INR 428 crores for H1 FY'25. The company continues to maintain strong asset quality, with Gross Non-Performing Assets (GNPA) declining 6 bps YoY to 1.29% and a collection efficiency of 99%. The portfolio remains entirely retail and secured, with home loans constituting 74% of AUM and an average ticket size of INR 10 lakhs. Aadhar Housing Finance expanded its distribution network, adding 9 branches in Q2 FY'25 to reach a total of 545 branches across 21 states. Management expressed optimism about the sector's growth prospects, driven by government initiatives and urbanization, and reiterated their commitment to serving the low-income housing segment.

## Business Strategy & Drivers

*   **Focus on Low Income Housing:** Aadhar Housing Finance continues its mission to serve the underserved section of society, emphasizing its role in India's inclusive growth story.
*   **Distribution Network Expansion:** Strategic addition of branches, with 9 new branches in Q2 FY'25, bringing the total to 545 across 21 states. The focus is on reaching Tier 4 cities and beyond.
*   **Deeper Impact Strategy:** Aiming to reach niche markets in Tier 4 cities and small district headquarters, with plans to extend this strategy to more states.
*   **Digital Transformation:** Investments in technology and data analytics are enhancing operational efficiencies and driving growth.
*   **Portfolio Prudence:** Maintaining a 100% secure retail portfolio with home loans at 74% and loan against property at 26%. Average ticket size of INR 10 lakhs and LTV of 59% underscore prudent lending.
*   **Risk Management:** Emphasis on maintaining asset quality, with a declining GNPA and healthy collection efficiency.
*   **Moderated Growth Approach:** Management plans a moderate, rather than aggressive, approach to competition, leveraging their established distribution network and experienced team.

## Management Accountability & Consistency Audit

**Performance Against Stated Targets (Q2 FY'25 vs. Previous Guidance/Expectations):**

*   **AUM Growth:** Achieved 21% YoY growth in H1 FY'25, aligning with the previous guidance of 20-21% by Year 3 and company's overall growth trajectory.
*   **Disbursement Growth:** Q2 FY'25 saw 18% YoY growth, which management highlighted as a normalization after the Q1 FY'25 bulge due to an RBI circular. They remain confident in achieving the 18-20% disbursement growth target for FY'26.
*   **PAT Growth:** H1 FY'25 PAT grew 24% YoY, exceeding the previous quarter's 18% YoY growth. The stated guidance of 23-27% for profit growth is on track.
*   **Cost to Income Ratio:** H1 FY'25 at 36.4%, showing progress towards the stated goal of an 80-100 bps reduction for the full financial year.
*   **Spreads:** Exit spread at Q2 FY'25 was 5.9%, consistent with previous guidance.
*   **Asset Quality (GNPA):** GNPA at 1.29% in Q2 FY'25, showing a YoY improvement, which management views as a positive outcome.
*   **Branch Expansion:** Added 22 branches in H1 FY'25, indicating continued execution of the branch network expansion strategy.

**Narrative Shift/Headwinds Addressed:**

*   **Disbursement Growth in Q2:** Management clarified that the 18% YoY growth in Q2 FY'25 was strong and normalized after the Q1 FY'25 impact of an RBI circular. They reaffirmed that the full-year disbursement growth target remains achievable.
*   **Yields vs. Rate Hikes:** Management acknowledged that while rate hikes were implemented, the impact on overall yields was moderated by factors like product mix and competition. They clarified that the reported 14% yield is an exit yield, and incremental yields are lower.
*   **Cost of Borrowing:** The cost of funds rose to 8.1% (as per Q2 presentation), with an expectation to exit the year between 8.2% to 8.25%. Management anticipates potential further pass-throughs from MCLR increases by some banks.
*   **Economically Weaker Section (EWS) Share:** The share of EWS clients marginally declined to 69% from 71% due to rising income levels, but management reiterated their continued focus on this segment.
*   **BT Out Trend:** A substantial YoY improvement was noted in BT out rates, down to 5.8% annualized, which management considers a comfortable position.
*   **Fee Income:** The fee income as a percentage of disbursement has slightly decreased to 2.1% but is expected to be modeled conservatively between 2.2% to 2.4%.

**New Forward-Looking Promises, Guidance, Margins, or Timelines:**

*   **AUM Growth Guidance (FY'26):** 22% to 24%.
*   **Disbursement Growth Guidance (FY'26):** 18% to 20%.
*   **Profit Growth Guidance (FY'26):** In the range of 23% to 27%.

*   **Cost to Income Ratio Guidance (FY'26):** Aim to reduce by another 80 bps to 100 bps from FY'24 levels.

*   **Incremental Yields:** Expectation to increase incremental yields from Q3 FY'25 onwards.
