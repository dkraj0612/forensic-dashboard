## Executive Overview

Aadhar Housing Finance (AADHARHFC) closed FY'26 on a strong note, achieving a significant milestone with Assets Under Management (AUM) crossing INR 30,000 crores, reaching INR 30,571 crores, a 20% YoY growth. The company reported its highest-ever quarterly disbursement of INR 3,087 crores in Q4 FY'26, contributing to a full-year disbursement of INR 9,556 crores (17% YoY growth). Profit After Tax (PAT) for FY'26 stood at INR 1,096 crores (20% YoY growth). Asset quality remained robust with GNPA at 1.08% (a sequential improvement) and collection efficiency consistently above 99.8%. The company expanded its branch network to 626 branches across 22 states, adding 46 branches in FY'26. Management reiterated its commitment to a 20% AUM growth and 20% profit growth for FY'27, expecting similar disbursement growth of 17-18%. The company is confident in the continued strength of the Affordable Housing segment, driven by stable end-user demand, favorable demographics, and policy support.

## Business Strategy & Drivers

*   **Focus on Affordable Housing:** Continued commitment to the low-income housing segment, leveraging strong structural demand and supportive government policies like PMAY.
*   **Geographical Diversification:** Maintaining a balanced presence across urban and emerging markets, with a strategy to increasingly shift incremental business contribution towards emerging markets. No single state contributes disproportionately to AUM, disbursements, or distribution.
*   **Balanced Portfolio:** Maintaining a retail-secured loan book with a balance between home loans (73%) and loan against property (LAP) (27%).
*   **Digital Transformation:** Leveraging AI and analytics for enhanced sourcing, underwriting, collections, and improved turnaround times and risk assessment.
*   **Branch Network Expansion:** Calibrated expansion to reach more underserved markets, with a target of ~750 branches by FY'28.
*   **Liability Management:** Diversifying funding sources with a strong base of bank borrowings and NHB, alongside NCDs and ECBs.
*   **Risk Management:** Maintaining strong underwriting discipline and collection processes, evidenced by healthy asset quality and high collection efficiency.
*   **Customer Segmentation:** Focus on end-user driven demand, particularly first-time homebuyers, across emerging markets.

*   **Disbursement Growth:** Achieved 17% YoY growth in FY'26, aligning with the previously guided 17-18% for FY'26.
*   **Profit Growth:** Achieved 20% YoY growth in FY'26, meeting the previously guided 20% for FY'26.
*   **Cost to Income Ratio:** Achieved 35.9% in FY'26, meeting the guidance of approximately 50 bps drop YoY.
*   **GNPA:** GNPA at 1.08% in Q4 FY'26 represents a sequential improvement, demonstrating stable asset quality.
*   **Branch Expansion:** Added 46 branches in FY'26, aligning with the strategy of adding 50-60 branches annually.

**Narrative Shift/Headwinds Addressed:**

*   **Geopolitical Impact (West Asia War):** Management stated no immediate impact on bounce rates or ground operations, citing customer base's focus on essential commodities and minimal NRI exposure. Collection teams are on alert.
*   **Loan Against Property (LAP) Growth:** Management clarified the slower growth in LAP was a deliberate, planned decision due to tariff issues and the geopolitical situation, indicating a cautious approach. They intend to calibrate LAP growth prudently.
*   **Fee Income (Assignment Income):** The increase in assignment income was attributed to better spreads (80-85 bps) and some deals pushed from Q3 to Q4. The net impact of assignment income (upfront and unwinding) remained stable YoY.
*   **Other Income:** Explained as being due to two one-off items: an IT refund (~INR 2.5 crores) and old write-backs (~INR 3.5 crores), totaling ~INR 6-7 crores.
*   **ESOP Cost:** Confirmed the ESOP cost of INR 13.5 crores in Q4 FY'26 and full year FY'26 is a one-time expense with no accrual into FY'27.
*   **Interest Rate Environment & Yields:** Management anticipates interest rates to remain stagnant for the next 2-3 quarters. They are focused on protecting spreads rather than solely on yields, noting that increasing LAP and emerging market business can provide a yield boost.
*   **Opex to AUM:** The sequential increase in Q4 was attributed to employee contests and other one-time expenses typical for the quarter, while noting a YoY improvement in the Cost-to-Income ratio.
*   **Ticket Size Trends:** While the average book ticket size is around INR 10.9 lakhs, incremental disbursement ticket size was INR 12.5 lakhs in Q4 FY'26. Management indicated a focus on maintaining ticket sizes in the INR 10-11.5 lakh range, with emerging markets generally having lower ticket sizes. They are comfortable sustaining current ticket sizes and see potential for a slight increase due to construction costs.
