## Executive Overview

Aadhar Housing Finance (AADHARHFC) commenced FY'26 with a robust Q1 performance, reporting an AUM of INR 26,524 crores, a significant 22% YoY growth. Disbursements for Q1 FY'26 stood at INR 1,979 crores, showing an impressive 32% YoY growth. The company reported a PAT of INR 237 crores for Q1 FY'26 (19% YoY growth). Asset quality remained healthy with GNPA at 1.34% and collection efficiency consistently above 98%. The company expanded its reach to 22 states with over 591 branches, adding 11 new branches in Q1 FY'26. A key strategic initiative, the division of the country into "urban" and "emerging" locations, is in its first quarter of implementation, with the company seeing positive initial results and a focus on emerging markets for future growth. CARE upgraded its rating outlook to AA+, reflecting consistent loan book growth, robust capital adequacy, and strong asset quality.

## Business Strategy & Drivers

*   **Low-Income Housing Focus:** Continued leadership in the affordable and low-income housing finance sector, leveraging macro tailwinds and customer aspirations.
*   **Urban & Emerging Market Segmentation:** First quarter of implementation for the strategy, tailoring ticket size, potential, yield, and manpower for urban (top 15 cities, ~130 branches) and emerging markets (rest of India, categorized A, B, C based on potential). Emerging markets are expected to drive future growth composition.
*   **Digital Transformation & Data Analytics:** Continued leverage of TCS-enabled core system for digitization and evolving data science, AI/ML capabilities for enhanced decision-making.
*   **Branch Network Expansion:** Calibrated expansion, adding 11 branches in Q1 FY'26, with a focus on emerging markets (15 out of 50-60 annual additions planned for urban, 35 for emerging).
*   **Liability Management:** Diversified funding mix with continued engagement with banks, NHB, NCDs, and ECB.
*   **Portfolio Prudence:** Maintaining a 100% retail-secured book with no corporate or developer exposure. Focus on home loans and prudent LTV ratios.
*   **Risk Management:** Emphasis on underwriting and collection, with stable asset quality and collection efficiency. Conservative approach to loan against property (LAP), with 95% of LAP being self-occupied residential property.
*   **Government Initiatives:** Benefiting from PMAY 2.0, with Aadhar being the first to disburse subsidies to consumers under this scheme.

## Management Accountability & Consistency Audit

**Performance Against Stated Targets (Q1 FY'26 vs. Previous Guidance/Expectations):**

*   **AUM Growth:** Achieved 22% YoY growth in Q1 FY'26, aligning with the previously guided 20-21% for FY'26.
*   **Disbursement Growth:** Q1 FY'26 disbursements grew 32% YoY, exceeding the previously guided 18-20% for FY'26.
*   **Cost to Income Ratio:** Q1 FY'26 ratio of 36.1% showed a further drop YoY, continuing the positive trend from FY'25.
*   **Asset Quality (GNPA):** GNPA at 1.34% is slightly up from Q1 FY'25 (1.31%), which management attributes to seasonal effects and the normal flow from Stage-2 to Stage-3. This is within expected seasonal fluctuations.
*   **Branch Expansion:** Added 11 branches in Q1 FY'26, on track with the plan of adding 50-60 branches annually.

**Narrative Shift/Headwinds Addressed:**

*   **Stage-2 & Stage-3 Assets:** Management clarified that the movement from Stage-2 to Stage-3 in Q1 is a normal seasonal phenomenon, allowing for legal action (SARFAESI) on delinquent accounts. The overall 30+ DPD pool is expected to reduce from 4.65% in Q1 to 3.5-4% by year-end, consistent with historical trends.
*   **MSME Stress:** Management reiterated that Aadhar does not operate in the MSME segment. Their micro-LAP portfolio (27% of AUM, average ticket size ~INR 9 lakhs) is not showing any stress, with steady bounce rates and NPAs.
*   **Competition:** Management acknowledges competition but highlights Aadhar's leadership in AUM, disbursements, and distribution network within its affordable housing niche. They are not seeing increased competition or significant BT-out pressures in their segment. BT-out rate for the quarter was 5.3%.
*   **Credit Culture in Southern Markets:** Management strongly refutes claims of slowdown or credit culture issues in southern markets (Tamil Nadu, Karnataka, Andhra Pradesh, Telangana), citing strong performance and Aadhar's secured lending model as being unaffected by MFI ordinances.

**New Forward-Looking Promises, Guidance, Margins, or Timelines:**

*   **AUM Growth Guidance (FY'26):** Maintain AUM growth guidance of 20-22% YoY.
*   **Disbursement Growth Guidance (FY'26):** Maintain disbursement growth guidance of 18-20% YoY.
*   **PAT Growth Guidance (FY'26):** While not explicitly stated for the full year, Q1 PAT growth was 19%.
*   **NPA Target (FY'26):** Expected to reduce from Q1's 1.34% to around 1.10% by year-end, in line with historical seasonal trends.
*   **Credit Costs:** Expected to be in the range of 25-27 bps for the full year.
*   **Spread:** Expected to remain in the same range of 5.7%-5.8% as cost of funds benefits are passed on.
*   **Emerging Market Share:** Intent to shift incremental business contribution from 55% urban/45% emerging to 55% emerging/45% urban.
*   **Branch Addition (FY'26):** Plan to add 50-60 branches annually for the next three years, with 15 in urban and 35 in emerging locations.
*   **ROA Target (FY'26):** Targeting ROA in the range of 4.2% to 4.3%.
*   **Aspirational ROE:** Long-term aspirational ROE of 17-18% (translating to ~4.2-4.3% ROA).
*   **Self-Construction vs. Purchase:** Self-construction at 25%, purchase of ready-built property at 43%, plot purchase and construction at 7%, micro-LAP at balance.
*   **Urban vs. Emerging Yields:** Urban incremental yield ~12-12.5%; Emerging incremental yield ~14.5-16%.
*   **MFI Exposed Customers:** Slight improvement observed with delinquencies dropping and NPAs around 550 customers.
*   **Branch Network (Long Term):** Aiming for approximately 750 branches by FY'28.
