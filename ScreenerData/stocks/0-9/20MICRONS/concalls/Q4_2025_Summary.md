## Executive Overview

20 Microns Limited reported its Q4 FY25 and full-year results, showcasing robust annual revenue growth and improved profitability. The company achieved an annual revenue of ₹9,127 million for FY25, representing a significant 17.4% year-on-year increase. Quarter 4 FY25 also demonstrated strong sequential growth, with revenue advancing by 6% over Q3 FY25 and by a notable margin over Q4 FY24. EBITDA margins for Q4 FY25 improved to 12.7% from 12.2% in Q3 FY25 and 12.5% in Q4 FY24, indicating enhanced operational efficiencies. Profit After Tax (PAT) for Q4 FY25 saw a healthy increase to 6.7% against 6% in Q3 FY25 and 6.4% in Q4 FY24. For the full year FY25, the company maintained EBITDA margins at 12.8% despite facing headwinds like raw material import pressures, freight costs, and forex volatility. Management highlighted strategic execution through onboarding new clients, expanding the product portfolio, and strengthening the distribution network as key drivers. The company also confirmed its commitment to shareholder returns with a 25% dividend declaration and strategic capital allocation, including a successful Malaysian acquisition.

## Business Strategy & Drivers

*   **Profitable Growth:** Focus on revenue growth that contributes to the bottom line, achieved through strategic market presence expansion.
*   **Operational Excellence:** Continuous enhancement of operational efficiency as a core objective, maintaining margins despite external pressures.
*   **Strategic Capital Allocation:** Prioritizing reinvestment in high-return opportunities, maintaining a strong balance sheet, and considering shareholder returns. This enabled the Malaysian acquisition and dividend declaration.
*   **International Expansion:** Successful acquisition of Malaysian subsidiaries (GTLQ and IQ marbles), with ongoing processes to secure full ownership.
*   **Product Portfolio Expansion:** Diversification across various industrial minerals and specialty chemicals, including efforts in value-added segments and functional additives.
*   **Market Penetration:** Strengthening distribution networks and onboarding new clients.
*   **Innovation & R&D:** Emphasis on research and development, supported by government recognition (DSIR certification) for its labs and product application centers.

## Management Accountability & Consistency Audit

*   **Stated Targets vs. Actual Performance:**
    *   **FY26 Revenue Growth (13-15% guidance from Q3 FY26):** Management now explicitly states a target of 15-18% year-on-year growth for FY25-26. This is an upward revision from the previously stated 13-15% for FY26. The company has demonstrated strong 17.4% YoY growth in FY25, indicating a positive trajectory towards achieving this new, ambitious target.
    *   **EBITDA Margins (13-15% historical guidance):** The reported EBITDA margin of 12.8% for FY25 is slightly below the lower end of the historical 13-15% range. However, management highlights maintaining this margin amidst significant headwinds (raw material imports, freight, forex volatility) as a success. The Q4 FY25 margin improved sequentially to 12.7%, showing positive momentum. Management states they are vigilant and calculated in their investment and borrowing strategies.
    *   **Malaysian Mining Asset:** The transcript confirms the ongoing process to acquire remaining shares in Malaysian subsidiaries GTLQ (90% owned) and IQ marbles (86.68% owned). Mining operations are expected to commence within the next 2 months. Management stated it's too early to comment on margin impact from Malaysian mining but expects reduced dependency on external suppliers.
    *   **Freight Costs & Supply Chain Volatility:** Management acknowledges these as persistent headwinds impacting raw material sourcing, especially for imported materials (36-37% of total). The increase in borrowings is partly attributed to building higher inventory levels to mitigate these supply chain risks and freight costs, utilizing bulk shipments.
    *   **CapEx Plan:** While no specific quantum for FY26 is detailed, management indicates a significant investment in CapEx from this year onwards to generate better capacities and acquire more mines. They express a preference to fund CapEx from reserves rather than working capital borrowings. An investment of ₹15-18 crore is being made in 20 Microns Nano Minerals for a calcination facility and specialized paint grades.
    *   **Nano Business Performance:** 20 Microns Nano Minerals closed FY25 at ₹104 crores, up from ₹88 crores in FY23-24. This represents a growth of approximately 18.2%.
    *   **Rare Earth Minerals:** No mention of rare earth minerals in this transcript.
    *   **Dorfner JV:** Clarified that the Dorfner JV is a marketing-based venture and not a production-based one.
    *   **Sievert JV:** Recently inaugurated a JV with Sievert for construction chemicals, starting with tile adhesives and planning for liquid construction chemicals in phase two.

*   **New Forward-Looking Promises/Guidance for Next Quarter/Year:**
    *   **FY25-26 Revenue Guidance:** Management is targeting 15-18% year-on-year growth for FY25-26, citing positive trends but acknowledging economic and global situation uncertainties.
    *   **Value-Added Niche Segment Revenue:** Expecting 18-20% growth rate over the next 2-3 years for these segments, considering longer approval cycles.
    *   **Malaysian Mining Operations:** Expected to commence within the next 2 months.
    *   **Margin Expansion from Malaysian Mine:** Too early to quantify but expects reduced dependency on external suppliers and the ability to sell non-required ore externally.

    *   **Borrowing Utilization:** Primarily for working capital to build additional inventory due to reliance on imported raw materials and supply chain considerations.

    *   **New Sievert JV:** Focus on construction chemicals, starting with tile adhesives and liquid construction chemicals.
    *   **Dividend:** Dividend for FY25 of 25% to be subject to shareholder approval at the AGM.
