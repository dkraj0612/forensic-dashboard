## Executive Overview

20 Microns Limited reported Q3 FY26 results, showcasing resilience amidst a challenging operating environment, particularly within the paint industry. The company reported consolidated revenue of ₹2,148.2 million, a marginal year-on-year growth of approximately 0.1%. Despite the subdued demand in the paint segment, the company achieved a notable 15.1% year-on-year increase in Profit After Tax (PAT) to ₹148.7 million, attributed to improved operational efficiencies. EBITDA margins expanded to 12.9% from 12.2% in the prior year, driven by a favorable product mix and disciplined cost management. Earnings Per Share (EPS) increased to ₹4.24 from ₹3.65 in Q3 FY25. For the nine months ended December 2025, consolidated revenues saw a 1.1% YoY increase to ₹6,927.7 million, with PAT rising by 3.8% to ₹490.8 million. A significant development during the quarter was the consolidation of ownership of its Malaysian subsidiary, 20ML Malaysia, making it a wholly-owned subsidiary and strengthening its international footprint. While the paint segment remained subdued, the plastics segment showed growth, and the rubber segment performed strongly. The company is set to commence operations at a new manufacturing facility in Malaysia, targeting Asia-Pacific and African markets.

## Business Strategy & Drivers

*   **Innovation and Diversification:** Continued strategic focus on innovation, particularly in the Plastics and Rubber segments, to drive future growth and improve margins.
*   **Operational Efficiencies & Cost Management:** Emphasis on margin improvement through disciplined cost management and favorable product mix.
*   **International Expansion:** Consolidation of Malaysian subsidiary ownership (100% acquisition) to strengthen global footprint. Upcoming small-scale operations at a new Malaysian manufacturing facility targeting Asia-Pacific and African markets.
*   **Market Positioning:** Participation in international trade fairs (e.g., K Fair 2025) to enhance brand presence, strengthen customer engagement, and showcase high-performance minerals, specialty additives, and sustainable solutions.
*   **Segmental Focus:** Leveraging strong performance in the rubber segment and continued growth in the plastics segment to counterbalance subdued demand in the paint industry.
*   **Product Development:** Introduction of new manufacturing capacities (Vadodala, Hosur, Alwar, Tirunelveli), diversification into Kaolin and Pozzolanic materials, and initiation of functional additive manufacturing. Expansion of functional additives portfolio to newer applications.
*   **Global Partnerships:** Exclusive distribution agreement with Dorfner GmBH for Hydrous Kaolin in Europe. Joint venture with Sievert Baustoff for construction chemicals and building-related products.
*   **Raw Material Sourcing:** Expansion of Malaysia operations for Calcium. Acquisition of limestone extraction and processing businesses in Malaysia.
