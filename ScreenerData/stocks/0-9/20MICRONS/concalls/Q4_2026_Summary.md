## Executive Overview

20 Microns Limited (20ML) reported its Q4 FY26 and full-year results, demonstrating resilience and a strategic transition amidst a challenging demand environment and macroeconomic uncertainties. The company achieved a full-year revenue of ₹9,530 million for FY26, reflecting a **14.8% year-on-year growth**, driven by a recovery in paint and polymer/rubber demand. Q4 FY26 showcased strong sequential growth of **21.5%**, indicating positive momentum. Profitability remained stable, with EBITDA margins at **12.9%** for the full year and **around 12%** for Q4 FY26, attributed to pricing discipline and a better product mix. Profit After Tax (PAT) grew by **16.6%** year-on-year in Q4 FY26 and has nearly tripled over the last five years. The company is strategically transitioning from a traditional industrial mineral player to a diversified specialty material and functional additive platform, evidenced by increased value-added product contributions and a strengthened international presence. A significant **₹100 crore CapEx plan** is underway, focusing on specialty product capabilities, operational productivity, backward integration, international scaling, and margin enhancement.

## Business Strategy & Drivers

*   **Specialty & Functional Additive Platform:** Strategic shift towards higher-value specialty products and functional additives.
*   **R&D and Product Application Centers:** Continued investment in R&D for customized solutions and new product development, launching 35-40 products annually. Focus on advanced nano-sized materials.
*   **International Expansion:** Focus on Malaysian operations (mines operating, plant construction underway), and the Sievert JV (first phase established, second phase progressing).
*   **Strategic CapEx:** ₹100 crore CapEx plan allocated to Malaysian operations (40%), specialty capabilities, operational productivity, backward integration, and scaling international operations.
*   **Diversification:** Growth anticipated from specialty and high-performance materials, polymer and rubber applications, export expansion, and construction chemicals (B2B and B2C).
*   **Operational Excellence:** Emphasis on operational efficiency, disciplined pricing, and leveraging R&D for product mix improvement. Automation planned for margin improvement.
*   **Financial Discipline:** Maintaining a healthy balance sheet, reducing leverage, and strengthening cash flow generation. Aspiration to be debt-free is a long-term goal.

## Management Accountability & Consistency Audit

*   **Stated Targets vs. Actual Performance:**
    *   **FY25-26 Revenue Growth (Guidance of 15-18%):** The company achieved 14.8% YoY revenue growth in FY26, falling slightly short of the lower end of the 15-18% guidance for FY25-26. Management attributed the full-year performance to resilience amidst restrained demand and external factors. Q4 FY26 sequential growth of 21.5% indicates strong current momentum.
    *   **EBITDA Margins (Historical 13-15% / 200-250 BPS expansion):** FY26 EBITDA margins were maintained at 12.9%, stable year-on-year and slightly below the historical target range. Management highlights this stability as a success amidst industry softness, pricing discipline, and a better product mix. The plan to achieve 200 BPS margin expansion is now targeted over the next three years.
    *   **Malaysian Mining Asset:** Mines have started operating, and plant construction is in progress, expected to be commissioned within 12 months. Meaningful contribution to operations is anticipated in early FY27.
    *   **CapEx Plan and Funding:** The ₹100 crore CapEx is funded through internal accruals for domestic plans and a 70:30 debt-to-equity ratio for Malaysian operations.
    *   **New Sievert JV:** First phase established, second phase expected in a few months; meaningful contribution anticipated by the end of FY27.
    *   **Raw Material Sourcing:** Approximately 30% of raw material requirements are met from domestic mines (some under environmental clearance), with 70% from external sources. No own mines domestically except for Malaysian ones.
    *   **Fuel/Gas Shortage:** Management acknowledged facing issues with fuel/gas availability and costs but stated it is not affecting production due to efficient management and multiple sourcing strategies. Future government restrictions remain a concern.
    *   **Geopolitical/Supply Chain Impact:** Management acknowledged challenges from fuel/gas hikes, forex volatility, supply chain disturbances, and increased freight costs. They confirm passing on costs to customers over time on a case-by-case basis.

*   **New Forward-Looking Promises/Guidance for Next Quarter/Year:**
    *   **FY27 Revenue & Margins:** Management has refrained from providing specific guidance for FY27 due to current volatile scenarios but is focusing on increasing revenue and maintaining PAT. They anticipate crossing the ₹1,000 crore revenue benchmark in FY27 if conditions improve, particularly in the second half.
    *   **CapEx Completion:** New CapEx projects are planned to be accomplished by FY30, assuming stable geopolitical scenarios.
    *   **Return on Capital:** RoCE is expected to reach around 20% by FY30.
    *   **Malaysian Operations Contribution:** Expected to show operational results in early FY27.
    *   **Sievert JV Contribution:** Meaningful earnings contribution expected by the end of FY27.
    *   **New Products Contribution:** Currently contributes 4-5% to revenue, with potential for growth as older products are upgraded.
    *   **Net Working Capital (FY27):** Expected to remain consistent with FY26 levels.
    *   **Demand Environment:** Near-term demand is expected to be volatile. Outside paints, plastics, rubber, inks, and construction chemicals are identified as key growth drivers.
    *   **Construction Chemicals Segment:** Both B2B and B2C segments show potential, with dedicated teams and JV efforts.
    *   **Debt-Free Aspiration:** A long-term aspiration, with plans in place, details to be shared closer to timelines.
    *   **Automation & Margin Improvement:** Automation plans are in place across older processes and new milling processes for power cost reduction and capacity improvement. Renewable energy integration (solar, hybrid, wind) and other functional automation are planned over the next three years for cost reduction and margin improvement.
    *   **Food/Pharma Grade Calcium Carbonate:** No current licensing for food/pharma grade, but future entry is anticipated if market potential is identified, requiring a different operational setup.
    *   **Revenue Potential of ₹100 Cr CapEx:** Expected to drive an overall revenue growth of 18% CAGR over the next three years, with a potential margin expansion of 200 BPS in the medium term.
    *   **Market Share:** Ranges from 10% to 30% depending on the product. A list of competitors will be provided separately.
    *   **April-June Quarter:** Demand is currently weaker, making it difficult to confirm if it will be the highest revenue quarter.
    *   **Paint Segment Revenue:** Maintained flat growth in FY26 compared to the previous financial year.
