## Executive Overview

20 Microns Limited's Q4 FY24 earnings call, held on May 21, 2024, reveals continued commitment to innovation and market expansion. While specific financial figures for Q4 FY24 were not detailed in this transcript, the management reiterates a positive outlook for FY25. The company is focusing on product development for EV and semiconductor industries, with commercialization potentially by the end of FY25. They have also secured new clients like JSW and Grasim, with initial supplies already commenced. The strategy involves a balanced approach to revenue growth, prioritizing bottom-line stability. International expansion is shifting focus to Middle Eastern and South Asian markets, driven by perceived growth opportunities and more favorable freight dynamics. The construction chemicals and mineral fertilizer segments are in a product development and network expansion phase, with long-term aspirations for household recognition. Management confirmed dividend payout and internal accruals for future CapEx.

## Business Strategy & Drivers

The company's strategic priorities include:

*   **Product Innovation:** Continued R&D for specialized products, particularly for EV battery and semiconductor industries, with potential commercialization by end of FY25. Development of new products for construction chemicals and mineral fertilizers remains ongoing.
*   **Market Expansion:** Increased focus on Middle Eastern, South Asian, and Far East markets for exports, alongside continued engagement in Latin America. Shift away from North American and Western European markets due to observed challenges.
*   **Client Acquisition & Deepening Relationships:** Secured initial supplies to new clients like Grasim and continued regular supply to established clients like JSW.
*   **Import Substitution & China Plus One Strategy:** Leveraging global efforts to reduce dependency on Chinese products. This includes developing products with an edge over Chinese counterparts and exploring opportunities arising from anti-dumping duties on Chinese goods.
*   **Value-Added Products:** Continued focus on enhancing the value-added segment (functional additives), which currently contributes ~20% of revenue.
*   **Mining Operations:** Ongoing evaluation of new mining opportunities in India and internationally for minerals like calcium carbonate, clays, and talcs. Currently using ~50% of required mining materials in-house for Kaolin, with plans to increase this.
*   **JV for Construction Chemicals:** Progressing with Sievert (Germany) JV, focusing on bringing German-made construction chemicals to India. Long-term vision is to establish these as household names, with a focus on niche market positioning.
*   **Operational Efficiency:** Exploring bulk shipments over containerized transport to mitigate freight cost increases.
