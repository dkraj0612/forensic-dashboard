## Executive Overview

20 Microns Limited's Q2 FY25 earnings call reveals a significant revenue surge of 20.3% year-on-year, reaching INR 2,401.8 million, and a 20.6% increase for H1 FY25 to INR 4,707 million, indicating strong business momentum. Despite this top-line growth, EBITDA saw a modest 3% increase in Q2 FY25 to INR 308 million and an 8% rise in H1 FY25 to INR 608 million. This led to a slight reduction in EBITDA margins, attributed by management to rising raw material and freight costs. Profit After Tax (PAT) grew by 2.5% in Q2 FY25 to INR 164.5 million and by 10.9% in H1 FY25 to INR 343.5 million. The company highlighted resilient performance amidst external challenges, including an extended monsoon. Future outlook remains optimistic, with a focus on innovation, strategic alliances, and capitalizing on domestic market demand.

## Business Strategy & Drivers

The company's strategy continues to emphasize:

*   **Product Innovation & Diversification:** Focus on developing new products for plastics and rubber industries to gradually shift the revenue mix. Developing niche products for EV/battery/semiconductor allied industries is in R&D phase.
*   **Technological Advancement & R&D:** Continued investment in R&D, with a dedicated team of 45-50 professionals focusing on developing a broad range of products.
*   **Market Expansion:** Increased focus on Asia, particularly Middle East and South Asian markets, due to stronger demand prospects.
*   **Strategic Alliances & JVs:** Progressing with the formation of a JV for construction chemicals with Sievert's Germany, targeting production in FY26. A JV for color quartz is functional but in the marketing phase, with product sales imported from Germany.
*   **Capacity & Operational Efficiency:** Maintaining high capacity utilization (around 90% overall, varying by product range). Planned CapEx over 18 months of INR 70-80 crores for capacity expansion, nano product enhancements, JV setup, and R&D upgrades.
*   **Import Substitution:** Nano products are positioned as import substitutes, benefiting from current global freight and supply chain dynamics.
*   **Value-Added Products:** Continued development of Lithomer and Zinkomer as partial replacements for expensive segments, with margins in the 12-13% range.
*   **Secure Raw Material Sourcing:** Planned acquisition of mines in Malaysia to ensure structured procurement of raw materials, focusing on quality improvement and reduced dependency on external owners.

## Management Accountability & Consistency Audit

*   **Stated Targets vs. Actual Performance:**
    *   **Revenue Growth:** The company achieved a revenue growth of 20.3% in Q2 FY25 and 20.6% in H1 FY25, exceeding the previously guided FY24 growth of 15.0%-18.0%. Management has reiterated a 15%-18% YoY growth target for the next two years.
    *   **EBITDA & Margins:** EBITDA growth (3% in Q2, 8% in H1) lagged behind revenue growth, leading to a reduction in EBITDA margins from 14.99% in Q2 FY24 to approximately 12.8% in Q2 FY25. This is a deviation from the previous quarter's sentiment of maintaining or improving the 15% EBITDA margin. Management attributed this decline to external factors like rising raw material and freight costs.
    *   **PAT Growth:** PAT growth of 2.5% in Q2 FY25 and 10.9% in H1 FY25 shows a positive trend, although the margin compressed compared to the previous year.

*   **Narrative/Tone Shift Regarding Headwinds:**
    *   The previous transcript highlighted reductions in "power and fuel cost" and "export logistic costs" as drivers for margin improvement. This current transcript signals a shift, with management explicitly citing "rising costs associated with our raw materials, freight and transportation" as the primary reason for EBITDA margin suppression. The tone regarding cost management has become more defensive, emphasizing the challenges of external factors.

*   **New Forward-Looking Promises/Guidance for Next Quarter:**
    *   **Revenue Growth Guidance:** Management expects to achieve 15.0%-18.0% YoY revenue growth for FY25 and maintains this guidance for at least the next two years.
    *   **Margin Improvement Guidance:** While Q2 FY25 margins compressed, management is focused on maintaining current margins and expects a potential 50-100 bps improvement for FY24 over the existing 15% benchmark (this guidance was from the previous transcript and the current Q2 FY25 EBITDA margin is below 15%, indicating a challenge in achieving this). The expectation for future margin improvement is less explicit in this transcript, with the focus shifting to "maintaining" and "sustaining" margins amidst cost pressures.
    *   **JV Update:** The construction chemicals JV with Sievert's Germany is on track, with company formation expected in the "next few weeks." Production is targeted to start in FY26 (Phase 1) and FY27 (Phase 2).
    *   **EV/Semiconductor Products:** These are still in the R&D phase and will take "a couple of more quarters" to have something ready to announce. No specific timeline beyond that is provided due to the nascent nature of these markets in India and the specialized raw material requirements.
    *   **International Expansion:** Focus has shifted to Asia, specifically the Middle East and South Asian markets, due to stronger demand prospects and past disruptions in American and South American markets due to freight costs.
    *   **Capacity Utilization:** Company-level capacity utilization stands at 90%. Nano product capacity utilization varies by product range (some at 90%, others at 50%).
    *   **New Product Contribution:** Products launched in the last three years contribute 5-6% of sales, with potential to reach 10% in 2-3 years.
    *   **Value-Added Products (Mineral Fertilizers & Construction Chemicals):** These currently contribute ~1-2% of revenue (around INR 10 crore), with expectations to grow 5x to INR 50 crore in 5 years.
    *   **CapEx:** An 18-month CapEx plan of INR 70-80 crores is in place, including INR 25 crores for the Malaysian mine acquisition, capacity expansion for current and nano products, JV setup, and R&D/renovations.
    *   **Malaysian Mines:** Production from the Malaysian mines is expected to begin by Q2 of the next financial year (FY26), focusing on improving raw material quality and reducing wastage, rather than directly boosting margins.
    *   **Nano Revenue:** H1 FY25 revenue for 20 Microns Nano Minerals Limited was INR 54 crore, with a 30% YoY growth. Margins are expected to remain in the 12-13% range. Future growth is anticipated as recently approved products gain traction and new products are developed.
    *   **Quartz JV:** Currently functional in the marketing phase, clocking INR 3 crores in H1 FY25. Products are imported from Germany, with 20 Microns Nano selling them to the JV company for distribution in India.
    *   **Trade Receivables:** Trade receivables increased due to higher revenue, but holding days have remained stable, around 60-62 days.
    *   **Inventory Build-up:** Inventory has been built up, particularly for imported materials, in anticipation of future demand.
    *   **Interest Costs:** High interest costs are attributed to discounting charges for major customers, not regular debt interest rates.
