## Executive Overview

20 Microns Limited held its maiden Q4 FY19 earnings conference call, reporting robust financial performance for the quarter and the full fiscal year. Revenue saw significant growth, driven by a strategic shift towards higher-margin specialty chemicals. Profitability also improved considerably, with expanded EBITDA and PAT margins. The company outlined a clear strategy focused on product innovation, market expansion (both domestic and international), and a transformation of its business mix towards value-added and retail products. Management expressed confidence in achieving future growth targets, supported by a strong R&D pipeline and efforts to improve operational efficiencies.

## Business Strategy & Drivers

The company's strategy is centered on transforming its business model and expanding its market reach:

*   **Shift to Specialty Chemicals:** A primary focus on growing the specialty chemicals segment, which offers higher margins and is a key driver for future profitability.
*   **Product Innovation & R&D:** Continued investment in R&D to develop new products, aiming for ultrafine minerals up to 0.2 microns and expanding the specialty product portfolio.
*   **B2C/Retail Expansion:** Targeting the B2C market with waterproofing additives and fertilizer brands, aiming for higher-margin business through retail channels and product branding.
*   **Export Growth:** Increasing the contribution of exports to total sales, with a target of reaching 25% in the next couple of years by entering newer markets and promoting new products.
*   **Diversification:** Expanding product offerings and client base by targeting industries beyond traditional paint, paper, and plastics.
*   **Operational Efficiency & Sustainability:** Working towards becoming a "Zero Waste Company" and implementing cost-saving measures and operational efficiencies through cost control committees.
*   **Capital Allocation:** Focusing on internal cash accruals for business growth, particularly for the retail segment, to avoid significant new debt. Limited CAPEX planned, primarily for minor repairs and installations, leveraging existing assets.

## Management Accountability & Consistency Audit

*   **Stated Targets vs. Actual Performance:** Management's reported standalone Q4 FY19 revenue growth of 15% and PAT growth of 72% align with the presented figures. The EBITDA margin expansion of 138 bps YoY is also reflected in the results. The company successfully demonstrated a shift towards specialty chemicals, which was a key narrative driver in previous periods. The consolidated FY19 figures also show positive growth in revenue (+11%) and PAT (+22%), with margin expansion across both EBITDA (+50 bps) and PAT (+50 bps), indicating consistency in strategic execution.
*   **Narrative/Tone Shift Regarding Headwinds:** The narrative strongly emphasizes the positive impact of the strategic shift towards specialty chemicals and export growth. While raw material costs for imported specialty chemicals were noted as a factor that could increase RMC costs, management expressed confidence in balancing this through pricing adjustments and continued focus on operational efficiencies. The tone is confident and forward-looking, highlighting opportunities rather than dwelling on past headwinds.

**New Forward-Looking Promises/Guidance for Next Quarter:**

*   **Promoter Shareholding:** Working towards increasing promoter shareholding above 50% and unpledging shares in the near future.
*   **Revenue Growth:** Expecting specialty chemicals to double in the next two to three years. Calcium carbonate to see regular low-single-digit YoY growth. Talc and kaolin exports to double. Kaolin to see regular 20-25% growth.
*   **EBITDA Margin Expansion:** Targeting 50 to 100 bps of EBITDA margin increase year-on-year, gradually over the next couple of years.
*   **Specialty Chemicals Contribution:** Aiming to increase the specialty chemicals share of total sales from the current low-single-digit to 20% in the next couple of years.
*   **Export Target:** Increasing exports to 25% of total sales in the next couple of years.
*   **Product Focus:** Prioritizing waxes in the plastics industry and matting agents for the paint industry as key growth drivers within specialty chemicals.
*   **CAPEX:** No major CAPEX planned for the next few years, with maintenance CAPEX estimated at Rs. 3-4 crores annually.
*   **Working Capital:** Aiming to reduce the working capital cycle slightly by optimizing payment terms for imports and collection of receivables, but not expecting substantial reduction.
*   **Market Share:** Actively working on methodologies to calculate and report market share for key products.
*   **Top Specialty Products:** Top three specialty products contribute about 50% of specialty chemical sales. Waxes and matting agents contributed Rs. 25-30 crores in FY19 and are targeted to at least double or 2.5 times.
