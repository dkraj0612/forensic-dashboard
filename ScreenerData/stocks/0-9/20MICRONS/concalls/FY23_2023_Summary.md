## Executive Overview

20 Microns Limited has reported its FY23 results, showcasing a revenue growth of 14.4% year-over-year, reaching Rs. 7,016.87 Mn. EBITDA saw an 8.00% increase to Rs. 793.26 Mn, with EBITDA margins remaining stable at 12.9%, despite facing cost pressures in power, fuel, and logistics. Profit After Tax (PAT) grew by 20.83% to Rs. 347.28 Mn. The company continues to emphasize its strategic shift towards value-added specialty products and R&D, with new manufacturing locations and diversification into areas like agrochemicals and construction chemicals. The "China + 1" strategy and focus on import substitution remain key pillars.

## Business Strategy & Drivers

The company's strategy and growth drivers are centered around:

*   **Product Innovation & Value Addition:** Continued focus on developing and offering high-end specialty chemicals and nano technology-based minerals to replace imports. This includes specialized coating formulations, micronized waxes, lubricants, and processing aids for polymer, PVC, and cable industries.
*   **Capacity Expansion & Resource Acquisition:** Plans to invest in expanding capacity at existing and new manufacturing locations. Aggressively planning to acquire mines of various minerals domestically and globally.
*   **R&D and Technological Advancement:** Significant investment in R&D to develop value-added solutions and tailor-made products. Focus on nano sizing chemistry, moving beyond sub-micron technology. Development of micro-nutrients for the agro-industry and innovative construction chemicals.
*   **Market Diversification & Expansion:** Entering new product segments like agrochemicals (Minfert) and construction chemicals (20 MCC). Expanding sales channels in European and Latin American continents.
*   **Customer-Centric Approach:** Working closely with customers to provide customized solutions and maintaining high customer retention through a robust go-to-market strategy.
*   **"China + 1" Strategy & Import Substitution:** Capitalizing on the "China + 1" theme to become a preferred supplier and developing products that serve as import substitutes.
*   **Strategic Alliances & Joint Ventures:** Forming strategic alliances and joint ventures with global players to expand business and access new markets, exemplified by the JV with Dorfner.
