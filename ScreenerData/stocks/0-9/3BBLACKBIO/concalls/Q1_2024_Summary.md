## Executive Overview

Kilpest India Limited, operating through its subsidiary 3B BlackBio Dx Limited, continues to focus on its dual business verticals: Agrochemicals and Molecular Diagnostics (MDx). The Q1 FY 2024-25 update showcases significant progress in the MDx segment, particularly in global market penetration, product portfolio expansion, and the performance of its UK subsidiary. The Agrochemical division remains stable, with a focus on government tenders and brand building, though export sales to Bangladesh face potential impact due to the current political climate. Key financial highlights for Q1 FY 2024-25 include INR 1,694.44 Lakhs in Revenue from Operations and INR 1,458.53 Lakhs in EBITDA for the diagnostics business. The company is progressing with its amalgamation and is preparing for an NSE listing.

## Business Strategy & Drivers

*   **Dual Business Focus:** Kilpest India Limited operates both Agrochemical and Molecular Diagnostics (3B BlackBio Dx Limited) businesses.
*   **Agrochemicals Strategy:**
    *   Continued focus on government procurement via e-tenders (GeM).
    *   Expansion of customer base into new regions.
    *   Intensified efforts in brand recognition for micro fertilizers, bio fertilizers, and public health products.
    *   Sales remain stable, and the business operates with minimal debt.
*   **Molecular Diagnostics (3B BlackBio Dx Limited) Strategy:**
    *   **Market Penetration:** Bolstering sales with existing customers and channel partners by expanding product portfolios.
    *   **Geographical Expansion:** Penetrating new laboratory markets and untapped territories through direct engagement or distributors. TRUPCR® assays accepted in over 55 countries. Appointing new channel partners globally and completing product registrations in several countries. Securing annual rate contracts through international tenders. Exploring partnerships in Africa, LATAM, APAC, and the Middle East.
    *   **Portfolio Expansion:** Enhancing product offerings in qPCR and NGS, and exploring Digital PCR (dPCR) technology. Launched new NGS panels (PAN-MYELOID NGS Panel, BRCA Plus Kit, Endometrial Cancer Panel Kit) and reviving NGS kit revalidations. Offering comprehensive panels for syndromic testing. Developing RT-PCR and lateral flow assays for Antimicrobial Resistance (AMR).
    *   **TRUPCR® Europe Limited (UK Subsidiary):** Achieving significant revenue growth (over 85%) driven by European expansion. Manchester Site expected to achieve a 40-50% growth rate this fiscal year, with integrated functions (sales, marketing, technical support, R&D) aimed at reducing TAT and enhancing customer support. "Made in UK" manufacturing as a key selling point. Increased visibility through participation in international conferences (Medlab Asia 2023, Medlab Middle East 2024, APCON 2023, Microcon 2023) leading to distributorship and OEM inquiries. Ongoing validation processes in new labs.
    *   **Mergers and Acquisitions (M&A):** Exploring strategically targeted M&A for inorganic growth in the medium to long term, but only if financially sound and meeting internal IRR criteria.
    *   **Amalgamation & NSE Listing:** Amalgamation is complete. The company will review eligibility criteria and formalities for an NSE listing post-completion.
    *   **Share Buyback:** Considering a share buyback in FY23/24 post-merger if a suitable acquisition candidate is not secured.
    *   **Long-Term Contracts:** Over 15 contracts in place (Reagent-Rental Model) projected to contribute 20-25% business for this and coming years.
    *   **Expansion and Additional Capacity:** Continuous investment in building additional manufacturing and R&D facilities.

## Management Accountability & Consistency Audit

### Audit of Previous Commitments:

*   **Non-COVID Diagnostics Growth:** Management's guidance for 20-25% growth in Non-COVID sales is being significantly exceeded, with Q1 FY 2024-25 reporting approximately 40% growth. This demonstrates strong execution in expanding the non-COVID diagnostics portfolio and market penetration.
*   **International Expansion:** The transcript confirms TRUPCR® assays are now accepted in over 55 countries, a notable increase from the previous 40+. The appointment of new channel partners in various regions, successful product registrations, and securing annual rate contracts through international tenders align with and surpass previous commitments for global reach. Discussions with large international distributors are ongoing and expected to materialize within the fiscal year.
*   **UK Subsidiary Progress:** The reported over 85% revenue growth for TRUPCR® Europe Limited and the Manchester Site's projected 40-50% growth rate for the fiscal year are tangible achievements validating prior guidance. The integrated operations, "Made in UK" manufacturing, and increased visibility through conference participation are consistent with previous strategic objectives.
*   **Product Development (AMR, NGS, dPCR):** The revival of NGS kit revalidations and the launch of three new NGS panels (PAN-MYELOID, BRCA Plus, Endometrial Cancer) indicate significant progress in this vertical. The successful completion of collaborative R&D for AMR Rapid Tests is further reinforced by the sampling and initial orders for the TRURAPID® RESIST 5 OKNVI Rapid Test. The development of assays based on Digital PCR (dPCR) technology is a new, forward-looking development.
*   **Amalgamation & NSE Listing:** The completion of the amalgamation is a direct fulfillment of the previously stated timeline. The company is now proceeding to review the necessary steps for an NSE listing.
*   **Agrochemicals Strategy:** The division's debt-minimal status and stable sales remain consistent. The focus on government tenders and brand building continues. However, the outlook for agrochemical exports to Bangladesh is now explicitly flagged as potentially impacted by the current political situation, a nuanced update to the previously expressed hope for "decent export sales."
