## Executive Overview

3B BlackBio DX Limited's Q3 FY26 earnings call, held on February 21, 2026, focused on the company's performance for the nine months ended December 31, 2025. The acquisition of Coris Bioconcept SRL was a significant factor, contributing to a substantial reported revenue growth of INR 99.06 crores compared to INR 64.80 crores in the prior year (which excluded Coris). Excluding Coris and accounting for seasonal fluctuations (Dengue, Flu), the core 3B business demonstrated a growth of over 20%. The company projects a full-year revenue and EBITDA growth of 10-15%, with potential EBITDA impact from one-time M&A expenses. Management reiterated their strong position in India's Molecular Diagnostics (MDx) market (12-15% share of a INR 400-500 crore TAM) and robust export performance, now reaching 70 countries. TRUPCR Europe continues to be a strong contributor to exports. The company is developing automated extraction systems and a "sample to answer" machine, aiming to expand its addressable market beyond reagents. Coris, the Belgian acquisition, is focused on lateral flow and AMR products, with efforts underway for US FDA registration, expected to take 1-2 years for approval and 3-4 years for full market penetration. Increased competition in the MDx space is attributed to higher margins, with new players entering from ELISA and culture segments. Credit periods have extended to 3-4 months, aligning with industry standards. The company is actively exploring further M&A opportunities and is on track to potentially list on NSE after the audited financial results for March are finalized.

## Business Strategy & Drivers

*   **Core Diagnostics Business (3B):**
    *   **Market Focus:** Infectious diseases and oncology.
    *   **Product Strategy:** Continue offering high-quality reagent kits in the "open kits market." Developing automated extraction systems and a "sample to answer" machine to address new segments. Focus on multiplexed kits (e.g., respiratory, AMR).
    *   **India Market:** Maintain leadership in MDx, leveraging existing customer relationships and expanding product adoption.
    *   **Global Expansion:** Expand export reach to 70 countries, with TRUPCR Europe driving growth in Europe, Middle East, and Latin America. Leveraging "Made in UK" kits for credibility.
    *   **R&D and Product Development:** Continuous development of new kits and technologies. Aiming to remain technologically relevant by developing dPCR products.
*   **Coris Bioconcept SRL (Acquisition):**
    *   **Focus:** Lateral flow tests, particularly Antimicrobial Resistance (AMR) products, and POC solutions.
    *   **Growth Strategy:** Focus on US FDA registration (1-2 years for approval, 3-4 years for market penetration), new product launches, and tapping untapped markets. The HAT contract is expected to provide revenue for the next two years.
    *   **Synergies:** Initially operating as a standalone entity to focus on its growth objectives. Long-term, exploring cross-selling of 3B's PCR kits through Coris's network, though this is a gradual process to avoid distracting from Coris's core focus.
    *   **Financial Target:** Aiming for EBITDA positive status in FY27, targeting 2-4% EBITDA margins.
*   **Mergers and Acquisitions (M&A):**
    *   Actively seeking acquisition targets globally, particularly in the IVD space, with a dedicated reserve of ~INR 50 crores and capacity for further acquisitions. Appointed consultants and dedicate time to this.
*   **Technology & Quality:**
    *   Developing advanced technologies like dPCR.
    *   Focus on IVDR compliance for European markets to ensure continued sales and market access. Selectively pursuing IVDR for high-revenue contributing products.
    *   Maintaining high standards in assay sensitivity, specificity, and workflow efficiency to ensure customer stickiness and competitive advantage.
*   **Agrochemicals Division:** (Not explicitly discussed in this transcript, but implied from previous context) Minimal focus, stable sales, potential divestment.
*   **NSE Listing:** Exploring eligibility and aiming to approach NSE post finalization of audited financial results for March.
