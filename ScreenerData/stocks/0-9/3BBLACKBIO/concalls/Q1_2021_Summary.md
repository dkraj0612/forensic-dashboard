## Executive Overview

This Q1 2021-22 transcript for 3B BlackBio Biotech India Limited (formerly Kilpest India Limited) marks a significant transitionary period following an exceptional FY 2020-21 driven by COVID-19 sales. While acknowledging the substantial revenue and profit surge from its TRUPCR® SARS-CoV-2 RT-qPCR Kits, the company emphasizes a return to non-COVID sales growth and portfolio expansion. The diagnostics segment remains central, with plans to develop rapid antigen and NGS kits. The agro-chemical division is to maintain its focus on Public Health, Warehousing, and Exports with a debt-light, cash-flow positive model. The company is actively exploring inorganic growth through acquisitions and considering a share buyback. The ongoing amalgamation with its subsidiary, 3B BlackBio, is progressing, with expected completion in approximately one year to unlock subsidiary value.

## Key Financial Metrics

*   **Revenue (Q1 2022-23):** ₹ 942.51 Lakhs (includes COVID sales of ₹ 176.78 Lakhs). Compares favorably to pre-COVID Q1 2019-20, indicating growth on a normalized basis.
*   **Profit Before Tax (Q1 2022-23):** ₹ 3266.79 Lakhs (Note: This figure appears exceptionally high given the revenue and may refer to a different period or be a cumulative/annualized figure not clearly specified as Q1).
*   **Profit After Tax (Q1 2022-23):** ₹ 1958.01 Lakhs (Note: Similar to PBT, this figure's context within Q1 2022-23 revenue needs clarification).
*   **EBITDA (Q1 2022-23):** ₹ 600.41 Lakhs.
*   **Paid-Up Equity Share Capital (Q1 2022-23):** ₹ 78.10 Lakhs.
*   **Exports (Q1 2022-23, Molecular Diagnostic Kits):** ₹ 172.98 Lakhs.

## Business Strategy & Drivers

*   **Post-COVID Transition:** Focus on growth in non-COVID diagnostics and expanding the portfolio. The company explicitly states that Q1 2021-22 figures cannot be compared due to the "second wave" COVID sales impact.
*   **Diagnostics Portfolio Expansion:**
    *   **Rapid Antigen Test Kits:** Manufacturing plant is ready for trial runs; targeting Dengue NS1, Dengue IGM/IGG for FY 2022-23. COVID Rapid Antigen Test is ICMR approved but has low current demand.
    *   **Next Generation Sequencing (NGS):** Reviving revalidations and development. NGS vertical is expected to start adding revenue in "later quarters of FY 22-23 only." Pricing in India is considered unremunerative.
    *   **qPCR Diagnostic Kits:** Continued development of new products and increasing the product portfolio.
*   **Agro-chemical Division Focus:** Continues to concentrate on Government business (GeM/Tenders), Public Health, Warehousing, and Exports. Management is cautious about increasing financial exposure to the private agriculture market due to uncertainty. The division is debt-light and cash flow positive. Emphasis on brand building for Micro fertilizers/Bio fertilizers and Public Health Products.
*   **Market Penetration & Geographical Reach:** Aim to increase sales with existing customers by adding more products. Plan to reach new labs post-pandemic and expand into new markets directly or through distributors.
*   **Inorganic Growth:** Actively seeking selective mergers and acquisitions in similar business areas for medium to long-term growth. However, high valuations of life sciences and molecular diagnostic companies are a deterrent.
*   **Joint Venture (TRUPCR Europe Limited):** Established a joint venture with its primary European distributor, HS Biolabs Ltd., re-established as "TRUPCR Europe Limited" in the UK. Lab setup is complete, a technical team is appointed, and they are looking to expand the Sales & Marketing team. Certification as Manufacturing Site-II under ISO 13485:2016 is underway. This site aims to support EU customers faster and expand into other EU countries.
*   **Amalgamation & NSE Listing:** Scheme of amalgamation approved by BSE; SEBI approval pending, followed by NCLT application. Expected completion in approximately one year to unlock the value of 3B BlackBio. Post-completion, the company plans to initiate listing on the National Stock Exchange (NSE).
*   **R&D and Quality:** Continued investment in R&D with a highly educated team of scientists and specialized teams. Investments in laboratory infrastructure, including BSL 2 rooms and clean rooms. Stringent QC protocols for all TRUPCR® products. Over 120 CE-IVD marked parameters and USFDA-EUA for COVID RT-PCR Kit.

**Audit of Previous Commitments:**

*   **Agro-chemical Division Focus Shift:** Management confirms the continued focus on Government business, Public Health, Warehousing, and Exports, with a cautious approach to the private agriculture market due to uncertainty. This aligns with the previous strategy.

This slightly increases the previously stated "over 11 million" tests, showing continued success in this area.

However, they note high valuations are a hurdle, and they will only proceed if it "makes financial sense and meets our internal IRR criteria."
