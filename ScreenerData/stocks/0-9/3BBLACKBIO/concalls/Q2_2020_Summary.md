## Executive Overview

3B BlackBio Biotech India Limited (now Kilpest India Limited, soon to be renamed 3B BlackBio Dx Ltd.) is in the midst of a significant strategic transformation, divesting its agrochemical business to focus entirely on the high-growth diagnostics sector. This consolidation is being achieved through the amalgamation of its diagnostics subsidiary, 3B BlackBio Biotech, into the listed entity, Kilpest India. The renamed "3B BlackBio Dx Ltd." will concentrate on its TRUPCR® brand of molecular diagnostic kits, particularly in oncology and infectious diseases, and is expanding into rapid antigen and antibody tests. The company highlights strong performance in COVID-19 RT-PCR kits, securing US FDA Emergency Use Authorization (EUA) and significant government orders. Future growth will be driven by an expanded product portfolio, leveraging existing R&D capabilities, and penetrating both domestic and international markets, with a commitment to in-house R&D and operational efficiency.

## Business Strategy & Drivers

*   **Strategic Pivot to Diagnostics:** The core strategy remains the divestment of the agrochemical business and a laser focus on the diagnostics sector, leveraging 3B BlackBio Biotech's expertise.
*   **Brand Consolidation:** Amalgamation into "3B BlackBio Dx Ltd." aims to present a unified, diagnostics-focused entity to the market.
*   **Product Diversification:** Expansion beyond molecular diagnostics into Rapid Cards (Antigen and Antibody-based) and other scalable business verticals.
*   **COVID-19 Product Success:** Continued strong sales and market acceptance of TRUPCR® SARS-CoV-2 RT-qPCR Kits, including multiple versions and US FDA EUA.
*   **R&D and Quality Focus:** Emphasis on in-house R&D, stringent QC protocols, and development of clinically validated, precise, and accurate qPCR assays adhering to international standards.
*   **Market Penetration:** Expanding customer base in India and actively pursuing international markets (UK, South East Asia, Middle East, East Africa, USA, Europe).
*   **Government Business:** Securing government tenders and approvals (e.g., ICMR, CDSCO for COVID-19, NIV for H1N1) to drive sales in public health and hospital segments.
*   **Contractual Business:** Initiating long-term contracts (2-3 years) with large customers to ensure predictable revenue streams.
*   **Agrochemical Division Strategy:** While divesting, Kilpest will continue to focus on brand building for Micro fertilizers/Bio fertilizers and Public Health Products, and leverage its established distribution network in Central India. The company also seeks a buyer for its agrochemical brands and intangible assets.
*   **Asset Utilization:** Plans to utilize Kilpest's existing immovable assets for the diagnostics business expansion and R&D.
