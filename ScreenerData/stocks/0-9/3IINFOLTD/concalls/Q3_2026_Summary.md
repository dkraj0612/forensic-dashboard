## Executive Overview

3i Infotech reported Q3 FY26 consolidated revenue of ₹172.1 crore, a slight 1.6% sequential decrease from Q2 FY26. This moderation is attributed to recent regulatory changes impacting certain Business Process Services (BPS) engagements, a factor also affecting the overall revenue mix. Despite this, the company's core digital and technology services segments, particularly Application-Automation-Analytics (AAA) and Infrastructure Services (IS), showed steady performance. The United States and India remain key markets, contributing approximately 50% and 40% of revenue, respectively, with a noted increase in US contribution. AAA continues to be the largest segment, generating ₹121.2 crore in revenue. The company successfully secured over 80% of key client renewals, reinforcing client confidence and long-standing relationships. A significant development is the initiation of arbitration proceedings related to the RailTel WiFi Monetization Project, with the first hearing scheduled for February 20, 2026. Additionally, the company has filed a complaint against eMudhra Limited and its former executives for alleged financial fraud amounting to ₹128 crore plus interest. The company is prudently deploying funds raised from a rights issue (₹64.1 crore) into competency building, growth initiatives, and Centres of Excellence (CoEs).
