## Executive Overview

3i Infotech reported Q4 FY26 consolidated revenue of ₹175.7 crore, a slight 2.1% sequential increase from Q3 FY26, indicating maintained growth momentum. The company achieved profitability for the second consecutive year, reporting ₹35.1 crore in consolidated Profit After Tax (PAT) for the full year FY26, representing a significant 38.5% year-over-year increase. Full-year FY26 revenue stood at ₹693.3 crore. The US emerged as the largest market, contributing 49% of total revenue with 19.2% YoY growth, while India contributed 40%, though it saw a 15.6% YoY decrease. Application-Automation-Analytics (AAA) remained the largest segment, generating ₹493.2 crore in FY26. The company secured over 80 new client wins in FY26, including 19 in Q4 FY26. Investments from the rights issue continue to be deployed for working capital and strategic growth initiatives. Progress is being made on the e-Mudhra and RailTel legal matters.

## Business Strategy & Drivers

*   **Focus on Core Digital Services:** Continued emphasis on AAA and Infrastructure Services (IS), with AAA being the primary revenue driver. AI is being embedded horizontally across the organization.
*   **US Market Leadership:** The US is now the largest market, with revenue growth indicating a strong focus.
*   **Client Acquisition & Retention:** Onboarding over 80 new clients in FY26 demonstrates successful market penetration and continued client confidence.
*   **Strategic Repositioning of BPS:** BPS is being repositioned from an India-focused BFSI model to a digital-first, AI-led transformation business targeting new geographies and sectors.
*   **Investment in Capabilities:** Development of Centers of Excellence (CoEs) and enhanced capabilities in AI, automation, analytics, cloud, and cybersecurity are key to building integrated, scalable solutions.
*   **Outcome-Based Partnerships:** Continued emphasis on outcome-based pricing, SLA achievement, and delivering demonstrable business value.
*   **Legal Matters Progress:** eMudhra and RailTel matters are progressing, with statements recorded in the eMudhra case and the arbitration panel constituted for RailTel.
*   **FY27 Strategy Pillars:** Performance, People, Productivity, Platform, Partnering, and Profitability are the key pillars for the upcoming fiscal year, with a focus on accelerating revenue growth while maintaining margin discipline.

## Management Accountability & Consistency Audit

### Audit Against Q3 FY26 Historical Context:

*   **Revenue Performance:** Q4 FY26 revenue of ₹175.7 crore represents a sequential increase of 2.1% from Q3 FY26, reversing the slight sequential decline observed in the prior quarter. This indicates a return to positive sequential revenue growth. Full-year FY26 revenue of ₹693.3 crore shows a decrease compared to FY25's ₹725.8 crore, which is attributed to the closure of the KSA branch (₹30 crore in FY25), as noted in the Q3 FY26 context. Management reiterated that the YoY decrease in revenue is due to the closure of the KSA branch.
*   **Profitability:** Consolidated PAT for Q4 FY26 was ₹7.3 crore, a significant improvement from Q3 FY26's ₹2.1 crore. The full-year FY26 PAT of ₹35.1 crore shows a 38.5% YoY increase, aligning with the management's emphasis on profitable growth. The EBITDA margin for FY26 improved to 10.4% from 6.5% in FY25, demonstrating enhanced operational efficiency and cost control, consistent with the stated focus on profitability. The Q4 FY26 EBITDA margin of 6.9% is slightly lower sequentially than Q3 FY26's 7% but aligns with the company's performance in the previous year.
*   **Geographic Focus:** The US has indeed solidified its position as the largest market (49% of revenue) with strong YoY growth, confirming the strategic importance and success in this region, as highlighted in the previous context. India's revenue decline is noted, but its contribution remains significant.
*   **BPS Strategy:** Management continues to acknowledge that BPS is "under stress" and is actively pursuing strategic repositioning, aligning with the narrative of adapting BPS to regulatory and market changes.
*   **Legal Matters:** The update on the eMudhra and RailTel matters indicates ongoing progress, with the arbitration panel constituted for RailTel and statements recorded for eMudhra, demonstrating continued efforts to resolve these legacy issues.
