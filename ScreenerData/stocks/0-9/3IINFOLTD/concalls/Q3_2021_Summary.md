## Executive Overview

3i Infotech Limited, in its Q3 2021 investor call, presented a narrative of strategic transformation and a pivot towards a "Run, Grow, Build" organizational structure. The company emphasized its debt-free status following a CDR and product division divestiture, now focusing on funding growth and new initiatives through its "Run" operations. Key strategic wins highlighted include a significant deal with Bamboo Rose in the US and a strategic partnership with MDEC Malaysia, targeting the ASEAN mid-market and SMB segments. The company is actively incubating next-generation services through its resident entrepreneur program and by acquiring stakes in innovative startups in SD-WAN, SASE, and blockchain technologies. While revenue trajectory is showing signs of improvement with an expected 10-11% growth for the fiscal year, the focus is shifting towards bottom-line performance and EBITDA, with a clear strategy to exit non-profitable legacy accounts and projects. Investments in "Grow" and "Build" initiatives are ongoing, with a stated intention to segregate these operations financially in the next fiscal year.

## Key Financial Metrics

*   **Revenue:** Rs. 165 Crores for the quarter.
*   **Profit Before Tax (PBT):** Down 22.6% due to a one-time charge of Rs. 9.6 Crores related to FCCB early redemption.
*   **Investments in Grow & Build:** Approximately Rs. 22 Crores in total, with Rs. 5.1 Crores in next-gen technologies ("Build") and Rs. 9 Crores in grow infrastructure and competency centers ("Grow").
*   **Gross Margin (GM):** Targeting 18% GM for country-to-country business and 30-35% GM for digital/offshore mix. Run businesses currently at 14-15% GM.
*   **DSO:** Improved by 10 days, showing positive cash flow impact.
*   **Provisions for Slow-Moving Debts:** Rs. 14 Crores proactively booked in the previous quarter.

## Business Strategy & Drivers

*   **Run, Grow, Build Framework:**
    *   **Run:** Focus on fortifying existing services, improving margins, and funding "Grow" and "Build."
    *   **Grow:** Engine of growth through new service lines, consultative transformation-led sales, and building next-generation capabilities.
    *   **Build:** Investing in new products, technologies, and incubating startups to create future differentiators.
*   **Strategic Alliances & Partnerships:** Alliance with Oracle for ASEAN region and building sovereign cloud in Malaysia through a three-way partnership with MDEC.
*   **New Business Initiatives:**
    *   **Resident Entrepreneur Program:** Onboarding entrepreneurs to build next-generation technologies in cognitive computing and EdTech.
    *   **Startup Incubation:** Investing in SD-WAN/SASE and blockchain-powered video content lifecycle management.
    *   **Cloud-First Strategy:** Focusing on cloud migration and transformation, with a specific emphasis on Oracle cloud.
*   **Customer Strategy:** Ruthless exit from non-profitable legacy accounts to focus on strategic, named accounts with clear margin targets.
*   **Geographic Expansion:** Significant focus on ASEAN (Malaysia partnership), US (Bamboo Rose deal), and leveraging existing customer base in BFSI, manufacturing, retail, e-commerce, and Agritech.
*   **Talent Development:** Building competency centers in Tier 2/3 cities to address talent acquisition challenges.

## Management Accountability & Consistency Audit

**Target Hit/Miss Analysis:**

*   **Revenue Growth:** Management stated they expect to grow at a minimum of 10-11% over last year's services revenue exit, which appears to be on track for fiscal year-end.
*   **Debt-Free Status:** Achieved post-CDR and product division sale.
*   **Profitability of "Run" Business:** Management asserts that the "Run" business is profitable and funds other initiatives.
*   **Exiting Non-Profitable Accounts:** Management confirmed this is a key initiative, leading to some revenue reduction but focusing on margin improvement.
*   **Employee Costs:** A significant concern was raised by an analyst regarding employee costs as a percentage of revenue being exceptionally high (83%) compared to peers. Management acknowledged this as a legacy issue from the previous structure and a key area for improvement with the new "Grow" business model.

**New Forward-Looking Promises, Guidance, Margins, or Timelines:**

    *   **COE (Center of Excellence) within Build:** Expected to be market-ready with a commercial prototype in no more than 2 quarters.
