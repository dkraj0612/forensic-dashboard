## Executive Overview

5paisa Capital Limited (5paisa) reported its Q2 FY22 (quarter ended September 30, 2021) results, showcasing strong customer acquisition and revenue growth despite a fluctuating market. The company achieved a record 3.42 lakh new customer additions, a 23% increase quarter-over-quarter (QoQ), bringing the total client base to 1.97 million. Revenue grew by 13% QoQ to Rs 68.5 crore, driven by increased Average Daily Turnover (ADTO) of Rs 80,878 crore, up 40% QoQ. While customer acquisition costs (CAC) marketing saw a 10% QoQ decrease, overall CAC saw a slight increase due to higher other operational expenses. The company is heavily investing in marketing and technology, with significant expenditure leading to a depressed EBITDA margin of 32% for the quarter, a decline from the prior year and a strategic shift from previous quarters that emphasized profitability. 5paisa is focused on expanding its product bouquet beyond trading, including mutual funds, subscriptions, small cases, digital gold, and P2P lending, and leveraging strategic tie-ups for customer acquisition.

## Business Strategy & Drivers

*   **Customer Acquisition:** Continued focus on aggressive customer acquisition through a DIY (Do-It-Yourself) journey, with 51% of clients acquired this way in Q2FY22. Expansion of client base across Tier 3 & above, younger demographics (78% below 35), and increased female client onboarding (10%).
*   **Product Diversification:** Expanding offerings beyond online trading to include Mutual Funds (64,000+ active SIPs), Subscriptions (8,000+ added in Q2FY22), Small Cases (59,000 orders in Q2FY22), Digital Gold (3,000 transactions in Q2FY22), and P2P Lending (over ₹1.3 crore loan transacted in Q2FY22).
*   **Brand Building & Marketing:** Significant investment in marketing, with advertising and marketing expenses at 3% of total income for Q2FY22 and 41% of total income for the half-year ended September 2021, alongside winning marketing campaign awards.
*   **Technology & Platform:** Continuous enhancement of the digital platform, evidenced by an increased 4.3-star rating on app downloads (8.57 Mn+).
*   **Strategic Tie-ups:** Leveraging existing tie-ups (e.g., Google Pay) and actively pursuing larger associations for customer acquisition.

## Management Accountability & Consistency Audit

**Audit of Stated Targets vs. Actual Performance (Q2 2021 / Current Transcript Q2 FY22):**

*   **Revenue Growth:** Management stated revenue was "Up 13% Q-o-Q" to Rs 685 Mn, aligning with the previous quarter's reported growth of 13%. This indicates consistency in topline performance.
*   **Customer Acquisition:** The transcript explicitly states "Client Acquisition - 3.42 lakh in Q2FY22 (Up 23% Q-o-Q)," matching the historical summary's figures and confirming a strong quarter for customer additions.
*   **ADTO Growth:** The transcript reports ADTO up 40% QoQ, a significant increase and positive indicator for trading volumes. This contrasts with the previous quarter's context of peak margin reporting impacting volumes, suggesting a recovery and strong market participation.
*   **CAC Reduction:** Management highlighted "Consistent Reduction in CAC," with CAC Marketing down 10% QoQ. However, "CAC Other Opex" was up 12% QoQ, leading to a slight net increase in overall CAC when considering all components. This indicates a mixed performance on cost efficiency for acquisition.
*   **Profitability:** The transcript shows a significant shift in profitability focus. For Q2 FY22, EBITDA margin is 32% and PAT margin is 16%. This represents a considerable decline from the previous quarter's context (where PBT was Rs 1.7 Cr, suggesting a higher margin then) and the historically positive profit trend. Management frames this as strategic investment, aligning with the narrative from the previous quarter about increased expenditure.

**New Forward-Looking Promises/Guidance for Next Quarter:**

*   **EBITDA Margins:** Management anticipates EBITDA margins to remain "depressed" for "the next two or three quarters" due to ongoing investments in advertising, marketing, and manpower. Specific margin targets were not provided, but the trend is expected to continue.
*   **Technology Spend:** Technology expenditure is expected to continue for "one or two quarters" and then stabilize, with no further increases anticipated after that period.
*   **New Tie-ups:** Larger association tie-ups are expected to "start showing the impact in the coming quarter, in the running quarter and also in the next quarter."
