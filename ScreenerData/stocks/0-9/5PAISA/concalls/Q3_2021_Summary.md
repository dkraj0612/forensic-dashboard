## Executive Overview

5paisa Capital Limited (5paisa) reported its Q3 FY22 (quarter ended December 31, 2021) results, demonstrating robust customer acquisition and revenue growth amidst market volatility. The company surpassed 2 million customer mark in October, taking just one year to double its initial customer base. Mobile app downloads crossed 10 million with an improved rating. Revenue saw a significant 17% sequential growth and 62% year-on-year growth, driven by an increased Average Daily Turnover (ADTO) to Rs 98,000 crore, up from Rs 80,878 crore in Q2 FY22. The margin funding book also grew by 24% sequentially, reaching an all-time high of Rs 360 crore. The company continues to invest in marketing and technology, with a stated focus on customer acquisition translating into revenue growth.

## Business Strategy & Drivers

*   **Customer Acquisition Focus:** Continued emphasis on acquiring customers with a target to improve acquisition run rate and revenue translation. Investment in branding, customer acquisition, and product/technology remains a priority.
*   **Product Diversification & Cross-selling:** Expansion of product suite with a focus on increasing penetration beyond 1.1 products per customer. Key revenue drivers include subscriptions, mutual funds, and digital gold. Launched "5Paisa Wealth," a discretionary portfolio management service (DPMs) in partnership with Renaissance and Abacus, targeting a mid-segment with a minimum investment of Rs 2.5 Lakhs.
*   **Technology Investment:** Continued investment in technology, with IT costs increasing in Q3 FY22, expected to stabilize in the coming quarters.
*   **Brand Building & Marketing:** Shift in acquisition strategy towards organic and referral-based methods (65-70% of acquisitions) to manage costs and improve customer quality.
*   **Margin Funding Growth:** Expansion of margin funding book driven by increased delivery-based trading and customer acquisition.
*   **Derivatives Shift:** Noted a significant shift in trading activity from the cash segment to derivatives, particularly options, driven by market volatility and increased customer understanding.

## Management Accountability & Consistency Audit

**Audit of Stated Targets vs. Actual Performance (Q3 2021 / Current Transcript Q3 FY22):**

*   **Customer Acquisition:** Management reiterated the focus on customer acquisition and reported 4.05 lakh customers in Q3 FY22, an 18% QoQ increase. This aligns with the consistent improvement in quarterly acquisition run rate stated in the previous call.
*   **Revenue Growth:** The reported 17% QoQ and 62% YoY revenue growth directly supports the stated focus on translating customer acquisition into revenue growth.
*   **Margin Funding Book:** The book grew by 24% QoQ to Rs 360 crore, indicating successful expansion in line with broking activity.
*   **Technology Investment:** IT costs increased to Rs 12 crore for the quarter, as anticipated, with management expecting stabilization. This is consistent with the previous guidance of increased IT spend for one to two quarters.
*   **Cost to Income Ratio:** The cost to income ratio has increased to approximately 59-60%, primarily due to technology investments. Management has set a target to reduce this to around 50% over two to three quarters. This is a strategic shift from previous focus on profitability to growth investment.
*   **Customer Acquisition Cost (CAC):** While CAC was Rs 769 in Q3 FY22, management believes there is still room for a 10-15% reduction. The shift towards organic and referral-based acquisition is a proactive measure to control CAC and improve customer quality.

**New Forward-Looking Promises/Guidance for Next Quarter:**

*   **EBITDA Margins:** Not explicitly discussed in this transcript, but the focus on investment suggests margins may remain under pressure compared to historical highs.
*   **Technology Spend:** Expected to stabilize in the next one to two quarters, with no further increases anticipated.
*   **Cost to Income Ratio:** Target to bring down to approximately 50% over the next two to three quarters (not including customer acquisition costs).
*   **Customer Acquisition Costs:** Management believes there is still room for a 10-15% reduction in CAC.
