## Executive Overview

5paisa Capital's Q4 FY22 corporate presentation highlights continued growth across key metrics, reinforcing its position as a digital-first financial services provider. The company achieved a total client base of 2.73 million by March 2022, driven by strong quarterly acquisitions of 3.59 lakh customers, despite a sequential slowdown. Emphasis remains on attracting "quality accounts" through optimized lead sources and a streamlined onboarding process, contributing to increased derivative segment orders. Investments in digital infrastructure and team expansion continue, reflected in initiatives like Digilocker implementation. Financial performance shows consistent revenue and ADTO growth, alongside an optimized cost structure leading to improved gross margins and positive EBITDA/PAT trends. The company also reported consistent efforts in reducing Customer Acquisition Cost (CAC) through strategic adjustments in marketing and operational expenditure.

## Business Strategy & Drivers

*   **Efficient Customer Acquisition:** Focus on optimizing lead source mix and improving lead quality, coupled with a simple, digital onboarding journey to reduce CAC.
*   **Technology & Digital Enhancement:** Continued increase in digital team size, implementation of Digilocker, and data validation processes to enhance customer experience and operational efficiency.
*   **Diversified Offerings:** Offering a wide bouquet of investment and lending solutions including Online Trading, Digital Gold, Stock SIPs, and 5paisa WEALTH.
*   **Customer Satisfaction:** Emphasis on improving customer satisfaction, as evidenced by earlier stated high NPS scores and internal efforts.
*   **Derivatives Market Focus:** Leveraging derivative segment orders to drive overall transaction volumes.
*   **Cost Optimization:** Strategic reduction in CAC (other opex) despite slight increase in marketing CAC, indicating a focus on overall cost efficiency.

## Management Accountability & Consistency Audit

### Audit of Stated Targets vs. Actual Performance

*   **Customer Acquisition Growth:** **Meets Expectation.** The previous summary noted FY22 acquisition of 1.38 million customers (102% YoY) and a general goal of significant growth. This Q4 FY22 presentation shows a total client base of 2.73 million as of March '22 (+15% QoQ) with 3.59 lakh new clients added in Q4 FY22. While Q4 acquisition saw an 11.22% QoQ decline, the overall client base growth remains strong and consistent with the objective.
*   **Quality of Customers:** **Meets Expectation.** The previous summary highlighted a focus on "quality accounts" and ADTO growth. This presentation reinforces this by mentioning "Optimised Lead Source Mix & better Lead Quality" and that "Derivative segment orders help to increase the numbers of orders," implying engaged and profitable clients.
*   **Revenue Growth:** **Meets Expectation.** The previous summary mentioned 53% YoY top-line growth. This presentation shows Q4 FY22 revenue at INR 883 million, up 10% QoQ, demonstrating continued revenue expansion.
*   **Profitability Improvement:** **Generally Meets Expectation (Q4 specific).** The previous summary noted a guidance of 500% sequential profit growth in Q1 FY23 (vs Q4 FY22). While this Q4 FY22 presentation doesn't project that specific Q1 FY23 figure, it shows positive "EBIDTA & PROFIT GROWTH TREND" charts for Q4 FY22, indicating a foundation for future profitability. The improved gross margin (80%) also suggests better operational efficiency.
*   **Technology Investment Impact:** **Meets Expectation.** The previous summary mentioned increased digital team and app revamp. This presentation confirms "Increase in Digital Team" and "Digi locker Implementations & Data Validations," showcasing ongoing technology investment.
*   **Customer Acquisition Cost (CAC):** **Meets Expectation.** The previous summary stated Q4 FY22 CAC at Rs. 767 and a potential for 5-10% further reduction. This presentation explicitly highlights "Consistent Reduction in CAC" with "CAC Other Opex down 8.8 % Q-o-Q," indicating active management of acquisition costs, even if marketing CAC saw a slight increase.
