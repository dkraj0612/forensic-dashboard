## Executive Overview

5paisa Capital Limited (5paisa) reported strong performance for the fiscal year ending March 31, 2021 (FY21), marking a significant turnaround from the previous year. The company achieved profitability for the first time, reporting a Profit After Tax (PAT) of Rs. 14.67 crores, a stark contrast to the loss of Rs. 8.05 crores in FY20. This profitability was driven by an impressive 80% year-on-year revenue growth, which surged from Rs. 108 crores to Rs. 194.57 crores. The company attributes this success to consistent efforts in reducing customer acquisition costs, improving operational efficiencies, and delivering a seamless trading experience. In FY21, 5paisa nearly tripled its client base to 1.3 million from 5.4 lakhs in FY20, a 150% increase, while marketing and advertising expenses grew by only 36%. This strategic approach led to a reduction in Customer Acquisition Cost (CAC) from Rs. 1,483 in March 2020 to Rs. 897 in March 2021. Looking ahead, with a proposed capital infusion of Rs. 250 crores, 5paisa plans to accelerate its transition from a discount brokerage to a comprehensive digital financial platform, with a focus on product development, customer-facing technology, cross-selling, and profitable new customer acquisition.

## Key Financial Metrics

*   **Profit After Tax (PAT):** Rs. 14.67 Crores (FY21) - **Turnaround from Loss of Rs. 8.05 Crores in FY20**
*   **Revenue:** Rs. 194.57 Crores (FY21) - **+80% YoY** (from Rs. 108 Crores in FY20)
*   **Total Customers:** 1.3 Million (as of March 31, 2021) - **+150% YoY** (from 5.4 Lakhs in FY20)
*   **Customer Acquisition Cost (CAC):** Rs. 897 (as of March 31, 2021) - **Reduced from Rs. 1,483 in March 2020**
*   **Marketing & Advertising Expenses:** +36% (FY21 vs FY20), significantly lower than customer growth.
*   **Client Funding Book:** Rs. 180 Crores (as of March 31, 2021)
*   **Technology Expenses (non-HR):** Rs. 25 Crores (FY21) - **Increase from Rs. 15-16 Crores in FY20**
*   **Interest Income (client money):** ~3% - 3.5%
*   **Charges on client money:** ~1.5%
*   **Margin Funding Interest Charge:** 0.06% per day (~21.9% annualized)

## Business Strategy & Drivers

*   **Transition to Digital Financial Platform:** Accelerating the shift from a discount broker to a comprehensive digital platform, supported by a proposed Rs. 250 crore capital infusion.
*   **Product Investment & Customer-Facing Technology:** Significant focus on enhancing product offerings and investing in technology to improve user experience and acquire customers.
*   **Cross-Selling:** Expanding product suite beyond core broking services, aiming to increase revenue from multiple products per customer. New products include P2P lending, international stocks, and gold. Insurance and other investment products are planned.
*   **Customer Acquisition:** Continued focus on acquiring new customers, with a strategy shifting towards organic and referral-based methods to improve efficiency and reduce CAC.
*   **Cost Efficiency:** Ongoing efforts to reduce CAC and improve operational efficiency, particularly within the sales team.
*   **Profitability Focus:** Achieving full-year profitability in FY21 and aiming for continued profitable growth.

## Management Accountability & Consistency Audit

**Audit of Stated Targets vs. Actual Performance (FY21):**

*   **Profitability:** Management successfully achieved profitability in FY21 with a PAT of Rs. 14.67 crores, fulfilling the objective of turning around the company's financial performance. This contrasts with the loss in the previous year.
*   **Revenue Growth:** The reported 80% YoY revenue growth to Rs. 194.57 crores significantly surpassed the previous year's performance and aligns with the company's growth ambitions.
*   **Customer Acquisition:** Client base grew by 150% to 1.3 million, demonstrating strong execution on customer acquisition targets. This growth was achieved with a proportionally lower increase in marketing expenses.
*   **Customer Acquisition Cost (CAC):** CAC was successfully reduced from Rs. 1,483 to Rs. 897 by March 2021, indicating effective cost management and a shift towards more efficient acquisition channels.
*   **Technology Investment:** Technology expenses increased to Rs. 25 crores in FY21 from Rs. 15-16 crores in FY20, reflecting the stated strategy of investing in product and technology.

**New Forward-Looking Promises/Guidance for Next Quarter/Year:**

*   **Capital Infusion:** A proposed Rs. 250 crore capital infusion is expected to provide a significant impetus for growth.
*   **Strategic Focus:** The capital will be utilized for customer acquisition, product development, technology investment (data analytics, AI), and working capital to support business growth and regulatory requirements.
*   **Cross-Sell Revenue Target:** While a specific timeline for reaching Rs. 100 crores from cross-sales is difficult to pinpoint, management is focused on increasing the revenue share from cross-sales, aiming for more sustainable and recurring income.
*   **New Product Launches:** Insurance and other investment products are planned to be added to the platform. The company will also focus on scaling its peer-to-peer (P2P) lending subsidiary.
*   **Margin Funding:** The margin funding book stood at Rs. 180 crores as of March 31, 2021.
*   **CAC Reduction:** Management believes there is scope for further reducing CAC through organic and referral channels. The payback period for CAC is estimated at 9-10 months.
*   **Revenue per Customer:** Currently estimated at Rs. 1500-2000 per year, with a focus on increasing this metric.
*   **Platform Revamp:** Revamping of the EXE and web platforms, as well as the open ETI channel for algo traders, is expected within the next 15 days to three weeks to improve user experience and market share in derivatives.
*   **P2P Lending:** The P2P lending business is at a nascent stage, focusing on unsecured lending (personal loans or working capital for MSMEs/SMEs) with low ticket sizes. 5paisa operates as an NBFC in this segment.
*   **Customer Definition:** An active customer is defined as someone who trades at least one trade in a year (NSE definition). Approximately 70-80% of active customers trade in the cash segment, with a significant portion engaging in intraday trading.
*   **Competitive Intensity:** Management acknowledges the intense competition but believes in a market structure that can support 4-5 players growing exponentially. They focus on product quality, customer experience, and profitability over aggressive "money burning" strategies by competitors.
*   **Branding & Visibility:** While customer acquisition will predominantly be through organic, referral, and partnership channels, there will be a component of brand building, requiring some branding expenditure, though specific plans are not yet disclosed.
