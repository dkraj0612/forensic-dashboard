## Executive Overview

360ONE (formerly IIFL Wealth) demonstrated a strong performance in Q1 FY22, marking historical highs in quarterly revenues and Profit After Tax (PAT). The company achieved significant milestones in its transformation towards a recurring revenue model. Assets Under Management (AUM) grew by 14% QoQ to Rs 2.35 lakh crore, with custody assets reaching Rs 8.3 lakh crore. Crucially, Annual Recurring Revenue (ARR) assets crossed the 50% mark of total AUM, standing at Rs 1.17 lakh crore, reflecting a 15% QoQ and over 60% YoY increase. This shift is also evident in the revenue mix, with recurring revenues now constituting 67% of total revenues, up from 40% at the start of the transformation journey. Net flows were robust at over Rs 14,000 crore, with significant contributions from both wealth management (Rs 9,700 crore) and asset management (Rs 4,600 crore). The company declared a special dividend of Rs 35 per share, underscoring its focus on capital efficiency.

## Business Strategy & Drivers

*   **Transformation to Recurring Revenue Model:** Significant progress in shifting from a transactional revenue model to a sustainable recurring revenue-based model. ARR assets now constitute 50% of total AUM, and ARR revenue represents 67% of total revenue. Over 96% of net flows in the last 5 quarters have been ARR in nature.
*   **IIFL-One Growth:** Continued focus on IIFL-One, encompassing advisory and PMS (Discretionary and Non-Discretionary). AUM crossed the Rs 30,000 crore mark. Retentions in IIFL-One improved to 33 bps, with DPMS nearing 50 bps.
*   **Alternate Focused Asset Management:** Strong growth in this segment, with AUM doubling over the last quarter to Rs 44,000 crore. Focus on 5-6 key strategies with potential to scale to $2-3 billion each. Retentions remain strong at approximately 70 bps.
*   **Cost and Capital Efficiency:** Reduced cost to income ratio to 50.4% through focused efforts on tightening spends, balanced by strategic investments in digital and technology. Maintaining a strong focus on driving capital efficiency.
*   **Digital and Technology Investment:** Significant investment in reimagining client and banker journeys and deploying technology for operational efficiencies and integration with external service providers. Exploring digital-first approaches for future growth.
*   **NBFC Business Optimization:** Stabilization of the NBFC book is expected in the Rs 4,000-4,500 crore range, with potential to reach Rs 5,000 crore. Focus on optimizing excess liquidity and reducing cost of borrowings.
*   **Investment Book Efficiency:** Aiming to reduce sponsor and non-sponsor investments in AIFs to 1-1.25% of AUM, leading to a potential reduction in the investment book by Rs 200-250 crore.

## Management Accountability & Consistency Audit

*   **Stated Targets & Narrative Consistency:**
    *   **AUM Growth:** Achieved 14% QoQ growth, significantly exceeding the historical 7.2% (Q3 FY21) and demonstrating strong momentum.
    *   **ARR Asset & Revenue Growth:** Delivered 15% QoQ growth in ARR assets and achieved 67% ARR revenue share, surpassing the stated goal of ~70-75% by end of FY22. The narrative consistently highlights the strategic importance of this shift.
    *   **IIFL One AUM:** Crossed Rs 30,000 crore mark, which is a significant step towards previously implied targets for FY21 (e.g., Rs 26,000-30,000 crore in Q3 FY21).
    *   **Net Flows:** Strong net flows of over Rs 14,000 crore, significantly exceeding previous expectations and demonstrating robust client engagement and market recovery.
    *   **Tangible ROE:** Achieved the target of 20% tangible ROE, a critical milestone mentioned previously.
    *   **Cost to Income Ratio:** Reduced to 50.4%, showing progress towards stated targets of sustaining around 50-51%. The narrative on strategic investment in technology and talent aligns with potential short-term pressure on this ratio, but the trend is positive.
    *   **Dividend Policy:** Declaration of a special dividend of Rs 35, in addition to interim dividends, aligns with the commitment to shareholder value and capital efficiency, maintaining a payout policy of 75-80% of PAT.
    *   **NBFC Loan Book:** The narrative around stabilizing the NBFC book and improving earnings through cost of borrowing reduction remains consistent with prior discussions.
    *   **Employee Costs:** Management acknowledges elevated employee costs due to RM compensation for the transition to advisory, expecting them to normalize to 32-35% of revenues in FY23/FY24, as guided.
    *   **Fintech Disruption:** Management's nuanced view on Fintech disruption, distinguishing between different client segments and emphasizing the "phygital" model and platform dependency for RMs, remains consistent and well-articulated.

*   **New Forward-Looking Promises/Guidance:**
    *   **NBFC Book Stabilization:** Expected to stabilize in the range of Rs 4,000-4,500 crore over the next 6-9 months, potentially reaching Rs 5,000 crore.
    *   **Investment Book Reduction:** Expects a reduction of Rs 200-250 crore over the next 6-9 months.
    *   **Employee Cost Ratio:** Aims for employee costs to stabilize at 32-35% of revenues post FY22-23, down from the current 38-40% level.

    *   **Senior Bankers:** Aim to add 10-15 senior bankers through the year to meet net flow targets. Current count around 70 senior bankers.
