## Executive Overview

360ONE WAM (formerly IIFL Wealth) presented a solid Q2 FY24 performance, marked by a 30.7% year-over-year increase in Total ARR AUM to ₹2,02,536 Crs. The company reported strong net inflows of ₹5,743 Crs for the quarter and ₹19,686 Crs for H1 FY24, contributing to robust revenue growth. Recurring revenues grew by 9.6% YoY to ₹311 Crs in Q2 and 8.3% YoY to ₹635 Crs in H1 FY24, constituting 76% of overall operating revenue. Profitability remained strong, with PAT at ₹185 Crs for Q2 FY24 (up 7.3% YoY) and ₹367 Crs for H1 FY24 (up 10.2% YoY). The company declared an interim dividend of ₹4 per share, bringing the total interim dividend to ₹12 per share for the fiscal year. Key strategic initiatives include expansion into the HNI segment and global markets, alongside a focus on product innovation in asset management. Management expressed confidence in the India growth story and the company's platform.

## Business Strategy & Drivers

*   **Focus on ARR Growth:** Continued emphasis on increasing ARR AUM and Recurring Revenues as a core strategy.
*   **Wealth Management Enhancement:**
    *   **HNI Segment:** Developing a digital-first HNI proposition with a 100+ CUG pilot underway. Industry veteran Satheesh Krishnamurthy appointed to head this business, with a market launch targeted for the end of FY24.
    *   **Global Indian Segment:** Building a Wealth and Asset platform for the growing "Global Indian" client segment, leveraging existing diaspora connections. Vikram Malhotra appointed to lead this initiative.
    *   **Geographical Expansion:** Hiring senior talent for wealth management across different locations to strengthen domestic reach beyond Tier I cities.
*   **Asset Management Strategy:**
    *   **Diversified Strategies:** Utilizing diverse strategies across listed, unlisted, credit, RE & Infra for resilience in volatile market cycles.
    *   **Channel Presence:** Deepening channel presence in the domestic market, especially through Mutual Fund Distributors (MFDs).
    *   **Global Institutions:** Increased traction from global institutions across various strategies.
    *   **Planned Distributions:** Managed significant planned distributions/redemptions (~₹3.5k Crs) in the quarter, while seeing strong flows in listed equities and new PE funds.
*   **Talent Acquisition & Retention:** Attracting strong talent, with over two-thirds of middle & senior leadership having a vintage exceeding 5 years and low overall attrition rates.
*   **Product Shelf:** Enhancing the innovative, comprehensive wealth and alternates-focused product shelf.

## Management Accountability & Consistency Audit

*   **Stated Targets & Narrative Consistency:**
    *   **AUM Growth:** The reported Total ARR AUM of ₹2,02,536 Crs (up 30.7% YoY) and H1 FY24 flows of ₹19,686 Crs align with the company's growth trajectory. Management expressed comfort with their full-year PAT guidance of ₹800 Crs, acknowledging an additional ₹30-40 Crs in costs related to new initiatives, which they aim to offset through transaction revenue.
    *   **ARR Revenue Focus:** Recurring Revenues increased by 9.6% YoY and constituted 76% of operating revenue, demonstrating continued adherence to the ARR strategy.
    *   **Cost Management:** Operating costs rose by 19.5% YoY to ₹214 Crs, with the Cost-to-Income ratio at 48.6%. Management attributed the elevated costs (3-4% higher than guided) to investments in new initiatives (HNI, Global, geographical expansion) and senior hires, calling it a "temporary dislocation." They expect core business costs to remain stable and aim to bring the overall cost-to-income ratio down to 45% in the medium term.
    *   **Profitability:** PAT for H1 FY24 grew by 10.2% YoY to ₹367 Crs, broadly in line with their guidance. Tangible ROE remained robust at 28.3%.
    *   **Active ARR AUM Disclosure:** Introduced enhanced disclosures on "Active ARR AUM" to explain the retention on non-discretionary advisory propositions, noting a lag effect for new client flows to become regularly fee-bearing. A firm-wide initiative is underway to convert these mandates over the next 6-9 months.
    *   **Private Equity/AMC Outflows:** Outflows in AMC segments (Private Equity, Long-Short) were explained as episodic (fund closure) or temporary due to ongoing fund raises, with expectations for inflows in Q4 FY24.

*   **New Forward-Looking Promises/Guidance:**
    *   **Full Year PAT Guidance:** Comfortable with the ₹800 Crs PAT guidance for FY24, despite an expected incremental cost of ₹30-40 Crs, aiming to achieve ₹790-800 Crs.
    *   **Full Year Net Flows Guidance:** Confident in achieving ₹35,000-40,000 Crs for FY24, with potential for a stronger FY25.
    *   **HNI Business Reporting:** Expects to start reporting separately on the HNI business from April 2024 (Q1 FY25).
    *   **Global Business Reporting:** Global business reporting to commence potentially in July 2024, worst case September 2024 (Q2/Q3 FY25).
    *   **Incremental AUM from New Businesses:** Global and geographical expansion initiatives are expected to add 20-30% to the existing AUM base over the next three to four years (on an expanded future base).
    *   **Q4 FY24 Inflows:** Expects private equity outflows seen in Q3 FY24 to return in Q4 FY24.
    *   **Transaction Revenue (TBR) Guidance:** Comfortable with a range of ₹350-450 Crs for the full year FY24, with ₹400+ Crs being a likely outcome.
    *   **Mumbai Angels Launch:** App launch expected by end of November or December, with distribution efforts to follow. New version to launch in December-January.
    *   **Mid-Market Cost-to-Income:** Initial 60-70% C/I ratio for the first couple of years, with a target of eventually reaching 45%.
    *   **Cost-to-Income Ratio Target:** Aim to bring the core business C/I ratio down from 45% to 42-43% and absorb incremental costs to achieve an overall 45% ratio within 12-18 months. Expects to be below 50% from Year 3 onwards for the mid-market business.
    *   **Institutional Mandates:** One mandate partially converted in Q3 FY24; expects at least a couple more mandates to close by end of March FY24.
    *   **Global Platform Development:** Comprehensive advisory global platform expected by September 2025 at the latest.
    *   **Discretionary PMS Momentum:** Positive outlook for next year, expecting significant momentum over the next 3-4 quarters.
    *   **Nondiscretionary Advisory Yield:** Stabilize at 30-35 bps.
    *   **Discretionary PMS Yield:** Stabilize at 50-55 bps.
    *   **Equity Brokerage Share:** Approximately ₹14-15 Crs per quarter.
    *   **NSE/Stock Parking in TBR:** Estimated around ₹30-35 Crs for H1 FY24.
