## Executive Overview

360 ONE WAM (the "Company") reported its Q4 FY25 results, showcasing resilient performance amidst a volatile capital market. The company achieved its highest ever annual Profit After Tax (PAT) of ₹1,015 Crs, an increase of 26.6% YoY. Total ARR Assets Under Management (AUM) grew 23% YoY to ₹2,46,828 Crs, driven by strong net flows of ₹25,974 Crs for the year, a significant jump from FY24's ₹16,136 Crs. Wealth ARR net flows were particularly strong, up 42% YoY to ₹22,334 Crs. The company announced a significant strategic exclusive collaboration with UBS AG, involving business collaboration, a 4.95% stake acquisition by UBS, and the integration of UBS India's wealth management business. The B&K Securities acquisition is progressing towards regulatory approval, and the ET Money acquisition has been formally completed. The company reiterated its focus on client acquisition, particularly in the HNI segment, and expressed confidence in India's long-term growth story.

## Business Strategy & Drivers

*   **UBS Strategic Collaboration:** An exclusive collaboration with UBS AG, involving cross-border wealth management solutions, access to global products, potential asset management and capital market cooperation, a 4.95% stake acquisition by UBS in 360 ONE, and integration of UBS India's wealth management business. This aims to create a unique global wealth management platform.
*   **B&K Securities Acquisition:** Regulatory approvals are underway, with hopes for completion within the quarter. The business continues to perform strongly, with Q4 operating revenues at ₹65-70 Crs and PAT of ~₹25 Crs.
*   **ET Money Integration:** Formal completion of the acquisition in Q4. The business is contributing to AUM and revenue, with efforts to reduce its burn rate and chart a growth map.
*   **Client Segment Focus:** Continued focus on UHNI, HNI, and retail affluents with an advisory mindset. Equity broking and investment banking are identified as key drivers for the Capital Markets segment.
*   **Platform Expansion:** Consolidation across business lines with a focus on retaining leadership in Wealth Management and Alternates, while growing in Global and Capital Markets segments.
*   **Talent & Technology:** Emphasis on innovation, high performance, and investment in platform & technology to sustain growth and scale.

## Management Accountability & Consistency Audit

### Audit of Stated Targets & Narrative Consistency:

*   **AUM Growth:** ARR AUM grew 23% YoY to ₹2,46,828 Crs, broadly in line with expectations for steady growth, though slightly lower than the 30% YoY growth seen in the prior comparable period (Q1 FY26 end from previous summary).
*   **Net Flows:** Full-year net flows of ₹25,974 Crs (Wealth ARR net flows at ₹22,334 Crs) significantly exceeded the prior year's performance and demonstrated strong client acquisition. The target of 12-15% of opening ARR AUM for the full year was not explicitly restated for this reporting period, but the actual flows indicate a strong achievement.
*   **Revenue & Profitability:** ARR Revenues grew 28.2% YoY to ₹1,701 Crs, and the company achieved its highest ever annual PAT of ₹1,015 Crs, up 26.6% YoY. The cost-to-income ratio improved to 45.9% from 48.7% in FY24, validating management's focus on operational efficiency alongside growth.
*   **B&K Integration:** Progress towards regulatory approvals and continued strong business performance align with previous updates.
*   **ET Money Integration:** Formal completion of the acquisition aligns with stated timelines.
*   **UBS Collaboration:** The announcement of a strategic exclusive collaboration, including regulatory steps and business integration plans, demonstrates significant forward movement on a key strategic initiative.
*   **Talent Churn:** Management acknowledged a degree of talent churn, attributing it to employee aspirations and cultural fit, but expressed confidence in attracting new talent and mitigating the impact on client relationships. This is a consistent theme management addresses.
*   **ET Money Burn Rate:** While not explicitly quantified in this transcript, the narrative around ET Money's contribution and integration implies continued focus on its financial performance and growth map.

### New Forward-Looking Promises/Guidance:

*   **FY26 AUM Growth:** Target to grow AUM by 20-25% annually, driven by 12-15% growth in AUM and 8-10% from mark-to-market.
*   **FY26 Revenue Growth:** Target of 16-18% revenue growth, resulting from AUM growth.
*   **FY26 Profit Growth:** Target of 20-25% PAT growth.
*   **ARR Revenue Mix:** Aim to maintain at least 75% of revenue from ARR.
*   **TBR Revenue Growth:** Need for an additional ₹1,000 Crs from TBR over the next 3-5 years to reach revenue targets of ₹5,000-5,500 Crs.
*   **Equity Brokerage Growth:** Significant opportunity to increase equity brokerage's contribution to TBR, aiming for 8-10% of overall revenue from the current ~2.5-3%.
*   **UBS Collaboration Commercials:** Referral fees are expected on both inbound and outbound referrals, with exact commercials to be defined by the Collaboration Committee.
*   **Global Aspirations:** Leveraging the UBS collaboration for global aspirations, with initial focus on spokes in India.
*   **Mid-Market Scale-Up:** Execution focus on feet-on-street with a team of 45-50 relationship managers on the HNI side; initial B2B presentations have shown positive responses.
*   **UBS Collaboration Timeline:** Collaboration agreements expected to be finalized soon, with operational integration targeted for Q3 of the current financial year.
*   **Alternate Asset Management Flows:** Expectation of natural redemption cycles with block redemptions largely over. Funds raised annually will be redeemed annually. Net flow target of ~10-12% of opening AUM (~₹27,000 - 30,000 Crs) overall, with ~35% allocated to Asset Management.
*   **Discretionary PMS Yield:** Expectation to report similar annualized yields as previously, with a focus on profit share mandates.
*   **ESOP Costs:** ESOP costs are expected to remain at similar levels for the next two years, with a gradual reduction thereafter, following the "Golden ESOPs" program.
*   **Transaction Revenue Mix (TBR):** Future mix expected to be broadly one-third equity brokerage, one-third unlisted transactions, and one-third yield-plus transactions (including REITs, InvITs, commercial assets, and debt).
*   **Global Offshore Team:** International offices will continue to function as small offices for Asset Management distribution.
*   **UBS Non-Compete:** Collaboration agreement includes reasonable, commercially defined, and time-limited non-compete clauses on both sides.
