## Executive Overview

360ONE (formerly IIFL Wealth) presented its Q1 FY23 earnings call, highlighting resilience amidst market volatility. While overall AUM saw a sequential decline due to mark-to-market fluctuations, the company reported an 11% YoY increase in total AUM to over ₹314,000 crore. Excluding custody, AUM grew 7% YoY to ₹252,000 crore. A key positive was the strong net flows of ₹6,078 crore, comprising ₹5,000 crore in Wealth Management and nearly ₹1,000 crore in Asset Management. The company achieved its highest ever Operating Profit Before Tax (OPBT) for the quarter, reaching ₹207 crore, up 59% YoY. Profit After Tax (PAT) stood at ₹160 crore, a 35% YoY increase. The strategic focus on recurring revenues continued to pay off, with recurring revenues increasing 33% YoY to ₹255 crore, representing 69% of total revenues. The Cost to Income ratio significantly improved to 45% from 52% in the previous quarter, driven by effective cost optimization. Management expressed confidence in navigating market uncertainties and sustaining growth.

## Business Strategy & Drivers

*   **Resilience through Recurring Revenue:** Continued emphasis on recurring revenue streams, which now constitute 69% of total revenue, providing stability amidst market volatility.
*   **Strong Net Flows:** Demonstrated ability to attract and retain clients, evidenced by robust net flows in both Wealth Management and Asset Management segments.
*   **Cost Optimization:** Successful implementation of cost-saving measures leading to a significant reduction in the Cost to Income ratio.
*   **Platform Expansion (Mumbai Angels Acquisition):** Acquisition of Mumbai Angels to strengthen early-stage venture investment capabilities and support the mid-market segment strategy.
*   **Talent Acquisition:** Key hires in critical roles (Chief Human Resources Officer, Chief Technology Officer) to bolster strategic capabilities.
*   **Mid-Market Segment Focus:** Developing a "phygital" client experience for the ₹5-20 crore and ₹10-25 crore AUM segments.
*   **Asset Allocation Framework:** Utilizing a balanced asset allocation framework and increasing appetite towards debt instruments to reduce portfolio volatility and enhance client experience.

## Management Accountability & Consistency Audit

*   **Stated Targets & Narrative Consistency:**
    *   **AUM Growth:** While overall AUM declined QoQ due to market conditions, the 11% YoY growth in total AUM and 7% YoY growth in overall AUM (excl. custody) aligns with the company's narrative of consistent growth. Management acknowledged AUM is not insulated from mark-to-market fluctuations.
    *   **Net Flows:** The reported ₹6,078 crore in net flows for Q1 FY23 is strong and aligns with management's historical focus on attracting new assets. The full-year guidance of ₹35,000-37,000 crore remains in place.
    *   **Recurring Revenues:** The increase to 69% of total revenues (up from ~55% in Q4 FY22) strongly reinforces the strategic shift towards recurring revenue and shows significant progress.
    *   **Cost to Income Ratio:** The significant improvement to 45% for Q1 FY23 demonstrates successful execution of cost optimization, surpassing previous levels and aligning with stated goals for improved efficiency.
    *   **Profitability:** Achieving the highest OPBT and a strong PAT growth YoY validates the company's performance and profitability enhancement efforts. Tangible ROE remains robust at 26%.
    *   **Employee Costs:** Management reiterated that employee costs are normalizing to the 32-34% of revenue range, addressing concerns from the previous transition phase.
    *   **Acquisition Strategy:** The acquisition of Mumbai Angels aligns with the strategy to build out platform capabilities, particularly for the mid-market segment and early-stage investments, rather than focusing on revenue multiples of the acquired entity.
    *   **Mid-Market Segment Development:** The company is proceeding with its mid-market segment strategy, emphasizing a "phygital" client experience, as previously communicated.

*   **New Forward-Looking Promises/Guidance:**
    *   **FY23 Net Flows Guidance:** The full-year guidance of ₹35,000-37,000-40,000 crore for net flows is reiterated, with the caveat that deep market downturns could impact this.
    *   **Cost to Income Ratio:** Expected to remain in the 44-46% range for the current year. Management believes there is potential for a further 2-3% reduction in the medium to long term through productivity and span of control improvements.
    *   **Employee Costs:** Fixed at approximately 33% of revenue, with total compensation (fixed + variable) expected to remain in the 32-34% range of net revenue.
    *   **Administrative and Other Expenses:** Expected to be around 12% of revenue.
    *   **Brokerage Transaction Income:** Comfortable guidance of ₹75-85 crore per quarter.
    *   **Mumbai Angels Acquisition:** Enterprise value of ₹42 crore plus cash and cash equivalents. Expected to be a platform for early-stage investments and syndicating deals.
    *   **Mid-Market Segment Rollout:** Initial hypothesis for the 10-25 crore segment will focus on light financial planning, asset allocation, and guided distribution, as a distribution platform rather than a brokerage platform.
    *   **Structured Credit Fund:** Currently raising its 4th fund in this strategy, targeting 11-14% yield.
    *   **REITs and InvITs Fund:** Planning a fund focused on REITs and InvITs for steady income and capital appreciation.
    *   **Multi-Asset Bespoke Strategies:** Aiming for ₹650-750 crore in commitments and ₹1000-1500 crore in drawdowns by the end of the year, serving 15-25 families.
    *   **IIFL One Revenue:** Largely influenced by mark-to-market effects (8-11% QoQ impact).
    *   **Relationship Manager Capacity:** Sufficient capacity to grow the client base by 30-40% without a proportional increase in RMs, by improving productivity and span of control.
    *   **Advisory Performance Benchmarking:** Performance is benchmarked against synthetic indices and client-specific investment portfolio guidelines, with communication focused on outperformance relative to these benchmarks.
    *   **Asset Maturing in FY23:** Approximately 40% of assets from which revenue was booked in FY22 is expected to mature in FY23.
