## Executive Overview

360 ONE WAM (the "Company") reported a robust Q1 FY26 performance, characterized by significant growth in ARR AUM and ARR Revenues, driven by strong net flows and strategic acquisitions. The integration of ET MONEY is progressing well, with a notable reduction in its monthly burn rate. The successful consummation of the B&K Securities acquisition has enhanced the Company's broking and transactional platform within its Wealth Management franchise. Furthermore, regulatory approvals for the strategic collaboration with UBS have been received, paving the way for a significant global partnership and integration of UBS India's wealth management business. Management highlighted strong client acquisition, particularly in the HNI segment, and reiterated a positive outlook for India's long-term growth story as a tailwind for the wealth and asset management sector.

## Key Financial Metrics

*   **Total ARR AUM (Q1 FY26 end):** ₹2,87,317 Crs (up 30% YoY)
*   **Net Flows (Q1 FY26):** ₹20,950 Crs
    *   **ARR Net Flows (from B&K acquisition):** ₹18,266 Crs
*   **ARR Revenues (Q1 FY26):** ₹511 Crs (up 35.9% YoY)
*   **ARR Revenues as % of Total Revenue from Operations:** 77%
*   **Total Revenue (Q1 FY26):** ₹725 Crs
*   **Total Costs (Q1 FY26):** ₹351 Crs (up 32.7% YoY)
*   **Cost-to-Income Ratio (Q1 FY26):** 48.4% (improved from 50.7% in Q4 FY25)
*   **PAT (Q1 FY26):** ₹287 Crs (up 18% YoY) - *Highest ever quarterly PAT*
*   **Tangible ROE (Q1 FY26):** 19.6%
*   **ARR Retention (Q1 FY26):** 79 bps overall; 70 bps ex-carry

## Business Strategy & Drivers

*   **Acquisition Integration:** The B&K Securities deal has been successfully consummated, with people and processes integration underway. The ET MONEY business has seen significant progress in reducing its burn rate and has a clear growth map.
*   **Global Collaboration with UBS:** Regulatory approvals are in place for the UBS strategic collaboration, encompassing business collaboration across geographies, UBS AG's stake in 360 ONE, and integration of UBS India's wealth management business. This aims to create a unique global wealth management platform by leveraging UBS's global expertise and research.
*   **HNI & UHNI Focus:** The Company continues to see growth in its HNI business with a strong client base and AUM. Technological developments for this segment are being extended to UHNI clients.
*   **Asset Management Growth:** Focus remains on deepening channel presence (MFDs), with a strong pipeline for new funds and international institutional mandates. The company plans to extend alternative strategies to institutional clients.
*   **Mid-Market & Broking:** The B&K acquisition strengthens the broking and transactional platform within Wealth Management. The company aims to grow its research-based equity brokerage platform for institutional corporates, family offices, and UHNIs by 20-25% annually.
*   **Product Diversification:** Excitement around budget changes positively impacting unlisted and fixed-income products. The co-investment business is also poised for growth.

## Management Accountability & Consistency Audit

### Audit of Stated Targets & Narrative Consistency:

*   **AUM Growth:** ARR AUM grew 30% YoY to ₹2,87,317 Crs, aligning with the narrative of strong structural growth.
*   **Net Flows:** The stated guidance of 12-15% of opening ARR AUM for the full year was reiterated. Q1 saw robust flows, though tempered by team departures affecting Wealth Management net flows by approximately ₹3,500-4,000 Crs. The company maintains confidence in achieving the full-year target.
*   **Revenue & Profitability:** ARR Revenues grew 35.9% YoY to ₹511 Crs. The Company achieved its highest ever quarterly PAT of ₹287 Crs, up 18% YoY. The cost-to-income ratio improved to 48.4% from 50.7% in Q4 FY25, reflecting efforts to manage costs alongside growth.
*   **ET MONEY Burn Rate:** Management reported significant progress in reducing ET MONEY's burn rate from ~₹5-6 Crs per month to ₹6-7 Crs per quarter, validating previous statements on cost rationalization.
*   **B&K Integration:** The deal has been successfully consummated and integration has begun, aligning with previously announced timelines.
*   **UBS Collaboration:** Regulatory approvals are obtained, and integration is being worked on, meeting stated milestones.
*   **Yields:** Management acknowledged a potential 2-3 basis point compression in overall Wealth Management ARR yield due to business mix shift, but expressed confidence in maintaining headline yields across different business lines. The lending book yield is expected to stabilize around 4.8% to 5%.
*   **Attrition:** Management acknowledged a recent increase in attrition impacting AUM but highlighted proactive hiring of new talent and teams to offset these losses and drive future growth.

### New Forward-Looking Promises/Guidance:

*   **Full Year Net Flow Guidance:** 12-15% of opening ARR AUM (expected to be ₹27,000 Crs to ₹34,000-35,000 Crs in total).
*   **Wealth Management Quarterly Run Rate:** Need for approximately ₹5,500 Crs net flows per quarter to achieve full-year targets, factoring in estimated net outflows from team departures.
*   **ET MONEY Monetization:** Growth map and strategy are charted out, with confidence in achieving net positive bottom-line contribution eventually. Burn rate reduced to ₹25 Crs annually.
*   **Transaction Income Growth:** Aim to grow transaction income from ₹550-600 Crs to ₹1,000 Crs over the next 2-3 years, with a target of ₹250 Crs on a quarterly run rate basis.
*   **Transaction Brokerage Income:** Target to maintain transaction brokerage income at around 20% of total income.
*   **Lending Book Yield:** Comfortable with yields around 4.8% to 5% ballpark.
*   **B&K Contribution:** Expected to add 20-25% growth to B&K's revenue and 10-14% to PBT compared to last year. B&K's ARR revenue contribution is estimated at 20-25% of its total revenue.
*   **Institutional Mandate Addition:** Expectation to secure at least one institutional mandate in the financial year.
*   **Lateral Hiring Impact:** Expectation of significant AUM accretion from lateral hiring, with costs manageable and not expected to materially impact cost-to-income ratios unless growth hiring significantly exceeds plans.
*   **UBS ARR AUM:** Anticipated to be sub ₹10,000 Crs. Total ARR AUM addition from B&K, UBS, and organic growth to be between ₹60,000 to ₹65,000 Crs.
*   **UBS Collaboration Timeline:** Collaboration agreements expected to be finalized in the next month or two. Quantifying AUM accretion is too early, but key collaboration pillars are identified (feeding into UBS global products, asset management products on UBS platform, referring Indian clients to UBS global, UBS referring global NRIs with Indian portfolios).
*   **Asset Management Net Flows:** Target of ₹8,000-10,000 Crs annually, driven by new funds and capital drawdowns.
*   **Global Offshore Team Costs:** Costs were present last quarter and will not be there from the current quarter onwards.
