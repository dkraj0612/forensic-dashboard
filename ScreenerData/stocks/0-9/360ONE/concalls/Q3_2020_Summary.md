## Executive Overview

360ONE (formerly IIFL Wealth) delivered a robust performance in Q3 FY22 (Q3 2020 calendar year), maintaining sustained growth and stable operating metrics amidst a resurgence in market confidence. The company reported a significant 7.2% QoQ increase in overall Assets Under Management (AUM) to Rs 2,00,494 crore, crossing the Rs 2 lakh crore mark. Annual Recurring Revenue (ARR) assets surged 14.4% QoQ to Rs 90,500 crore, while IIFL One AUM grew 16.5% QoQ to Rs 25,948 crore, nearing its FY21 target.

Revenues for the quarter rose 13.4% QoQ to Rs 280 crore, with ARR revenue up 10.1% QoQ to Rs 154 crore. Profit Before Tax (PBT) increased 13.3% QoQ to Rs 128 crore, and Profit After Tax (PAT) reached Rs 97 crore. Net new flows for the 9-month period stood at Rs 8,000 crore, with management expressing confidence in meeting revised full-year targets of Rs 11,000-12,000 crore, or potentially higher, driven by improved client sentiment and monetization activities. Client risk appetite returned strongly, leading to improved equity flows and a balanced debt-equity mix. The firm continued its focus on cost and capital efficiency, made strategic investments in technology, and progressed significantly on regulatory-mandated client segregation. An interim dividend of Rs 30 per share was declared, aligning with capital allocation policy.

## Business Strategy & Drivers

*   **Client Sentiment & Risk Appetite:** Strong return of client risk appetite, with flows towards equities improving and overall debt-equity mix returning to near pre-COVID levels. Increased interest in fixed income and alternate investments.
*   **Client Acquisition & Engagement:** Robust new client acquisitions (1,790 families in 9 months) and continuous high engagement with clients (24x7).
*   **Flagship Offering Growth:** IIFL One continues to gain market acceptance with significant AUM growth and strong new flows into Discretionary PMS.
*   **Asset Management Momentum:** Strong growth in Asset Management business with positive net flows driven by fund performance and institutional mandates for alternate strategies.
*   **Regulatory Adaptation:** Active management of business segregation into Advisory, Distribution, Discretionary, and Non-Discretionary segments as per new regulations, aiming for client clarity by March 31, 2021.
*   **Cost & Capital Efficiency:** Continued focus, with strategic investments in technology, risk, and external support leading to some administrative cost increases. Employee variable costs increased due to incentivizing ARR/IIFL One strategies and ESOP front-loading.
*   **Technology Focus:** Significant push on digital transformation across client journey and internal operations to enhance efficiency and enable scalable expansion.
*   **Lending Book Rebound:** Uptick in lending opportunities, driven by good transactions fitting credit matrix and strategic fit with core businesses. Declining cost of funds through new, lower-rate borrowings is expected to enhance competitiveness.
*   **Organizational Strengthening:** Key promotions and new leadership appointments across Wealth and Asset Management to build bandwidth and focus.
*   **Merchant Banking:** Acquired SEBI merchant banking license primarily to generate revenue in the holding company and explore debt syndication deals.

## Management Accountability & Consistency Audit

*   **Stated Targets & Narrative Consistency:**
    *   **AUM Growth:** Achieved strong 7.2% QoQ growth (vs. 5.6% in Q2), primarily driven by mark-to-market. Management maintains that 7-8% growth from net flows is their focus, with mark-to-market as an additive factor.
    *   **ARR Growth:** Delivered impressive 14.4% QoQ growth in ARR assets (vs. 8.2% in Q2) and 10.1% in ARR revenue (vs. 9.7% in Q2). This aligns with the continued strategic focus on recurring revenue streams.
    *   **IIFL One AUM Target:** The Rs 25,948 crore AUM is very close to the lower end of the FY21 target (Rs 26,000-30,000 crore) stated in Q2, indicating consistent progress despite previous minor adjustments to expectations.
    *   **Net Flows:** Acknowledged that 9-month net flows of Rs 8,000 crore were "marginally lower than where would have liked to be" compared to pre-COVID aspirations of Rs 12,000-15,000 crore. However, management expresses high confidence in a strong Q4 to meet their revised FY21 full-year target of Rs 11,000-12,000 crore, potentially reaching Rs 14,000-15,000 crore. This shows a slight adjustment in short-term expectations but continued optimism for year-end.
    *   **Productivity & Cost Optimization:** The cost-to-income ratio remained flat at 54% (same as Q2), not yet showing the "visible impacts" or "push towards 52-53%" for next year as guided in Q2. Management explained this by citing additional, strategic costs for employee variable pay (tied to ARR/IIFL One) and administrative costs (technology, risk, external support). The narrative remains consistent on the *focus*, but the immediate *impact* on the ratio is diluted by these new investments.
    *   **Regulatory Adaptation:** Management provided further clarification on the "advisory business exit" misunderstanding, aligning with their Q2 emphasis on detailed engagement types. They reiterated the ongoing client segregation by March 31st deadline, demonstrating proactive adaptation.
    *   **Dividend Payout Policy:** The declaration of an interim dividend of Rs 30 per share is consistent with the stated Q2 commitment of a 75-80% profit payout policy (Q3 states 60-80% of annual PAT).
    *   **Lending Book NIM Improvement:** Consistent with Q2 guidance, management confirmed declining cost of funds and incremental new borrowing at lower rates, expecting blended cost reduction by March-April. This confirms the previously anticipated benefits.
    *   **Third-Party Product Recycling:** Management noted negative net flows in TBR AUM, stating it is "as per the expectation of a gradual shift of TBR assets to ARR earning assets," consistent with Q2’s detailed guidance on recycling these assets.
    *   **Office Space Reduction:** No direct update was provided regarding the Q2 expectation of rental cost reductions in 3-6 months. The increase in general "admin costs" makes it unclear if these specific savings materialized or were offset.

*   **New Forward-Looking Promises/Guidance:**
    *   **Full Year Net Flows (FY21):** Expects to end FY21 with net flows of Rs 11,000-12,000 crore, with potential to reach Rs 14,000-15,000 crore.
    *   **Discretionary PMS Retentions:** Expects retentions to return to over 50 bps "shortly" (after being lower in Q3).
    *   **Cost Structure:** Fixed administrative costs expected to remain around Rs 40 crore per quarter. Variable employee cost to be in the 18-22% range of fixed costs in "normal years" (excluding ESOP front-loading which caused a 35-36% ratio this year).
    *   **Yields:** Projects a 2 basis points lower overall yield for next year due to an assumption of softer capital market activity impacting transactional (TBR) revenue. ARR yields are expected to stabilize at current levels. Other income is also expected to be lower next year due to a one-time claw back in the current year.
    *   **Lending Book Growth:** Expects a 10-15% increase in the loan book over the next quarter, and a 15-20% increase from current levels over a longer term, with the structural average loan book expected to be around 3% of AUA (including sponsor investments).
    *   **Investment Book:** The remaining Rs 700 crore from the G-SEC hedge product is expected to mature and reduce the investment book by end of calendar year/March next year.
    *   **Regulatory Segregation:** Confident that 60-70% of large clients will have clarity on their chosen engagement type (Advisory, Distribution, Discretionary, Non-Discretionary) by the end of the financial year.
    *   **Revenue & PAT Run Rate:** By the end of Q4 FY21, expects to achieve a revenue and PAT run rate equivalent to the levels from before the business model restructuring began in 2018.
    *   **Technology Investment Focus:** Initial technology investments will primarily target internal processes and interfaces to enable digital expansion without a significant load on administrative expenses, rather than client-facing aspects.
