## Executive Overview

360ONE WAM (formerly IIFL Wealth) reported a strong Q1 FY24, demonstrating resilience and growth despite prevailing market uncertainties. The company saw significant year-over-year growth in Assets Under Management (AUM), particularly in Recurring Revenue Assets (ARR), driven by robust net inflows. Profitability improved, supported by steady retentions and effective cost management. Key strategic initiatives, including the ongoing rebranding to 360ONE and the integration of acquisitions, underscore a continued focus on platform expansion and an enhanced client proposition. Management expressed confidence in navigating the economic environment and outlined ambitious plans for future growth, including geographical expansion and a focused approach to the mid-market segment.

## Key Financial Metrics

*   **Total ARR AUM:** ₹191,390 Crs (up 33.3% YoY)
    *   Wealth ARR AUM: ₹1,26,285 Crs (up 42% YoY)
    *   AMC ARR AUM: ₹64,105 Crs (up 20% YoY)
*   **ARR Net Flows (Q1 FY24):** ₹12,975 Crs
*   **Recurring Revenues (Q1 FY24):** ₹323 Crs (up 7.1% YoY, up 15.5% QoQ)
    *   Share of Recurring Revenues in Operating Revenue: 80% (includes ₹40 Crs accrual of carry income)
*   **Total Revenues (Q1 FY24):** ₹434 Crs (up 17.6% YoY, up 12.7% QoQ)
*   **Retentions on ARR Assets (Q1 FY24):** 73 bps (Wealth: 70 bps, Asset Management: 80 bps)
*   **Operating Costs (Q1 FY24):** ₹210 Crs (up 25% YoY)
*   **Cost to Income Ratio (Q1 FY24):** 48.4% (vs. 48% in Q4 FY23)
*   **PAT (Q1 FY24):** ₹181 Crs (up 13.4% YoY, up 16.9% QoQ)
*   **Tangible ROE (FY24 YTD):** 28.6%

## Business Strategy & Drivers

*   **Focus on ARR:** Continued emphasis on growing ARR AUM and revenues, with a strategic shift in accounting for carry income within recurring revenues.
*   **Wealth Management Expansion:**
    *   **UHNI Segment:** Deepening relationships, increasing wallet share, and selective offshore expansion.
    *   **HNI/Mid-Market Segment:** Developing a proposition for the ₹5-50 Crs segment, targeting a market of 160-170K households, with a go-to-market strategy planned for end of Q3/beginning of Q4 FY24.
*   **Asset Management Growth:** Leadership in Alternates (AIF & PMS) with a focus on Private Equity, Private Credit, Infra, and RE. Exploring differentiated Mutual Fund offerings and building distribution reach.
*   **Geographical Expansion:** Domestic expansion to ~40 cities and international presence in Dubai and Singapore are key focus areas.
*   **Digital Transformation:** Continued investment in technology to enhance client experience and operational efficiency.
*   **Product Innovation:** Pipeline includes credit funds, real assets funds, and global dollar bond funds for the Asset Management arm.

## Management Accountability & Consistency Audit

*   **Stated Targets & Narrative Consistency:**
    *   **AUM Growth & ARR Focus:** Management reiterated their commitment to ARR growth. The reported ARR AUM of ₹191,390 Crs (up 33.3% YoY) and net flows of ₹12,975 Crs align with this narrative. The 80% share of recurring revenues in operating revenue, including the accrual of carry income, further solidifies this focus.
    *   **Cost Management:** While operating costs rose 25% YoY, the management explained this was due to strengthening the sales team and higher marketing expenses, including one-off events. They expect the Cost to Income ratio to moderate, aligning with historical guidance trends.
    *   **Profitability:** YoY PAT growth of 13.4% and a strong Tangible ROE of 28.6% demonstrate continued operational efficiency and the success of their recurring revenue strategy.
    *   **Rebranding:** The rebranding to 360ONE is in progress.
    *   **Acquisitions:** The Mumbai Angels acquisition was completed in the previous period and is integrated into the platform strategy.

*   **New Forward-Looking Promises/Guidance:**
    *   **FY24 Cost to Income Ratio Guidance:** Expected to be in the region of 43.5%-45%.
    *   **Mid-Market Offering Launch:** Targeted for end of Q3 / beginning of Q4 FY24.
    *   **Geographical Expansion:** Building out sales teams in Singapore and Dubai.
    *   **Private Equity Flows:** Expected to pick up in Q3 FY24 as they prepare to launch a new fund after investing ~60-70% of current capital.
    *   **Listed Equity Flows:** Expected to see significant improvement from Q2 FY24 onwards.
    *   **Institutional Mandates (Listed Equity):** Expect at least a couple to come through in the next 9 months.
    *   **Transactional Revenue:** Aim to reduce this bucket to the minimum possible, targeting around ₹200 Crs annually, shifting the rest to long-term income over 12-24 months.
    *   **Other Income Guidance:** Modeled at ₹30-40 Crs for the full year, but could reach ₹70-80 Crs if market levels remain stable.
    *   **Steady State Yields:**
        *   Discretionary PMS: 50-55 bps.
        *   Non-discretionary PMS/RIA: 35-40 bps.
    *   **Employee Costs:** Expected to be around 30%-33% of revenue.
    *   **Other Expenses:** Expected to be around 11%-12% of revenue.
    *   **Total Cost to Income:** Approximately 44.5%-45%.
    *   **Salesforce Expansion:** Focus on Senior Private Bankers, Relationship Managers (including Junior Bankers), and Asset Management sales teams.
    *   **Mid-Market Segment:** Potential to capture 7%-10% market share over the next 3-5 years.
    *   **Product Pipeline:** Well-set for the year with ongoing innovation in Alternates, Mutual Funds (Flexi cap fund launched), and Institutional Mandates.
    *   **New Team/Rollout Costs:** Employee cost increase delta is broadly split between salary increments and new team costs (approx. ₹10 Crs for new teams).
    *   **Admin Costs:** Expected to be around ₹210-213 Crs for FY24, a slight increase from previous guidance.
    *   **Cash Balance/AIF Investments:** No immediate plans for significant acquisitions; focus on acquiring teams rather than businesses. Investments planned for Singapore and Dubai hubs.
    *   **ARR vs. TBR Flows:** Less than 20% of Q1 ARR inflows were a shift from TBR; 80-85% were net new money.
    *   **Yield on RIA vs. NDPMS:** While current yields appear different due to onboarding timing, steady state yields are expected to converge. The company prefers onboarding clients onto the RIA license medium to long-term.
