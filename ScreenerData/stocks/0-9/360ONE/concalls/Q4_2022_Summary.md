## Executive Overview

360ONE (formerly IIFL Wealth) reported strong Q4 FY22 results, demonstrating continued growth across key financial and operational metrics. Assets Under Management (AUM) grew to over ₹327,000 crore, a 33% increase YoY. Excluding custody, overall AUM rose 26% YoY to ₹261,000 crore. Recurring Revenue Assets (ARR Assets) saw robust 42% YoY growth to ₹144,000 crore, now representing approximately 55% of total AUM, reinforcing the company's strategic shift towards recurring revenue models. Total revenues increased 57% YoY to ₹449 crore, with recurring revenues up 57% YoY to ₹252 crore. The company achieved its highest ever quarterly Profit After Tax (PAT) of ₹168 crore, a 64% YoY increase, and reported a strong Tangible Return on Equity (ROE) of 28% for the quarter. Management expressed confidence in sustained growth, improved operating efficiencies, and further profitability enhancement in FY23.

## Business Strategy & Drivers

*   **Growth in Wealth Creation:** Capitalizing on the ongoing ultra-HNI and HNI wealth creation cycle in India, with a focus on capturing new wealth generated, particularly in Tier 2 and Tier 3 cities. Leveraging digital capabilities to extend the wealth management proposition to newer client segments.
*   **Alternate Assets Expansion:** Identifying significant growth potential in the nascent Indian alternate assets market, mirroring global trends where alternate assets attract a substantial share of asset management revenues.
*   **Resilience through Recurring Revenue:** Successfully transitioning a large portion of AUM and revenue to the recurring fee model, nearing a steady-state model. Maintaining a balanced AUM mix across equity (54%) and debt (46%) for wealth clients provides market resilience.
*   **Client and Employee Retention:** High client retentions (42% with the company for over five years) and strong senior relationship manager continuity (78% with the company for over five years) are key to client retention and productivity.
*   **Agility and Product Innovation:** Continuously innovating product offerings and structuring solutions for clients across various market cycles. Enhancing digital and technology platforms for seamless transactions and improved organizational productivity.
*   **Mid-Market Expansion:** Targeting the ₹5-20 crore AUM segment in Tier 2/3 cities and the ₹10-25 crore segment in top cities, focusing on building a "phygital" (physical + digital) client experience.

## Management Accountability & Consistency Audit

*   **Stated Targets & Narrative Consistency:**
    *   **AUM Growth:** The reported 33% YoY growth in Total AUM and 26% YoY growth in Overall AUM (excl. custody) aligns with the company's narrative of consistent growth.
    *   **ARR Assets:** The increase to 55% of Total AUM is consistent with the strategic focus on recurring revenue and shows progress towards stated targets.
    *   **Net Flows:** FY22 net flows of ₹31,400 crore have met and exceeded previous guidance, indicating strong execution.
    *   **Revenue Growth:** 57% YoY growth in total revenues and 56% YoY growth in recurring revenues for FY22 demonstrate strong performance against the backdrop of market volatility.
    *   **Cost to Income Ratio:** The FY22 CTI of 51% is in line with expectations and management's stated goal of improving operating efficiencies.
    *   **PAT & Tangible ROE:** Highest ever quarterly PAT and a Tangible ROE of 28% for the quarter and 25% for the year underscore strong profitability and capital efficiency. Management maintained their confident tone on future profitability improvements.
    *   **Employee Costs Guidance:** Management clarified that employee costs are normalizing to 32-34% of revenue from FY23 onwards, as a one-time incremental cost related to the transition from upfront to trail revenue booking has ceased from April 2022. This addresses previous concerns about higher employee costs.

*   **New Forward-Looking Promises/Guidance:**
    *   **FY23 Cost to Income Ratio:** Expected to improve to approximately 45%.
    *   **FY23 Employee Costs:** Expected to stabilize at 32-33% of revenue.
    *   **FY23 Other Costs (Technology, etc.):** Expected at around 12% of revenue.
    *   **Profitability Growth:** Expected to show healthy 20%+ YoY growth.
    *   **Tangible ROE:** Expected to improve to over 25% in FY23 and trend towards 30% in FY24.
    *   **IIFL One Growth:** Expected to contribute approximately 50% of the growth in ARR Wealth, targeting ₹20,000-22,000 crore increase.
    *   **Carry Component:** Approximately $10 million (₹50-75 crore) factored in for both FY23 and FY24.
    *   **Mid-Market Segment Approach:** Developing a "phygital" client experience for the ₹5-20 crore (out of top cities) and ₹10-25 crore (in top cities) segments, targeting a cost-to-serve ratio of 45-50 clients per RM. Ready for market launch in Q1 CY23 or Q1 FY23.
    *   **Net Flows Run Rate:** Minimum quarterly net flows expected between ₹3,500-4,000 crore, and maximum around ₹14,000-15,000 crore.
    *   **Accenture Platform Development:** Moving into the implementation phase with a budget of ₹5-7 crore per quarter for the next 12-18 months.
    *   **Citi Wealth Clients:** Management expressed interest in attracting talent from Citi's wealth business and potentially expanding into that market base, complementing their existing platform.
    *   **Managed Accounts (PMS/AIF distribution):** Expects substantial growth in recurring revenue from managed accounts as the business model shifts to trail fees, mirroring the growth trajectory of other business segments.
