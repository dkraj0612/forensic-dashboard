## Executive Overview

360ONE (formerly IIFL Wealth) held its Q3 FY23 earnings call, reporting a resilient performance amidst continued market volatility and macroeconomic uncertainty. The company showcased robust growth in Assets Under Management (AUM), particularly in Recurring Revenue Assets (ARR), driven by strong net flows. Profitability improved YoY and QoQ, supported by steady retentions and effective cost management. Key strategic initiatives, including rebranding to 360ONE and the completion of the Mumbai Angels acquisition, were highlighted, signaling a focus on enhanced client proposition and platform expansion. Management expressed confidence in navigating the ongoing challenging environment.

## Business Strategy & Drivers

*   **Rebranding to 360ONE:** Aims to reinforce the company's approach to advice and service, symbolizing a holistic view of the client's interests ("360" for holistic view, "ONE" for client's interests first). This marks the third chapter of growth.
*   **Mumbai Angels Acquisition:** Completion of acquisition (91% stake) strengthens early-stage venture investment capabilities with a Cat 1 AIF license. Two new funds launched: an angel fund (₹1000 Cr target) and a VC fund (₹300 Cr target).
*   **Truescale Capital Partnership:** Funds managed and sponsorships by Truescale will transfer to IIFL AMC, strengthening its Private Equity track record. Sameer Nath joins as CIO and Head for PE strategy.
*   **Mid-market Segment Focus:** Continued development of a digital-first proposition for clients with ₹5-25 Cr AUM, targeting an April-May launch of strategies. This segment is seen as product-driven, requiring innovation and value-added services.
*   **Technology Investment:** Ongoing investment in a comprehensive data platform for Wealth and Asset businesses, reimagining banker journeys, and enhancing cybersecurity.
*   **IIFL ONE Platform:** Critical for new flows, expecting 60-70% of net new acquisitions to come through this umbrella, evolving from 10:90 to a 60:40 split in favor of IIFL ONE. Focus on a consolidated platform with holistic advice and execution.
*   **ARR Focus:** Continued emphasis on growing ARR Assets and ARR Net Flows to drive stable and predictable revenue streams.

## Management Accountability & Consistency Audit

*   **Stated Targets & Narrative Consistency:**
    *   **AUM Growth:** Management reported strong YoY and QoQ growth in Total AUM and Overall AUM (excl. custody), aligning with the narrative of consistent growth. They acknowledge the impact of market volatility on AUM but highlight resilience.
    *   **Net Flows:** Robust ARR Net Flows of ₹10,386 crore for the quarter and ₹22,000 crore for 9MFY23 are tracking in line with guidance. The shift of some TBR assets to ARR is noted as a part of the strategy.
    *   **Recurring Revenues:** Increased by 12% YoY to ₹276 crore, comprising 66% of operating revenue, demonstrating continued progress towards increasing the recurring revenue pie.
    *   **Cost to Income Ratio:** Maintained at 45.5%, within the guided range of 44-45%, showing effective cost control.
    *   **Profitability:** PAT grew 16% YoY to ₹180 crore, with Tangible ROE remaining strong at 28.6%, validating operational efficiency and core business performance.
    *   **Rebranding:** The name change to 360ONE is being implemented, with pending regulatory approvals for some entities. Management views it as a strategic step for clarity, identity, and the next growth phase.
    *   **Mumbai Angels Acquisition:** Management confirmed the acquisition is complete and is a strategic move to enhance platform capabilities.
    *   **Mid-Market Segment:** Development is on track, with strategies expected by April-May. The definition of mid-market (₹5-25 Cr AUM) is distinct from traditional definitions.

*   **New Forward-Looking Promises/Guidance:**
    *   **FY24 Net Flows Guidance:** Aiming for ₹35,000-40,000 crore in net flows specifically on the ARR side.
    *   **Cost to Income Ratio:** Expected to remain within the 44-45% range for the current year.
    *   **Employee Expenses:** Expected to remain approximately 32-34% of revenue, with potential for 4-6% productivity gains, partially offset by new initiatives.
    *   **Administrative & Other Expenses:** Expected to be around 12% of revenue.
    *   **Transactional Revenues (TBR):** Management expects this to moderate to ₹100-125 crore per quarter, potentially varying by 10-20% due to a shift towards fixed-income denominated products.
    *   **IIFL ONE Platform:** Expected to contribute 60-70% of net new acquisitions and 40-50% of net new flows. The target is for 75-80% of new acquisitions to come through IIFL ONE in the next five years.
    *   **IIFL ONE Fee Structure:** Aiming for 40-50 bps retentions on the overall IIFL ONE platform, excluding corporate treasury.
    *   **Carry Income:** Expected to be in the range of ₹70-75 crore to ₹110 crore per year, with predictability for the next 12-18 months.
    *   **Mid-market Strategy Rollout:** Detailed strategy disclosures expected around April-May. The focus is on clients with ₹5-25 Cr AUM, differentiating from banks by offering specialized wealth management solutions.
    *   **City Expansion:** Plan to expand presence to 38-40 cities, targeting an additional 14-15 cities in the next 3-6 months.
    *   **Dividend vs. Buyback:** Prefers the dividend route for capital allocation due to predictability and ease of execution, considering buybacks as an episodic activity.
    *   **Synergies between AMC and IIFL ONE:** Complete Chinese walls are maintained, with separate development and distinct skill sets, though the depth of research and analysis required is similar.
    *   **Fee Curve for IIFL ONE:** No dramatic changes in percentages (35-50 bps) but challenges arise with fee capping for larger clients (north of ₹10 lakh per month or ₹1-2 crore annually) on the non-discretionary side.
    *   **TBR to ARR Transition:** Expects 50-60% of non-earning TBR assets (₹4,000-6,000 crore) to move into ARR.
    *   **Rebranding Impact:** Primarily qualitative, aiming for a distinct brand identity, which is expected to translate into better client and employee retention and, eventually, improved quantitative results.
    *   **FY24 Guidance:** Broadly in line with current articulations, with detailed guidance expected at the end of the current quarter after board approval.
    *   **Cost Savings & Expenses:** Cost savings are factored in, with confidence in employee expense management. Technology spend is the main variable, with operating leverage expected to be high on other expenses. Potential for 4-6% productivity gains in employee expenses, partially offset by new initiatives.
    *   **Mid-market Unit Economics:** Expected to be platform-intensive rather than people-intensive, requiring continuous platform investment and value-added services, contrasting with the more people-intensive legacy business.
    *   **Inorganic Opportunities:** Currently not evaluating large inorganic opportunities, focusing on organic growth within asset management and wealth management.
