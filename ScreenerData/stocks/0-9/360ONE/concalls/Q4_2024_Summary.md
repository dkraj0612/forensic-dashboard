## Executive Overview

360ONE WAM delivered a strong close to FY24, reporting its highest ever quarterly and full-year PAT, underscoring robust growth in Assets Under Management (AUM) and revenues. Total Recurring Revenue (ARR) AUM expanded significantly by 36.3% YoY to ₹2,27,879 Crs, propelled by strategic net flows, with active ARR AUM reaching ₹1,99,606 Crs. Recurring Revenues for FY24 grew 13.6% YoY to ₹1,331 Crs, maintaining a substantial 72% share of operating revenue, despite a notable uplift in transactional income in Q4. Full-year PAT climbed to ₹802 Crs, with Tangible Return on Equity (ROE) strengthening to 30.1%.

The company continued its strategic investments in expanding into the High-Net-Worth (HNI) and global Indian segments, alongside geographical deepening for its Ultra-HNI core. These growth initiatives, while increasing operating costs, are viewed as foundational for future revenue accretion and achieving long-term cost efficiencies. Management affirmed confidence in India's wealth creation story and the firm's diversified platform, actively managing the transition of inactive ARR AUM for improved client engagement and yields.

## Business Strategy & Drivers

*   **India Market Opportunity:** The firm sees significant, structural growth ahead, with UHNI and HNI financial assets projected to grow at 13-14% CAGR. India's robust economic momentum and market activity (e.g., $27bn value unlocked in FY24 via OFS/IPOs) are key tailwinds.
*   **Strengthening Core & Recurring Revenue:** Continued emphasis on scaling the recurring revenue business model, with a focus on converting inactive ARR AUM to active, advisory, or distribution mandates with optimized fee structures.
*   **Strategic Growth Initiatives:**
    *   **Geographic Deepening:** Expanding market reach for core UHNI clients.
    *   **HNI Segment (Mid-Market):** Developing a dedicated proposition for the HNI segment, leveraging existing client networks and experienced relationship managers.
    *   **Global Indian Proposition:** Building a global platform for NRIs, targeting an October 2024 launch.
*   **Asset Management Growth:** Strong performance in the alternatives segment, with gross sales of ₹6,500-6,700 Crs in FY24, offsetting one-time redemptions from maturing early-stage private equity funds. A robust product pipeline is in place to sustain momentum.
*   **Talent & Efficiency:** Strategic hiring of over 35 senior private bankers in FY24 to enhance market coverage, coupled with industry-leading employee retention (5.4% voluntary attrition). Prudent capital management and sustained focus on cost efficiency are driving strong Tangible ROE.
*   **Technology & Data:** Continued investment to enhance platforms and client service capabilities across business segments.

## Management Accountability & Consistency Audit

*   **Stated Targets & Narrative Consistency:**
    *   **AUM Growth:** Total ARR AUM growth to ₹2,27,879 Crs (up 36.3% YoY) exceeded Q3 levels and consistently aligned with management's bullish "India growth story" narrative. Active ARR AUM reaching ₹1,99,606 Crs highlights strong execution on the core strategy.
    *   **ARR Revenue Focus:** FY24 Recurring Revenues grew 13.6% YoY. While its share in operating revenue (72%) was slightly lower than 9M FY24's 76%, this was primarily due to an exceptionally high transactional income in Q4. Management reiterated the strategic focus on increasing this share.
    *   **Cost Management:** The FY24 Cost-to-Income (C/I) ratio of 48.7% was in line with Q3's 9M C/I of 48.9%. The reported business-as-usual C/I of 44.4% for FY24 remained consistent with the "just over 45%" stated for the core business in Q3. Management consistently attributed elevated costs to strategic investments in new growth engines and senior hires, affirming expectations for the ratio to normalize as new initiatives accrue revenue.
    *   **Profitability:** FY24 PAT of ₹802 Crs and a Tangible ROE of 30.1% represent strong performance, exceeding Q3's 9M PAT growth and ROE, demonstrating consistent delivery on profitability and capital efficiency.
    *   **AMC Outflows:** Management confirmed "muted" net flows for the year in AMC due to "one-time activity of a block of funds getting redeemed," directly aligning with previous disclosures of "planned outflows due to maturity."
    *   **FY24 Net Flows Guidance:** FY24 total ARR Net Flows of ₹26,915 Crs came in *below* the Q3 guidance of ₹35,000-40,000 Crs. Management clarified that the prior guidance encompassed both active and inactive ARR, with the active component estimated at ₹23,000-24,000 Crs. This implies a revised focus on 'active' flows, indicating a slight recalibration in how flows are communicated.
    *   **Yields:** The non-discretionary yield (360ONEPLUS) remained at 17 bps, significantly below the 29-30 bps observed in Q3 and the Q2 FY25 target of 35 bps. Management attributed this to client re-engagement decisions and potential movement from advisory to distribution for some assets, causing "volatility in the inactive ARRAUM flows." This deviation from prior yield targets warrants close monitoring.
    *   **Dividend Payout:** The interim dividend for FY25, coupled with prior interim payouts, suggests the total FY24 payout will be at the lower end of the 70-80% policy, justified by capital conservation for loan book growth and AIF sponsorship.

*   **New Forward-Looking Promises/Guidance:**
    *   **Active ARR AUM Growth (Overall):** Targets 20-25% growth, including 8-9% mark-to-market and 15-17% from net flows.
    *   **Active ARR Net Flows (FY25):** Aims for ₹20,000-30,000 Crs (10-15% of opening active ARR AUM of ₹200,000 Crs).

Expect 4-5% increase in employee costs and 10-12% increase in admin/tech costs, leading to an overall cost increase of 5-6% (gross) or 8-10% (excluding one-offs).
    *   **Cost-to-Income Ratio (Long-term):** Expects overall C/I to reduce to 44% over the next 2-3 years.
    *   **New Business Profitability Timelines:** Geographical expansion to yield results in 18 months. HNI (mid-market) and Global businesses expected to become profitable in 24-30 months.
    *   **New Business Revenue Contribution (FY25):** Mid-market business to contribute ~2-2.5% of overall revenues. Global business revenue contribution will not be significant, launching Oct 1st.
    *   **New Business Revenue Contribution (3 years):** Mid-market to scale to 2.5-10% of overall revenues; Global 8-10%, Mid-market 15-17% of overall business (including AMC).
    *   **Transaction/Brokerage Income:** Expected annual range of ₹400-600 Crs, with a quarterly base of ₹85-90 Crs, potentially pushing to ₹125-150 Crs.

    *   **Global Business AUM Target:** First block target of ~$1 billion (~₹8,300 Crs) by end of FY25.
    *   **Mid-market Business AUM Targets:** First goal ₹10,000 Crs, then ₹25,000 Crs and ₹50,000 Crs over a reasonable period.
    *   **Reporting:** Active and Passive ARR to be reported historically from next quarter.
