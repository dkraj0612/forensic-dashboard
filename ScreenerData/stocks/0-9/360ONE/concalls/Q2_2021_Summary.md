## Executive Overview

360ONE (formerly IIFL Wealth) delivered another record-breaking quarter in Q2 FY22, showcasing robust growth across key financial and operational metrics. The company surpassed previous all-time highs in revenue, operating profit before tax, profit before tax, and PAT. Assets Under Management (AUM) continued its upward trajectory, with total AUM crossing ₹3.15 lakh crore (including custody assets). A significant milestone was achieved as Assets Under Management (ARR) assets reached ₹1.32 lakh crore, representing nearly 52% of total AUM and a strong year-on-year growth of over 67%. This reinforces the company's successful transformation towards a recurring revenue model, with recurring revenues now accounting for 71% of revenue from operations. Net flows remained steady, and the company demonstrated its commitment to capital efficiency by announcing an interim dividend of ₹20 per share, in addition to its ongoing dividend payout policy.

## Business Strategy & Drivers

*   **Recurring Revenue Model Dominance:** The company continues to execute its strategic shift to a recurring revenue model, with ARR assets now over 50% of total AUM and ARR revenue at 71% of operating revenue. Over 95% of net flows in the last six quarters have been in ARR assets, demonstrating strong client and banker confidence.
*   **IIFL One Growth:** While IIFL One AUM is progressing, management noted that its growth could be faster, acknowledging that current market momentum has favored direct investments and alternatives. Retentions on IIFL One remain steady around 33 bps, with an expectation to reach 35-45 bps excluding pure corporate advisory.
*   **Alternate Focused Asset Management:** This segment continues to scale, with AUM crossing ₹50,000 crore and closing Q2 FY22 at approximately ₹52,000 crore. Key drivers include strong momentum in listed and private equity strategies, and robust contributions from institutional, family office, and HNI client segments. Retentions remain strong at 70 bps plus.
*   **Product Innovation & Team Expertise:** Continued investment in building deep expertise across various investment strategies (long-short, real estate, private equity) and developing industry-leading propositions, including pre-IPO and alternative funds.
*   **Digital and Technology Integration:** Significant progress is being made in reimagining client and banker journeys and deploying technology for operational efficiencies. This remains a key investment area for future growth.
*   **Market Momentum & Client Behavior:** Management observed a recent preference from clients towards direct investment decisions and alternatives over setting up advisory or engagement platforms, attributing this to current market momentum rather than a long-term trend.

## Management Accountability & Consistency Audit

*   **Stated Targets & Narrative Consistency:**
    *   **AUM Growth:** Exceeded previous expectations, with total AUM crossing ₹3.15 lakh crore and wealth management AUM surpassing ₹200,000 crore, aligning with the general positive outlook.
    *   **ARR Asset & Revenue Growth:** Achieved 52% ARR asset share and 71% ARR revenue share, surpassing stated goals and demonstrating consistent execution on the recurring revenue model. The narrative consistently emphasizes this strategic shift.
    *   **IIFL One AUM:** While progressing, the management acknowledges that growth in this area could be faster, noting the current market favoring direct investments. The previous target of reaching around ₹30,000 crore seems to have been surpassed, but the pace of growth is being closely watched.
    *   **Net Flows:** Strong net flows continue, supporting the growth narrative. The impact of corporate treasuries on quarterly flows was explained as a temporary fluctuation.
    *   **Tangible ROE:** Achieved 23.7% tangible ROE, significantly exceeding the previous quarter's 20% and a key indicator of profitability.
    *   **Cost to Income Ratio:** Reduced to 48%, successfully bringing it below the 50% mark, indicating effective cost management and operating leverage.
    *   **Dividend Policy:** The declaration of an interim dividend of ₹20 per share, in addition to the commitment to a 75-80% annual PAT payout, aligns with stated capital return intentions.
    *   **NBFC Loan Book:** The loan book shows growth, contrary to previous expectations of stabilization around ₹4,000-4,500 crore. However, management clarifies the growth is primarily due to IPO funding and ESOP financing, and the core book is expected to remain stable.
    *   **Employee Costs:** Management acknowledges elevated employee costs due to the ongoing transition but expects them to normalize as a percentage of revenue going forward, as guided.
    *   **Fintech Disruption:** The focus remains on a "phygital" model and platform dependency, with no significant shift in narrative regarding Fintech.

*   **New Forward-Looking Promises/Guidance:**
    *   **Employee Cost Ratio:** Employee costs are expected to remain between 37-40% of revenue for Q3 FY22, Q4 FY22, and Q1 FY23. Post-transition (from Q2 FY23 onwards), the aim is to reduce total employee expenses to 32.5-35% of revenues and other expenses to around 12%, leading to a cost-to-income ratio of 45% in the longer term.
    *   **Transaction Revenue:** Expected to remain relatively stable around ₹75 crore, with potential for an additional ₹10-15 crore due to market conditions. The aim is to reduce transaction revenue as a percentage of total revenue to 15-20% over the next 12-24 months, driven by ARR revenue growth.
    *   **ARR Revenue Growth:** Expected to grow substantially, driving overall revenue growth from the current ₹1300 crore level towards ₹2000 crore.
    *   **Transition to ARR:** The transition from upfront revenue models to ARR assets is progressing well, with 60-75% of old assets finding their way into new products or ARR assets in the last six months. The majority of this transition is expected to be completed by Q1 FY23.
    *   **Guidance Revision:** The company has surpassed its previous guidance and will revise its guidance for the next two years towards the end of Q3 FY22, expecting a better outlook due to positive contributions from client numbers, RMs, AUM growth, predictability of business, and mark-to-market movements.
    *   **Asset Management New Strategies:** Focus on yield assets (credit, InvITs, REITs) and private equity/fund-of-fund strategies. Also exploring discretionary platforms for LRS investments. Launches will depend on platform readiness.
    *   **Loan Book Stability:** The loan book is expected to remain stable in the range of ₹3.5 to ₹4.5 crore, with current growth primarily driven by IPO funding and ESOP financing.
    *   **IPO Funding Impact:** Revenue impact from IPO funding is negligible and is viewed as a client servicing activity rather than a revenue driver. Regulatory changes are not expected to have a revenue impact.
