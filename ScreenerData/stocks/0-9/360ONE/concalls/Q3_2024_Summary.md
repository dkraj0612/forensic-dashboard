## Executive Overview

360ONE WAM (formerly IIFL Wealth) delivered a strong Q3 FY24, showcasing robust growth in AUM and revenues, with a continued strategic focus on Recurring Revenue Assets (ARR). Total ARR AUM reached ₹2,20,768 Crs, a 32.6% YoY increase, fueled by significant net inflows of ₹8,865 Crs for the quarter and ₹27,500 Crs for the first nine months. Recurring Revenues grew by 10.8% YoY to ₹338 Crs in Q3 and 9.2% YoY to ₹973 Crs in 9M FY24, maintaining their strong position at 76% of operating revenue. Profitability remained healthy, with PAT at ₹194 Crs for Q3 FY24 (up 7.7% YoY) and ₹561 Crs for 9M FY24 (up 9.3% YoY). The company declared an interim dividend of ₹4.5 per share, bringing the total interim dividend to ₹16.5 per share for the fiscal year. Strategic investments are being made in expanding into the HNI and global segments, with management expressing confidence in the India growth story and the company's platform despite higher operating costs due to these new initiatives.

## Business Strategy & Drivers

*   **Focus on ARR Growth:** Continued strategic emphasis on increasing ARR AUM and Recurring Revenues, with ARR AUM exceeding ₹2.2 lakh Crs.
*   **Wealth Management Enhancement:**
    *   **Client Acquisition:** Onboarded over 400 new clients with ₹5 Crs+ ARR AUM in 9M FY24. Clients with ARR AUM above ₹50 Crs increased by over 100. Total clients with ₹5 Crs+ AUM stand at 3,750+.
    *   **Senior Advisor Hiring:** Onboarded 35+ new hires at Partner & above level in Wealth Sales teams to expand market coverage. Average tenure for senior leadership remains high at 8+ years with low attrition (<4.5% annualized).
    *   **HNI Proposition:** On track for Q1 FY25 go-live, with expanded pilot underway. This segment targets an additional ~160-170K households.
    *   **Global Business:** Potential first-week of July launch for the global platform.
*   **Asset Management Strategy:**
    *   **Fund Performance:** Funds across various strategies and vintages remain in the top quartile.
    *   **New Product Pipeline:** SOF 12 launch this week (first close in Q3 FY24), aiming for ₹3,000-4,000 Crs. Healthcare fund targeting ₹1,000-1,500 Crs, and a new Credit Fund aiming for ₹2,000-2,500 Crs planned.
    *   **Alternatives Focus:** Bullish on alternatives, projecting 20%+ CAGR growth, leveraging market leadership and proven track record.
    *   **Channel Presence:** Deepening channel presence via Mutual Fund Distributors (MFDs) and national distributors, with improved brand and performance recognition.
*   **Capital Management:** Maiden NCD public issue for NBFC arm was over-subscribed, demonstrating investor confidence.

## Management Accountability & Consistency Audit

*   **Stated Targets & Narrative Consistency:**
    *   **AUM Growth:** Reported Total ARR AUM of ₹2,20,768 Crs (up 32.6% YoY) and 9M FY24 flows of ₹27,500 Crs aligns with stated growth trajectory. Management reiterates bullishness on the "India growth story."
    *   **ARR Revenue Focus:** Recurring Revenues increased by 10.8% YoY and constituted 76% of operating revenue, reinforcing the ARR strategy.
    *   **Cost Management:** Operating costs rose by 24.3% YoY to ₹232 Crs, with the Cost-to-Income ratio at 49.6%. Management attributed the elevated costs to investments in new segments (HNI, Global) and senior hires, noting these are yet to generate corresponding revenues. They expect the C/I ratio to settle down over the next few quarters as new initiatives contribute. The core business C/I is stated to be just over 45%.
    *   **Profitability:** PAT for 9M FY24 grew by 9.3% YoY to ₹561 Crs. Tangible ROE remained robust at 28.9%.
    *   **Active ARR AUM & Yields:** Active ARR AUM increased by ₹14K crore in the quarter. Non-discretionary yields have stabilized at 29-30 bps, with an expectation to move towards 35 bps by Q2 FY25 for active non-discretionary assets. Discretionary yields are expected to improve next year after platform rejig.
    *   **AMC Outflows:** Planned outflows due to maturity of late-stage/pre-IPO funds were noted (₹3.5k Crs in Q3, ₹1.2k Crs in Q2). Gross traction remains strong, supporting confidence in strategies.

*   **New Forward-Looking Promises/Guidance:**
    *   **FY24 Net Flows Guidance:** Expects to reach ₹35,000-40,000 Crs for FY24.
    *   **FY25 Net Flows Guidance:** Targets at least 20-30% higher flows than FY24, potentially ₹50,000-55,000 Crs for the core wealth business. Global and HNI businesses are expected to add a combined ~₹10,000 Crs in the next financial year (approx. 60-70% HNI, 30-40% Global).
    *   **Active Flow Ratio:** Aim to push the active ARR flows percentage closer to 65-70% of total ARR flows.
    *   **HNI Business Reporting:** Will start reporting separately from April 2024 (Q1 FY25).
    *   **Global Business Reporting:** Expected to commence potentially in July 2024.
    *   **Wealth Sales Team Maturity:** Teams mature and break even in 15-18 months; contributions expected by Q3/Q4 FY25.
    *   **New Business Contribution Timeline:** Global and HNI businesses are expected to see numbers coming through from Q4 FY25 onwards.
    *   **Cost-to-Income Ratio Trajectory:** FY25 to remain elevated with 2.5%-3% incremental C/I due to investments in Global and HNI businesses. Target to claw back to 45% by FY26.
    *   **Non-Discretionary Yield Improvement:** Active non-discretionary assets to move towards 35 bps by Q2 FY25. Blended discretionary and non-discretionary yields targeted at 40-45 bps.
    *   **HNI/Global Business Retentions:** Expected at 70-75 bps.
    *   **HNI Segment Growth Potential:** Within 3-5 years, yearly flows from HNI business could reach 30-50% of UHNW flows, potentially equaling UHNW flows in 4-5 years.
    *   **Non-Discretionary Assets Not Earning Yield:** ~₹34,000 Crs out of ₹2.2 lakh Crs ARR are not earning yield, with conversion expected over the next four quarters.
    *   **Transaction Income (TBR):** Expected to remain stable around ₹75-115 Crs per quarter, due to its nature being less correlated to capital markets activity than ECM/Investment Banking fees.
    *   **AMC Cost-to-Income:** Expected to improve with AUM growth; teams are in place to manage 2x-2.5x current AUM.
    *   **Carry Income:** AMC carry income benchmarked at ~₹15 Crs per quarter (15 bps on ₹40,000 Crs non-listed/carry AUM). Wealth carry income estimated at ~₹20 Crs annually, episodic. Total carry income between ₹15-20 Crs per quarter.
    *   **New Business Margin Profile:** Expected to be similar to the core Wealth Management business (45%-47% C/I ratio).
    *   **RM Count Addition:** Wealth sales senior capacity is largely built out (85-90% done).
    *   **Geographical Expansion Margins:** Margin profile in new geographies is expected to be similar to existing locations.
    *   **Ultra-HNI Yield:** Pure advisory yield at 40-45 bps. Distribution yield also around 40-45 bps blended. Long-term yield for alternatives expected at 75-80 bps. Overall blended yield maintained around 70 bps.
    *   **NBFC Capital Book:** Maintain ₹1,500 Crs NBFC capital book with a gearing of around 4x.
    *   **AIF Investments Release:** Expects release of ₹300-400 Crs per year from investments in own AIFs over the next 2-3 years.
    *   **ESOP Policy:** Approved by shareholders, linking incentives to time spent, business plan achievement, and firm performance.
