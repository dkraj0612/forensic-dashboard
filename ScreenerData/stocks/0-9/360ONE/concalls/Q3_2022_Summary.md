## Executive Overview

360ONE (formerly IIFL Wealth) reported a strong Q3 FY22, demonstrating robust growth across key financial metrics driven by increasing Assets Under Management (AUM) and a sustained focus on recurring revenue models. Total AUM surpassed ₹328,000 crore, with a significant 31% YoY increase in overall AUM (excluding custody) to ₹2.62 lakh crore. Crucially, Recurring Revenue Assets (ARR Assets) saw a 54% YoY surge to ₹1.39 lakh crore, now comprising approximately 53% of total AUM, reinforcing the company's strategic shift. Revenue from operations grew by 58% YoY to ₹378 crore, with recurring revenues up 59% YoY to ₹245 crore. The company achieved its highest ever quarterly Profit After Tax (PAT) of ₹155 crore, marking a 59% YoY increase. Tangible Return on Equity (ROE) also saw a significant improvement, reaching approximately 27% for the quarter. Management provided revised guidance for FY22 and FY23, signaling confidence in sustained growth and profitability.

## Business Strategy & Drivers

*   **Recurring Revenue Focus:** Continued emphasis on increasing the share of ARR assets in total AUM, with a target of 55-60% over the next 12-24 months.
*   **Diversified Asset Management:** Building leadership in Listed Equity and Private Equity, with recent success in raising a large late-stage tech fund. Expanding into credit and yield strategies with successful fund raises.
*   **Distribution Network Expansion:** Significant improvement in distribution acceptability, with 35-40% of asset management collections now coming from outside distributors, projected to reach 60-65% in 12-18 months. Focus on building an institutional distribution network.
*   **Product Innovation & Team Building:** Continued investment in hiring senior professionals (e.g., Aakash Desai for Private Credit) and enhancing product offerings across various asset classes.
*   **Digital Transformation:** Significant investment in technology and data-based intelligence across client and RM journeys to enhance analytics, client engagement, and operational efficiency.
*   **Client Engagement:** Focus on serving the ultra-HNI and HNI segments, with particular strength in the ₹15 crore+ AUM category. Efforts to re-invent for the ₹5-15 crore segment.
