## Executive Overview

360 ONE WAM delivered a strong Q3 FY26, exceeding previous performance benchmarks with its highest-ever quarterly PAT of ₹331 Crs, a 20.3% YoY increase. The company demonstrated robust growth across its AUM, reaching ₹3,17,906 Crs (+28% YoY), with Wealth ARR AUM at ₹218,957 Crs and Asset Management ARR AUM at ₹98,949 Crs. Net flows were substantial at ₹14,758 Crs for the quarter and ₹46,890 Crs for the nine months ended December 31, 2025, indicating strong client acquisition and deepening wallet share. Key strategic initiatives, including the UBS Global Collaboration Framework and the rebranding of B&K Securities to 360 ONE Capital, are progressing well, laying the groundwork for future synergies and enhanced service offerings. The company's focus remains on consolidating its full-stack financial services platform, driven by a commitment to talent acquisition and technological advancement.

## Business Strategy & Drivers

*   **Core Business Strength:** Continued robust net flows in Wealth Management, driven by both wallet share expansion from existing clients and new client acquisitions. Asset Management saw strong mobilization across various strategies, including real assets, private credit, and listed mid/small-cap funds.
*   **Strategic Integrations & Rebranding:** The UBS Global Collaboration Framework for Wealth is in its early stages with cross-border referrals showing traction. B&K Securities has been rebranded to 360 ONE Capital, integrating its corporate and institutional equities capabilities to enhance the company's comprehensive financial services platform.
*   **HNI & ET Money Development:** The HNI (Reserve) business has grown significantly to over ₹3,000 Crs and is on track to accelerate scaling. ET Money is transitioning into a comprehensive wealth platform for the affluent segment.
*   **Alternate Asset Management:** Continued buoyancy in the Alternatives business, supported by new SEBI initiatives like co-investment vehicles and large value funds. Performance of existing funds remains strong.
*   **Global & Capital Markets Expansion:** Focus on expanding the global presence, including exploring opportunities in Gift City. The equity brokerage and investment banking practice is under development.
*   **Technology & Talent:** Continued investment in technology, including AI-powered pilots. Emphasis on attracting and retaining top talent, with a structured approach to compensation and empowerment.

## Management Accountability & Consistency Audit

### Audit of Stated Targets & Narrative Consistency:

*   **AUM Growth & Net Flows:** The company has demonstrated strong AUM growth (+28% YoY) and robust net flows (₹14,758 Crs in Q3 FY26), aligning with and exceeding expectations set in previous quarters. The commentary indicates sustained momentum and confidence in achieving full-year targets.
*   **Profitability:** Achieved the highest-ever quarterly PAT, validating the company's growth trajectory and operational efficiency. The Tangible ROE also saw an improvement.
*   **UBS Collaboration:** The Global Collaboration Framework for Wealth was signed, indicating progress on this strategic initiative. Actual business translation is expected to commence from April onwards.
*   **B&K Integration:** The rebranding to 360 ONE Capital signifies continued integration, with management expecting to see fruits in equity brokerage over the next 12 months and in banking thereafter.
*   **HNI Segment:** The Reserve (HNI) business has seen substantial AUM growth and is expected to break even in the next 3-6 months. Management acknowledges that revenue growth will take time to accumulate due to its trail-based nature.
*   **Talent Acquisition & Retention:** Management reiterated its philosophy of careful and selective talent acquisition, focusing on culture and long-term alignment rather than aggressive hiring. Protection of existing talent is being managed through culture, product offerings, and aligned interests.
*   **TBR Sustainability:** The integration of B&K is expected to enhance the sustainability and predictability of Transactional Business Revenue (TBR). The quality of TBR is noted as improved.

### New Forward-Looking Promises/Guidance:

*   **FY27 Guidance:** While specific numbers for FY27 were not explicitly stated, management reiterated its model of needing 10-12% net flows of closing AUM for consistent growth, implying a higher absolute target for FY27 based on FY26's closing AUM.
*   **Cost to Income Ratio:** Target for improvement to 45-46% in the next year (FY27). Expectation of 150-200 bps recovery from HNI (Reserve) and ET Money businesses breaking even, and 100-150 bps improvement from core business productivity gains.
*   **HNI Segment Break-even:** Expected within the next 3-6 months.
*   **ET Money Break-even:** Expected on a run-rate basis towards the end of FY27.
*   **Equity Brokerage Growth:** Expected 25-30% increase in high-net-worth equity brokerage over the next 12 months.
*   **Banking Practice Development:** Expected to build out significantly in the year after next (FY28), with initial hiring of 6-8 business heads and selective hiring thereafter.
*   **Profitability (3-Year Outlook):** Aiming for ₹1,800-2,100 Crs of Profit After Tax in three years from April 2025 (i.e., by April 2028), based on 22-24% AUM growth, 16-18% revenue growth, and 22-24% profit growth.
*   **Carry Assumption:** 20-25 bps for Alternatives (Alt AUM). Combined retention for Alts (management fee + carry) approximately 95-100 bps. Listed side retention around 60 bps. Blended retention around 80 bps.
*   **Quarterly Carry Recognition:** Approximately ₹25-40 Crs per quarter is the typical assumption, with potential for uptick from specific events.
*   **UHNI RM Growth:** Target to reach 300-350 RMs over the next 3-4 years, adding 40-50 bankers annually. Current UHNI RM count is ~191.
*   **UBS Collaboration Monetization:** Actual numbers are expected to translate from April onwards. Unit economics will be market-based, with early years favouring the introducer.
*   **Discretionary PMS Retention:** Expected to remain around 45-50 bps (outside of specific mandate impacts). Non-discretionary PMS retention between 28-35 bps.
*   **AMC Product Pipeline:** Multiple products in private, Mutual Fund (SIF), Real Assets, and REITs segments, along with GIFT City offerings.
*   **Capital Allocation:** Continued deployment in Alternates and NBFC business. Dividends expected to be 45-70% of profits (ex-Alts and NBFC). Aspiration for ROE in mid-20s (ex-intangibles) and high teens (including goodwill).
