## Executive Overview

IIFL Wealth Management (now 360ONE) reported a stable financial performance for Q3 FY20, with growth across key metrics driven by strong client acquisition and AUM growth, particularly in its Wealth Management and Asset Management segments. The company saw a 5% QoQ increase in overall AUM to Rs 150,762 Cr, with a notable 10% QoQ growth in Annual Recurring Revenues (ARR) to Rs 70,434 Cr. Overall revenues grew 15% QoQ to Rs 244 Cr. Profit Before Tax (PBT) saw a 22% QoQ increase to Rs 100 Cr, and Profit After Tax (PAT) rose 9% QoQ to Rs 76 Cr. The company declared an interim dividend of Rs 10, reinforcing its commitment to a 50-75% dividend payout policy. Strategic initiatives like the integration of Wealth Advisors (WAI) and the pending acquisition of L&T Finance's wealth management business are expected to further bolster growth. Management noted an improvement in client sentiment for new investments, though macroeconomic factors remain an overhang.

## Business Strategy & Drivers

*   **Client Acquisition & Retention:** Strong focus on adding new clients and maintaining high retention rates, with over 100 "relevant families" added in the last quarter.
*   **AUM Growth:** Driving growth across Wealth Management and Asset Management through product enhancements, strategic acquisitions, and increased distribution coverage.
*   **Product Focus:** Continued emphasis on flagship propositions like IIFL ONE (PMS) and scaling up AIF/PMS products in Asset Management.
*   **Productivity & Cost Optimization:** Ongoing initiatives to improve efficiency, with impacts expected in Q4FY20/Q1FY21.
*   **Consolidation:** Leveraging industry consolidation trends for client and talent acquisition opportunities.
*   **Dividend Policy:** Commitment to maintaining a dividend payout of 50-75% of yearly PAT.
*   **Strategic Acquisitions:** Awaiting statutory approval for the acquisition of L&T Finance's wealth management business.
*   **Wealth-NBFC Synergy:** NBFC acting as an enabler for the Wealth Management business, with zero credit losses on its loan book.
