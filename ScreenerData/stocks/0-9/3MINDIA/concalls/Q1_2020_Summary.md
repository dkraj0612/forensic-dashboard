## Executive Overview

The 33rd Annual General Meeting (AGM) of 3M India was held virtually due to the COVID-19 pandemic. The meeting covered the company's performance for the Financial Year 2019-20 and the first quarter of FY 2020-21, alongside updates on business strategy and management actions. The company navigated a challenging economic environment marked by a softening Indian economy and the unprecedented impact of COVID-19. Management emphasized resilience, business continuity, employee safety, and supply of essential commodities, particularly N95 masks and hand sanitizers. While FY 2019-20 saw a consolidated revenue de-growth of 1% and a decline in operating margins, the company focused on cost discipline and cash flow management. The first quarter of FY 2020-21 experienced a significant sales decline (57%) and a Profit Before Tax (PBT) loss due to the pandemic's impact, but sequential improvements were noted in May and June. The company highlighted its commitment to leveraging its global network and local capabilities for sustainable growth, with a focus on specific sectors and continued investment in innovation and local manufacturing.

## Key Financial Metrics

*   **FY 2019-20 (Consolidated):**
    *   Sales: ₹2987 Crores (1% lower than previous year)
    *   EBITDA: 16.3%
    *   Profit Before Tax (PBT): ₹433 Crores (14.3% margin)
    *   Profit After Tax (PAT) from continuing operations: ₹322 Crores
    *   Consolidated Other Expenses (excluding employee and material costs): Reduced by 14% YoY.
*   **FY 2019-20 (Standalone):**
    *   Revenue from operations: De-grew by 1.56%
    *   Operating Margin: 13.59% (vs. 19.02% in FY 2018-19)
*   **Segment Performance (FY 2019-20):**
    *   Safety & Industrial: -4.3% growth
    *   Transportation & Electronics: 0% to ~5% growth
    *   Healthcare: 0% to ~5% growth
    *   Consumer: 0% to ~5% growth
*   **Q1 FY 2020-21 (Consolidated):**
    *   Sales: ₹346 Crores (57% decline YoY)
    *   Profit Before Tax (PBT): Loss of ₹58 Crores
    *   Expenses (excluding employee costs): Reduced by ~30% vs. prior year.
    *   Cash position: Improved ~8% consolidated by end-June 2020 vs. end-March 2020.

## Business Strategy & Drivers

*   **COVID-19 Response:** Prioritized employee safety (remote work, safety protocols), ensured supply of essential commodities (respirators, hand sanitizers), and supported communities through CSR initiatives.
*   **Resilience for Sustainable Growth:** Focus on mitigating near-term impact while positioning for long-term growth.
*   **Leveraging Global & Local Capabilities:** Utilizing 3M's global R&D, technology platforms, and local manufacturing capacity (Ranjangaon plant) to meet demand and contribute to crisis response.
*   **Sector Focus:** Continued focus on Healthcare, Consumer, Energy, and critical industries, aligning with current priorities and early recovery trends.
*   **Customer Engagement:** Virtual customer engagement programs focused on safety, sanitization, and hygiene. Accelerating adoption of digital commerce and e-commerce channels.
*   **Innovation & Product Development:** Development of new products in vehicle interior cleaning, road safety, and new multi-pack SKUs for consumers. Local manufacturing projects for hand sanitizers, disinfectants, and BS-VI emission control materials.
*   **Portfolio Management:** Decision to exit the automotive graphics business (contributing <5% of revenue) due to inadequate technology differentiation and diluted margins.
*   **CSR Initiatives:** 100% deployment of CSR budget, supporting education (Project Nanhi-Kali), skilling (Project PAHAL), and road safety initiatives.

## Management Accountability & Consistency Audit

*   **Stated Targets/Guidance:**
    *   **FY 2019-20 Performance vs. Previous Year:**
        *   **Revenue:** Consolidated revenue de-grew by 1%, against an implied expectation of stable or modest growth given the prior year's performance. Management acknowledged "softening Indian economy" and "weak external market conditions."
        *   **Margins:** Operating margin declined from 18.75% to 16.34% (consolidated) and standalone operating margin fell to 13.59% from 19.02%. Management attributed this to weak market conditions and exercised cost discipline.
        *   **PBT:** Consolidated PBT was ₹433 Crores, a decrease from the prior year, with nearly ₹80 Crores of the dip attributed to an impairment charge.
    *   **Q1 FY 2020-21 Performance vs. Expectations:**
        *   **Sales:** A significant decline of 57% was reported, a direct consequence of the COVID-19 lockdown, exceeding the previously discussed "headwinds."
        *   **PBT:** A loss of ₹58 Crores was incurred, a stark contrast to prior profitable quarters, but the company noted sequential improvement from April to June.
    *   **Narrative/Tone Shift Regarding Headwinds:**
        *   Management consistently acknowledged prevailing economic challenges and the impact of COVID-19, framing their response around resilience and business continuity.
        *   The tone shifted to acknowledge the severe impact of the pandemic in Q1 FY 2020-21, leading to unprecedented declines in sales and a PBT loss, while emphasizing sequential recovery.
        *   While no specific targets were explicitly missed in the transcript, the reported decline in revenue and margins for FY 2019-20 and the significant drop in Q1 FY 2020-21 represent a departure from the consistent growth seen in prior periods (as per historical context).
        *   The company attributed PBT decline in FY19-20 to impairment charges and acknowledged that the consumer business, though performing better segment-wise, remains the smallest.

*   **New Forward-Looking Promises/Timelines:**
    *   **Consumer Business Growth:** Aim to increase contribution to the total portfolio (currently 11.6%) by strengthening brands, broadening portfolios in cleaning/scouring/home improvement, and accelerating digital/e-commerce channels.
    *   **Capex Plans:** Capex remains "lumpy." Ongoing projects include new lines for BS-VI compliant emission control materials, increased capacity for hand sanitizers and disinfectants, and new ranges of disinfectants. Current projects exceed ₹100 Crores.
    *   **Local Manufacturing:** Continue to improve local manufacturing content (currently 56-57% of mix) to improve PBT.
    *   **Mask Supply:** Increased N95 mask supply from <5 lakh to ~50 lakh pieces between April and July 2020, prioritizing healthcare, government, and critical industries.
    *   **Electro and Communications (E&C) Amalgamation:** Following the failure to pass the amalgamation via the 90% assent route (due to non-responses, not negative votes), the company is proceeding through the normal NCLT process and will provide updates.
    *   **Castrol Tie-up:** Engaging with Castrol to revive the vehicle care products program, with good expectations following a pause due to COVID-19.
    *   **Website Improvement:** Acknowledged feedback regarding the investor-unfriendly nature of the website and will take note of suggestions for improvement.
    *   **Share Split:** The board will continue to review all options for share split and other growth opportunities.
    *   **CEO Tenure:** Management will convey the point on the tenure of Managing Directors to the nomination and remuneration committee of the board.
    *   **Intellectual Property Protection:** A dedicated cell is actively reviewing logo infringements and fake products, working with social media sites and law enforcement.
    *   **Product Visibility:** Acknowledged the need for better product visibility and potentially experience centers, will consider distribution pamphlets.
    *   **RPT Approvals:** The omnibus approval for RPTs (estimated 2.5 times higher than FY20 actuals) was approved pre-COVID and will align with sales activities and evolving manufacturing mix.
    *   **Dividend Policy:** No dividend was declared, with management reiterating the focus on reinvesting capital for growth and maintaining strong cash reserves due to market uncertainty. The board will continue to review dividend options.
