This revision stems mainly from reduced production estimates in India and several other Asian countries, with global output now forecasted at 189.1 million metric tons. Looking ahead, Green Pool projects a recovery in 2025-26, with a surplus of 1.14 million metric tons and global sugar production revised upward to 199.1 million metric tons. In contrast, the International Sugar Organization (ISO) has issued a lower estimate, pegging 2024-25 global production at 175.5 million metric tons. ❑In Centre-South Brazil, final sugar production for the 2024-25 season has been reported at 40.17 million metric tons. Expectations of a bumper crop in the 2025-26 season are reinforcing a bearish sentiment in the market. Currently, raw sugar prices are hovering around 17.5 cents per pound, while white sugar is trading at approximately USD 490 per metric ton. The anticipation of higher supply in the upcoming season continues to exert downward pressure on price. ❑For India, net sugar production in 2024-25 is projected at 26.4 million tons, factoring in the diversion of around 3.5 million tons for ethanol. This implies a gross production of 29.9 million tons-down over 12% from the previous season’s gross output of 34.11 million tons (with 2.15 million tons diverted for ethanol, resulting in a net production of 31.96 million tons). The year-on-year decline is mainly due to a poor sugarcane crop in Maharashtra and Karnataka, impacted by adverse weather, and reduced yields and recovery rates in Uttar Pradesh. ❑While international agencies are forecasting a strong 2025-26 crop for India, ISMA has yet to release its official estimate. However, ISMA has projected year-end stock levels at 5.4 million tons, factoring in 1 million tons of exports and 28 million tons of domestic consumption. This equates to approximately 2 to 3 months of consumption. ❑Given the adequate opening stock and expected sufficient closing stocks for the 2024-25 season, the government has permitted the export of 1 million tons of sugar. Additionally, mills have been granted the flexibility to either surrender or exchange their export quota with the domestic quota. Sector outlook ❑Domestic sugar prices remained subdued below Rs. 38,000 per metric ton through the end of the third quarter of FY 2024-25, largely due to weak international prices, uncertainty surrounding domestic production estimates, and higher monthly release quotas. However, beginning January 2025, prices began to recover, supported by improved market sentiment and the announcement of export permissions. Currently, sugar prices are hovering around Rs. 4,000 per quintal. ❑According to recent reports, approximately 707 crore liters of ethanol were blended during the Ethanol Supply Year (ESY) 2023-24. Of this, slightly less than 50% was supplied by the sugar sector, while the remaining came from the grain sector. The overall ethanol blending rate achieved during the year stood at 14.6%. At the beginning of Sugar Season (SS) 2023-24, the government had imposed restrictions on the use of sugarcane juice, syrup, and B-heavy molasses for ethanol production. However, for ESY 2024-25, these restrictions were lifted. This enabled potential diversion of up to 3.5 million tons of sugar towards ethanol production, significantly boosting ethanol output for the year. According to the latest reports from the Government of India, Oil Marketing Companies (OMCs) have procured approximately 369.54 crore liters of ethanol as of March 31, 2025. Of this, the sugar sector contributed 181.03 crore liters, while the grain sector supplied 188.51 crore liters- representing a contribution share of 48.98% and 51.02%, respectively. As of March 31, 2025, India has achieved a current average ethanol blending rate of 18.36%, with a monthly average of 19.78% recorded for March 2025. ❑Despite repeated appeals from ISMA, the government has not revised the ethanol procurement prices for ethanol produced from sugarcane juice/syrup and B-heavy molasses for the second consecutive Ethanol Supply Year (ESY). This stagnation in pricing comes despite an increase in the Fair and Remunerative Price (FRP) of sugarcane, which was intended to be the basis for determining ethanol procurement rates. This has discouraged many sugar mills from utilizing cane juice or syrup for ethanol production, prompting a shift toward a more remunerative strategy-focusing on increased sugar production and using B-heavy molasses as the preferred feedstock for ethanol. ❑During Sugar Season (SS) 2023-24, Uttar Pradesh produced approximately 10.41 million tons of sugar. However, for SS 2024-25, net sugar production in the state is estimated to fall below 9.5 million tons, after accounting for higher diversion of cane juice and B-heavy molasses toward ethanol production. ❑The Uttar Pradesh State Government has announced the State Advised Price (SAP) for the sugar season 2024-25, maintaining it at the same level as the previous season.
